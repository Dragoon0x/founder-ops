# Founder Fundraising Toolkit

## Pre-Fundraise Checklist
- [ ] Metrics dashboard live and accurate (MRR, growth, churn, CAC, LTV)
- [ ] Financial model with 3 scenarios (base, bull, bear)
- [ ] Cap table clean and up-to-date
- [ ] Deck draft reviewed by 3 trusted advisors
- [ ] Data room organized (see below)
- [ ] Target investor list with warm intro paths identified
- [ ] Forwardable blurb written
- [ ] Calendar cleared for 4-6 weeks of intense meetings

## Data Room Structure

```
├── /Financials

│   ├── Balance sheet
│   ├── Financial projections (24 months)
│   └── Revenue by customer (anonymized if needed)
├── /Metrics
│   ├── MRR bridge (new, expansion, contraction, churn)

│   └── Funnel metrics

```

## Investor Meeting Prep

### Before every meeting
- Research the investor: recent investments, blog posts, thesis
- Know their fund size and check size (don't pitch a $500M fund on a $1M check)
- Prepare for their likely questions based on their portfolio (if they invested in X, they'll compare you to X)

### The pitch (20 min presentation, leave 10 min for Q&A)
1. Hook (30 sec): The most compelling stat or insight about your market
2. Problem (2 min): What's broken? Who suffers? How much does it cost them?
3. Solution (3 min): What you built. Demo if possible.
4. Traction (3 min): Numbers that prove it's working. MRR, growth, retention, notable customers.
5. Market (2 min): How big is the opportunity? (Bottom-up TAM, not top-down)
6. Business model (2 min): How you make money. Unit economics.
7. Team (2 min): Why this team? Relevant experience, founder-market fit.
8. Competition (2 min): Who else is here? Why do you win?
9. Ask (1 min): How much, what milestones, how the money will be used.
10. Close (1 min): The reason this investor specifically should be excited.

### After every meeting
Log in your CRM within 1 hour:
- Meeting date + investor name + firm
- Vibe: hot / warm / cool / cold
- Key questions asked
- Concerns raised
- Next steps (and date)
- Follow-up items
