# Customer Research Toolkit

## Research Methods Menu

| Method | Best For | Time | Cost | Sample Size |
|--------|---------|------|------|-------------|
| Customer interviews | Deep understanding, discovery | 45-60 min each | Low | 10-20 |
| Surveys | Quantifying patterns | 2-3 weeks | Low-Medium | 100-500 |
| Usability testing | UX problems, workflow issues | 30-45 min each | Low | 5-8 |
| Session recordings | Understanding actual behavior | Passive | Low (tool cost) | 50-100 |
| Support ticket analysis | Pain point patterns | 2-4 hrs | Free | All tickets |
| Sales call analysis | Objection patterns, language | 1-2 hrs/week | Free | 10-20 calls |
| NPS follow-up | Promoter/detractor insights | 15-20 min each | Low | 10-20 |
| Jobs-to-be-Done interviews | Market positioning, messaging | 45-60 min each | Low | 12-20 |

## Customer Interview Guide

### Interview structure (45 min)
1. **Context** (5 min): "Tell me about your role and your team"
2. **Current workflow** (10 min): "Walk me through how you currently handle [problem area]"
3. **Pain points** (10 min): "What's the hardest part? What takes the most time? What frustrates you?"
4. **Impact** (5 min): "What does this problem cost you in time/money/stress?"
5. **Solutions tried** (5 min): "What have you tried to solve this? What worked/didn't?"
6. **Product feedback** (5 min): "How does our product fit into your workflow? What's missing?"
7. **Wrap-up** (5 min): "What would make this product a no-brainer for you?"

### Interview rules
- Ask open questions. "Tell me about..." not "Do you like..."
- Follow the energy. When they get animated, go deeper.
- Listen for behavior, not opinion. "What do you actually do?" not "What would you do?"
- Don't pitch. You're here to learn, not sell.
- Record (with permission). You'll miss details in notes.
- Debrief within 1 hour. Key insights fade fast.

### Analysis framework
After 10+ interviews, synthesize:
1. Group quotes and observations into themes
2. Count frequency of each theme
3. Map themes to your product/roadmap
4. Identify the top 3 pain points that are: frequent, painful, and solvable by you
5. Present findings to the team with direct quotes (quotes are more persuasive than summaries)

## Survey Design Rules
- Max 10 questions for a general survey
- Max 5 questions for a pulse check
- One question per topic (don't ask two things in one question)
- Scale questions: 1-5 or 1-7 (not 1-10, too many options)
- Always include one open-ended question: "What's the one thing you wish we'd change?"
- Send at natural moments: after onboarding, after support interaction, at renewal time
- Response rate benchmarks: in-app survey > 20%, email survey > 10%, cold survey > 3%
