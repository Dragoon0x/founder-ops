# Burn Multiple

## Definition
How much cash you burn to generate each dollar of net new ARR.

## Formula
```
Burn Multiple = Net Cash Burned / Net New ARR
```

Both measured over the same period (quarterly or annually).

## Interpretation
| Burn Multiple | Rating | Meaning |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| < 1x | Amazing | You're generating more ARR than you're burning |
| 1-1.5x | Great | Efficient growth |
| 1.5-2x | Good | Typical for growth-stage companies |
| 2-3x | Mediocre | Burning too much relative to growth |
| > 3x | Bad | Unsustainable without a clear path to improvement |

## Why burn multiple beats "growth at all costs"
In 2021, investors rewarded pure growth. Post-2022, efficiency matters.
Burn multiple captures both growth AND efficiency in one number.

A company growing 100% YoY with a 5x burn multiple is less impressive than a company growing 80% YoY with a 1.5x burn multiple.

## Benchmarks by stage

| Stage | Target Burn Multiple |
|-------|---------------------|
| Pre-seed | N/A (pre-revenue, burn is expected) |
| Seed | 3-5x (acceptable while finding PMF) |
| Series A | 1.5-2.5x |
| Series B | 1-2x |
| Series C+ | < 1.5x |

## Improvement levers
1. **Increase ARR growth rate** (more revenue from same spend)
2. **Reduce sales & marketing spend** (improve conversion, not just volume)
3. **Reduce hiring pace** (do more with current team before scaling)
4. **Improve pricing** (higher ACV from same customers)
5. **Reduce infrastructure costs** (optimize cloud spend, vendor negotiation)

## Common mistakes
1. Using gross burn instead of net burn (doesn't account for revenue)
2. Measuring quarterly when seasonality is strong (use TTM)
3. Ignoring one-time expenses (a large one-time cost inflates the multiple temporarily)
4. Not tracking trend (a burn multiple of 2x and improving is different from 2x and worsening)

## Alert thresholds
- Burn multiple > 3x for 2+ consecutive quarters
- Burn multiple increasing while growth rate is flat or declining
- Burn multiple > 2x and runway < 18 months
