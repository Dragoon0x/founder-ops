# Rule of 40

## Definition
The sum of your revenue growth rate and profit margin should equal or exceed 40%.

## Formula
```
Rule of 40 Score = Revenue Growth Rate (%) + Profit Margin (%)
```

Typically measured annually. Revenue growth rate is YoY ARR growth. Profit margin can be EBITDA margin, operating margin, or FCF margin (specify which you're using).

## Interpretation
| Score | Rating | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| > 60 | Elite | Exceptional combination of growth and profitability |
| 40-60 | Strong | Healthy, investable business |
| 25-40 | Acceptable | Needs improvement in growth or efficiency |
| < 25 | Concerning | Burning cash without sufficient growth to justify it |

## Tradeoff spectrum
The Rule of 40 captures the growth-profitability tradeoff:
- 80% growth + -40% margin = 40 (hyper-growth, burning cash)
- 40% growth + 0% margin = 40 (strong growth, breakeven)
- 20% growth + 20% margin = 40 (moderate growth, profitable)
- 5% growth + 35% margin = 40 (slow growth, highly profitable)

All score 40, but they're very different businesses. Investors generally prefer the higher-growth version at earlier stages and the higher-margin version at later stages.

## When to use it
- Board reporting (simple, widely understood)
- Benchmarking against public SaaS companies
- Strategic planning (helps frame the growth vs. efficiency tradeoff)

## Limitations
1. Doesn't account for the quality of revenue (expansion vs. new)
2. Doesn't distinguish between recurring and non-recurring revenue
3. A company with 100% growth and -60% margin scores 40 but may be unsustainable
4. More relevant for companies > $10M ARR (below that, focus on growth)

## Benchmarks (public SaaS)
| Company Profile | Typical Score |
|----------------|--------------|
| Top quartile public SaaS | > 50 |
| Median public SaaS | 25-35 |
| Bottom quartile | < 20 |
