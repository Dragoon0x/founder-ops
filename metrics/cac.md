# Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

## Definition
The total cost to acquire one new paying customer.

## Formula
```
CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Spend / Number of New Customers Acquired
```

In the same time period (typically monthly or quarterly).

## What to include in S&M spend
- Sales team salaries + commissions + benefits
- Marketing team salaries + benefits
- Advertising spend (paid search, social, display, sponsorships)
- Content production costs
- Marketing tools/software
- Events and conferences (including travel)
- Sales tools/software (CRM, outreach tools)
- Agency fees

## What NOT to include
- Product development costs
- Customer success / support costs (post-sale)
- General overhead allocated to marketing
- One-time brand investments (website redesign, brand identity)

## Variants

### Blended CAC
Total S&M spend / all new customers.
Mixes organic and paid acquisition. Good for board-level reporting.

### Paid CAC
Only paid marketing spend / customers acquired through paid channels.
Better for channel-level optimization.

### Fully-loaded CAC
Includes all costs (even overhead allocation).
Most conservative. Some investors prefer this.

## Benchmarks

| Business Model | Good CAC | Payback Target |
|---------------|---------|----------------|
| PLG / self-serve | < $500 | < 6 months |
| Inside sales (SMB) | $500-5,000 | < 12 months |
| Inside sales (mid-market) | $5,000-25,000 | < 15 months |
| Field sales (enterprise) | $25,000-100,000 | < 18 months |

## CAC by channel (typical ranges)
| Channel | CAC Range | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Organic / SEO | $50-500 | Low cost but slow to build |
| Content marketing | $100-1,000 | Compounds over time |
| Paid search | $200-2,000 | Fast but expensive at scale |
| Paid social | $300-3,000 | Good for awareness, weaker for conversion |
| Referral | $100-500 | Best CAC, limited scale |
| Outbound sales | $5,000-50,000 | High cost, high ACV deals |
| Events | $2,000-20,000 | Relationship-building, hard to attribute |

## Common mistakes
1. Not including sales salaries (biggest undercount)
2. Measuring in wrong time period (spend today, customer converts in 60 days)
3. Not segmenting by channel (blended CAC hides expensive channels)
4. Ignoring ramp time for new sales reps (first 3 months are expensive)
5. Counting free users as "customers" (inflates denominator, deflates CAC)

## Alert thresholds
- CAC increasing MoM for 3+ months without corresponding ACV increase
- CAC payback > 18 months
- LTV/CAC ratio < 3:1
- Any single channel CAC > 2x blended CAC (channel is too expensive)
