# Pipeline Velocity (Sales Velocity)

## Definition
The speed at which your sales pipeline generates revenue.

## Formula
```
Pipeline Velocity = (Number of Opportunities × Win Rate × Average Deal Size) / Sales Cycle Length (days)
```

Result is in $/day of revenue generation capacity.

## Components breakdown

### Number of Opportunities
Qualified opportunities entering the pipeline per period.
Not raw leads. Opportunities that meet your qualification criteria (BANT, MEDDIC, or your framework of choice).

### Win Rate
Opportunities won / total opportunities that reached a decision (won + lost, excluding still-open).
- Good: 20-30%
- Great: 30-40%
- If > 50%: you're probably not being aggressive enough with pipeline (too selective at top)

### Average Deal Size
Average ACV of closed-won deals.
Segment this: SMB deals and enterprise deals should be tracked separately.

### Sales Cycle Length
Days from opportunity creation to closed-won.
- SMB: 14-30 days
- Mid-market: 30-60 days
- Enterprise: 60-180 days
- Strategic: 120-365 days

## Example
```
100 opportunities × 25% win rate × $15K ACV / 45 days = $8,333/day
Monthly capacity: ~$250K in new ARR
```

## Improvement levers
Each component is a lever:
1. **More opportunities**: Marketing + SDR investment
2. **Higher win rate**: Better qualification, competitive positioning, sales enablement
3. **Larger deals**: Move upmarket, improve packaging, add upsell at point of sale
4. **Shorter cycle**: Better demo, faster security review process, reduce decision-maker layers

## Alert thresholds
- Pipeline velocity declining QoQ
- Any single component degrading > 20% without improvement in another
- Win rate < 15% (qualification problem)
- Sales cycle lengthening > 20% (buying process or competitive issue)
