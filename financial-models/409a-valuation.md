# 409A Valuation

The 409A sets your common stock strike price. Founders often ignore it. Employees don't.

## What is it

IRS rule 409A requires private companies to establish fair market value (FMV) of common stock at least annually, or on material events (priced round, secondary, etc.). Stock options are priced at this FMV as strike price.

Too low = IRS penalties for employees, safe harbor lost.
Too high = options less attractive, talent leaves for competitors with cheaper strikes.

## Who sets it

A third-party firm (Carta, Scalar, Equidam, etc.). Costs $2-6k. Founders do not set it; auditors will reject that.

## Drivers of valuation

1. **Latest preferred price.** Common is usually 20-40% of preferred FMV.
2. **Stage.** Earlier stage = bigger discount. Seed common often 15-25% of pref. Series C+ moves toward 60-80%.
3. **Liquidation preferences.** Heavy prefs = bigger gap between pref and common.
4. **Revenue / metrics.** Growth changes risk profile.
5. **Comparable public multiples.** Public SaaS comps tanking? Your 409A drops too.

## Timing games (legitimate)

- **After layoffs or bad quarter:** 409A goes down. Good time to grant options to new hires.
- **Before a priced round closes:** last chance to grant at low strike.
- **After a secondary transaction:** usually raises 409A.

## Underwater options (the reality of 2022-2024)

Many employees hold options struck at 2021-era 409As that are now above preferred price. Options are worthless.

Moves:
- Option repricing (requires board approval, possible shareholder approval)
- Exchange program (old options for new at current strike, usually fewer shares)
- Cash bonus pool in lieu of options
- Accept attrition

Repricing carries accounting and legal implications. Don't freelance — get counsel.

## Post-termination exercise window

Standard is 90 days. Founders who want to be pro-employee extend to 7 or 10 years post-termination. Cost: a bit more share count floating around. Benefit: employees can afford to leave and still own their options.

## Early exercise

Some companies allow early exercise (exercise options before they vest, subject to repurchase). Lets employees start the long-term capital gains clock early. Requires 83(b) election within 30 days.

Complicates cap table. Worth it for founders and VERY early employees. Often not worth it for anyone else.

## When to refresh

- Annually (minimum)
- Priced round
- Secondary transaction
- Material business change (acquisition, layoffs, new revenue milestone)
- Material market change (2020, 2022 sell-offs)

Cost of running a 409A frequently is low. Cost of a stale 409A is much higher. Err on refreshing.
