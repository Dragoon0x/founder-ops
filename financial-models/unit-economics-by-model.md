# Unit Economics by Business Model

Every business model has its own unit-economics shape. Know yours.

## SaaS (subscription, B2B)

**Core unit:** customer / account.

**Key metrics:** CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC, gross margin, NRR, payback period.

**Healthy ranges:**
- Gross margin: 70-85%
- LTV:CAC: 3:1+
- Payback: <12 months (SMB), <18 months (mid-market), <24 months (enterprise)
- NRR: 110%+ (best-in-class 120-140%)
- Gross churn: <10% annual

## Marketplace (two-sided)

**Core unit:** transaction or GMV.

**Key metrics:** take rate, liquidity, supply/demand balance, repeat rate, GMV/user.

**Healthy ranges:**
- Take rate: 10-25% (higher = more defensible, but drives disintermediation risk)
- Repeat rate: >60% at 6 months (buyers), >80% (sellers)
- Contribution margin: 50-70% of take
- Payback per user acquired: <12 months

Marketplaces are harder to model because you have two CACs (buyer and seller) and liquidity matters more than either individual unit.

## Consumer subscription (B2C)

**Core unit:** subscriber.

**Key metrics:** CAC, LTV, monthly churn, trial conversion, payback.

**Healthy ranges:**
- Trial conversion: >30% (weekly plans), >50% (annual plans)
- Monthly churn: <5% (best-in-class <2%)
- LTV:CAC: 3:1+
- Payback: <6 months (hard — consumers churn fast)

Consumer is unforgiving. CAC goes up every quarter as Meta/Google ads saturate. Organic channels are the only moat.

## Developer tools (bottom-up / PLG)

**Core unit:** organization (seats).

**Key metrics:** seat expansion, usage-based growth, activation rate, conversion from free to paid.

**Healthy ranges:**
- Free-to-paid conversion: 3-7% (bottoms-up)
- Expansion: 20-40% of new ARR from expansion
- NRR: 120-150%+
- Payback: <12 months
- Time to activate: <5 min from signup

## Transactional (per-use / usage-based)

**Core unit:** transaction or API call.

**Key metrics:** gross margin per transaction, retention, volume growth.

**Healthy ranges:**
- GM per transaction: 40-80% (varies wildly by vertical)
- Retention (same customer repeat): >70% at 12 months
- Monthly usage growth per customer: 5-15%

Volatile revenue. Customers can ramp down without churning.

## Hardware / devices

**Core unit:** unit sold + recurring (service, consumables).

**Key metrics:** BOM, gross margin, ASP (average selling price), attach rate (service, consumables), return rate.

**Healthy ranges:**
- Hardware GM: 30-50% (pure hardware), 50-70% (hardware + software bundle)
- Return rate: <3%
- Service attach rate: 30-60% of units
- Working capital cycle: 60-120 days

Hardware is brutal because working capital eats cash. You pay suppliers 30-60 days before customers pay you.

## Fintech (lending, payments)

**Core unit:** dollar processed or dollar lent.

**Key metrics:** take rate, charge-off rate (lending), interchange, fraud rate, CAC/LTV.

**Healthy ranges (payments):**
- Take rate: 1-4% (varies by vertical)
- Fraud rate: <0.1%
- Gross margin: 30-60%

**Healthy ranges (lending):**
- Net interest margin: 3-8%
- Charge-off: <3% (consumer), <2% (SMB)
- CAC per $ lent: <3%

Regulatory capital requirements dominate fintech. Model those into unit economics.

## Healthtech

**Core unit:** patient encounter or contract.

**Key metrics:** reimbursement rate, collection time, contract renewal, patient acquisition cost.

**Healthy ranges:**
- Collection rate: 85%+
- Days to collect: <60
- Gross margin (software-heavy healthtech): 60-75%
- Gross margin (clinical services): 30-50%
- Contract renewal: 85%+

Long sales cycles. Reimbursement risk. Model both.

## Takeaway

Don't borrow SaaS benchmarks for a marketplace. Don't benchmark a hardware company against SaaS gross margins. Each model has its own shape, and investors who know your model will see through misapplied comps instantly.
