# Burn Model

Burn is the only number that can kill you faster than customer churn. Model it with the same rigor.

## Gross vs net burn

- **Gross burn:** total cash out per month (OpEx + COGS paid, not accrued)
- **Net burn:** gross burn minus cash received from customers
- **Runway:** cash on hand ÷ net burn (monthly)

Report net burn to the board. Report gross burn to yourself.

## The burn model structure

Monthly columns, 18-24 months out. Rows:

```
Starting cash
+ Cash from customers (collections, not bookings)
+ Cash from financing
- Salaries + benefits
- Contractors
- Tools / software
- Hosting + infra
- Legal + accounting
- Marketing spend
- Office + travel
- Taxes
= Ending cash
```

## Scenarios (required)

1. **Base case:** current trajectory
2. **Slow case:** revenue -30%, hiring held flat
3. **Crash case:** revenue -60%, hiring frozen, cut 20% of spend
4. **Bridge case:** can you survive 18 months without raising?

If crash case runs out in under 9 months, you are playing with fire. Either cut now or raise now.

## Burn multiple

Burn multiple = net burn / net new ARR

| Range | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| < 1 | Outstanding |
| 1-1.5 | Great |
| 1.5-2 | Good |
| 2-3 | Suspect |
| > 3 | Bad. You are burning money to rent growth. |

Use this every month. It's the single cleanest capital efficiency metric.

## Default alive vs default dead

Paul Graham's test. Project growth at current rate and current burn. Do you become profitable before cash runs out?

- **Default alive:** yes (growth closes the gap)
- **Default dead:** no (you'll need to raise or cut)

Most seed-stage companies are default dead. That's fine. But you must know which you are.

## Cost cutting, by impact

Ranked by cash saved per dollar of pain:

1. Tools audit — cut unused SaaS (5-15% of tool spend is waste)
2. Cloud right-sizing — 20-40% savings usually available
3. Contractors first, then FTE
4. Hiring freeze (no layoffs yet)
5. Salary cuts at exec level
6. Layoffs (cut deep once, not twice)
7. Equity-for-cash swaps
8. Sub-lease office
9. Defer payables (relationship cost)
10. Customer prepay incentive (pull cash forward)

## Triggers

Write these down before you need them:

- Runway < 12 months: start fundraise prep
- Runway < 9 months: active fundraise, cut discretionary
- Runway < 6 months: emergency mode, hiring freeze, cut 20%
- Runway < 4 months: layoffs, bridge round, or down round conversations

If you wait until 4 months to start raising, you are negotiating from terror.
