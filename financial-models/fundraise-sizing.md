# Fundraise Sizing

How much should you raise? Almost everyone gets this wrong.

## The two dominant failure modes

1. **Raise too little.** Run out before next round. Can't hit milestones.
2. **Raise too much.** Over-dilute. Invite scrutiny you can't support. Burn faster because the cash is there.

The middle path: raise for 18-24 months to the next defensible milestone, with ~6 months buffer.

## Milestones by stage

| Round | Typical milestone for next round |
|-------|----------------------------------|
| Pre-seed | Prototype + early signal |
| Seed | PMF signal, early revenue |
| Series A | $1-3M ARR, evidence of scaling CAC |
| Series B | $8-15M ARR, efficient growth |
| Series C | $25M+ ARR, clear path to $100M |

Your fundraise needs to get you past that milestone, not to it.

## The calculation

```
Raise = monthly net burn × 24 + 20% buffer
```

Example: if monthly burn is $300k, raise $300k × 24 × 1.2 = $8.64M ≈ $9M.

**Adjust for:**
- Expected burn growth (if hiring into the raise)
- Revenue ramp (reduces burn over time)
- Milestone timing (if milestone hits at month 12, need 12 more months of runway after)

## Dilution as a guardrail

Typical dilution per round:

| Round | Typical dilution |
|-------|------------------|
| Pre-seed | 10-15% |
| Seed | 15-25% |
| Series A | 20-25% |
| Series B | 15-20% |
| Series C+ | 10-15% |

If the calculation above pushes dilution above typical range for your stage, question whether you need all of it. Maybe hiring plan is too aggressive, or milestone can be proven with less.

## The "too much cash kills" problem

Studies (First Round Review, others) show overcapitalized companies underperform. Why:

1. Urgency drops
2. Hires are made earlier than needed
3. Experiments get expensive instead of scrappy
4. Founders focus on deploying cash instead of finding PMF
5. Next round pressure is higher (can't take a down round)

Raising $15M at Series A when $8M would do is not conservative. It is aggressive in a way founders don't see.

## The "just enough" trap

The opposite. Raising $5M when you really need $8M. Saves dilution today. Costs 4x that dilution when you have to raise a bridge at worse terms in 12 months.

If you're close to the line, err up, not down.

## Secondary (founder liquidity)

At Series B+, some founders take secondary — sell some shares to new investors for personal cash. Typical: $2-5M per founder. Usually capped at 5-10% of round.

Helps: eliminates founder "get rich at exit" pressure, lets founder stay longer at less equity.
Hurts: signals to investors founder is hedging. Can create board tension.

Optional. Discuss with lead. Don't demand at seed or Series A — looks bad.

## Questions to answer before picking a number

1. What milestone does this round buy?
2. How much burn does it take to hit that milestone?
3. What's the buffer if things take longer?
4. What's the dilution at different raise amounts?
5. What's the reasonable next round size at each scenario?

Write it down. Reference when temptation hits to raise more.
