# Hiring Plan Model

Hiring is how startups spend cash. Model it with the same rigor as revenue.

## Full cost of a hire

| Component | Cost |
|-----------|------|
| Base salary | X |
| Bonus (annual) | 0-20% of base (stage dependent) |
| Equity (expensed) | Per 409A, usually 0.1-1% of company |
| Benefits | 15-25% of base |
| Payroll taxes | 7.65-15% of base (varies by country) |
| Tools / software | $100-500/mo |
| Laptop + setup | $3-5k one-time |
| Office / remote stipend | $0-500/mo |
| Recruiting cost | 20-25% of first year comp (agency) OR 5-10% (in-house) |

**Fully loaded multiplier:** 1.3-1.5x base salary.

## Hiring plan structure

Rows = roles. Columns = months.

| Role | Oct | Nov | Dec | Jan | Feb | ... |
|------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Sr Eng #1 | 20k | 20k | 20k | 20k | 20k | ... |
| Sr Eng #2 | | 20k | 20k | 20k | 20k | ... |
| PM #1 | | | 20k | 20k | 20k | ... |

Each cell = fully-loaded monthly cost (base/12 × 1.4).

## Trigger-based hiring

Tie each hire to a revenue or milestone trigger:

- Sales rep #3: hired when reps #1 and #2 both at 80%+ of quota
- CSM #2: hired at 50 customers or $2M ARR
- Eng #5: hired when Eng #4 has shipped first major feature
- Ops hire: hired at 15 employees

Hiring without triggers = hiring on vibes. Triggers are how you avoid over-hiring.

## Sales hiring math

Rule of thumb: one sales rep costs ~$300k fully loaded, should produce 3-5x cost in ARR within year 1 (so $900k-1.5M).

Before hiring reps #2+, founder must have proven:
- Founder sold $X million alone
- Repeatable playbook documented
- Sales rep #1 hit quota
- ICP is clear

Skip these = burn $300k to learn reps don't work yet.

## Eng hiring math

Rule of thumb: engineer-months to ship is roughly 2x what you estimate. Plan for it.

Hiring 5 engineers in 3 months:
- Month 1: all 5 start, 0 productivity (ramping)
- Month 2: 30% productivity
- Month 3: 60%
- Month 4-6: 90%+

You pay full cost from month 1. You get partial output for 3 months. Plan the model around this, not your wish.

## Critical function mix by stage

| Stage | Eng | Product | Sales | CS | Marketing | Ops/G&A |
|-------|-----|---------|-------|-----|-----------|---------|
| Pre-seed | 60% | 20% | 20% | 0% | 0% | 0% |
| Seed | 50% | 15% | 15% | 10% | 5% | 5% |
| Series A | 40% | 15% | 20% | 10% | 10% | 5% |
| Series B | 35% | 15% | 25% | 12% | 10% | 3% |
| Series C | 30% | 12% | 30% | 15% | 10% | 3% |

Deviations should be intentional. 70% eng at Series A either means you have a technical moat story or you're under-investing in GTM.

## Avoiding the hiring drift

Symptoms of hiring gone wrong:
- Adding roles because you can, not because you must
- Hiring 2 when 1 would do to "move faster"
- Hiring senior when junior would work
- Hiring before the problem is crisp

Every open req should have: problem statement, success metric, 30/60/90 plan, and a trigger that activated the need.
