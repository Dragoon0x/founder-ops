# Stage-Specific Playbook

Founder-ops adapts recommendations based on stage. Here's what each stage looks like.

## Pre-Seed (No funding → $500K raised)

### Reality
- 1-3 founders
- 0 employees (or 1-2)
- No revenue or < $10K MRR
- Running on savings or small angel round
- Runway: 6-12 months
- You're the entire company

### Focus
1. **Customer discovery** (interview 30+ potential customers)
2. **MVP scope** (what's the smallest thing worth testing?)
3. **First 10 customers** (they'll teach you everything)
4. **Founder fit** (can you co-found with this person?)
5. **Fundraising path** (friends/family, angels, pre-seed funds)

### Most Useful Modes
- `/founder-ops discovery` — The most important mode at this stage
- `/founder-ops positioning` — Figure out who you serve and why
- `/founder-ops product` — Scope the MVP
- `/founder-ops prioritize` — Kill 80% of your ideas

### Avoid
- Hiring full-time (consultants if anything)
- Fundraising before you have product signal
- Spending on paid marketing
- Building features before talking to customers
- Building a team culture (there's no team yet)

### Key Metrics
- Interviews conducted per week
- Percent of interviews that produce insight
- Number of willing design partners
- Hours spent on customer-facing work (target: >70%)

## Seed ($500K - $3M raised)

### Reality
- 2-4 co-founders or founding team
- 2-8 employees
- $10K - $100K MRR
- 12-18 months runway
- Starting to build a team, still founder-centric

### Focus
1. **Product-market fit** (retention > 6 months)
2. **Early revenue growth** (month over month)
3. **First hires** (careful — bad hires at 5 people poison everything)
4. **Channel validation** (which acquisition channels work?)
5. **Story for Series A** (what will the narrative be in 12-18 months?)

### Most Useful Modes
- `/founder-ops retention` — Keep early customers alive
- `/founder-ops growth` — Test 1-2 channels deeply
- `/founder-ops hire` — First 5 hires are foundational
- `/founder-ops metrics` — Start tracking the right things
- `/founder-ops finance` — Monitor runway closely

### Avoid
- Hiring senior execs (too early)
- Enterprise sales if you don't have infrastructure
- International expansion
- Over-engineering (ship fast, learn fast)
- Long roadmaps (too early to plan 12 months)

### Key Metrics
- MRR growth rate (target 15-20% MoM early on)
- Monthly churn (target < 5%)
- 90-day retention (target > 80%)
- CAC payback (can be > 18 months at this stage, but trending down)

---

## Series A ($3M - $15M raised)

### Reality
- Full founding team + early employees
- 10-30 employees
- $1M - $5M ARR
- 18-24 months runway
- Team is forming, processes emerging

### Focus
1. **Repeatable GTM** (can new AEs hit quota?)
2. **Unit economics** (healthy LTV:CAC, payback < 18 mo)
3. **Team building** (hire managers, not just ICs)
4. **Operational maturity** (processes, not heroics)
5. **Market expansion** (second customer segment or geography)

### Most Useful Modes
- `/founder-ops strategy` — Set the 3-year direction
- `/founder-ops team-culture` — Codify what makes you work
- `/founder-ops sales` — Build the sales machine
- `/founder-ops operations` — Systematize the business
- `/founder-ops board` — Start running proper boards

### Avoid
- Everyone doing everything
- Single-channel dependency
- No management layer (bottleneck on CEO)
- Skipping security/compliance (will bite you)
- Pivoting core strategy (you raised on this)

### Key Metrics
- ARR growth (target 3x YoY)
- Net retention (target 110%+)
- Gross margin (target 75%+)
- Magic Number (target > 0.75)
- Employee NPS (target > 40)

---

## Series B ($15M - $50M raised)

### Reality
- 30-100 employees
- $5M - $30M ARR
- Multiple managers, forming departments
- Founders doing less day-to-day operations
- Board engaged, governance matters

### Focus
1. **Scale** (not just growth — scale the organization)
2. **Second product or market** (diversify revenue)
3. **Executive team** (VP-level hires)
4. **Operational excellence** (systems for everything)
5. **International** (if it makes sense)

### Most Useful Modes
- `/founder-ops strategy` — Long-range planning
- `/founder-ops experiments` — Portfolio of bets
- `/founder-ops partnerships` — Ecosystem plays
- `/founder-ops operations` — Run a real business
- `/founder-ops board` — Sophisticated board management

### Avoid
- Still doing everything yourself
- Ignoring culture drift
- Underinvesting in infrastructure
- Skipping analytics/data team
- Short-term thinking (you can afford longer bets now)

### Key Metrics
- ARR growth (target 2x YoY)
- Net retention (target 120%+)
- Rule of 40 (growth % + profit %)
- Revenue per employee
- Category ranking

---

## Series C+ / Growth Stage

### Reality
- 100+ employees
- $30M+ ARR
- Full org, multiple functions
- Multiple products or markets
- IPO or M&A conversations beginning

### Focus
1. **Category leadership** (become THE answer)
2. **Profitability path** (Rule of 40+)
3. **M&A (buy or be bought)** (strategic)
4. **Talent density** (top 10% hires only)
5. **Long-term architecture** (decisions for 10-year horizon)

### Most Useful Modes
- `/founder-ops strategy` — Category definition
- `/founder-ops compete` — Know the moat dynamics
- `/founder-ops marketing` — Brand building
- `/founder-ops legal-deeper` — IP, international, M&A readiness
- `/founder-ops team-culture` — Scale without culture decay

### Avoid
- Startup bad habits (undercommunicating, chaos)
- Late-stage decisions at early-stage pace
- Hiring "for scale" prematurely
- Losing what made you great

---

## The Stage Trap

Most founders struggle when they're:
- Pre-seed trying to Series A
- Seed trying to Series B
- Series A trying to be Google

Stage-appropriate advice beats ambitious advice. Be the best version of
your current stage before trying to be the next one.
