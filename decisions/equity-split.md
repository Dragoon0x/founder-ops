# Decision: Co-Founder Equity Split

## Context
Deciding how to divide equity among co-founders. Do this early, do it once, get it right.

## Principles

### Equal split (50/50 or 33/33/33)
Best when:
- Co-founders started at the same time
- Contributions are roughly equal (different types but equal value)
- You want to signal true partnership
- You can't objectively differentiate contributions

Reality: most VCs and advisors recommend equal splits for 2-3 co-founders. The data shows companies with equal splits perform slightly better on average (Wasserman, "The Founder's Dilemmas").

### Unequal split
Best when:
- One founder had the idea AND significant traction before others joined
- One founder is full-time while others are part-time
- Founders have dramatically different experience levels relevant to the business
- One founder is providing significant capital

### Dynamic split (slicing pie model)
Best when:
- Very early stage, unclear who will contribute most
- Contributions will vary significantly over time
- Some founders might leave early

Track contributions (time, money, IP, relationships) and allocate equity proportionally. Recalculate quarterly.

## Vesting Schedule
No matter the split, ALL co-founder equity should vest:
- **Standard**: 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
- **Accelerated**: 3-year vesting, 6-month cliff (for experienced founders)
- **Cliff**: if a co-founder leaves before the cliff, they get nothing
- **Single trigger acceleration**: 25-50% accelerates on acquisition
- **Double trigger**: 50-100% accelerates on acquisition + termination

## The hard conversation framework
1. Each founder independently writes down their proposed split and reasoning
2. Share simultaneously (prevents anchoring)
3. Discuss the delta, not the numbers. Why does each person value their contribution the way they do?
4. If you can't agree within 2 sessions, bring in an advisor or mediator
5. Document the agreement. Get it in writing with a lawyer.

## Red flags
- One founder refuses to discuss equity at all (they'll resent whatever you decide)
- Founder wants 80%+ with equal co-founders (ego problem)
- No vesting (someone leaves in month 3 with 33% of the company)
- Split based on "idea credit" rather than execution commitment
