# Decision: Hire a Technical Co-Founder vs CTO vs Dev Shop

## Context
Non-technical founder needs to build the product. Three paths.

## Options analysis

### Technical co-founder
**Best when:** Pre-product, pre-revenue. You need someone who cares as much as you do.

Pros:
- Aligned incentives (equity)
- Deep commitment
- Can make architectural decisions that scale
- Investors strongly prefer technical co-founders

Cons:
- Hard to find (and easy to get wrong)
- Equity dilution (25-50%)
- If it doesn't work out, messy separation
- Takes 2-6 months to find the right person

Where to find them:
- Your existing network (best source)
- Technical communities (meetups, hackathons, open source)
- Co-founder matching platforms (YC co-founder matching, Entrepreneur First)
- NOT job boards (a co-founder is not a hire)

### First CTO hire
**Best when:** You have revenue or funding. You need execution more than vision.

Pros:
- Faster to hire than co-founder
- Less equity dilution (1-5% vs 25-50%)
- Can hire for specific technical skills you need
- Clearer power dynamics

Cons:
- Employee mindset, not founder mindset
- May not push back enough on bad product decisions
- Harder to attract top talent without co-founder title
- Salary cost ($150-250K+ for good CTOs)

### Dev shop / agency
**Best when:** You need an MVP to validate before committing to a technical path.

Pros:
- Fastest to start (days, not months)
- No equity given up
- Can fire them without drama
- Some agencies have strong domain expertise

Cons:
- They build to spec, not to vision
- Code quality varies wildly
- No ongoing ownership of the codebase
- Technical debt almost guaranteed
- Cost: $50-200K for an MVP

## Decision matrix

| Factor | Co-founder | CTO | Dev Shop |
|--------|-----------|-----|---------|
| Stage | Pre-product | Post-funding | Pre-validation |
| Equity cost | 25-50% | 1-5% | 0% |
| Cash cost | $0 (sweat equity) | $150-250K/yr | $50-200K project |
| Time to start | 2-6 months | 1-3 months | 1-4 weeks |
| Long-term ownership | High | Medium | Low |
| Investor signal | Strong positive | Neutral | Slight negative |

## What not to do
- Don't give co-founder equity to a dev shop ("we'll be partners!")
- Don't hire a CTO before you know what you're building
- Don't use a dev shop for your core product long-term
- Don't settle for a technical co-founder just because they're available
