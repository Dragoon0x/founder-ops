# Decision: Remote vs Office vs Hybrid

## Context
How should your team work together physically?

## Framework

### Full remote works when:
- Team is < 15 people and everyone is self-directed
- You're hiring for rare skills in a global talent pool
- Your work is primarily async-compatible (writing, coding, design)
- You have strong documentation culture
- You're willing to invest in regular in-person gatherings (2-4x/year)

### Office works when:
- You're pre-PMF and need to iterate very fast (0→1 phase)
- Your work requires high-bandwidth collaboration (brainstorming, design)
- You're in a talent-dense market (SF, NYC, London, Bangalore)
- Culture is a competitive advantage and you want to build it through proximity
- You're willing to limit your talent pool to one geography

### Hybrid works when:
- You have 3+ required in-office days (2 or fewer is basically remote with commute costs)
- You establish clear norms for which work happens in-office vs remote
- You avoid the "two-class" problem (remote people feel excluded from decisions)

## Costs to model

| Factor | Remote | Office | Hybrid |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Office lease | $0 | $500-1,500/person/month | $300-800/person/month |
| Equipment stipend | $2-3K one-time | $1-2K one-time | $2-3K one-time |
| Co-working budget | $200-500/person/month | $0 | $0 |
| Team gatherings | $3-5K/person/year | Minimal | $2-3K/person/year |
| Salary adjustment | Can hire in lower-cost markets | Market rate for location | Market rate or adjusted |

## Common mistakes
1. Going hybrid without clear norms (worst of both worlds)
2. Full remote without async culture (meetings all day, timezone hell)
3. Office-first but hiring remote exceptions (creates second-class citizens)
4. Not budgeting for the hidden costs of remote (loneliness, slower onboarding, weaker culture)

## Review
Revisit at:
- Every major funding round (budget for office changes)
- When team crosses 10, 25, 50, 100 (dynamics shift at each threshold)
- If attrition rate exceeds 20% (may indicate culture/work-style mismatch)
