# Decision: Freemium / PLG vs Sales-Led

## Context
How should customers discover, evaluate, and buy your product?

## Framework

### Product-led growth (PLG) / Freemium when:
- Your product delivers value quickly (< 5 min to "aha" moment)
- Target users can self-serve without training
- ACV < $10K (economics don't support sales for small deals)
- Your product has natural virality (collaboration, sharing, network effects)
- The buyer IS the user (not buying for someone else)

### Sales-led when:
- ACV > $25K (complex, high-value deals)
- Product requires configuration, integration, or training
- Buyer is different from user (executive purchasing for team)
- Compliance/security review required before adoption
- Market is < 10,000 potential customers (you can name them all)

### Hybrid (PLG + Sales)
Most successful B2B companies eventually land here:
- Free/self-serve tier for individuals and small teams
- Sales motion kicks in above a usage/seat threshold
- Product usage data feeds sales prioritization (PQLs not MQLs)

## Key metrics by motion

| Metric | PLG Target | Sales-Led Target |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Time to value | < 5 min | < 1 week (with help) |
| Free → paid conversion | 2-5% | N/A |
| Sales cycle length | N/A | 30-90 days |
| CAC payback | < 6 months | < 18 months |
| Logo churn | < 3% monthly | < 10% annual |
| Expansion revenue | > 30% of new ARR | > 40% of new ARR |

## Transition patterns
- **PLG → Sales**: Add sales when you see clusters of free users at the same company. Signal: > 5 users from same email domain.
- **Sales → PLG**: Add a free tier when you want to expand TAM downmarket. Risk: cannibalizing existing paid customers.

## Review triggers
- When ACV shifts significantly (growing deals = add sales, shrinking = add PLG)
- When CAC payback exceeds 18 months (motion isn't working)
- When competitors adopt a different motion successfully
