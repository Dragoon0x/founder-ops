# Decision: Hire vs Outsource

## Context
You need work done. Should you hire a full-time employee or use contractors/agencies?

## Decision Framework

### Hire FTE when:
- The work is ongoing (> 6 months continuous need)
- The work requires deep company context / institutional knowledge
- The role is core to your competitive advantage
- You need someone to grow into a leadership role
- The work involves sensitive data or strategic decisions

### Outsource when:
- The work is project-based with a clear end date
- The skill is specialized and not needed full-time (design, legal, tax)
- You're testing whether you need this function at all
- Speed of hiring matters (contractors start in days, FTEs in weeks/months)
- Budget is constrained and you need flexibility

## Cost Comparison Template

| Factor | FTE (Annual) | Contractor (Annual) |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Base comp | $X | $X (typically 30-50% higher hourly) |
| Benefits + taxes | +25-35% of base | $0 |
| Equipment | $2-5K | $0 (they provide) |
| Recruiting cost | $5-15K | $0-2K (platform fees) |
| Ramp time | 2-4 months to full productivity | 1-2 weeks |
| Management overhead | Ongoing | Project-scoped |
| Risk of bad hire | High (severance, morale, re-hire) | Low (end contract) |

## Transition plan
If you start with a contractor and want to convert to FTE:
- Discuss intent early (some contractors prefer contract work)
- Offer conversion after 3 months (enough time to evaluate fit)
- Adjust comp for benefits (contractor rate → FTE salary is typically 70-80% of contractor hourly×2080)
- Legal: ensure contract allows conversion (no non-compete from their agency)
