# Monthly All-Hands Template

For companies of 10-100 people. Monthly cadence. 45-60 minutes. CEO leads; team members present portions.

---

## Pre-meeting

- Send pre-read 24 hours before (3-5 slides + data link)
- Questions collected async in [channel] in the week prior
- Agenda pinned

---

## Agenda (45-60 min)

### 1. Welcome + housekeeping (3 min)
- New joiners introduced by their manager (30 sec each)
- Departures acknowledged (respectfully)
- Reminders on any upcoming company events

### 2. North star update (5 min)
CEO shares:
- Where we are on annual / quarter north star metric
- What that means concretely

### 3. KPI dashboard (5 min)
Four-six metrics that matter most, shared transparently:
- Revenue / ARR / MRR
- Customers
- Cash and runway
- Product quality metrics
- Team size
- One people metric (eNPS or similar)

### 4. What shipped (10 min)
Rotate by team each month. Each team lead gets 3-4 min max.
- Engineering: what shipped
- Product: what insights / decisions
- GTM: wins, losses, pipeline shape
- Operations/finance: what changed

### 5. Theme / deep-dive (15 min)
One topic per month. Rotates:
- Customer story (CSM presents)
- Product strategy update (PM lead)
- Market/competitive update (GTM lead)
- Technical architecture discussion (CTO)
- Financial deep-dive (CEO/CFO)

### 6. What's next (5 min)
CEO previews the next 30-45 days of focus. Keeps continuity.

### 7. Q&A (10-15 min)
Questions from the pre-read channel + live questions.
- Answer uncomfortable questions honestly; flag if something can't be shared
- "I don't know" is a valid answer
- Do not punish question-askers with long non-answers

---

## Rules for good all-hands

1. **Numbers transparent by default.** Obscuring revenue or runway destroys trust when it's discovered.
2. **Bad news first.** If the past month was rough, say so in the first 3 minutes.
3. **Celebrate people by name.** Shout-outs for work above and beyond. Get them from managers in advance.
4. **Don't read slides.** Talk. Slides are the artifact, not the content.
5. **Decisions made in all-hands are rarely good.** Use all-hands to share decisions and context, not to debate.
6. **No meetings before or after.** All-hands deserves its own attention. Block 1.5 hours.
7. **Distributed team accommodations.** If there's a time-zone hostile schedule, record and share with context.

---

## Annual variations

- **January:** Vision refresh, annual plan, annual rewards
- **April:** Q1 close, Q2 frame, mid-year hiring plan
- **July:** H1 retro, H2 plan
- **October:** Q3 close, Q4 push, next-year preview
- **December:** Year in review, holiday, thank-yous

---

## What to avoid

- Reading every slide verbatim
- Mystery around business health
- Anniversary / birthday bloat that makes meeting 90 minutes
- Turning Q&A into a complaint forum (moderate tone actively)
- Having the CEO speak for 40 of 45 minutes (boring; grow other leaders)

---

## Post-meeting

- Recording posted within 24 hours
- Notes in shared doc
- Follow-up items from Q&A tracked
- Slack debrief channel for questions that didn't get answered live

---

## Health signal

A healthy company all-hands:
- Starts and ends on time
- Has at least 3 different people talking
- Generates at least 5 Q&A questions
- Has 90%+ attendance
- Feels like everyone has approximately the same level of context afterward

Watch for the opposite pattern — it means you need to intervene on meeting quality.
