# Equity Grant Letter Template

> **DISCLAIMER:** This is a template, not legal advice. Have a lawyer review every equity grant.

[Date]

Dear [Employee Name],

This letter confirms the equity grant approved for you on [date] in connection
with your role as [Title] at [Company Name].

## Grant Details

- **Type:** [Incentive Stock Options (ISO) / Non-qualified Stock Options (NSO) / Restricted Stock Units (RSU)]
- **Number of shares:** [X] shares of [Company] common stock
- **Grant date:** [date]
- **Vesting commencement date:** [start date]
- **Vesting schedule:** [4 years with 1-year cliff is standard]
  - 25% of shares vest on [cliff date]
  - Remaining 75% vest in 36 equal monthly installments thereafter
- **Strike price:** $[X] per share (fair market value as of grant date per [409A valuation])
- **Total option value at grant (FMV):** $[X]
- **Exercise window after termination:** [90 days standard / 10 years if extended]
- **Acceleration:** [None / Single trigger / Double trigger — specify]

## Cap Table Context

To help you understand this grant:
- Total fully-diluted shares outstanding: [X]
- Your grant represents approximately [X]% of fully-diluted ownership
- Last preferred valuation (post-money): $[X] ([month/year])

## Important Notes

1. **This is not the final agreement.** You will receive formal stock option
   documents from our equity administrator within [X] weeks. Those documents
   are the binding ones.

2. **Vesting is contingent on continued service.** If you leave or are terminated,
   only vested shares are yours. Unvested shares return to the pool.

3. **You must exercise vested options after leaving.** You have [90 days]
   from termination to exercise vested options. Unexercised options expire.

4. **Tax implications are real.** ISOs and NSOs have different tax treatments.
   Consult a tax advisor before exercising.

5. **Early exercise is available.** If allowed by the plan, you may exercise
   unvested options early (subject to vesting still applying). This can have
   tax advantages — talk to a tax advisor.

6. **Equity is not a guaranteed payout.** It's only valuable if the company
   succeeds AND we have a liquidity event. Most startups don't reach a positive
   exit.

For tax / legal questions, please consult your own advisor.

Welcome to the team.
