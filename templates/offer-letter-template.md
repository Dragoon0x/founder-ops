# Offer Letter Template

This is a lightweight letter template. Legal review required before first use. State-specific variations needed.

---

We are excited to offer you the position of [Title] at [Company Name]. We think you'll make a meaningful impact, and we're looking forward to building this together.

**Role details**

- **Position:** [Title]

- **Start date:** [Date]
- **Work location:** [Location or Remote with tax implications noted]

**Compensation**

- **Base salary:** [$X] per year, paid [frequency]

- **Equity grant:** [X] stock options, vesting over [4] years with a [1]-year cliff, subject to board approval. Grant will be priced at fair market value as of the board approval date.

**Benefits**

- Health insurance: company pays [X]% of employee premium, [X]% of dependents

## Notes for the hiring manager

- Send the offer the same day you decide. Momentum matters.
- Call to walk through before emailing the document.
- Be explicit about the negotiation range; candidates guessing is worse than candidates knowing.
- If the candidate has a competing offer, do not pressure. Acknowledge it and offer your best.
- If the candidate asks for more time, default to yes within reason (3-5 extra business days).
- Never make a verbal promise that isn't in the letter.

## Notes for legal

State-specific variations to check:
- At-will statement language
- Non-compete language (illegal in many states for most roles)
- Bonus clawback enforceability
- Arbitration clauses
- Post-termination exercise window on options

## Red flags in candidate responses

- Asking to delay signing for >2 weeks without explanation
- Asking to backdate start date
- Requesting unusual provisions mid-offer (non-compete waiver, extended exercise window, early vesting)

These are not all disqualifying, but they deserve a conversation.
