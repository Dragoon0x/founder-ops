# Cold Investor Email Template

```
Subject: [Specific + intriguing — 6-8 words max]

Hi [First name],

[ONE sentence: shared context — their portfolio company, their tweet, their thesis paper. Specific, not generic.]

[TWO sentences: what you're building + ONE specific traction metric.]

[ONE sentence: why you're emailing them SPECIFICALLY (not "I admire your work").]

[ONE sentence ask: 15-min call this week, OR send the deck.]

[Sign-off]
[Name + role + 1-line credibility]

P.S. [Optional: one more hook — a specific result, a customer quote, a milestone]
```

## Examples by category

### To a fund that recently led an adjacent investment
```
Subject: Faster path to enterprise PMF than [their portfolio company]

Hi Sarah,

Saw the [their portfolio co] announcement — congratulations to the team.

I'm building [your company] in the same neighborhood — [one-line description].
We hit $40K MRR in 4 months from launch with [N] enterprise design partners.

Your thesis on horizontal infrastructure for [domain] feels like the closest match
I've seen — would love 15 minutes when you're back from PSL?

[Name]
Founder, [Company]

P.S. We're not raising yet, just want to start the conversation.
```

### To a partner who tweeted about your problem
```
Subject: Your tweet on [topic] — we built the thing

Hi [Name],

Your tweet last week about [specific tweet] — we agree, and we built it.

[Company] is [one-line value prop]. [N] customers paying [$X K] MRR after [Y months].

Would the 12-min product walkthrough be interesting? Happy to send the deck instead.

[Name]
[Company]
```

### To a partner you met at an event
```
Subject: Following up from [event name]

Hi [Name],

Great to meet you at [event] last week — your question about [topic from convo]
stuck with me.

Wanted to share what we're building: [one line]. Since we talked, we've [recent traction milestone].

Free for 15 min next week?

[Name]
```

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- "Quick question" subject (says nothing)
- "I hope this email finds you well" (delete)
- Mass-personalized blast (they can tell)
- Sending the deck without permission
- Copying multiple partners at the same fund (looks spammy)
- "Game-changing" / "revolutionary" (every founder claims this)
