# Customer Interview Script — Discovery

## Pre-Interview (5 min before)

- Review their LinkedIn / company
- Note their role, tenure, company stage
- Have your hypothesis written down (what are you trying to learn?)
- Prepare to take notes (Notion, paper, whatever)
- Test your video / audio if remote
- Have ZERO product slides open

## The Script (30-45 min)

### Phase 1: Their world (10 min)
"Thanks for taking the time. I'm trying to understand how people in your role
think about [problem space]. Mind if I ask a few questions about your work?"

Open with broad questions:
- "Walk me through a typical week in your role"
- "What's [the part of their job related to your problem] look like for you?"
- "What tools / processes do you use today?"

### Phase 2: The pain (10 min)
"What's the hardest part of [specific aspect of their job]?"

Follow-ups:
- "Walk me through the last time that happened"
- "What did you try? Did it work?"
- "How often does this come up?"
- "What's the cost when this goes wrong?"

### Phase 3: Workarounds (10 min)
"What are you doing about [problem] today?"

- "What tools have you tried?"
- "What works about your current solution?"
- "What doesn't work?"
- "Have you ever paid for something to solve this?"

### Phase 4: The future (5 min)
"If you had a magic wand to change one thing about [problem area], what would it be?"

- "What would have to be true for you to switch tools / processes?"
- "Who would need to weigh in on a decision like that?"

### Phase 5: Close (5 min)
"This has been incredibly helpful. Two final asks:
1. Anyone else in your network you think I should talk to about this?
2. Mind if I check in again in a few months when we have something to show?"

## What NOT to say

| Don't say | Say instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Would you use a product that did X?" | "What are you doing about X today?" |
| "Do you like this idea?" | "What surprised you most about [problem]?" |
| "Would you pay $X?" | "What have you paid for to solve this?" |
| "We're building..." | (Don't pitch in discovery interviews) |
| "Most people we talk to..." | (Don't lead with what others say) |

## Post-Interview (within 30 min)

Write the synthesis IMMEDIATELY. Memory degrades fast.

Use the template at `examples/sample-discovery-interview.md`.

## Conversion (if appropriate)

If they showed strong interest AND fit your ICP:
"Based on what you described, I think you'd benefit from being one of our
first design partners. We're free for 6 months in exchange for honest feedback.
Would you be interested?"

Don't pitch unless the signals are clear. Discovery should feel like discovery,
not a sales call.
