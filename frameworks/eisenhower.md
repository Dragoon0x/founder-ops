# Eisenhower Matrix

## The Four Quadrants

|   | Urgent | Not Urgent |
|---|--------|-----------|
| **Important** | Q1: DO NOW | Q2: SCHEDULE |
| **Not Important** | Q3: DELEGATE | Q4: DELETE |

## Quadrant Deep Dive

### Q1: Urgent + Important (Do Now)
- Real crises
- Hard deadlines
- Customer-blocking issues
- Health emergencies

**Reality check:** Most things that FEEL Q1 are actually Q3 (someone else's urgent).

### Q2: Important but Not Urgent (Schedule)
- Strategic planning
- Relationship building
- Learning and growth
- Preventive work
- Long-term investments

**This is where founders should spend 50%+ of time.**

Most founders under-invest here because it never feels urgent.

### Q3: Urgent but Not Important (Delegate)
- Other people's requests
- Most emails
- Most meetings
- Interruptions

**The biggest time-sink for most founders.**

Fix: Delegate, batch, or say no.

### Q4: Not Urgent AND Not Important (Delete)
- Low-value meetings
- Social media scrolling
- News cycle anxiety
- Busy-work

**The easiest to cut.** Just stop doing these.

## Founder-Specific Examples

### Q1 (Do Now) — Rare if you're operating well
- Production outage affecting customers
- Critical team member quitting suddenly
- Legal threat requiring immediate response
- Board member urgently needs info for investor

### Q2 (Schedule) — Where the value is
- Strategic planning
- 1:1s with direct reports
- Customer interviews (non-urgent)
- Hiring pipeline development
- Writing investor updates
- Content creation
- Exercise, sleep, relationships

### Q3 (Delegate)
- Most inbound meetings
- Status update emails
- Calendar management
- Routine customer support
- Process documentation

### Q4 (Delete)
- Most conferences
- Most webinars
- Social media engagement (unless part of strategy)
- Low-value networking events

## The Q1 Trap

Founders often live in Q1, feeling productive but not strategic. Signs:
- Every week feels like firefighting
- No time for Q2 work
- Exhausted but not progressing
- "I'll do X when things calm down" (things never calm down)

## The Q2 Discipline

Protect Q2 time like your life depends on it (it does):

- Block 4-hour windows for Q2 work
- Defend the blocks (don't reschedule for Q3 interruptions)
- Phone in airplane mode
- Out of Slack/email

## Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Everything feels Urgent | Can't prioritize | Ask: "what happens if this waits?" |
| No Q2 time | Reactive life | Block calendar for Q2 |
| Delegating reluctantly | Team doesn't grow | Delegate even when it's faster to DIY |
| Q4 activities disguised as Q2 | Rationalized procrastination | Test: "Is this moving a core metric?" |

## Weekly Eisenhower Audit

At end of each week, categorize how you spent time:
- Hours in Q1:
- Hours in Q2:
- Hours in Q3:
- Hours in Q4:

Target for founders:
- Q1: < 15% (real crises are rare)
- Q2: > 50% (strategic work)
- Q3: < 25% (delegate ruthlessly)
- Q4: < 10% (delete ruthlessly)

If Q1 + Q3 > 60%, you're reactive. Redesign your week.
