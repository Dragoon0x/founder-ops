# North Star Metric

## What It Is

ONE number that, if maximized, captures the core value you deliver to customers.

## The Criteria

A good North Star:
- **Simple:** Expressible in one sentence
- **Outcome-oriented:** Measures customer value, not activity
- **Leading:** Predicts future revenue, not just current
- **Actionable:** Teams can move it
- **Understood:** Everyone in company can explain it

## Examples by Business Model

| Business | North Star | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| Slack | Weekly Active Users (in paid workspaces) | Usage = stickiness = retention |
| Spotify | Time Spent Listening | Usage proxy for perceived value |
| Airbnb | Nights Booked | Core transaction |
| Facebook (early) | Monthly Active Users | Network effects depend on scale |
| Amazon | Purchases per Customer | Retention + expansion |
| Uber | Rides per Week | Liquidity on both sides |
| Netflix | Hours Watched | Engagement = retention |

## Bad North Stars

| Metric | Why Bad |
|--------|---------|
| Revenue | Measures result, doesn't guide action |
| Profit | Too downstream |
| Employee count | Activity, not customer value |
| Page views | Vanity |
| App downloads | Acquisition only, not retention |
| Signups | Doesn't capture value delivered |

## How to Find Yours

### Step 1: Ask "What would our customers do more of, if we were 10x better?"

For Slack: Send messages and collaborate in channels.
For Netflix: Watch more content.
For Stripe: Process more payments.

### Step 2: Quantify that behavior

Messages sent per user per day.
Hours watched per subscriber per month.
Payment volume processed.

### Step 3: Pressure test

- Does this metric increase when customers get more value? (leading indicator of retention)
- Does this metric differentiate power users from casual users? (usage depth)
- Would increasing it MEAN the company is doing well? (causal connection)

## The North Star Formula

For most B2B SaaS:

```
North Star = (Number of customers) × (Usage depth per customer) × (Value per usage)
```

Decompose each:
- **Number of customers:** Who's in
- **Usage depth:** How much they use
- **Value per usage:** What they get from each use

This becomes your KPI tree.

## Changing the North Star

**Warning signs your North Star is wrong:**
- Teams hit the target but revenue doesn't grow
- Customers are happy but metric is flat
- Metric is growing but customers churning
- Team games the metric without real impact

**When to change:**
- Business model shifted
- Core product changed
- Market matured (different value driver)

**How to change:**
- Announce clearly: "We're moving from X to Y because..."
- Update dashboards
- Retrain teams on the new north
- Give it a quarter to stabilize before judging

## Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| 3-5 "North Stars" | Not a north star, just metrics |
| Revenue as North Star | Can't act on it directly |
| Metric that grows while customers complain | Missing the point |
| Changing quarterly | Team confused, no compounding |
| No North Star | No alignment, no focus |
