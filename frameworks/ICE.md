# ICE Framework

## Formula
**Score = Impact × Confidence × Ease**

Simpler than RICE. Use when you don't have reach data (early-stage) or
need to decide quickly.

## Definitions (all 1-10)

### Impact
How much will this move the key metric?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Transformational — changes the trajectory |
| 7-8 | Major — clear improvement |
| 5-6 | Meaningful — notable but not game-changing |
| 3-4 | Minor — marginal improvement |
| 1-2 | Negligible — not worth the effort |

### Confidence
How sure are you Impact will actually materialize?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Tested in market, proven pattern |
| 7-8 | Customer signal, reasonable certainty |
| 5-6 | Informed guess, untested |
| 3-4 | Speculative |
| 1-2 | Complete shot in the dark |

### Ease (NOT Effort)
How easy is this to do? Higher = easier.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | < 1 day of work |
| 7-8 | 1-2 weeks |
| 5-6 | 1 month |
| 3-4 | Quarter |
| 1-2 | Multi-quarter |

## Worked Example

**Idea: Add a pricing page FAQ**
- Impact: 4 (modest — might help conversion 5-10%)
- Confidence: 7 (seen work elsewhere)
- Ease: 9 (2 hours of writing)
- **Score: 4 × 7 × 9 = 252**

**Idea: Build an iOS mobile app**
- Impact: 7 (could unlock mobile-first customers)
- Confidence: 4 (no signal yet from mobile users)
- Ease: 1 (6+ months of engineering)
- **Score: 7 × 4 × 1 = 28**

**Verdict:** FAQ scores 9x higher despite lower impact. Ship it today. Revisit mobile when you have signal.

## When to Use ICE vs RICE

| Use ICE when | Use RICE when |
|-------------|---------------|
| Early stage, no user data | Mature product with user metrics |
| Growth experiments | Product features |
| Need fast decisions | Need justification |
| Binary-ish decisions | Comparing many options |

## Common Mistakes

- Inflating all scores (doesn't differentiate)
- Using Effort instead of Ease (flips the math)
- Not calibrating across people (your 7 ≠ their 7)
- Scoring in isolation (rank-order a batch together for better calibration)
