# Hardware — Common Pitfalls

### 1. Underestimating the timeline
"We will ship in 6 months" for a new hardware product almost never holds. 18-30 months is realistic for a first product from scratch. Plan cash accordingly.

### 2. Software-company burn rate
Hardware companies that burn like SaaS companies die before product-market fit. Stage gates: prototype → EVT → DVT → PVT. Do not pour cash until the prior gate passes.

### 3. Single-source critical components
One supplier has a fire, goes bankrupt, cuts you off, or ups price 40%. You are out of business for 3-6 months. Dual-source critical components before you ramp.

### 4. Ignoring working capital
You have $5M revenue, $1M profit, and you are still cash-negative because inventory ate the difference. Model working capital per unit shipped.

### 5. Over-customizing components
Custom silicon, custom connectors, custom displays for a 1000-unit product is an economic and schedule disaster. Use off-the-shelf until scale justifies custom.

### 6. DFM (Design for Manufacturing) late
Designing a beautiful product that cannot be manufactured economically. Get CM input in early prototype, not at DVT.

### 7. Certifications as afterthought
FCC, CE, UL, country-specific radio certifications can take 8-16 weeks and $30k-200k. Build into the schedule from day one.

### 8. Shipping before reliability testing
Reliability issues appear at month 3-6 of field use. Skipping HALT/HASS, drop, thermal, EMC testing means you find failures through returns instead.

### 9. No field firmware update path
Shipping connected products without OTA update capability is shipping a grenade. Every field bug requires an RMA.

### 10. Channel margin math
Retailer wants 40%, distributor wants 15%, marketing development funds 5%, returns reserve 3%. Your "consumer price $100" is actually $37 to you. Build margins for the stack, not just your margin.

### 11. Tariff and logistics blindness
A tariff change adds $5 to $100 COGS. Your 40% margin becomes 35%. Model tariff scenarios; diversify country of origin.

### 12. Cash on the water
30-60 days of inventory in transit ($$$). Delays, customs issues, port strikes all hit cash. Air freight escape-valve should be budgeted.

### 13. Bad NPI (New Product Introduction) discipline
Launching a new product requires process: SKU setup, documentation, service training, spare parts, packaging, compliance. Missing any one delays launch.

### 14. Spec creep
Every week an engineer adds a feature. Tooling changes, validation restarts. Lock specs at DVT. Period.

### 15. No manufacturing champion
Shipping physical product without a dedicated manufacturing operations hire by PVT is malpractice. This is the most commonly underhired role.

### 16. Underestimating inventory aging and obsolescence
Last year's model with 5000 units in inventory and a new version shipping? Write-down incoming. Plan end-of-life.

### 17. Retail distribution before readiness
Launching in 500 Best Buy stores with no sell-through plan = returns and shelf-pull. Validate with limited distribution first.

### 18. Packaging as afterthought
Packaging affects shipping cost, damage rate, unboxing experience, and sustainability messaging. Design it; do not let the CM design it.

### 19. Poor cost-down roadmap
Hardware margins improve with engineering cost-down work. If you have no plan for 2nd-gen BOM cost -15%, you will never hit healthy gross margin.

### 20. Ignoring the secondary market
Used products, returns, B-stock, scratch-and-dent. Poor handling destroys brand and margin. Plan the full lifecycle.
