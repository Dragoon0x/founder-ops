# Consumer / DTC — Common Founder Pitfalls

## Acquisition Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Single-channel dependency | Channel changes, business dies (Facebook 2018-2022) | Multi-channel from year 1 |
| Optimizing for ROAS not LTV:CAC | Profitable acquisition that loses money long-term | Track payback period |
| Ignoring brand search | Free customers you're not capturing | Bid on brand terms |
| Influencer-heavy without strategy | One-time spikes, no compounding | Build affiliate program with creators |
| No retention spending | Acquisition gets harder; existing customers churn | Email, SMS, loyalty programs |
| Ignoring TikTok/short-form | Where attention is | Test even if "not your audience" |

## Pricing & Margin Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Pricing on cost-plus | Leaves money on table | Price on willingness-to-pay |
| Not factoring returns into pricing | True margin is much lower | Price assuming 10-25% returns |
| Free shipping baked into price | Customers don't perceive premium | Free shipping at $X threshold |
| Constant discounting | Trains customers to wait | Promotional calendar, not always-on |
| No premium tier | Missing high-value customers | Add a "premium" version |

## Brand Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Generic brand | Indistinguishable, commoditized | Strong POV, distinctive design |
| Ignoring packaging | Unboxing matters in DTC | Investment in packaging design |
| Treating brand as logo | Brand = experience + perception | Hire brand thinking, not just visual design |
| Focusing on features not story | Features get copied; story is moat | Customers buy stories |
| No founder voice | Modern DTC needs personality | Founder content, transparent communication |

## Operations Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Shipping everything yourself | Doesn't scale past few hundred orders/mo | 3PL by 500 orders/mo |
| Single fulfillment location | Slow to coast-to-coast | Multiple 3PLs by Series A |
| No returns process | Customer rage, refund costs | Pre-paid return labels, easy process |
| Stockouts | Lose orders, lose customers | Forecast properly, hold safety stock |
| Overstocking | Tied-up cash, end-of-season writedowns | Forecast, MOQs (minimum order quantities), staged buys |
| Manual inventory tracking | Errors, oversells | Inventory management system from day 1 |

## Customer Service Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Slow response times | Negative reviews, refund requests escalate | Sub-24-hour response standard |
| No self-service | Same questions over and over | Help center, return portal, order tracking |
| One support channel | Email-only doesn't fit modern customers | Email + chat + SMS |
| Outsourcing too early | Lose customer voice and brand consistency | Founder/team responds for first 6 months |
| Ignoring social media complaints | Public visibility | Monitor mentions; respond to negative reviews |

## Email/SMS Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| No flow setup | Missing 30%+ of revenue | Welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase, win-back flows |
| Email blast culture | Unsubscribes, spam complaints | Segmented sends; relevance over frequency |
| Spam tactics | Sender reputation tanks | Confirmed opt-in, real value in every send |
| No SMS at all | Highest engagement channel | Build SMS list (with explicit consent) |
| Buying email lists | Hurts deliverability, illegal in many jurisdictions | Build organically only |

## Strategic Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Trying to be a category leader | Most categories are saturated | Win a niche first |
| Premature wholesale | Distracts, low margin, high risk | Direct first; wholesale later if at all |
| Premature retail | Capital intensive, slow | E-commerce first |
| International expansion at <$10M revenue | Operational nightmare | US/home market first |
| Adding SKUs faster than channels | Inventory complexity explodes | Hero products before line extension |
| Following trends | Customers see through it | Conviction in your specific positioning |

## Investor / Fundraising Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Treating DTC like SaaS | Different unit economics, different metrics | Use DTC-specific framing |
| Vanity GMV | Investors care about gross profit | Show contribution margin and LTV |
| Skipping payback discussion | Series A+ wants short payback | Track, optimize, present clearly |
| Underestimating capital needs | DTC is capital-hungry (inventory, ads, returns) | Raise enough; don't over-optimize valuation |
| Not understanding DTC investor landscape | Many generalists don't get it anymore | Target DTC-specialist funds: Forerunner, Bullish, etc. |
