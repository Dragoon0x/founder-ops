# Healthtech — Common Pitfalls

### 1. Building for patients when the buyer is the payer
Patient love does not pay bills unless patients pay directly. Most healthtech is sold to payers, providers, or employers. Know who writes the check.

### 2. Underestimating sales cycles
"We will close 10 hospital deals this year" when one hospital deal takes 12 months. Forecasts are fantasy; plan for 2-3 per year per AE at a Series A.

### 3. HIPAA as afterthought
Retrofitting HIPAA compliance after building the product is 5x harder than designing for it. BAA template, audit log architecture, encryption at rest and in transit, least privilege access: from day one.

### 4. FDA regulatory path surprise
"We did not think we were a device" is a sentence that has killed many startups. If your software makes or informs a clinical decision, you likely are regulated. Get regulatory counsel in the first 90 days.

### 5. Single champion risk
One VP Clinical loves you. That VP leaves. Contract dies. Health system sales require at least 3 stakeholders bought in: clinical, IT/security, finance.

### 6. Misreading "pilot" as "sale"
Free or heavily discounted pilots rarely convert at healthy rates. Price pilots at 30-60% of target ACV; free pilots convert at 10-20%.

### 7. EHR integration scope
Integrating with Epic, Cerner, Athena, etc. takes 3-12 months per EHR and has political as well as technical dimensions. Plan and staff accordingly.

### 8. Reimbursement hope strategy
"We will get a CPT code eventually" is not a revenue plan. Build a reimbursement path before raising a Series A for most DTx/device companies.

### 9. Clinical workflow mismatch
Your clinician user has 6 minutes with the patient. Your tool adds 90 seconds. You lose. Measure time-to-complete in real workflows, not demos.

### 10. State-by-state telehealth compliance
Practicing medicine across state lines requires licensing in each state. Corporate practice of medicine restrictions vary by state. Plan rollout accordingly.

### 11. Ryan Haight Act (controlled substances)
Prescribing controlled substances remotely has specific rules that periodically tighten. If your business depends on controlled scripts via telehealth, you have regulatory tail risk.

### 12. Ignoring the information blocking rule
The 21st Century Cures Act information blocking rule means refusing to share data can be a compliance violation. Your product must handle it.

### 13. Under-resourcing compliance and legal
Healthtech needs 2-3x the legal/compliance budget of equivalent SaaS. Underinvestment shows up as enforcement letters.

### 14. Value-based care economics miscalibration
Assuming you can reduce costs 30% when the industry has reduced them 2-5% in decades. Underwrite for modest improvements.

### 15. Clinical outcomes without a study
Claims like "reduces readmissions by 30%" need evidence. Without a study, you cannot publish, cannot sell to risk-bearing entities, and cannot reimburse.

### 16. Selling against the EHR
Products that compete with EHR functionality face an entrenched incumbent whose products are bundled. Build adjacent or deeply vertical, not horizontal.

### 17. Procurement process naivety
Hospital procurement has 5-15 steps, committees, and standard contract terms. "Procurement" adds 3-9 months. Budget for it.

### 18. Insurance mix ignorance
Medicare, Medicaid, commercial, self-pay each have different rules, rates, and incentives. Product and GTM vary by mix.

### 19. Ignoring the privacy officer
Privacy officer / compliance officer / security officer opinions can single-handedly kill deals. Engage them early.

### 20. Underestimating workflow change management
"They will love it once they use it" ignores that clinician workflow change is 12-24 months of organizational work. Partner with operations, not just clinical.
