# Healthtech — Industry Overview

Last updated: 2026-Q1

## Why healthtech is hard

Long sales cycles, regulated buyers, regulated data, fragmented reimbursement, adversarial stakeholders (patient vs. payer vs. provider vs. pharma), and outcomes measured in years not quarters. The market is gigantic ($4.5T US health spending) which makes every founder think their wedge will be easy. It will not.

## Sub-verticals

| Sub-vertical | Buyer | Typical sales cycle |
|--------------|-------|---------------------|
| B2B SaaS for providers | Hospital IT / practice admin | 9-18 months |
| B2B SaaS for payers | Insurance company | 12-24 months |
| Telehealth / virtual care | Direct to consumer or employer | 3-6 months (employer) |
| Medical devices | Hospital capex committee | 12-36 months |
| Digital therapeutics (DTx) | Payer reimbursement | 24-48 months |
| Drug discovery / biotech tools | Pharma R&D | 6-18 months |
| Health data / infra | Mix | 6-12 months |
| Care delivery / risk-bearing | Patient / payer (capitated) | varies |

## Regulatory surface

The alphabet soup that matters:

- **HIPAA.** Data protection, business associate agreements
- **HITECH.** Breach notification, enforcement
- **FDA.** Devices (Class I/II/III), SaMD (Software as Medical Device), drug/device clearance paths (510(k), De Novo, PMA)
- **CMS.** Reimbursement codes (CPT), coverage determinations, Medicare/Medicaid rules
- **State medical boards.** Telehealth licensing, corporate practice of medicine
- **DEA.** Controlled substances (Ryan Haight Act)
- **ONC.** Interoperability, EHR certification, info blocking rule
- **State privacy laws.** CMIA, Washington MY Health MY Data, growing

## Key numbers (2026-Q1)

- US health spending: approximately $4.5T / year, about 18% of GDP
- Healthtech startup funding, 2025: approximately $18B globally, down from 2021 peak
- Digital health exits 2025: median deal size about $140M, IPO window narrow
- Typical B2B healthtech CAC: $25k-250k depending on buyer
- Typical payback: 18-36 months
- Provider EHR implementation cost: $15k-70k per provider seat
- Hospital procurement cycle: 6-18 months, multi-stakeholder
- Typical healthtech gross margin: 60-75% SaaS, lower for services

## Business model levers

- **SaaS / subscription** (most provider and payer tech)
- **Per-visit or per-encounter fees** (telehealth, services)
- **Capitated / at-risk** (value-based care, risk-bearing entities)
- **Pharma sponsored** (adherence programs, DTx reimbursed by pharma brand)
- **DME / device sales** (one-time + service)
- **Enrollment fees** (employer-sold programs)

## What good looks like at each stage

**Pre-seed/Seed.** Signed pilot with a real health system, written clinical workflow validated by clinicians, HIPAA BAA template ready, basic SOC 2 scoping done.

**Series A.** 3-5 paying health system / payer customers, repeatable sales motion identified, clinical outcomes preliminary, SOC 2 Type 2, HITRUST started.

**Series B+.** Multi-state / multi-payer, clinical outcomes published or submitted, reimbursement strategy validated, expansion revenue from existing accounts.

## The healthtech buyer stack

A B2B healthtech sale typically needs: clinical champion + department chief + IT / security review + legal / privacy review + procurement + CFO sign-off. Missing any one kills or delays the deal 3-6 months.

## Common moats

- **Clinical evidence.** Published outcomes, RCTs, endorsements from professional societies
- **Integrations.** EHR integrations (Epic, Cerner/Oracle, Athena) take 6-18 months and block competitors
- **Reimbursement.** Covered CPT/HCPCS codes or pharma reimbursement programs
- **Specialty depth.** Deep clinical knowledge of one specialty beats general horizontal tools
- **Network.** Provider networks, lab networks, pharmacy networks
- **Certifications.** Joint Commission, NCQA, URAC
