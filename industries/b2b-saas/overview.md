# B2B SaaS — Industry Overview

## What "Wins" Looks Like

The dominant business model in software for the last 20 years. Goal: build a product
that companies pay for monthly/annually. Win conditions:
- $100M ARR with 70%+ gross margins
- 110%+ net revenue retention
- Payback period under 18 months
- Predictable, repeatable sales motion

## The Three Sub-Categories

### Bottoms-Up (Self-Serve / PLG)
- **Examples**: Notion, Figma, Linear, Vercel, Postman
- **Buyer**: Individual user signs up with credit card, expands within team
- **GTM**: Free tier or trial, paywall on collaboration / advanced features
- **Sales motion**: Self-serve under $20K ACV, sales-assist above
- **Key metrics**: Activation rate, free→paid conversion, NRR via expansion
- **CAC**: Low-to-moderate, payback under 12 months ideal

### Sales-Led (Mid-market)
- **Examples**: HubSpot, Gong, Pendo, Outreach
- **Buyer**: Department head (VP Sales, VP Marketing, Head of CS)
- **GTM**: Inbound lead gen + outbound BDRs
- **Sales motion**: Demo, trial, pilot, contract — 2-6 month cycle
- **Key metrics**: Pipeline coverage (3-4x quota), win rate, ACV growth
- **CAC**: Moderate, payback 12-18 months

### Enterprise (Large Deals)
- **Examples**: Snowflake, Datadog, MongoDB
- **Buyer**: Procurement + IT + business unit
- **GTM**: Field sales, partnerships, RFPs
- **Sales motion**: 6-18 month cycle, multi-stakeholder
- **Key metrics**: Logo land, expansion revenue, deal size growth
- **CAC**: High, payback 18-36 months acceptable if NRR is strong

## Ideal Founder Profile

- Domain expertise in the buyer's world (you've felt the pain)
- Sales aptitude (or hire it early — mid-market and enterprise need it)
- Patience (B2B is multi-year)
- Comfort with complexity (procurement, security reviews, integrations)

## Founder Mistakes (Common)

1. **Pricing too low** — "Just $9/mo" leaves money on the table; B2B buyers expect to pay for value
2. **Building for SMB when targeting enterprise** — Different feature sets entirely (SSO, audit logs, SLAs)
3. **Selling to anyone who'll buy** — Pollutes ICP, wrecks support burden, kills retention
4. **Ignoring procurement / security** — One enterprise deal dies on a security review you can't pass
5. **Hiring AEs before product-market fit** — They'll churn within 6 months and damage the brand
6. **Conflating MRR with health** — A high-MRR customer who hates the product is a churn time bomb
7. **No expansion motion** — Land + expand is most of the value in B2B SaaS

## When B2B SaaS Is The Wrong Model

- Your buyer is a consumer (use consumer/DTC pack)
- Your product is fundamentally transactional (use marketplace pack)
- Your "users" are developers integrating an API (use developer tools pack)
- Your offering requires manufacturing (use hardware pack)
