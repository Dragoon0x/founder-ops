# B2B SaaS — Industry-Specific Playbooks

## Playbook: Land Your First Enterprise Logo

### Pre-Conditions
- Product handles your existing customers reliably (no fires)
- You have at least 3 mid-market customers as proof
- Founder has 2+ months of bandwidth to dedicate

### The Sequence

**Month 1: Target identification**
- Build list of 30-50 logical first enterprise targets
- Filter: industry fit, size (1K-5K employees ideal for first), no procurement nightmares
- Find the buyer (usually VP/Director of [department your tool serves])
- Identify warm paths: shared investors, your customers, advisors, conference

**Month 2: Outreach + first meetings**
- Personalized outreach (no batch, 30-50 sends)
- Goal: 5-10 first meetings
- First meeting agenda: 30 min — discovery (their pain, their stack, their buying process)
- Don't pitch product in first meeting

**Month 3: Pilots**
- For 2-3 most engaged: propose 30-day pilot
- Pilot terms: free, defined success criteria, named champion required
- During pilot: weekly check-ins, fix issues fast, document wins

**Month 4: Convert**
- Pilot review meeting with full buying committee
- Present results vs. success criteria
- Pricing: their first contract should be priced 30-50% lower than your "real" enterprise price (call it "design partner discount")
- Get a 1-year contract minimum (not month-to-month)
- Get a case study commitment in the contract

**Month 5-6: Reference + expand**
- Help them go live successfully
- Get the case study / quote
- Use as proof for next 5 enterprise deals
- Look for expansion within the org (other departments, more seats)

### Common Failure Modes
- Skipping discovery → pitching solution before knowing problem
- No champion → deals die when champion goes on vacation
- Bad pilot success criteria → no clear "success" or "failure" decision
- Underpricing the first deal → sets pricing precedent for the customer's whole org
- No contract clause for case study → they go live, never give the testimonial

## Playbook: Move Upmarket (SMB → Mid-Market)

### Why Move Up
- SMB churn is structural (companies fail, get acquired, change tools)
- Mid-market deals are 5-20x larger
- Higher ACV reduces support burden per dollar of revenue

### The Sequence

**Step 1: Assess readiness (4 weeks)**
- Can your product handle 100+ user orgs without breaking?
- Do you have basic enterprise features: SSO, role-based access, audit logs?
- Can you support 30-day pilot processes without burning the team?

**Step 2: Build the missing 20% (8-12 weeks)**
- SSO (SAML or OIDC) — usually 4 weeks
- Audit logs (who did what when) — 2-3 weeks
- Admin controls (centralized user management) — 3-4 weeks
- Security questionnaire pre-fills — ongoing

**Step 3: Reposition (4-6 weeks)**
- New landing page focused on team / org, not individual user
- Case studies featuring mid-market companies
- New pricing page with "Business" tier ($X-$Y per user/mo)
- Sales/contact form gating for enterprise inquiries

**Step 4: Build sales motion (3-6 months)**
- Hire one Account Executive with mid-market SaaS experience
- Build outbound sequences (target VP-level)
- Focus on 1-2 verticals first to compound learnings

### Pitfalls
- Hiring AE before features → AE quits because product can't sell
- Repositioning before product-readiness → losing SMB without gaining mid-market
- Pricing the new tier too aggressively → killing PLG signups

## Playbook: Reduce Churn (when you spot a churn problem)

### Diagnose First (Week 1-2)
- Pull 3 months of churn data
- Categorize each churn:
  - **Bad fit** (shouldn't have been customer)
  - **Failed onboarding** (never got to value)
  - **Lost champion** (their power user left their company)
  - **Better alternative** (competitor)
  - **Budget cut** (company-wide cost cutting)
  - **Product gap** (missing critical feature)
- Identify the dominant cause (usually one accounts for 40-60%)

### Fix the Top Cause

**If "Bad fit" dominates:**
- Tighten ICP definition
- Add qualifying questions to signup
- Train sales to disqualify

**If "Failed onboarding" dominates:**
- Define "activation" precisely (specific actions)
- Track activation rate weekly
- Improve onboarding flow until 70%+ activate

**If "Lost champion" dominates:**
- Build multi-threading into accounts (3+ users minimum)
- Quarterly business reviews with execs (not just users)
- Power user playbook to identify next champion

**If "Better alternative" dominates:**
- Win/loss interviews to confirm
- Prioritize the gap (use prioritize mode)
- Consider strategic pivot if competitor has structural advantage

**If "Budget cut" dominates:**
- Move to annual contracts (locks in budget)
- Reframe pricing as ROI, not expense
- Show usage/value reports to customers monthly

**If "Product gap" dominates:**
- It's a roadmap problem — feed into prioritize mode
- Consider buy/partner if you can't build fast enough
