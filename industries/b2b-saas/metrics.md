# B2B SaaS — Key Metrics & Benchmarks

## The Core 5 Metrics

Every B2B SaaS founder must know these cold:

### 1. ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)
- **What**: Annualized value of all active subscriptions
- **Formula**: MRR × 12 (or sum of annual contracts)
- **Why it matters**: The single most important number. Investors evaluate everything against ARR.

### 2. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- **What**: How much existing customers' revenue grew/shrunk
- **Formula**: (Starting ARR + Expansion - Churn - Contraction) / Starting ARR × 100
- **Benchmarks**:
  - <90%: Broken (customers leaving faster than expanding)
  - 90-100%: Below average
  - 100-110%: Average
  - 110-120%: Good
  - 120-130%: Excellent
  - 130%+: Best in class (Snowflake-tier)

### 3. Gross Margin
- **What**: Revenue minus cost of delivering the product
- **Formula**: (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100
- **B2B SaaS benchmarks**:
  - <50%: Probably not actually SaaS, or you have an infra problem
  - 50-65%: Below average
  - 65-75%: Average
  - 75-85%: Good
  - 85%+: Excellent (Datadog tier)
- **What's in COGS**: Hosting, third-party APIs you pass through, customer support cost

### 4. CAC Payback Period
- **What**: How many months until a customer's gross profit pays back what you spent to acquire them
- **Formula**: CAC / (Monthly ARPU × Gross Margin %)
- **Benchmarks**:
  - <6 months: Excellent (PLG tier)
  - 6-12 months: Good
  - 12-18 months: Acceptable
  - 18-24 months: Concerning
  - 24+ months: Broken unit economics

### 5. LTV:CAC Ratio
- **What**: Lifetime value of a customer divided by what it cost to acquire them
- **Formula**: (ARPU × Gross Margin / Monthly Churn) / CAC
- **Benchmarks**:
  - <1: Losing money on every customer
  - 1-3: Tight, hard to scale
  - 3-5: Healthy
  - 5+: Either excellent or you're under-investing in growth

## The Rule of 40

For Series B+ companies:

**Rule of 40 = Growth Rate (%) + Profit Margin (%)**

- Above 40%: Healthy
- 40-60%: Excellent
- 60%+: Best in class

Example: 80% growth + (-30%) margin = 50% Rule of 40 → healthy
Example: 30% growth + 25% margin = 55% Rule of 40 → excellent

|--------------------|-----|-----------|------------|
| 6 | 80% | 90% | 95% |

If you're significantly below: investigate onboarding, value delivery, account management.

## Quarterly Reporting Standards

Every Q, calculate and trend:
- ARR and ARR growth (QoQ and YoY)
- New ARR (new logos)
- Expansion ARR (existing customers growing)
- Churned ARR (customers leaving)
- Net New ARR (sum of above)
- Logo count and net new logos
- ACV (average contract value) and trend
- NRR and GRR
- CAC by channel
- Payback period
- Burn multiple ((Net Burn / Net New ARR) — target <1)
