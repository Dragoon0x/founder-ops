# B2B SaaS — Common Founder Pitfalls

## Pricing Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Pricing too low | Leaves 50%+ revenue on table; signals low value | Test with willingness-to-pay research; double price for new signups, measure |
| Per-seat when usage is the value | Customers add fewer seats than they should | Move to usage-based or hybrid pricing |
| One pricing page for all segments | SMB sees enterprise price, leaves; enterprise sees SMB, doesn't take seriously | Tiers + "Contact us" for enterprise |
| No annual discount | Higher churn, harder forecasting | Offer 15-20% annual discount |
| Discounting to close deals | Trains sales team and customers to expect discounts | Discount via term length, not price |
| Free tier with no upgrade path | Acquires users who never pay | Free tier must hit a paywall at value |

## Sales Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Founder selling forever | Doesn't scale past $1-2M ARR | Hire first AE around $500K-1M ARR |
| Hiring AEs before PMF | Burns money, damages brand | Wait until founder can repeatably close |
| No qualification framework | Pipeline full of bad deals | Implement BANT/MEDDIC by AE #2 |
| Selling to anyone | Wrong customers churn, drag support | Strict ICP enforcement |
| Demo-first sales motion | Prospects can't articulate need | Discovery-first, demo-second |
| No multi-threading | One champion leaves, deal dies | Always 3+ contacts per account |
| Forecasting in PowerPoint | No accountability | CRM by 5+ employees |

## Product Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Build by sales | Product becomes Frankenstein | PM (or founder) owns roadmap; sales requests routed through it |
| Building for biggest customer | Other customers don't need it | "5-customer rule" — won't build unless 5 ask |
| Enterprise features for SMB users | Bloats UX | Hide advanced features in admin |
| No deprecation policy | Codebase becomes museum | Sunset features with low usage every 6 months |
| Shipping without measurement | Don't know what works | Every feature ships with success metric |
| 0→100 features (no MVP) | Slow, often wrong | Ship the 20% that delivers 80%, learn |

## Hiring Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Hiring at wrong stage | Senior hires wait for direction; junior hires need it | Match seniority to stage |
| Hiring "consultants" as employees | They contribute but don't commit | Different role, different bar |
| Comp packages without reference | You over- or under-pay | Use Levels.fyi, Pave, or Carta benchmarks |
| No equity for early hires | Loses talent to competitors | First 5 hires: 0.5-2% equity |
| Hiring before defining role | Hire is set up to fail | Write the JD, defines what success looks like |
| Founder is sole interviewer | Hire reflects founder bias | 3+ interviewers, structured rubric |

## Fundraising Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Raising too early | Bad terms, dilution, distraction | Raise when growth is provable |
| Raising too much | Forces unnatural growth, hurts profitability | Raise 18-24 months runway |
| Targeting wrong fund stage | Wastes weeks | Match round size to fund's typical check |
| Single-track fundraising | One pass = no leverage | Run process in parallel |
| Skipping warm intros | 10x lower response rate | Warm intros via portfolio companies |
| Bad cap table by Series A | Investors flag, valuation hit | Clean cap table early — clear up advisor equity, dead founders |
| Optimizing for valuation | Can backfire (down rounds, hard term sheets) | Optimize for terms, partner, fund quality |

## Operations Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| No metrics dashboard | Flying blind | Build by 10 employees |
| All-hands too long | Exhausts team | 30 min weekly max |
| Founder in every meeting | Bottleneck | Empower decision owners |
| No company values written | Culture drifts | Write at 5-10 employees |
| No engineering hiring bar | Dilutes team quality | Same bar as first 3 engineers |
| Skipping security/compliance | One breach = company over | SOC 2 by 25 employees |

## Customer Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| No CS function past $2M ARR | Churn rises, expansion stalls | First CSM at $1-2M ARR |
| One-on-one customer calls scale until they don't | Founder time → bottleneck | Tiered CS by customer size |
| Ignoring small customers | They're future big customers | Self-serve everything <$5K ACV |
| No NPS measurement | Don't know who's at risk | Quarterly NPS by customer cohort |
| Chasing logos for vanity | Big logos = high churn risk if poor fit | Quality over logo prestige |

## Strategic Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it kills you | Fix |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Premature scaling | Burns cash before PMF | Demonstrate repeatable GTM first |
| Chasing every market | No market dominance | Win one segment, expand later |
| Following competitors | Reactive, not strategic | Build for customer, not competitor |
| Ignoring competitive moats | Replicable = commoditized | Network effects, data, switching cost |
| No vision past 12 months | Team disengages | Write 3-year vision; revisit annually |
