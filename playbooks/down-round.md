# Playbook: Down Round

## When to Use This
Your last round priced at $X. The market has shifted. New investors
won't pay $X. You have 2-6 months of runway. Your choices are: raise
at a lower valuation, sell, or shut down.

## First: Really Exhaust Alternatives

Before accepting a down round, check:

1. **Cost cuts** — Can you get to 18+ months runway by cutting non-essentials?
   Often possible. A down round is permanent damage; cuts are reversible.

2. **Revenue push** — Can you get to default-alive (CF-positive) in 3-4 months?
   Extreme focus on highest-ARR deals in pipeline.

3. **Insider flat round** — Would existing investors fund flat? Less punishing
   than an external down round. Ask directly.

4. **Strategic investor** — Is there a company in your stack who'd invest at
   your last valuation for strategic reasons?

5. **Acquihire/merger** — If the answer is "nothing works," sometimes a
   soft landing is better than a painful down round.

## If Down Round Is the Answer

### Timeline (6-10 weeks)

**Weeks 1-2:** Reset pricing honestly
- What's your ARR? Your growth rate? Your retention?
- At current market multiples, what's a fair price?
- Be honest — if your last round was bubble pricing, this is just re-pricing.

**Weeks 3-6:** Run the process
- Existing investors first
- New leads second — but expect harder terms
- Terms will likely include: 1x non-participating preferred, maybe liquidation
  preference stacking, maybe anti-dilution protection triggering

**Weeks 7-8:** Negotiate
- Valuation: accept market reality
- Participation: push hard for non-participating
- Liquidation multiple: fight for 1x, not 2x+
- Board seats: minimize changes

**Weeks 9-10:** Close and communicate

## Communication Playbook

### To existing investors
Brief and specific:
- Why the down round is happening (market + specific company factors)
- Who the lead is
- Terms
- Their pro-rata option
- Timeline

### To the team — after it closes

Do not hide it. People will look up Crunchbase. Say directly:

"We closed a round at a lower valuation than our last one. Market
conditions changed; we also didn't hit the metrics that would justify
our last price. This doesn't change our path — we have [X] months of
runway and a clear plan. It does mean common stock is reset. Here's
what this means for your equity: [specific]."

Offer: option refresh for key people. Don't let departures cascade.

### To customers

"We closed a new round led by [Investor]. Excited to keep building."
Move on. No one asks about valuation.

## What to Expect After

**Common equity:** Likely underwater for 12-24 months. Plan for this.

**Team retention:** Top performers will update LinkedIn. Get ahead with
honest conversations and equity refreshes.

**Future rounds:** Your next round will be measured against THIS new
valuation, not the old one. Makes the next round easier, not harder.

**Existing investors' reaction:** Seed investors often accept (they knew
early-stage is risky). Later-stage investors feel worse. Board dynamics
may shift.

## When to Shut Down Instead

Seriously consider shutdown over a down round when:
- [ ] Unit economics are fundamentally broken, not temporarily weak
- [ ] You've already pivoted 2+ times
- [ ] Core team has lost belief
- [ ] The down round doesn't solve the problem, just delays it

A graceful shutdown — returning capital to investors — preserves reputation
and enables future ventures. A slow bleed does not.
