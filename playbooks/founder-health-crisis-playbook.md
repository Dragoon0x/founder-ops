# Founder Health Crisis Playbook

Founder health crises are common and rarely discussed. Roughly 50% of founders report serious mental health concerns. A meaningful share experience acute physical health events — heart attack, stroke, cancer diagnosis, accident. This playbook is for when it happens to you or your co-founder.

## Classes of health crisis

### Acute physical (heart, stroke, accident, cancer)
- Days to weeks out
- Surgery and recovery timeline
- Return to work plan needed
- Insurance and disability coverage

### Chronic physical (autoimmune, chronic pain, fatigue)
- Ongoing management
- Energy budget changes
- Travel limitations
- Cognitive impact in flare-ups

### Mental health acute (suicidal ideation, psychotic break, severe PTSD)
- Inpatient or intensive outpatient
- Return to work gated on clinician sign-off
- Colleagues need not know details
- Suicide risk: do not leave them alone, call 988 in US

### Mental health chronic (depression, anxiety, bipolar, ADHD)
- Treatment ongoing
- Medication adjustments
- Performance impact during stabilization
- Stigma a real barrier to disclosure

### Substance use
- Disclosure controlled by founder
- Treatment plan coordinated
- Sobriety support system
- Return to work with explicit agreement

## First 72 hours: acute crisis

If the founder is incapacitated:

1. **Medical decisions**: family / healthcare proxy has authority, not co-founders
2. **Spouse / partner** is primary communication channel if available
3. **Board chair** notified within 24 hours
4. **Team leadership** notified on need-to-know basis
5. **Company operations**: pre-established succession plan activated
6. **Legal documents**: check for durable power of attorney, healthcare proxy, succession plan

## Business continuity

Before any founder health crisis, you should have:

- **Bus factor inventory**: what breaks if each founder is out for 30 days
- **Signing authority delegation**: cash controls, customer contracts, payroll
- **Cap table succession**: shares transfer in event of death (not default — set up)
- **Key person life insurance**: 2–3x annual salary minimum, company as beneficiary
- **Disability insurance**: short and long-term
- **Healthcare proxy and power of attorney**: notarized, filed, known location

If you don't have these, set them up before Friday.

## Internal communication

### Acute serious crisis (hospitalization, cancer)
- Team: "[Name] is dealing with a serious health issue and will be out for [timeframe]. [Interim leader] is running [function]. We're giving them space and support. Please respect their privacy."
- Do not: speculate, share medical details, predict return date

### Ongoing management (mental health, chronic)
- Disclosure is founder's choice
- If they disclose: team response is support, not questions
- If they don't: performance is addressed through normal channels
- Never disclose on their behalf

## Investor communication

- Board chair first
- Lead investor second
- Full board notification within 72 hours for material events
- Quarterly update frames as "founder health matter addressed, business continuity in place" — no more
- Avoid specifics

## Role changes during recovery

Options during recovery period:

1. **Full leave**: 3+ months, board chair or interim CEO runs company
2. **Reduced role**: 20 hours/week, specific workstreams dropped
3. **Advisory only**: strategic input, no operational responsibility
4. **Accommodation**: schedule changes, WFH, no travel

Document the change in writing. Revisit in 30 days. Don't pretend recovery is linear.

## Return to work

- Clinician sign-off required
- Written return plan (role, hours, escalation)
- Co-founder or chief of staff as primary interface for first 30 days
- No big decisions in first 2 weeks
- Check-in at 30, 60, 90 days

## When the crisis is permanent or extended

If recovery is not possible within 6 months:

- Founder role may need to change permanently
- Board and remaining founders negotiate role and equity treatment
- "Founder emeritus" titles exist for a reason
- Vesting acceleration often appropriate for incapacity
- Legal counsel for both sides

## Mental health specifically

- Confidential EAP (Employee Assistance Program) accessible to founders (often isn't, set up one)
- Therapist on standing relationship, not first-time search during crisis
- Peer CEO groups (YPO, EO, founder-specific)
- Medication management with specialist psychiatrist, not general physician
- Sobriety programs or groups (AA, SMART Recovery, Tempest)

## If you are the founder having the crisis

- Your health is not negotiable
- The company will survive your absence better than you think
- Decisions you make at 30% capacity will damage the company more than taking leave
- Seek help. Not "someday" help. Today help.
- Your team loves the company because of the product, not because you sacrificed your health

If you're in US crisis: 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline. Internationally: befrienders.org for local lines.

## If you are the co-founder

- You will feel scared, overwhelmed, resentful, and guilty. All at once.
- You need a therapist too.
- You need to run the company without them for an unknown period.
- You need to communicate with clarity and kindness.
- The hardest part: you cannot save them. You can only support them while they save themselves.

---

*Your health is part of the company's balance sheet. Protect it.*
