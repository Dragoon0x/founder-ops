# Playbook: Hitting 25, 50, 100 People

## Why These Sizes Matter

Companies break in predictable ways at predictable sizes. Knowing what's coming
lets you prepare instead of react.

## At ~25 People (the "lose direct contact" wall)

### What changes
- You can no longer have meaningful 1:1s with everyone monthly
- Decisions you used to make personally now get made by others (sometimes wrong)
- Onboarding new people becomes a real process, not "shadow me"
- Culture becomes harder to transmit (the original team has it; new hires don't automatically)
- You have your first "I don't know what [person] is working on" moment

### What to do BEFORE hitting 25
- [ ] Document core values explicitly (not "be a great teammate" — specific behaviors)
- [ ] Define how decisions get made (who decides what, with what level of input)
- [ ] Establish weekly all-hands rhythm
- [ ] Hire your first non-IC: usually a Head of Engineering or Head of Operations
- [ ] Define team structure (functional? pods? hybrid?)
- [ ] Set up an internal wiki / single source of truth (Notion, Confluence)

### Common breakdowns
- **"We used to be so fast"** → You're not slow, you're more careful. That's fine.
- **"The new people don't get it"** → Onboarding gap. Build it.
- **"I'm a bottleneck on everything"** → Delegate. Hard. Stop being the smartest person in every meeting.
- **"Decisions take forever now"** → Define decision rights explicitly.

## At ~50 People (the "first management layer" wall)

### What changes
- You need real managers (not player-coaches)
- HR, recruiting, ops become real functions (not founder side jobs)
- Politics emerges (factions, promotion frustration, comp comparison)
- Communication breaks (people genuinely don't know what's happening)
- Strategy needs to be re-articulated (it's not obvious anymore)

### What to do BEFORE hitting 50
- [ ] Hire experienced managers (or train your best ICs intensively)
- [ ] Hire first HR/People person (probably a generalist initially)
- [ ] Establish quarterly business reviews (formal, with metrics)
- [ ] Document compensation philosophy (bands, leveling, equity guidelines)
- [ ] Set up cross-functional team rhythm (not just functional silos)
- [ ] Stop being on the engineering / product / sales channels for everything

### Common breakdowns
- **"Our managers aren't great"** → You promoted ICs without training. Invest in management training.
- **"I don't know my own team anymore"** → True. Optimize for skip-levels and culture, not knowing everyone.
- **"Hiring is taking forever"** → Recruiting is a real function. Hire a recruiter.
- **"There's drama I didn't know about"** → Always was. Now you're hearing about it. Trust HR to handle it.

## At ~100 People (the "you're a real company" wall)

### What changes
- You have functions you barely interact with
- Strategy must be a document, not a vibe
- Performance management becomes formal
- Compensation reviews are systematic (not ad hoc)
- Legal, security, compliance become full-time concerns
- Office (or remote operations) becomes a real cost center
- Some early employees leave (this is normal — different companies need different people)

### What to do BEFORE hitting 100
- [ ] Articulate strategy in writing (multi-year, with explicit choices)
- [ ] Build the executive team (CFO, COO, CMO, CPO depending on business)
- [ ] Establish formal performance management cycle (twice a year minimum)
- [ ] Set up internal communications team (not just announcements — real comms)
- [ ] Implement security / compliance basics (SOC 2, GDPR, etc.)
- [ ] Hire first lawyer (in-house or fractional)
- [ ] Establish board governance (real board, real schedule, real materials)

### Common breakdowns
- **"We've lost the magic"** → Different stage, different magic. Define what's still true.
- **"Decisions are too slow"** → They are. The cost of speed is now wrong decisions. Pick your battles.
- **"Top performers are leaving"** → Compensation, growth opportunities, or culture issue. Diagnose carefully.
- **"I miss when we were 30 people"** → Of course you do. But 30 people couldn't do what 100 can. Stay forward.

## Founder Mindset Shifts at Each Stage

### 1-25
You're the smartest person about everything.
You hire to extend yourself.
You make most decisions.

### 25-50
You become the smartest person about strategy and culture.
You hire experts who are smarter than you in their domain.
You delegate operational decisions.

### 50-100
You become a coach and storyteller.
You hire people who could be CEOs themselves.
You make a few major decisions per quarter, the rest your team handles.

### 100+
You become a vision-setter and hard-decision maker.
You hire executives who run their own companies-within-a-company.
You're spending most of your time on board, fundraising, customers.

## Anti-Patterns at Every Stage

| Stage | Anti-Pattern | Symptom |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1-25 | Hiring "experienced" people who need structure | They get frustrated, slow you down |
| 25-50 | Avoiding hard conversations | Drama compounds |
| 50-100 | Trying to know everyone | Burnout, neglect strategy |
| 100+ | Still doing IC work | Critical decisions get neglected |
