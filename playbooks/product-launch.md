# Playbook: Product Launch

## Definition: What Counts as a Launch

Three types — different strategies:
1. **Soft launch** — Live for early users, no marketing push (most launches)
2. **Stealth launch** — Live but unannounced (rare, usually B2B with single design partner)
3. **Public launch** — Coordinated push with PR, social, communities (only when ready)

This playbook covers public launch.

## Pre-Launch Requirements

Don't launch publicly until:
- [ ] 20+ customers using it actively (week 4 retention > 40%)
- [ ] At least 3 customer references / case studies
- [ ] Pricing is finalized (don't change it within 30 days of launch)
- [ ] Onboarding can handle 100+ signups in a day (or you have a waitlist)
- [ ] Support process can handle 50+ tickets in a week
- [ ] You can articulate your differentiation in 1 sentence
- [ ] Landing page passes the 5-second test (someone can tell what you do)

## Launch Calendar (4 weeks)

### Week -4: Foundations
- Finalize launch date (Tuesday or Wednesday best — never Monday or Friday)
- Lock pricing and terms
- Build launch landing page
- Pre-write all assets (don't write the day of)
- Identify your launch channels (max 3 you'll seriously work)

### Week -3: Outreach
- Email influencers/journalists/podcasters in your space (NOT to "be covered" — to ask if they'd want early access)
- Schedule podcast appearances for launch week
- Line up customer testimonials for use in launch posts

### Week -2: Asset prep
- Demo video (under 90 seconds, no music required)
- 5-10 product screenshots (for blog, social, press)
- Launch blog post (your version of the story)
- Twitter thread (8-10 tweets)
- LinkedIn post (founder POV)
- Hacker News post (if applicable — most won't fit)
- Product Hunt launch package (if applicable)
- Email to existing list

### Week -1: Final checks
- Press kit live (logo, screenshots, founder photos, fact sheet)
- All assets approved by team
- Customer reference availability confirmed
- On-call rotation set for launch day support

### Launch day
- Hour 0 (US Pacific morning): Site goes live, blog post live, Twitter thread, LinkedIn
- Hour +1: Email to your list
- Hour +2: Hacker News (if applicable)
- Hour +6: Product Hunt (if launching there)
- Throughout day: Engage with comments, replies, support

### Week +1: Compound
- Personal thank-yous to every supporter
- Send press follow-ups with launch metrics
- Schedule follow-up content (case studies, deep dives)
- Begin "post-launch" growth experiments

## Channel Selection

You can't seriously work more than 3 channels. Pick based on where YOUR customers are:

| Channel | Best For | Effort | Avoid If |
|---------|----------|--------|----------|
| Hacker News | Dev tools, open source, technical products | High (write a great post + monitor 24h) | Non-technical product |
| Product Hunt | Consumer, productivity, design tools | High (need community) | B2B enterprise |
| Twitter/X | Building in public audience already | Medium (depends on existing audience) | No existing following |
| LinkedIn | B2B, enterprise, professional tools | Medium | Consumer, very young audience |
| Reddit | Niche communities aligned with product | High (need real community presence) | No prior community engagement |
| Direct email | Existing list, high-intent | Low | No list |
| Press / podcasts | Brand-building, B2B credibility | Very high (3-month lead time) | Want immediate signups |

## Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| Launching before product is ready | Users leave, never come back |
| Launching on Monday | Inbox overload, low engagement |
| Trying to launch on every channel | Spread thin, win nowhere |
| Coordinating launch with team news (funding, hire) | Diluted message |
| Buying ads for launch day | Looks desperate, low ROI |
| Posting and disappearing | Comments without responses kill momentum |
| Not having a "what's next" plan | Launch hype dies, you have nothing to follow up with |

## Success Metrics (measure 30 days post-launch)

- Total signups: depends on goal
- Activation rate: > 40% reach the "aha" moment
- Week 4 retention: > 30% still active
- Press / mentions: at least 3 organic mentions in target publications
- New customers from launch: aim for 2-3 months of normal acquisition in launch week
- Brand searches: organic search for your brand name should 3-5x

## What "Bad Launch" Looks Like (and what to do)

- Lots of signups, low conversion → onboarding problem (run customer-success mode)
- Few signups, high conversion → distribution problem (need more channels next time)
- Negative feedback / criticism → don't argue. Acknowledge. Iterate.
- Total silence → product doesn't have differentiation. Go back to positioning.
