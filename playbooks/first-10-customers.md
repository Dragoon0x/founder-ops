# Playbook: First 10 Customers

## Pre-requisite
You have a working prototype or MVP. Not a deck. Not a landing page.
An actual thing a customer can use.

## The Hard Truth
Your first 10 customers will NOT come from:
- A launch on Product Hunt (maybe 1-2)
- Social media virality
- SEO
- Paid ads

They will come from:
- People you already know who have the problem
- Cold outreach where you personally wrote 100 emails
- Showing up in communities where your ICP hangs out
- Warm intros from the 5 people who believe in you

## The Process

### Week 1-2: Narrow the ICP

Write down a specific person who has the problem:
- Company size:
- Role:
- Industry:
- How they currently solve the problem:
- What makes them feel the pain most acutely:
- Where they find new tools:

This should be so specific that you could name 10 real people who match it.

### Week 3-4: Build the list of 100

Not 1,000. Not 50. Exactly 100 people who match the ICP.

Sources:
- LinkedIn search with filters
- Community members (Slack/Discord/forums)
- Your network (friends-of-friends)
- Conference attendee lists
- People who engage with competitor content

For each: name, role, company, link to one specific thing they posted or
built that shows they have the problem.

### Week 5-6: Reach 50 of them

Send a personal note. Not a sales email. A real message:

> "Hi [name] — I saw you posted about [specific thing]. I built a tool
> that [specific outcome]. Would you be willing to try it for 15 minutes
> and tell me if it sucks? No obligation."

Goal: 10 replies. 5 calls. 2-3 first customers.

### Week 7-10: Convert and expand

- Onboard the first customers personally — white-glove
- Fix the things they can't figure out
- Ask each one: "Do you know 2 other people with this problem?"

### Week 11-12: Close the feedback loop

By now you should have 5-10 customers. Interview each one:
- Why did you pick us?
- What nearly stopped you?
- What would make you tell a friend?
- What would make you churn?

Feed these into pricing, positioning, product.

## The Metrics

By month 3 you should have:
- [ ] 10+ paying customers (even $10/mo each)
- [ ] Written answers from each on WHY
- [ ] 2+ unsolicited referrals
- [ ] A hypothesis about the best acquisition channel
- [ ] Clear sense of what the product actually does (which may differ from what you planned)

## Red Flags at 3 Months

**Can't get 10:** The pain isn't real, the solution doesn't solve it, or
your pricing is wrong. Talk to 20 more prospects before changing anything.

**10 but high churn (>20% at month 2):** The product doesn't deliver. Pause
acquisition. Fix product.

**10 from 10 different ICPs:** No repeatability. You'll never get to 100
this way. Pick the 3 that most resemble each other and double down.

**10 but no referrals:** The product is fine but not remarkable. Need to
understand why people won't tell others.

## Anti-Patterns
- Running ads before you have 10 organic customers
- Starting a Discord/community before you have users
- Building a landing page instead of shipping product
- Hiring a salesperson before you've sold it yourself
- Hunting for "scalable" channels when you haven't done the unscalable ones
