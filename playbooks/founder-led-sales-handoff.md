# Playbook: Founder-Led Sales Handoff

## The moment
You've been selling as the founder for 6–18 months. You've hired an
AE or a Head of Sales. You need to hand off without breaking what
works.

## The trap
Handing off too fast: new AE flounders, pipeline drops, you lose 3
months.

Handing off too slow: new AE can't own their pipeline, doesn't develop,
leaves in 6 months.

## The 90-day handoff

### Weeks 1–2: AE shadows everything
- AE joins every demo you give.
- AE joins every follow-up call.
- AE joins internal pricing and strategy conversations.
- No selling yet — pure observation.
- Daily debrief: what would you do differently? What's not intuitive?

### Weeks 3–4: AE runs parts of the demo
- AE handles the middle 20–30 min of the demo (the product walkthrough).
- Founder opens and closes.
- AE starts owning follow-up emails.
- AE runs first objection handling with founder ready to jump in.

### Weeks 5–6: AE runs full demos, founder attends
- AE owns end-to-end demos.
- Founder is on the call but silent unless the deal is at risk.
- Debrief after every demo.

### Weeks 7–8: AE runs demos without founder
- Founder drops off the demos.
- Founder still available for escalations.
- AE owns pipeline management.
- Weekly 1:1 reviews each deal.

### Weeks 9–12: AE owns the pipeline, founder selectively engaged
- Founder only joins specific escalations (e.g., C-level meeting,
  deal > $100K).
- AE manages their own calendar, pipeline, forecast.
- Founder reviews pipeline weekly, not daily.
- By end of month 3, founder shouldn't be in 90%+ of sales calls.

## What to document during handoff

1. **ICP one-pager**: who we sell to, who we don't.
2. **Discovery script**: questions you ask in the first call.
3. **Demo flow**: your standard demo with why.
4. **Objection handling**: top 10 objections and your answers.
5. **Pricing and discounting rules**: what's approved, what needs CEO
   sign-off.
6. **Competitive battlecard**: how you position vs competitors.
7. **Success stories**: 5–10 reference customers with permission.
8. **Lost deal patterns**: why we lose.

If you haven't documented these during founder-led sales, do it during
the handoff. It's the only way to transfer the knowledge.

## Where handoffs fail

### Failure mode 1: Never fully letting go
The founder stays in the weekly pipeline review, in every demo above
$50K, in every competitive deal. The AE doesn't own the outcome,
doesn't develop, and leaves in 6 months.

Fix: set explicit rules on when founder engages. Default: not.

### Failure mode 2: Handing off the wrong person
First AE is often hired without rigorous evaluation. Founders hire
"closer types" they like, not people who fit the specific motion.

Fix: a real interview loop with a pitch-back exercise and references.

### Failure mode 3: Handing off without documentation
AE has to learn by osmosis. Takes 6 months instead of 3.

Fix: document during the handoff, not before.

### Failure mode 4: Customer anxiety
Customers bought because of the founder. They now get an AE and feel
demoted.

Fix: Introduce AE to top customers with warm handoffs. "This is Sarah,
she's joined to work with you day-to-day. I'll still be involved on
strategic questions."

## Success metrics

At month 3:
- AE owns 80%+ of active pipeline.
- Close rate maintained within 10% of founder-led.
- Sales cycle maintained within 20%.
- Founder's time in sales drops to < 10 hours/week.
- AE is at 50–70% of expected productivity.

At month 6:
- AE at 80–100% of expected productivity.
- Founder engaged only on strategic deals.
- New pipeline without founder involvement.

## What to do after successful handoff

Founder moves to the next thing:
- Hire the second AE (AE1 can help interview).
- Build out other functions.
- Focus on product and strategy.

Do NOT re-engage in sales unless there's a specific reason. Your job
is the next thing, not this.

## When the handoff isn't working

Signs at month 4:
- Close rate down 30%+.
- Pipeline not replenishing.
- AE asking for founder involvement in every deal.
- Customers calling founder directly.

If this happens:
- Don't re-engage in sales as the fix.
- First: is the AE wrong for the role? If yes, make the change.
- Second: is the handoff incomplete? Which of the documents above are
  missing? Fix that.
- Third: is the motion actually repeatable? Maybe founder-led was
  special. Back to the drawing board.
