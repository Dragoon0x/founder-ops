# Playbook: First VP Hire

## The Crossroads
You have 8-15 people. Everyone reports to you. You're burning 70% of
your week on management. You need a VP — but the wrong VP hire can
destroy a year.

## Should You Hire VP Now?

Answer all 4:
1. Do I have 4+ people in the function today?
2. Is my time on that function >30% of my week?
3. Is there a metric I can measure a VP against (pipeline, retention, ship velocity)?
4. Do I have 12+ months of runway to give them room to work?

If 3-4 yeses: hire. If 1-2: too early. Hire a senior IC or manager instead.

## What VP You Actually Need

| Pain | Role | Wrong hire |
|------|------|------------|
| Pipeline stalled, CAC rising | VP Sales or CRO | Marketing VP won't fix sales |
| Good leads, bad conversion | VP Sales | VP Marketing puts more leads in the top |
| Not enough leads | VP Marketing / Growth | VP Sales can't create demand |
| Product ships slowly | VP Eng | VP Product won't speed up engineering |
| Products ship but users don't love them | VP Product | VP Eng can't fix product sense |
| Can't retain team | VP People | This is usually YOU not shipping vision |

## The Calibration Problem

The #1 VP hiring mistake: hiring someone who managed 500 people at
BigCo when you need someone to manage 8 and also do the work.

**The test:** Can they do the IC job of their own team for 3 months
if needed? If no, too senior.

**Stage fit:**
- Series A startup: VPs who've gone 5→25 or 10→40 in the function
- Series B+ startup: VPs who've gone 25→100 in the function

Get stage fit wrong and you'll either have a player-coach who can't
build, or a builder who refuses to do player work.

## Sourcing

Target 40-60 candidates. Pipeline breakdown:

- 30% — Warm network (referrals from investors, advisors, other founders)
- 30% — Backdoor into companies that have your target VP at your target stage
- 20% — People who DMed you saying "I love your product" who have the background
- 20% — Recruiter (only if above 3 tap out)

**Warning on recruiters:** Retained search at the VP level costs $60-100K.
For first VP, try network-first. Recruiters work but make you feel like
your only option is overpaying for the wrong fit.

## Interview Process (6-8 weeks)

### Stage 1: Founder chemistry (60 min)
- Why this company, not the other 5 they're talking to?
- What do they see as the #1 problem in the function today?
- Tell me about a time you were wrong and changed your mind. (Tests ego.)

### Stage 2: Function mastery (60 min)
- Walk me through how you'd build pipeline from zero. (If VP Sales/Marketing.)
- Walk me through how you'd diagnose shipping velocity. (If VP Eng.)
- Push them on specifics. Generalists at this level = red flag.

### Stage 3: Work session (2-3 hours, paid $1-2K)
- Give them a real problem in your company
- Have them come back with a 30-60-90 plan
- Listen for: depth of diagnosis, specificity of plan, realistic timing

### Stage 4: Team interviews
- Meet 3-4 direct reports or peers
- Critical question to YOUR team: "Would you be energized or deflated reporting to this person?"

### Stage 5: References (5+ total)
- 2 forward references (they provide)
- 2 backchannels (people who worked with them you found independently)
- 1 CEO/founder they worked with

**Backchannel script:** "I'm hiring [name] as VP [function]. I'd love
your honest read. What's the thing I'd need to manage around?"

## Compensation

First VP at Series A:
- Base: $180K-250K
- Bonus: 20-30% target
- Equity: 0.5%-2% (varies hugely — more for CRO/CTO level, less for VP-with-CRO-above-them)
- Vesting: 4-year, 1-year cliff, standard

First VP at Seed:
- Base: $150K-220K (cash is tight)
- Equity: 1-3%
- Explicit: "This is a founding exec role"

## First 90 Days — What to Measure

Do NOT set aggressive revenue/launch targets in the first 90 days.
Set diagnostic + foundation goals:

**Days 1-30:**
- Met every team member 1:1
- Met every direct report's top 3 people
- Understood current state of the function (written assessment)
- Met 5 customers
- Identified top 3 priorities to work on

**Days 31-60:**
- Initial plan for the function (30-60-90 for THEIR plan)
- 2 quick wins shipped
- Hired or fired at most 1 person (usually not yet)

**Days 61-90:**
- Plan executing
- Clear weekly rhythm with you
- Visible ownership by the team of them as leader

If month 3 and the team is not following them yet — major problem.

## How VP Hires Fail

1. **Over-hiring:** VP from 500-person org trying to run your 8-person team like it's 500
2. **Under-hiring:** Senior IC promoted to VP who can't actually manage
3. **Wrong function:** Hired VP Sales when the real problem was Marketing
4. **Ego mismatch:** Founder won't let go; VP can't do the job
5. **Skipping references:** Bad hires almost always have a backchannel red flag you'd have caught

## When to Let Go

If at month 4-6:
- Team is not rallied around them
- Metric you hired them for isn't trending
- You still carry their work
- They cannot name the top 3 priorities

→ Difficult conversation. Usually you KNEW by month 3 but wanted to be
charitable. Speed here matters — bad VP damages the team every week they stay.
