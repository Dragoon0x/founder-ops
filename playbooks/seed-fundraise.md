# Playbook: Seed Fundraise

## When to Use This
You have early traction (some combination of: 10+ paying customers, $5K+
MRR, or a strong technical/product signal) and want to raise $1-3M to get
to Series A metrics.

## Pre-Flight Checklist (before any pitch)
- [ ] Clear answer to: "Why this, why now, why you?"
- [ ] 3 months of MRR growth data showing trend
- [ ] Customer references who will take a call
- [ ] 10-slide deck (see `templates/pitch-deck-outline.md`)
- [ ] Data room: cap table, financials, customer list, org chart
- [ ] Target list of 40-60 funds ranked by fit

## Timeline (4-6 weeks for seed)

### Week -4 to -1: Prep
- Finalize deck. Get 3 founder friends to review (brutal mode)
- Write the 30-second, 2-min, 10-min versions of your story
- Line up 5 "warmup" meetings with non-target investors for practice
- Get 3 intro-takers in each target's network identified

### Week 1: Launch
- **Day 1 (Monday):** Send 15-20 warm intros. Aim for Tuesday-Thursday first meetings.
- Do NOT do "first call" cold outreach. Reserve those for week 2.
- **Goal:** 8-12 first meetings in week 1.

### Week 2: Momentum
- First calls happen. Tight 30 minutes each.
- **After each:** 48-hour follow-up with specific answers to their questions
- Start creating scarcity language honestly: "We're running a 4-week process"
- Add 10 more outreach targets

### Week 3: Deep dives
- Partner meetings with interested funds
- Reference calls start (they call your customers)
- You call their founders (reverse references)

### Week 4: Term sheets
- Serious funds circle. You will get 0-3 term sheets.
- Negotiate leads first, not terms. Who do you want on your board for 7+ years?
- If you're getting nothing, go to Week 5-6 cold outreach push.

## Decision Points

### After 15 first meetings with no serious interest → DIAGNOSE
Three possible causes:
1. **Wrong investors** — audit fit, 60%+ of list was off-thesis. Rebuild target list.
2. **Wrong story** — re-do the 2-min pitch with 3 trusted founders, take notes
3. **Wrong traction** — metrics don't cross seed bar for this category (check `benchmarks/`)

### With 2+ term sheets → NEGOTIATE
Decision order:
1. Lead investor (who specifically) — this is a 7-year marriage
2. Valuation (satisfactory, not maximum)
3. Board composition (2 founders, 1 investor, 1 independent = safe)
4. Protective provisions (keep minimal)

### With 1 term sheet → LEVERAGE
Use it to push other interested funds: "We have a term sheet, closing
Friday. If you want to lead, we need signal by Wednesday."

### With 0 term sheets at week 5 → PAUSE
Six-week fundraising with zero term sheets = signal. Either pause and
improve metrics for 3-6 months, or talk to existing investors about
bridging the round themselves.

## Output
- `output/fundraise-target-list.md` — 40-60 fund pipeline
- `output/fundraise-outreach-tracker.md` — daily status
- `output/investor-update-[week].md` — weekly update to anyone warming

## Anti-Patterns
- Leaking that you're raising before you're ready (shuts doors)
- Taking every meeting (90% waste of time)
- Negotiating valuation before lead investor is set
- Accepting complex terms for higher valuation
- Raising for longer than 6 weeks (you become a full-time fundraiser)
