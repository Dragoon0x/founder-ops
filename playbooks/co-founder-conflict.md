# Playbook: Co-Founder Conflict

## The Statistic
65% of startups fail due to co-founder conflict (Noam Wasserman, Harvard).
More companies die from this than from running out of money.

## Recognize Early Signals

- You stopped having 1:1s
- You dread certain conversations
- You work around them instead of with them
- Team notices you disagree in meetings
- You've started keeping score
- You've imagined the company without them

Any 2 of these = time to address. All 6 = code red.

## First: Is This Fixable?

Most co-founder conflicts fall into one of four buckets:

### 1. Role & decision rights (fixable, 70% of conflicts)
"I'm the CEO but I feel like I'm not the CEO."
"We're making product decisions together but that's not working."

→ Fixable. Clarify decision rights. Write it down.

### 2. Velocity mismatch (fixable, 20%)
"They take weeks to decide things I'd decide in a day."
"They want to move fast; I want to be careful."

→ Fixable in short term. Long-term, may recur.

### 3. Vision mismatch (harder, 8%)
"They want to build a lifestyle business; I want a rocketship."
"They want to pivot; I don't."

→ Fixable only if you can realign. Often leads to separation.

### 4. Trust fracture (rarely fixable, 2%)
"I can't trust their judgment anymore."
"They did something I can't forgive."

→ Almost always leads to separation. Don't pretend otherwise.

## The Process (6-8 weeks)

### Week 1: Diagnose privately
- Write down what you're feeling. No filters.
- Identify which bucket (above).
- Name 3 specific situations that crystallized the problem.

### Week 2: The conversation (do not delay)

**Setup:**
- Neutral location (not the office)
- 2-3 hours blocked
- No alcohol. No devices.

**Script:**
- "I want to talk about us as co-founders. I've been feeling [X]. Before I share more, tell me how you've been feeling."
- Listen. Actually listen. Don't interrupt. Don't defend.
- Share your observations as data: "When you did X, I felt Y. I may have misread; tell me what was happening for you."

**End state:** Both of you know the specific issues. Both want to fix them.

### Week 3-4: Structured fixes

If role/decision rights:
- Write down who decides what. Be specific. Not "product" — "pricing, roadmap priorities, UI approval" to CEO; "technical architecture, hiring engineers, tool choices" to CTO.
- 1:1 cadence (weekly minimum)
- Retro in 4 weeks

If velocity mismatch:
- Time-boxed decisions: "Decisions we'll make by Friday, not next quarter"
- Reversibility framing: "Is this a one-way or two-way door?"
- Disagree-and-commit protocol

If vision mismatch:
- Revisit company thesis together. Writing it down.
- If thesis diverges >30%: either one of you pivots their preference or one of you leaves.

### Week 5-6: External support (mediator or advisor)
If direct conversation didn't resolve it in 4 weeks, bring in outside help.
- An experienced founder you both trust
- An executive coach (YC has lists)
- A professional mediator (yes, really)

### Week 7-8: Decision point

Two paths from here:

**Path A — we're aligned:** Continue. Set 30/60/90 checkpoints. Sign a
"co-founder operating agreement" (written doc on how you'll work).

**Path B — separation:** One of you leaves or reduces involvement.

## Separation (If It Comes to That)

Separation is often the right answer. 1:1 of 10 founding partnerships
end cleanly — the 9 that don't, should have ended earlier.

### Options (least to most disruptive)

1. **Role shift:** One founder moves to an advisor/board role, keeps some equity, exits day-to-day
2. **Part-time / sabbatical:** Temporary step back, re-evaluate in 6 months
3. **Full separation with vested equity:** They keep what vested, you buy out rest
4. **Full buyout:** Company buys all their equity, they have zero going-forward stake

### The Math

If 2 years vested (8/16 quarters on 4-year vest):
- They keep 50% of their grant
- You acquire 50% back to company pool

Buyout of their retained shares: Typically 1-2x trailing 12-month
revenue multiple, OR an agreed buyout price that respects their
early contribution.

### Legal
- NDA + release of claims (mutual)
- Transition plan (2-4 weeks)
- Public narrative (aligned, always)
- Update cap table, board, investors

### Communication

To team (aligned statement):
"[Name] has decided to [transition to advisor / start something new /
step back]. We've aligned on this together and believe it's the right
step for [name] and the company. [Remaining plan]."

Do not trash-talk. Ever. Reputation is permanent; grudges decay your life.

## What NEVER to Do

- **Let it fester.** Conflict that festers for 6+ months usually ends in implosion.
- **Try to make them leave by making their life hard.** Unethical, usually illegal, always backfires.
- **Go to investors before trying direct resolution.** They can't fix it; you look unstable.
- **Involve team in the conflict.** They'll pick sides; you both lose.
- **Make unilateral changes to equity or role.** Legal minefield.
