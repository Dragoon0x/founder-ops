# Playbook: Seed to Series A — The 18-Month Plan

## The Problem
You raised a seed. You have 18-24 months of runway. The question isn't
"will you run out of money?" — it's "will you build a Series A story?"

## The 18-Month Clock

### Months 1-3: Validate the GTM hypothesis
**Goal:** Find 10-20 customers. Know who they are with precision.

- [ ] Kill the broad ICP. Pick ONE segment. Smaller than you think.
- [ ] Talk to 30 prospects in that segment (see `modes/discovery.md`)
- [ ] Get 10 paying customers. Same segment.
- [ ] Write down WHY each one bought (verbatim, no paraphrasing)

**Kill criteria for segment:** If 50%+ of "ideal" prospects don't convert
or churn in 90 days, change segment. Don't double down on wrong segment.

### Months 4-6: Build the motion
**Goal:** Repeatable acquisition. Know your CAC and payback.

- [ ] Identify top 2 acquisition channels from your 10 customers
- [ ] Pour time into those 2. Ignore everything else.
- [ ] Build a basic funnel metric dashboard
- [ ] Hit 20-30 customers
- [ ] Document the sales motion (even if founder-led) so #2 seller could learn it

**Kill criteria for channel:** If a channel's CAC payback is >18 months
after 3 months of optimization, cut it.

### Months 7-9: First scale test
**Goal:** Hire #1 non-founder seller or marketer. Can they get to 70% of
founder productivity in 90 days?

- [ ] Hire before you think you're ready (see `playbooks/first-hire.md`)
- [ ] Give them the playbook you wrote in month 4-6
- [ ] Measure: can they close deals with the process?

**Kill criteria:** If they can't hit 50% productivity in 6 months with
reasonable effort, the motion isn't repeatable yet. Go back to founder-led.

### Months 10-12: Retention proof
**Goal:** 90%+ gross retention over a rolling 12-month window.

- [ ] Calculate cohort retention by month
- [ ] Interview churned customers — what's the pattern?
- [ ] Fix the top 2 churn drivers
- [ ] Hit net retention >105%

**If you're at <85% gross retention by month 12:** Stop hiring. Stop
pushing growth. Fix product until retention is solid. Cannot raise A
with leaky bucket.

### Months 13-15: Build the story
**Goal:** Assemble the Series A data room.

- [ ] Clean cohort analysis (retention, expansion)
- [ ] Channel attribution model (where does growth come from)
- [ ] Team plan (who you'll hire with A money, why)
- [ ] Competitive landscape (who wins each deal and why)
- [ ] 18-month model (what happens if Series A delays 6 months)

### Months 16-18: Fundraise window
**Goal:** Close Series A before month 20. Leaves 4 months cushion.

- [ ] Start building investor relationships NOW (months 13-15)
- [ ] Run the process (see `playbooks/seed-fundraise.md` — Series A version)
- [ ] If process at month 18 isn't working: bridge (`playbooks/bridge-round.md`)

## The Monthly Rhythm

Every month, ask:
1. Did we grow new ARR? By how much?
2. Did we improve retention? Cohort-over-cohort?
3. Did our funnel conversion improve?
4. Did we lose any lighthouse customers? Why?
5. Is the team we want to build actually buildable?

Answer honestly. Adjust next month.

## The Hidden Trap: The 6-Month Plateau
Most seed-stage startups hit a plateau at month 6-9 where growth stalls.
Three causes:
1. **Founder is the bottleneck** (all deals go through you)
2. **Channel is saturated** (your best channel tapped out)
3. **Product can't expand beyond first use case** (ceiling on ACV/usage)

Diagnose first. Don't just "push harder."
