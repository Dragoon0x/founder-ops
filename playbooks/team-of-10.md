# Playbook: Crossing 10 Employees

## Why This Matters

Between 5-10 employees, you're a small group. Above 10, you're a company.
The way you communicated, made decisions, and operated breaks at this transition.

## Signs You're Hitting the Wall

- Meetings you used to know about now surprise you
- Decisions that used to be obvious now require meetings
- Customer conversations no longer reach everyone
- Onboarding new people takes too long
- "I thought X was going to do that" becomes common
- You can't remember everyone's birthday / personal context

## The Transition (over 2-3 months)

### Step 1: Document Who Does What
Run `/founder-ops people` (RACI mode)

For every function, name:
- Responsible (does the work)
- Accountable (owns the outcome)
- Consulted (weighs in)
- Informed (kept in loop)

Gaps revealed? Either fill with hire, merge with existing role, or cut.

### Step 2: Create Org Structure (even if flat)
Even at 10 people, have:
- CEO (founder)
- 2-4 functional leads (eng, product, GTM, ops)
- Individual contributors

Each person should know who their manager is (even if manager is CEO).

### Step 3: Design Meeting Rhythm
Run `/founder-ops internal-comms`

New cadence:
- Daily: Async standup in #team (2 sentences each)
- Weekly: All-hands (30 min)
- Weekly: Eng standup (30 min)
- Weekly: GTM sync (30 min)
- Weekly: Leadership (founder + functional leads, 45 min)
- Bi-weekly: 1:1s with each direct report (45 min)
- Monthly: Strategy review
- Quarterly: Retro + planning

### Step 4: Build the Onboarding System
Run `/founder-ops onboarding`

Week 1 for every new hire:
- Day 1: Setup, security, core tools, #introductions post
- Day 2-3: Product deep dive, customer calls shadowing
- Day 4-5: First small project
- End of week 1: Loom recording on their first impressions

30-day, 60-day, 90-day milestones documented.

### Step 5: Standardize Decision Making
Run `/founder-ops decisions`

Three decision types:
- **Type 1 (reversible, cheap):** Individual decides, doesn't need approval
- **Type 2 (mid-stakes):** Decision owner + consult 1-2 others
- **Type 3 (strategic/expensive):** Full discussion, CEO signs off

Train team on when to escalate.

### Step 6: Write the Values Document
Run `/founder-ops team-culture`

Values should:
- Be specific (not "integrity" or "excellence" — everyone says that)
- Be anti-values too (what we explicitly DON'T do)
- Show up in hiring, firing, promotions
- Be stress-tested with examples

Values at 10 people become culture at 50.

### Step 7: Establish Communication Norms
Run `/founder-ops internal-comms`

Clear norms:
- Slack etiquette (threads, @-mentions, urgency signals)
- Email vs Slack vs call (decision tree)
- Async vs sync (default async)
- Response time expectations
- Meeting rules (agenda, notes, action items)

### Step 8: Hire HR/Ops (at 10-15)
At this point, someone needs to OWN:
- Hiring pipeline
- Onboarding
- Payroll and benefits
- HR compliance
- Office / remote work setup

If founder keeps doing this, it blocks other work.

### Step 9: First Leadership Hires
Beyond team leads, you'll need functional heads:

| Headcount | Role to hire |
|-----------|--------------|
| 10-15 | First manager (eng or GTM) |
| 15-25 | VP Eng or VP GTM |
| 25-40 | Both VPs + first Product lead |
| 40+ | Full C-suite starts emerging |

### Step 10: All-Hands Discipline
Weekly all-hands becomes critical. Don't skip:
- Even during tough times
- Especially during tough times
- Even when "we're too busy"

Structure the time well (see internal-comms mode).

## Common Failures at This Transition

| Failure | Result |
|---------|--------|
| Staying flat too long | Decisions slow, team frustrated |
| Not documenting | New hires lost, old hires burned out |
| Skipping 1:1s | People leave quietly |
| Ignoring culture | Bad habits compound |
| CEO doing everything | Bottleneck, burnout |
| Reactive hiring | Wrong people, wrong roles |

## 90-Day Checklist

- [ ] Org chart posted (every person knows who their manager is)
- [ ] RACI complete for top 20 functions
- [ ] Meeting rhythm running for 4+ weeks
- [ ] Onboarding documented with 30/60/90 milestones
- [ ] Values written (tested on at least 3 real decisions)
- [ ] Decision framework explained to team
- [ ] Communication norms posted
- [ ] First managers identified/hired
- [ ] HR/Ops hire in progress or done
- [ ] All-hands running weekly
