# Playbook: Co-Founder Leaving

## When It Actually Happens
They tell you: "I'm out." Or you conclude together: "We should part ways."

The next 6 weeks decide whether this is a healthy transition or a public mess.

## Step 1 (Day 1): Contain the Information
- Only people who need to know: you, them, lawyer, board chair
- Do NOT tell team, customers, or broader network yet
- Agree on timing of announcement
- Begin drafting joint statement

## Step 2 (Week 1): Legal + Equity

Hire a corporate lawyer (1 hour is enough for setup). Questions:
- What's the vesting schedule? How much has vested?
- Is there a buyback or repurchase option for unvested shares?
- Founder acceleration language (if any)?
- IP assignment — is everything they created assigned to the company?
- Non-compete / non-solicit obligations (usually tied to equity receipt)

### Standard Terms to Negotiate

**Their retained equity:**
- Unvested shares: Return to option pool (standard)
- Vested shares: They keep; you CAN offer buyout
- Acceleration: Depends on their exit reason + original docs

**Buyout (if offered):**
- Fair market value based on recent 409A or last round
- Liquidity when the company has liquidity (most common)
- Immediate cash buyback (rare unless company has cash)

### Non-Disparagement (Mutual)

Both sides agree not to disparage the other. Critical for both.

### Non-Compete

Narrow. Time-bounded (12 months typical). Specific to your domain.
Overly-broad non-competes are unenforceable in most US states and create bad blood.

## Step 3 (Week 2): Draft the Plan

### Operational handoff
- What is this person currently doing?
- Who takes each thing over?
- What's the 2-4 week transition?

### Customer handoff (if any)
- Which customers have them as primary contact?
- Warm handoff call to each with new owner

### Team handoff
- Who reports to them today?
- Where do those people report going forward?

### Product handoff (if technical)
- Code ownership
- Architectural knowledge transfer
- Documentation of active work

## Step 4 (Week 3): The Announcement

### Pre-announcement: Key stakeholders
- Board (full email, 24 hours before team)
- Key investors (individual calls for top 3)
- Critical customers (top 5 accounts, individual calls)
- Advisors (email)

### Day of: Team all-hands
Both founders present together if possible.

**Script:**
"[Co-founder name] is [transitioning to advisor / moving on to start something new / etc]. We've worked through this together over the past [X] weeks. [Their work] will now be led by [person]. [Co-founder] will [continue as advisor for 3 months / fully step away]. We remain close friends and supporters of each other."

Accept questions. Keep answers boring. Don't over-share the drama.

### Day of: External announcement
Short LinkedIn post or email. Boring is good:
"I'm [transitioning / leaving] [Company]. It's been [genuine observation].
The company is in great hands with [remaining founder(s)]. I'm excited
about [what they're doing next or that they're thinking about it]."

The goal: no one can tell if this was amicable or not. Implies amicable.

## Step 5 (Week 4-6): Execution

- Handoffs happen
- Transition meetings with each stakeholder
- They leave by agreed date
- Cap table updated
- Board composition adjusted if needed
- Title updates (LinkedIn, bios, email)

## The Hardest Part: Grief

Losing a co-founder is a real loss. For both of you.

- Don't pretend it's fine
- Don't weaponize your feelings at the team
- Give yourself 30 days to feel weird
- Friendships often pause for 6-12 months then resume

## Financial Planning (For You, The Remaining Founder)

You now carry their work load until you hire. Budget:
- 2-4 weeks of reduced output
- 2-6 months to find a replacement (especially if technical co-founder)
- Possible runway extension needed
- Possible product roadmap delays

Tell your board. Update runway projections. Be honest with investors.

## Potential Blow-Up Scenarios

### They leave angry
- Overcommunicate with stakeholders so the narrative is yours
- Do NOT respond to disparagement in kind
- Document everything

### They take team members with them
- Enforce non-solicit if in place
- Focus on retention conversations with remaining team
- Do NOT compete on counter-offers; explain the vision clearly

### They start a competitor
- Legal review of non-compete
- Focus on execution, not drama
- Assume they'll say nothing in public; you do the same

### Investor concern / pullback
- Proactive 1:1 with every institutional investor
- Specific plan for what changes (and what doesn't)
- Demonstrate your ability to execute alone
