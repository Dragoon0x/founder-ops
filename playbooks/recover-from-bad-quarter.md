# Playbook: Recover from Bad Quarter

## When to Run
After a quarter where you materially missed your goals. Don't start too
soon — give yourself a weekend to breathe first.

## Phase 1: Face It Honestly (Week 1)

### Step 1: The Real Retro
Run `/founder-ops retro` (quarterly mode) with brutal honesty.

Don't sugarcoat:
- What was the goal?
- What was the actual?
- What's the delta (in absolute terms)?
- Was the goal wrong, or the execution, or both?

### Step 2: 5 Whys on the Biggest Miss
Run `/founder-ops`... apply the 5 Whys framework from `frameworks/5-whys.md`

Pick the single biggest miss. Ask why 5 times. Follow the chain to root cause.

### Step 3: Segment the Misses
Not all misses are the same:
- **Execution misses:** We tried and fell short → fix execution
- **Wrong goals:** We hit the goal but nothing improved → fix goal-setting
- **External shocks:** Market/competitor caused it → adapt, don't blame
- **Team issues:** People problems manifested as metric problems → address people

## Phase 2: Re-ground (Week 2)

### Step 4: Refresh the Strategy
Run `/founder-ops strategy`

Hard questions:
- Is the overall strategy still right?
- Which assumptions proved false?
- What's changed in the market?
- Are we serving the right customer?

Sometimes a bad quarter is tactical (keep going, fix execution).
Sometimes it's strategic (change direction).

### Step 5: Re-plan with Realism
Run `/founder-ops prioritize`

New quarterly plan:
- Ambitious but achievable (not the same targets that just missed)
- Prioritize FIXING the miss, not chasing new things
- Cut the bottom 50% of the roadmap (you don't have bandwidth)

## Phase 3: Communicate (Week 2-3)

### Step 6: Team Communication
Run `/founder-ops internal-comms`

All-hands on the bad quarter:
- Own it directly (no spinning)
- Share what you've learned
- Share the new plan
- Express confidence (grounded, not fake)

The team already knows. Your acknowledgment relieves pressure.

### Step 7: Investor Communication
Run `/founder-ops update`

The update:
- Lead with the miss (don't bury it)
- Show you understand why
- Show the revised plan
- Show early signals of change (if any)
- Specific asks to help the turnaround

### Step 8: Board Communication
Run `/founder-ops board`

If board meeting is coming:
- Pre-call each board member individually (no surprises at the meeting)
- Have your turnaround plan ready
- Ask for specific help
- Show you've already taken action

## Phase 4: Execute (Month 1-3)

### Step 9: Daily/Weekly Metrics
Run `/founder-ops metrics` every week:
- Leading indicators of the turnaround
- Early warning signs if plan isn't working
- Mid-course corrections

### Step 10: Kill Ruthlessly
Anything that doesn't directly serve the turnaround → pause or kill.

- Non-critical hires → delay
- Expansion plans → defer
- Nice-to-have features → cut
- Experimental channels → freeze

Focus is the recovery tool.

### Step 11: Hit Small Wins Early
Set up quick wins in first 30-60 days:
- Visible progress on something important
- Rebuilds team confidence
- Proof points for investors
- Permission to keep going

Don't wait for the big turnaround metric. Stack small wins.

## Phase 5: Assess (End of Quarter)

### Step 12: New Retro
Is the turnaround working?

- Leading indicators: improving?
- Team energy: recovered?
- Customer sentiment: shifted?
- Revenue: turning?

**If yes:** Double down. Remove the crutches of "turnaround mode" and return to growth.

**If no:** Deeper conversation needed. Is this a bigger issue than a bad quarter?
- Is the strategy wrong?
- Is the team wrong?
- Is the founder wrong?
- Is the company viable?

## When the Bad Quarter is Existential

Some bad quarters reveal deeper issues. Signs:
- Churn accelerating despite interventions
- Team departures increasing
- Market moving away from you
- Product-market fit never actually existed
- Co-founder doubt emerging

These require deeper conversations:
- Board-level strategic review
- Potential pivot discussion
- M&A consideration
- Honest CEO reflection (are you the right leader?)

## Output Artifacts
- `reports/retro-quarter-Q#-YYYY.md`
- `output/turnaround-plan-Q#.md`
- `output/strategy-refresh-post-miss.md`
- `output/all-hands-bad-quarter.md`
- `output/investor-update-bad-quarter.md`

## What NOT to Do

- Blame external factors (accurate or not, it's bad leadership)
- Hide the miss (investors/team already know)
- Fire people reactively (panicked terminations are almost always wrong)
- Make dramatic strategic changes immediately (bad quarter decisions are bad decisions)
- Return to "growth mode" before fixing what caused the miss
- Skip the team conversation (they'll fill the void with worse stories)
- Pretend nothing happened (erodes trust when it comes out)
