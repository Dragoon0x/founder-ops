# Executive Departure Playbook

When a VP or C-level leaves, the damage window is 30 days. Handled well, the team barely notices. Handled poorly, you lose three more people in 90 days.

## The three scenarios

### Scenario 1: They quit
- You have 2–8 weeks notice
- They want a clean exit
- Your job is smooth transition and protecting team

### Scenario 2: You fire them
- Decision made, executing now
- Legal and comms risk highest
- Your job is speed and narrative control

### Scenario 3: Mutual transition
- Both sides know it isn't working
- Negotiation of exit terms
- Your job is honest timeline and dignity

## First 24 hours (regardless of scenario)

1. Legal counsel briefed within 2 hours
2. Separation agreement drafted (scenarios 2 and 3)
3. Interim leadership decision made
4. Board chair / lead investor notified before public
5. Exec's direct reports notified 1-on-1 before team
6. Team notification plan drafted
7. External comms (if role is public) drafted

## Interim leadership

Options, ranked:

1. **Internal promotion**: best for morale, fastest continuity, rarely the best long-term hire
2. **Board member stepping in**: good for 30–90 days, expensive long-term
3. **Fractional exec**: good for specialized roles (CFO, CTO), 4–8 week ramp
4. **You (the CEO)**: only viable for 60 days max in critical functions

Decide before the announcement. "We'll figure it out" signals loss of control.

## Team notification

- Exec's direct reports first, individual 1:1s, 15 min each
- Full team within 4 hours of first 1:1
- Company all-hands within 24 hours
- Customers/partners (if exec was their primary contact) within 48 hours

## What to say

Scenario 1 (they quit):
> "[Name] has decided to [next thing]. We're grateful for their contribution. [Interim leader] will step in while we search. Here's how this affects you: [specifics]."

Scenario 2 (you fired them):
> "[Name] is no longer with the company, effective today. [Interim leader] will lead the team. I won't be sharing details of the decision out of respect for [name] and legal process. Here's what this means for the team: [specifics]."

Scenario 3 (mutual):
> "[Name] and I have agreed that [next chapter]. They'll stay through [date] to ensure transition. [Plan]."

## What not to say

- "I'm shocked." (you shouldn't be)
- "[Name] is taking on a new challenge." (if fired, this is dishonest)
- "We wish them the best!" (read as hollow; be specific or silent)
- Any indication of the real reason if you fired them (litigation risk)
- "This was mutual" when it wasn't

## Searching for the replacement

- Hire a retained search for VP+ roles (exec search: 28–33% of first-year comp)
- Timeline: 90–120 days from kickoff to accepted offer
- Do not compromise on level because you're anxious
- Do not promote someone who isn't ready because they are available
- Panel interview with at least 2 board members for C-level

## Legal minefields

- Trade secret protection: inventory their access, rotate credentials
- Non-solicit enforcement (check jurisdiction — California voids most)
- IP assignment confirmation (especially for CTO departures)
- Stock repurchase rights and vesting acceleration
- Confidentiality provisions in separation agreement
- Non-disparagement mutual, not one-way

## Customer and investor implications

- Top 5 customers: founder call within 48 hours if CRO/CSO departs
- Board: offline update within 24 hours, formal board note within 72
- LPs (if fund-relevant): notification per side letters
- Press: only if exec was public-facing and resignation will leak

## 30 / 60 / 90 day reset

**Day 30**
- Interim leader has 1:1 with every team member
- Reporting structure confirmed
- Any immediate process changes documented

**Day 60**
- Search for permanent replacement at finalist stage
- Team pulse check (anonymous survey)
- Any "surprise departures" in the function addressed

**Day 90**
- Permanent leader hired or final slate
- Strategic priorities re-confirmed
- Retention risk assessment for team

---

*Executive turnover is not a crisis if you treat it with speed, honesty, and respect.*
