# Hire or Outsource

## Context
You have work that needs doing. Employee, contractor, or agency?

## The tree

### Step 1: Is this work strategic or executional?
- **Strategic** (core product decisions, customer relationships, brand voice, hiring): hire.
- **Executional with company-specific context** (customer support after scale, analytics pipelines): hire or contractor for extended term.
- **Executional, commoditized** (bookkeeping, payroll, design for one-off, copyediting): outsource.
- **Specialized, short-term need** (security audit, brand identity, pitch deck): outsource specialist.

### Step 2: Is it a recurring need or project?
- Recurring, > 20 hrs/week forever: hire.
- Recurring, 5-20 hrs/week: contractor on retainer.
- Project with clear endpoint: agency or freelancer.

### Step 3: Does proximity matter?
- High (real-time collaboration, inside decisions): hire preferred.
- Medium (regular sync, but not real-time): contractor or fractional exec.
- Low (deliverable-based): outsource, possibly offshore.

### Step 4: Do you know the shape of the role?
- Yes: hire or fixed-scope outsource.
- No: outsource or fractional exec first. Learn the role by watching someone do it. THEN hire.

### Step 5: Economic comparison
Fully loaded employee cost = salary × 1.3 (US, benefits, taxes, equipment).
Good senior contractor = 1.5-2x equivalent hourly rate, no benefits, no commitment.
Agency = 2-3x hourly rate, turnover risk but depth of bench.
Offshore contractor = 0.3-0.6x US rate, more coordination cost.

Math: contractor is cheaper < 30 hrs/week. Employee is cheaper > 30 hrs/week for same quality. Agencies can be cheaper if you have sporadic need for senior depth.

## Tiebreakers
- **Do you have bandwidth to manage?** Contractor/agency requires tighter management. If you can't spend 4-8 hrs/week managing, you need an employee (who over time becomes self-directing).
- **Will this person be customer-facing?** Customers respond better to employees. Contractors fine for backend.
- **IP considerations.** Any IP-creating role needs clean IP assignment. Employees cleaner by default. Contractors need specific contracts.

## Common wrong answers
- "Hiring is expensive, let's contract." (Often false when full TCO of management overhead is counted.)
- "Let's agency for now and hire later." (Sometimes right, but don't build dependence on agencies for core functions.)
- "Let's hire because we feel we should have a team." (Ego hiring. Stop.)
- "Let's not hire this role because budget." (But you're already spending the money in other ways — burn tracking helps.)

## Reversibility
- Hire → fire: 1-3 months of effort, emotional cost, moderate $ cost.
- Contractor → let go: same day, clean. (If contracts are written well.)
- Agency → switch: 1-2 months, often high.

## Role archetypes and where to start
- **Engineering #1**: HIRE. Don't outsource.
- **Designer #1**: START OUTSOURCE, THEN HIRE at Series A.
- **Sales #1**: HIRE. Not outsource. Not fractional (long-term).
- **CFO/Finance**: FRACTIONAL → HIRE at Series A/B.
- **Marketing**: START AGENCY/FREELANCE, THEN HIRE once playbook exists.
- **Customer support**: HIRE (even first support hire is full-time). Outsource only if you're at scale and have clear playbooks.
- **Legal**: OUTSIDE COUNSEL FOREVER until Series B/C.
- **HR/People**: FRACTIONAL until 25-40 people. Then hire.
