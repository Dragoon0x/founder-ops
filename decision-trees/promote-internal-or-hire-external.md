# Promote Internal or Hire External

## Context
You have a leadership role to fill. Promote someone on the team or hire from outside?

## The tree

### Step 1: Does an internal candidate have the RIGHT skills for the NEXT scope?
Not past performance — forward scope. A great IC is not automatically a great manager. A great 10-person manager is not automatically a great 40-person manager.

- YES (clearly): promote.
- MAYBE: consider interim role or dual-track (internal interim while searching externally).
- NO: hire external.

### Step 2: What's the organizational signal of each choice?
- Promote: rewards tenure, builds retention, signals "path here is real."
- Hire external: signals "we hire the best regardless of tenure," can shock/rebuild culture, can demotivate those passed over.

Both signals matter. Read your team's current state.

### Step 3: How much new skill is required?
- < 20% new skill (adjacent scope expansion): promote.
- 20-50% new skill: tough call. Mentorship + stretch can work.
- > 50% new skill (e.g., promoting IC to manager; manager to exec): hire experienced external more likely right.

### Step 4: What's the downside of each?
- Bad external hire: 12-18 months lost, severance cost, cultural damage, team re-recruited.
- Bad promotion: 6-12 months in wrong role, hard conversation to unwind, team confidence drop.

Bad external hire is ~2-3x more expensive than bad promotion.

### Step 5: Have you trained the internal candidate?
If you haven't spent 6+ months explicitly preparing them for this role, you don't yet know if they'd succeed. You're guessing. Run a 60-90 day "acting" role to test.

## Specific patterns

### First management hire (functional leader)
Strong bias to experienced external hire for first head-of-X. Reason: no one internal has ever run the function. You need someone who's seen the movie.

### Scaling manager (team of 5 → team of 20)
Mixed. Some existing managers scale. Most hit a ceiling around 10 reports.

### Director → VP
Usually external. Very few directors jump cleanly.

### VP → C-suite
Usually external. Board / investor optics favor "grownup."

### "Our VP Engineering is struggling with a 30-person team"
Before firing: offer coaching + an experienced EM underneath them. If you need a new VP, start looking externally while keeping the current VP in place or moved to principal engineer / architect role.

## Tiebreakers
- Your board / investors. They'll often push external for senior roles. Their bias.
- The candidate's honesty. "I want this role but I know I'd need to grow into X, Y, Z" — strong signal.
- Your urgency. If you need results in < 90 days, external is faster to impact (assuming right hire).

## Common wrong answers
- "Promote because they've been loyal." (Loyalty + wrong role = mutual misery.)
- "Hire external because promoting feels weird." (Promote if they're ready.)
- "Default to hiring because it feels decisive." (Default to the best answer.)

## Reversibility
- Promote → demote: extremely painful. Most people leave.
- External hire → fire: painful but standard.
- Acting role → make permanent: natural progression.
- Acting role → pass: awkward but recoverable.
