# Accept Term Sheet or Shop

## Context
You have one term sheet. Sign it, or use it to run a process and get more?

## The tree

### Step 1: Is the term sheet clean (terms, not just price)?
- YES (1x non-participating, reasonable board, no ratchet, clean preferred): strong candidate to sign.
- NO (participating preferred, aggressive board, full ratchet): don't sign as-is regardless of shopping strategy. Either negotiate or walk.

### Step 2: How long have you been in market?
- < 4 weeks: probably haven't reached full investor universe. Shopping may find better.
- 4-8 weeks: mid-process. Could shop but be clear-eyed about time cost.
- 8+ weeks: pocket this one and run a tight second wave or sign.

### Step 3: Is this investor high-signal?
- Tier-1 brand with a strong partner who'd be engaged: lean accept.
- Mid-tier or unknown firm: shopping more defensible.
- The partner pattern-matches to great: accept.
- Partner is junior or you don't love them: shop cautiously.

### Step 4: What's the cost of shopping?
- Legal clock: typical exclusivity is 30 days. If you shop, you're saying "no" to them. They may walk.
- Reputation: investor community talks. Shopping a term sheet is legal; "backing out after handshake" is bad.
- Timeline: every week shopping is a week of runway burned.
- Momentum: you lose the momentum that got the TS.

### Step 5: Do you have a specific named alternative?
- "I have a meeting with [specific partner at named firm] next week who's shown real interest": shopping is rational.
- "I feel like we could get more if we tried": not a strategy. Sign or improve negotiation.

## The ethical line
- Shopping a term sheet BEFORE signing: acceptable if you're transparent with the issuing investor.
- Signing a term sheet and then shopping: burn-your-reputation territory.
- Running a process without disclosing you have an active TS: legal, but communicate carefully.

## Tiebreakers
- Your gut on the investor. Would you enjoy working with them for 7 years?
- Your co-founder's view. Don't override.
- Your board / existing investors' read (if applicable).

## Common wrong answers
- "Let's shop — couldn't hurt." (It can. You lose the TS.)
- "Let's sign — we finally have one." (Gratitude isn't strategy.)
- "Let's use it as leverage." (Clumsily done, burns relationships.)

## Reversibility
- Sign → regret: very painful. Lock-in for round.
- Shop → lose original TS: painful but you may find better or go back quickly.
