# Raise Now or Wait

## Context
You could raise now. Should you?

## The tree

### Step 1: How much runway do you have?
- < 6 months: raise NOW. No choice. Runway = negotiation leverage; you have none.
- 6-12 months: probably raise soon. Tight window.
- 12-18 months: you have options. Depends on signal.
- 18+ months: usually wait unless a specific opportunity requires capital.

### Step 2: What's your momentum curve?
- Up and to the right, accelerating: raise on the way up. Investors pay premium for acceleration.
- Flat: raising is hard. Fix momentum first.
- Down: don't raise unless you must. Fix what's broken OR raise a clear "war chest to pivot" narrative.

### Step 3: What milestone is in reach?
If you're 3 months from hitting a big milestone (product launch, $1M ARR, major customer, platform launch), wait and raise on the milestone.

If the milestone is 6+ months out and speculative: raise now.

### Step 4: What's the market doing?
- Deal flow strong in your sector, multiples expanding: good time to raise.
- Market cold, multiples compressing: harder. Consider bridge or smaller round.
- Inbound investor interest: raise while warm, even if not optimal for you. Cold-start is much harder.

### Step 5: Do you have a lead in hand?
- YES: you can run a tight 4-8 week process.
- NO: plan for 4-6 months to find a lead plus another 2-4 to close. So if cash runs out in 6 months and you have no lead, that's very tight.

## Signals you should raise NOW
- Inbound from 2+ credible investors in a week
- Traction metric growing 2-3x MoM for 3+ months
- A strategic window closing (competitor entering, regulatory shift)
- Clear "next milestone needs capital to reach" with < 12 months runway
- Your team needs a break from runway anxiety (real, but not sole reason)

## Signals you should WAIT
- You're 3 months from a clear metric step-change
- Market is cold and you're well-capitalized
- You're pre-PMF and unclear on story
- The round would require telling a story you don't believe

## What "raise later" means tactically
- Set specific milestone: "If we hit $X by [date], we start the raise."
- Keep warm conversations with 3-5 target investors via quarterly updates.
- Don't wait silent for 6 months then cold-open a process.

## Don'ts
- "Raise because everyone else is." (Peer cycles are misleading.)
- "Raise because founder salary could be higher." (Bad narrative.)
- "Wait because you're overwhelmed with fundraising." (Fine but don't let runway dip below 9 months.)
- "Wait because your deck isn't perfect." (It won't be. Start.)

## Reversibility
- Raise now → regret terms: very painful. Terms compound for years.
- Wait → need to raise in crisis: brutal. You get terrible terms.

The asymmetry favors raising slightly earlier than optimal, not later.
