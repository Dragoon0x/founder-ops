# Fire or Coach (underperforming employee)

## Context
Someone on the team isn't performing. Decision needed.

## The tree

### Step 1: Is the gap skill, will, or fit?
- **Skill gap (can't do it yet)**: coaching + training works if: gap is < 12 months of learning AND person has aptitude AND role has time.
- **Will gap (won't do it)**: coaching rarely fixes this. Diagnose why. Could be personal crisis (be patient), role mismatch (move them), or fundamental disengagement (exit).
- **Fit gap (values, team chemistry, culture)**: hardly ever fixable. Exit.

### Step 2: Have you actually given clear, written feedback?
- NO, just hints and subtle nudges: STOP. Give feedback first. Written, specific, with examples. 30-60 days to see response.
- YES, multiple times with no meaningful change: proceed to Step 3.

### Step 3: Do they know their job is at risk?
- NO: tell them. Today. "I want you to know: the gap between where you are and what the role requires is serious. Here's what has to change in 30 days for this to work out. If it doesn't, we're going to part ways."
- YES, and they've had 30+ days: proceed to Step 4.

### Step 4: Is the team already working around them?
- YES (teammates bypass them, work gets rerouted): team morale is eroding. Time to decide.
- NO: you might have more room than you think. But most teams see before managers do.

### Step 5: What does your best manager or coach say?
Ask them honestly. Often outsiders see things more clearly. If multiple people independently flag "this isn't going to work," believe them.

## Coach if all of these
- Skill or tenure gap in an otherwise-right person
- They want to improve (shown, not just said)
- You have runway to invest 2-3 months of coaching
- The role isn't on the critical path
- There's a clear, measurable improvement target

## Fire if any of these
- Values mismatch (can't be coached)
- Pattern of lies or cover-ups (trust is broken)
- They don't see the problem (no self-awareness = no change)
- You've coached for 60+ days with no material change
- The role is critical path and the company is paying the cost of the gap
- Your team is telling you it's time (directly or by quitting around them)

## Common wrong answers
- "Let's give them one more chance." (How many is this?)
- "They're almost there." (For 6 months.)
- "Firing would be cruel." (Cruel is leaving them in a role they're failing at.)
- "We need them for this project." (You're prolonging the inevitable.)
- "It's the manager's fault, not theirs." (Sometimes true. Fix manager. If not manager, fire.)

## The conversation
Firing is hard. Here's the shape:
1. **Get to the point fast.** "I'm going to let you go. Today is your last day. I want to explain why."
2. **Don't relitigate.** Share 2-3 specific examples, not a list. "You've heard this before."
3. **Severance + transition.** Walk through what you're offering.
4. **Stop talking.** Let them respond. Don't fill silence.
5. **Logistics quickly.** Access, property, announcement timing.
6. **Be human.** "I appreciate what you did. This isn't working. I hope the next thing lands well."

## Reversibility
- Coach successfully → keep employee: great outcome.
- Fire → rehire: rare. Possible but rarely works.
- Keep poorly-performing employee too long: damages team, damages customers, damages you. Least reversible of all.

## Rule
If you're asking the question "should I fire them," you usually know the answer. The question is whether you've done right by them in giving clear feedback first.
