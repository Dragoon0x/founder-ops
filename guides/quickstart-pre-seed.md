# Quickstart: Pre-Seed Founder

You haven't raised. Or you've raised a friends-and-family / angel round under $500k. You're a team of 1–3. This is your 90-day priority map.

## What matters now

1. Talking to 50+ potential customers
2. Building the smallest thing that could work
3. Earning the right to raise a seed round

Nothing else is real yet.

## What doesn't matter

- Perfect branding
- Fully built website
- CRM, HRIS, or any SaaS infrastructure
- Job titles
- Press
- Advisory board
- Legal entity structure beyond the basics

## Your first 14 days

Day 1–3: write a one-page narrative. What problem, whose problem, what evidence, what you'll build, why you.

Day 4–7: list 100 people who might be customers. Use LinkedIn, industry lists, cold networks. Get warm intros for 30 of them.

Day 8–14: do 20 customer conversations. 30 minutes each. Not pitch. Discovery. Follow Rob Fitzpatrick's *The Mom Test*.

## Days 15–45

- Synthesize customer conversations weekly
- Identify the sharpest pain pattern
- Build a prototype (whatever MVP means for your domain)
- Get 3–5 design partners committed in writing

## Days 45–90

- Ship working software or service to 3–5 partners
- Document every iteration
- Get at least 1 paid contract (any amount, even $100)
- Start writing seed deck in parallel

## Financial reality

- Runway from savings: 12–24 months typical
- Side income or part-time work is fine (just don't pretend you're full-time to investors)
- Co-founder splits: default equal. Revisit after 12 months with vesting.
- Cost of company formation (Stripe Atlas, Clerky): $500–1500

## What you should read

- *The Mom Test* — Rob Fitzpatrick
- *The Lean Startup* — Eric Ries
- Paul Graham essays (do things that don't scale, startup = growth)
- SAFE documentation on YC's site

## Common pre-seed mistakes

1. Hiring before customer signal
2. Pitching investors before having anything to show
3. Obsessing over equity splits that won't matter
4. Building in secret for 6 months
5. Falling in love with the solution, not the problem

## Signals you're ready for seed

- 3–5 paying customers, even small
- Clear pattern in customer problem
- You've built a prototype and iterated 3+ times
- You can articulate the wedge in 30 seconds
- Someone other than you cares enough to evangelize

## founder-ops modes to use at this stage

- `customer-discovery` — interview prep and synthesis
- `positioning-workshop` — early positioning drafts
- `fundraise-readiness` — honest diagnostic
- `mvp-scope` — what to build first
