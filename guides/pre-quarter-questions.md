# The questions to ask yourself before each quarter

Quarterly planning usually devolves into goal-setting theater. Real planning
starts with answering hard questions about the last quarter and the next one.

## Before the planning meeting, write answers to these

### About last quarter

1. **What did we say we'd do that we did?** (Be specific. Not "shipped a lot.")
2. **What did we say we'd do that we didn't?** (And why. Specifically.)
3. **What did we do that we didn't plan to?** (Was it good or interrupt-driven?)
4. **What did we learn that we didn't expect?**
5. **What worked that we should do more of?**
6. **What's broken that we kept ignoring?**
7. **Who on the team grew? Who plateaued? Who needs a change?**

### About the business

8. **Are we tracking to the year's goals? If not, what changes?**
9. **What's our runway? What does the burn need to be in 6 months for us to
   not be desperate?**
10. **What's the single metric that matters most this quarter?** (One. Not
    five.)
11. **What's the biggest risk this quarter? What would we do if it
    materialized?**

### About yourself

12. **What did I personally do this quarter that compounds?**
13. **What did I personally avoid that I shouldn't have?**
14. **What's the bottleneck only I can unblock?**
15. **What am I doing that someone else should be doing?**

### About the team

16. **Who do I need to give harder feedback to?**
17. **Who do I need to thank and promote?**
18. **What roles are we missing that we'd hire today if we could?**
19. **Where is the team confused about strategy?**
20. **Where is the team aligned but I'm secretly worried?**

## After answering, you'll know

- What this quarter's plan should focus on (and what to cut)
- Where the company is honest with itself and where it's not
- What conversations you've been avoiding
- Whether the team needs energy or focus or both

## Then plan the quarter

Now you can make the plan. 3-5 OKRs. One north-star metric. Explicit
constraints (what we will NOT do). Per-person commitments (not just "the
team will...").

## Don't skip the questions

The temptation is to jump to the plan. Resist. The plan is downstream of
clear thinking about where you are. A planning meeting that starts with
"what should we do next quarter" without first asking the questions above
produces a plan that's a vibes-based wish list.
