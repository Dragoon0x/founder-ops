# Quickstart: Seed Stage Founder

You've raised $1–6M. Team is 3–15. You have early product in market. This is your quarterly operating manual for the next 12 months.

## The goal

Reach "Series A ready" — defined as: $1.5M+ ARR, 15%+ MoM growth, clear ICP, repeatable sales motion.

Most seed companies take 18–24 months to get there. You have runway for one attempt.

## First 30 days after close

- Funds wired, update cap table
- Board meeting schedule set (monthly)
- Hire plan for next 12 months, budgeted
- 12-month plan written, reviewed by board
- Investor update cadence established

## Quarterly priorities

**Quarter 1 after seed**: prove initial revenue signal holds at slightly larger scale
**Quarter 2**: establish 1–2 repeatable acquisition channels
**Quarter 3**: build early sales or PLG infrastructure
**Quarter 4**: hit metrics that start Series A conversations

## Team building

Typical sequence:

1. First full-time engineer (if you're a non-technical founder) or first commercial hire (if you're technical)
2. Second engineer (specialist or generalist)
3. First sales or marketing hire (test before hire)
4. Designer
5. Customer success / support (when churn becomes real)
6. Second engineer in specialty

Don't hire a COO, VP Sales, VP Eng at seed. Too senior, too expensive, doesn't fit stage.

## Metrics to track weekly

- ARR / MRR
- Pipeline by stage
- Close rate
- Deal velocity
- Churn (logo and net)
- Active users (DAU/WAU/MAU)
- CAC payback period
- Cash balance and runway

## Fundraising preparation

Start Series A conversations 6–9 months before you need cash. Not pitching yet — building relationships.

- Identify 15–25 target A funds
- Get warm intros to partners
- Meet 2–3 per month for "just a coffee" conversations
- Share investor update monthly (build the narrative)
- When you hit the metric bar, signal intent to lead

## What you should read

- *From Impossible to Inevitable* — Aaron Ross
- *The Hard Thing About Hard Things* — Ben Horowitz
- *Lost and Founder* — Rand Fishkin
- First Round Review articles on seed-to-A

## Common seed stage mistakes

1. Hiring senior execs too early
2. Over-investing in product before GTM signal
3. Expanding scope of product before nailing wedge
4. Spending on marketing without attribution
5. Not writing investor updates (loses optionality)
6. Saying yes to every customer request (loses focus)
7. Raising more seed when you should raise A (signals weakness)

## Signals you should be worried

- Flat or declining growth for 2+ months
- New customer CAC rising without LTV rising
- Churn above 5% monthly (SMB) or 1.5% monthly (enterprise)
- Runway under 9 months with no raise started
- Co-founder drift (different priorities or visions)

## founder-ops modes to use at this stage

- `series-a-readiness` — quarterly diagnostic
- `hiring-at-scale` — job ladders and comp bands
- `pricing-workshop` — first real pricing iteration
- `founder-ops-monthly-ritual` — monthly rhythm
