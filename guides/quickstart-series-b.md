# Quickstart: Series B+ Founder

You've raised $25M+. Team is 50–200. ARR is $10M+. This is your operating manual for the growth stage.

## The goal

Either: become a profitable public company, or get acquired at a premium. Both require similar underlying discipline.

## Structural changes from A

- Formal executive team (C-suite complete)
- Multi-layer management (managers of managers)
- Finance, legal, HR functions robust
- Multiple product lines or segments
- Geographic expansion (often)
- First international offices possibly

## Priorities by year

**Year 1 post-B**: scale what worked; prove unit economics at higher ACV
**Year 2**: expand into adjacent products or segments
**Year 3**: become the category leader or consolidate the category

## Organizational maturity

### Finance

- CFO hired
- Controller and AP/AR
- FP&A function
- Monthly close in 5 business days
- Quarterly forecast vs actual variance <5%
- Audit-ready financials

### Legal

- GC hired or strong outside counsel
- Contract management system
- IP portfolio management
- Trademark / patent strategy
- Privacy and compliance program

### People

- CPO or VP People
- Compensation bands published
- Performance management system
- L&D budget and programs
- Engagement surveys quarterly
- Exec coaching and development

### Revenue

- CRO with matured sales org
- Sales enablement function
- Sales operations (ops, training, comp)
- Customer success teams by segment
- Partnerships team
- International sales teams (if applicable)

## Metrics discipline at scale

Board dashboard includes:

- ARR, NRR, gross margin, EBITDA, cash
- Cohort retention (by month and segment)
- Sales efficiency (magic number, CAC payback, LTV:CAC)
- Pipeline coverage (3–4x next quarter)
- Win rate, deal velocity, ACV trending
- Burn multiple
- Rule of 40
- Engineering productivity (features, uptime, incidents)
- Headcount plan vs actual

## Fundraising optionality

At this stage, options multiply:

- Continue equity raises (Series C, D, etc.)
- Venture debt (Silicon Valley Bank, Mercury, Brex, Atlas, Stifel)
- Tender offer / secondary for early employees
- Dividend recap (rare for SaaS)
- IPO preparation
- Strategic acquisition

Each has tradeoffs. Discuss with CFO and board.

## Exit planning

Starting 18–24 months before potential exit:

- Clean up financials (audit, tax, cap table)
- Consolidate entities
- Resolve outstanding legal matters
- Rationalize product lines
- Demonstrate durable growth
- Build relationships with potential acquirers and bankers
- Know your number (both for IPO and for M&A)

## International expansion

If not already:

- First market selection (UK, Germany, Australia typical first)
- Local hires or remote-from-HQ leadership
- Legal entity setup
- Tax structure optimization (transfer pricing)
- Localization (product, support, sales)
- Compliance (GDPR, local data laws)

## Common Series B+ mistakes

1. Premature CEO succession discussions
2. Over-expansion (multiple products, multiple geos simultaneously)
3. M&A indigestion (acquisitions that don't integrate)
4. Cultural drift past 150 people
5. Politics and fiefdom building
6. Over-hiring in pre-IPO preparation
7. Losing founding team (co-founders, early execs) without plan

## What you should read

- *The Hard Thing About Hard Things* — still relevant
- *Measure What Matters* — John Doerr
- *The Four Obsessions of an Extraordinary Executive* — Lencioni
- *An Elegant Puzzle* — Will Larson (for eng leaders you hire)
- Bill Campbell: *Trillion Dollar Coach*

## Governance at scale

- Board of 5–7 members
- Committees: audit, compensation, nominating
- Annual director evaluation
- Formal board book and decks
- Regular executive sessions (board without CEO)
- ESG / DEI reporting

## Signals you should be worried

- Founder burnout (you or co-founder)
- Rule of 40 below 20 for 2+ quarters
- NRR below 110% at this scale
- Key exec departures cluster
- Product innovation stagnation
- Culture surveys declining
- Customer concentration growing

## founder-ops modes to use at this stage

- `ipo-readiness` — quarterly diagnostic
- `pre-exit-prep` — 18-month acquisition/IPO prep
- `board-governance` — formal governance and committees
- `international-expansion` — new market entry
