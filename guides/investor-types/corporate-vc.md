# Working with Corporate VCs

Corporate VCs are dedicated fund arms of large companies. Examples: Comcast Ventures, Johnson & Johnson Innovation, BMW i Ventures, Salesforce Ventures (can be strategic or CVC).

## What they're good at

- Domain expertise in their industry
- Distribution to their customer base
- Deep pockets for follow-on
- Strong brand (enterprise sales credibility)
- Long-term capital (multi-fund commitments)

## What to watch for

- Parent company strategy shifts (your fund can get cut)
- Slow decision making (committee, board, compliance)
- Portfolio conflict management often poor
- Team turnover when parent company changes leadership
- ROFR and acquisition preferences

## Before taking their money

- CVC team stability (tenure of partners)
- Independent mandate from parent
- Committee structure and speed
- Parent company financial health
- Track record of exits
- Conflict management (are they investing in direct competitors)

## Questions to ask them

- "When was your fund last renewed, and when is next renewal?"
- "What happens to portfolio if parent company decides to exit CVC?"
- "How do you handle conflict of interest with parent's business?"
- "What's your typical hold period, and how do you exit?"
- "Who approves investments above $X?"

## Structuring the relationship

- Same as any other investor on board governance
- Separate investment from commercial partnership discussions
- Don't share roadmap beyond what customer-level makes sense
- Keep founders as primary commercial contact (not CVC partner)
- Track parent company strategic shifts

## When it's not working

- Parent strategy shifts away from your market
- CVC is restructured or closed
- Individual partner leaves
- Conflicts with other portfolio companies surface
- Parent acquisition approach becomes pressure

Exit options: secondary to another institutional investor.

## Red flags from corporate VCs

- Recent parent company layoffs
- CVC partner departures
- Heavy compliance / legal overhead
- Non-standard investment documents
- Requesting exclusive distribution or technology
- Parent company reorgs that affect strategic direction

## When CVC money is right

- Stable parent with clear multi-year CVC mandate
- Non-overlapping portfolio
- Genuinely helpful for customer access
- Founder-friendly terms (no ROFR, standard rights)
- CVC partner has operational experience, not just finance
