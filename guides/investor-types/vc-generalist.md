# Working with Generalist VCs

Generalist VCs invest across sectors. Examples: Sequoia, Benchmark, Accel, Kleiner, Greylock, USV.

## What they're good at

- Capital availability at scale
- Brand halo (recruiting, customer access, follow-on)
- Network density (intros that move things)
- Pattern matching across sectors
- Founder empathy (most partners were founders)

## What to watch for

- Less specific domain expertise
- Partner distraction (they have many boards)
- Follow-on pressure (must raise if they signal interest)
- "Brand tax" on terms (they expect better terms because of brand)
- Partner-level variance (some partners are dramatically better than others)

## Before taking their money

- Check whether partner is first-time on your sector
- Reference their 10 most recent portfolio founders
- Understand their fund's check size vs your round
- Understand their follow-on concentration rules
- Ask about specific value-add plans for your company

## Questions to ask them

- "Walk me through your last three investments where things went sideways."
- "When was the last time you passed on a deal that became a unicorn?"
- "Which of your portfolio founders would you not invest in again?"
- "What's your fund's reserve ratio for follow-on?"
- "Who on your team besides you will be involved?"

## Structuring the relationship

- Board cadence: monthly at early stage, quarterly at growth
- Investor update: monthly, tight format
- Personal connection: 1 coffee per quarter outside board
- Flag risks proactively (don't let board meeting be surprise)
- Use their network deliberately

## When it's not working

- They're distracted with too many boards
- Their investment thesis has shifted and you're not priority
- Partner is departing or rotating off
- Value-add hasn't materialized 12 months in

Options: observer instead of voting, new champion within firm, secondary to different investor.

## Red flags from generalist VCs

- Pushing for term sheet exclusivity without conviction
- Multiple meetings with no partner present
- "We need to meet the co-founder" late in process (often signals they won't decide)
- Vague on value-add
- Constantly checking with partners
- Unwilling to connect you with portfolio founders
