# Quickstart: Series A Founder

You've raised $8–25M. Team is 15–50. You have $1.5M–$5M ARR. This is your operating manual for the next 18 months.

## The goal

Reach Series B — defined as: $10M+ ARR, rule of 40 above 40, 1.2x+ NRR, scalable GTM, 20–30 month runway post-B.

Median time from A to B is 18–22 months. Your gap is closed by hitting the metric bar, not by time alone.

## Structural changes from seed

- First layer of management (team leads, managers)
- Formal org charts and reporting
- OKRs or similar goal system
- Weekly all-hands, monthly board, quarterly strategy
- People ops function (first HR hire)
- Financial function (controller or fractional CFO)
- Security and compliance investment (SOC 2 if B2B)

## First 60 days after close

- Board cadence formalized
- Exec team hired or identified (VP Sales or CRO typically first)
- Departmental OKRs for Q1 defined
- 18-month plan with quarterly milestones
- Hiring plan for 18 months (double team size typically)
- Compensation bands reviewed and published internally

## Exec hiring sequence

1. **VP Sales / CRO** — if PLG, maybe later; if sales-led, now
2. **VP Engineering** — around 15–20 engineers
3. **VP Marketing / Demand** — around first pipeline scale need
4. **CFO or fractional CFO** — around $5M ARR
5. **VP Customer Success** — around $5M ARR
6. **VP Product** — when engineering org gets big enough
7. **General Counsel** — $10M ARR or complex regulatory

Senior hires take 90–120 days to source and 60 days to ramp. Start early.

## GTM scaling

- Move from founder-led sales to AE-led (usually 3–5 AEs)
- SDR function established (inbound and outbound)
- Marketing function for demand generation
- Customer success for onboarding and renewal
- Partnerships exploration starts

Typical GTM ratios:

- 1 SDR per 2 AEs
- 1 AE per $1M quota (enterprise) or $500k quota (mid-market)
- CS to AE ratio: 1:3 at this stage
- Marketing spend: 30–60% of sales spend

## Product scaling

- Product manager hired
- Design function expanded
- Engineering tracks (platform, product, infra)
- On-call and incident response matures
- First security / compliance investments
- Data infrastructure (data team or shared)

## Metrics discipline

- Board dashboard standardized
- Weekly business review becomes operational rhythm
- Cohort analysis for retention
- ACV and ASP trending
- Pipeline coverage tracking
- Sales efficiency (magic number, LTV:CAC, payback)

## Common Series A mistakes

1. Over-hiring execs before they have a team
2. Under-investing in second-tier talent
3. Pursuing too many segments or ICPs
4. Ignoring the middle-management gap
5. Culture drift as team doubles
6. Not building operational rhythm early enough
7. Raising too much (dilution without runway)

## Fundraise planning

Start Series B relationship-building 9–12 months before raising:

- Identify top 20 B leads for your sector
- Monthly investor update discipline
- Quarterly coffee with 2–3 leads
- Reference customers at B scale
- Second-tier data room maintained continuously

## Governance

- Board composition solidified (often 2 investors + CEO + founder + independent)
- Independent director added (specialist: GTM, finance, engineering leader)
- Audit committee (lightweight at this stage)
- Option pool refreshed as needed
- Employee stock plan matures

## Signals you should be worried

- Sales efficiency below 0.5 for 2+ quarters
- NRR below 105% for 2+ quarters
- Exec departures in consecutive quarters
- Engineering velocity declining as team grows
- Customer concentration increasing (top 10 growing as % of revenue)
- Runway under 14 months without raise in motion
