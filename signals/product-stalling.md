# Signal: Product is Stalling

## What to Watch
- **Feature requests declining**: Customers stop asking for new features (they've given up)
- **Activation rate declining**: New users aren't finding value
- **DAU/MAU declining**: Existing users are less engaged
- **Support ticket mix**: More "how do I do X" and less "can you add X" (confusion, not demand)
- **Sales cycle lengthening**: Prospects need more convincing
- **Win rate declining**: Losing more competitive deals
- **NPS trending down**: From 40+ to sub-30 over 2-3 quarters

## Why It Matters
A stalling product is the leading indicator of a stalling company. Revenue follows product health by 2-4 quarters.

## Threshold for Action
3+ signals trending wrong for 2+ months.

## Recommended Response
1. **User research sprint**: 15 sessions in 2 weeks. Mix of: power users, new users, churned users.
2. **Usage data audit**: Where are users getting stuck? Where do they stop?
3. **Competitive feature review**: Where are alternatives pulling ahead?
4. **Product strategy offsite**: Is the product vision still right? Are we building for the right persona?
5. **One big bet**: Rather than 10 small fixes, identify the one thing that would make the product 10x better for the core use case. Build that.
