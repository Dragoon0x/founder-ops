# Signal: Team Morale Declining

## What to Watch
- **Voluntary attrition**: > 15% annualized (or > 10% if team < 20)
- **Glassdoor/anonymous feedback**: Negative trends in sentiment
- **Meeting energy**: Cameras off, silence in standups, low engagement
- **Sick days**: Increasing trend
- **Internal referrals**: Declining (happy employees refer friends)
- **Slack activity**: After-hours activity increasing (burnout) or overall decreasing (disengagement)
- **1:1 content**: Managers report more complaints and less initiative

## Why It Matters
Low morale → low productivity → higher attrition → remaining team burns out → death spiral.
Fixing morale is 10x cheaper than replacing departed employees.

## Threshold for Action
2+ signals trending wrong for 4+ weeks.

## Recommended Response
1. **Listen first**: Anonymous pulse survey (3 questions: How are you? What's frustrating? What would help?)
2. **Address the top issue**: Whatever the #1 complaint is, take visible action within 2 weeks
3. **Transparency**: If times are hard, say so. Ambiguity breeds anxiety. "Here's the situation, here's what we're doing about it, here's what I need from you."
4. **Quick wins**: Small improvements that show leadership is paying attention (tool upgrades, process fixes, recognition)
5. **Evaluate leadership**: Sometimes the morale problem is a management problem. Are managers equipped to lead?
