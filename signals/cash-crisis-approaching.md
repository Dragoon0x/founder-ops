# Signal: Cash Crisis Approaching

## What to Watch
- **Runway < 9 months** without a clear fundraise timeline
- **Burn rate increasing** while revenue growth is flat or declining
- **Large customer concentration**: > 30% of revenue from one customer
- **Invoice aging**: AR > 60 days increasing
- **Payroll as % of revenue** > 80% (no room for error)
- **Failed payment rate** increasing (customers' own financial stress)

## Why It Matters
Cash crisis has a 60-90 day lead time before it becomes visible. By the time you can't make payroll, it's too late.

## Threshold for Action
Runway < 12 months AND any 2 other signals present.

## Recommended Response
**Immediate (Week 1):**
1. Build a 13-week cash flow model (if you don't already have one)
2. Identify all non-essential expenses that can be cut immediately
3. Accelerate receivables: call every customer with outstanding invoices

**Short-term (Weeks 2-4):**
4. Evaluate fundraise readiness: if you can raise, start now
5. Explore bridge financing from existing investors
6. Reduce burn: pause hiring, cut discretionary spend, renegotiate vendor contracts

**If runway < 6 months and fundraise isn't viable:**
7. Scenario plan for layoffs (see layoffs playbook)
8. Explore acqui-hire or strategic acquisition
9. Consider revenue-based financing or venture debt as bridge
