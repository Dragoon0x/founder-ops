# Valuation Negotiation

## Before
- Lock your range before any investor conversation. Typically: target (what you'd celebrate), floor (what you'd accept grudgingly), walk (below this, you pass).
- Collect 5-8 comparable financings (stage, sector, geography, traction). TechCrunch, Crunchbase, AngelList, SEC filings, Carta reports.
- Have a crisp answer to "why this valuation" — the 90-second version and the 5-minute version.

## Opening frames
If they ask valuation first, deflect once:
> "Let's talk about fit first. If we're aligned on thesis and I think you're the right partner, the number will be straightforward. What's your perspective on [company thesis]?"

Second time they ask, answer:
> "We're raising $X on $Y pre. For context, that's [multiple] of last year's revenue and in line with [named comps]. I'm sensitive to market conditions — tell me if that's off your fairway."

## When they push down
Response depends on their reason:

**"Your metrics don't support it."**
> "Fair challenge. Which metric specifically? Let me show you what we're actually tracking." Walk through your best numbers. Often they're comparing you to later-stage metrics. Reframe to stage-appropriate benchmarks.

**"The market has repriced."**
> "I've been watching market comps carefully. Here are 4 closes in the last 90 days at or above what we're asking: [list]. These are closer comps than the aggregate index because [reason]. I'm willing to structure to bridge any gap, but the number reflects the market for companies like ours."

**"We can't write at that valuation for our fund size / ownership."**
> "That's a real constraint. What's the ownership you need? If we structure this as [smaller round with tighter syndicate / bridge + priced round in 12 months / secondary], does that work?"

**"Another investor is at [lower number]."**
> "Interesting. If you're confident that's going to close at that number, you should go with them. We're not going to chase a number that underprices where we are. But if it's an opening offer and not a final, let's talk about how to bridge."

## Structural creativity (if straight price won't work)
- **Tranched round.** Close at your number but release funds in 2 tranches tied to milestones. Risk-share.
- **Convertible top-up.** Accept a smaller priced round at their number + a convertible note for additional runway at a cap that implies your number on conversion.
- **Expanded option pool.** If they need more ownership, offer to expand pre-money option pool (which dilutes you but preserves price).
- **Secondary.** You sell some founder shares at a lower effective price while primary lands at your number. Nets to their ownership goal.

## When to walk
- They're > 30% below floor and unwilling to structure.
- They keep reopening agreed points.
- Their last 5 deals were all 30%+ below seller's initial ask. They negotiate for sport.

## Closing a price
When you hit a workable number, lock it:
> "So we're at $X pre, $Y raise, [board structure], [pref terms]. I'll send the key terms in an email this afternoon and loop counsel tomorrow to start on long-form. Sound right?"

## After
- Follow-up email with every agreed term.
- Do not share the exact number with other investors until you have an executed term sheet. You signal price with phrases like "we're highly confident about where we'll close" not "we have a term sheet at $X."
