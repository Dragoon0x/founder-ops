# Vendor Contract Negotiation (SaaS, infra, services)

## When to negotiate
- Renewal: 60-90 days before. Vendor discount authority peaks near their quarter-end.
- Growth: crossed a usage tier. Use the growth as leverage, not apology.
- Competition: got a competitor quote. Real, written quotes carry 10x the weight of "I heard."
- Cost pressure: runway concerns. Be honest — most vendors will work with you.

## Levers
- **Term length.** 24-month commit = 10-20% discount. 36-month = 20-30%. Know your lock-in tolerance.
- **Upfront payment.** Annual upfront vs monthly = 10-15% discount.
- **Logo rights.** Your logo on their site = 5-10% effective discount depending on brand.
- **Case study / reference.** 1 case study + 2 references = 5-15%.
- **Reduced support tier.** If you don't use their phone support, drop it.
- **Consolidating SKUs.** Buying 3 products from them instead of 1 = bigger discount on each.

## Scripts

**Annual renewal with price increase:**
> "Saw the 15% increase in the renewal. Walk me through what's driving it — more features? infrastructure cost? Just want to understand. For context, we're budgeting flat for next year. If flat isn't workable, let's talk about longer term or fewer seats."

**Crossed a tier and they auto-bumped:**
> "We hit the next tier. Before we sign, I want to look at the full package. Given our growth trajectory [X% last 12 months], we're headed for tier [N+2] by Q3. Can we negotiate on tier N+2 pricing now and lock it for 24 months?"

**Got a competitor quote:**
> "We got a serious quote from [competitor] at $[X] lower. I'd rather stay with you — the switching cost is real — but I can't justify the gap to our board. What can you do to close that?"

Don't lie about this. If they ask to see the quote, have it ready.

**Vendor is a startup, you're a startup, both tight:**
> "We value the partnership. Here's what's true: our runway means we need to cut $[X] in SaaS spend. We like your product. If we can't find a way to [reduce spend / defer billing / cut scope], we'll have to switch. What's possible?"

## Walk away cleanly
If they won't move:
> "Understood. We'll look at alternatives for the next renewal cycle. Appreciate the relationship." Then actually do the RFP. Don't threaten without following through.
