# Equity Refresh Negotiation (existing employee)

## When employees ask for refresh grants
- At 2-3 year tenure (approaching vesting cliff drop-off)
- After major contribution (launched product, closed big deal)
- Competing offer in hand
- Promotion

## Your frame
Refresh grants are a retention tool, not a reward for asking. Build a systematic policy so requests don't drive chaos.

## Standard refresh logic
- Every 2 years for top performers
- Size: ~40-60% of initial grant for the role's current level
- 4-year vesting from grant date
- No cliff on refreshes (they already passed initial cliff)
- Not automatic — performance-gated

## Scripts

**Employee asks in 1:1 for more equity:**
> "Thanks for bringing this up. Tell me more — what's driving it? Competitive offer, thinking about long-term wealth, or something else? Want to understand before I respond."

Their answer tells you what to solve for:
- Competitive offer: "Can I see it? I want to make sure we're solving the right thing."
- Long-term wealth: "Here's our refresh philosophy... Let's look at whether you're due."
- Feeling undervalued: "Tell me more. Equity can be one signal, but it shouldn't be the only one we talk about."

**They have a real competing offer at higher equity:**
Decide: do you want to keep them? Not everyone is worth a stretch match.

If yes:
> "I value what you're doing here. Here's what I can offer — [refresh grant + any other adjustments]. I can't always match big-company equity dollar-for-dollar, but I can make sure you're well-positioned for our outcome."

If no:
> "I understand. The offer you have is strong, and I'm not going to try to talk you out of something better for your career. Let's make the transition clean."

Honest > desperate.

**They ask pre-promotion:**
> "Equity refresh happens at promotion as part of the new level grant. Let's talk about the promotion path first. What would you need to see happen in the next 6 months to get there?"

## Don'ts
- Don't negotiate per person without a policy. Creates inequity, creates gossip.
- Don't commit verbally before checking comp bands.
- Don't refresh underperformers because they asked loudly.

## After
- Document grant with board approval (or within board-delegated authority).
- 409A compliant strike price.
- Grant doc via Carta or equivalent.
- Communicate to employee within 7 days of asking with a clear yes/no/when.
