# Co-Founder Equity Split Negotiation

## Before anything
- Read Noam Wasserman, "The Founder's Dilemmas." The core finding: equal splits negotiated in < 1 hour correlate with later breakups. Unequal splits negotiated carefully are healthier.
- Accept that this conversation is THE conversation. Rushing it creates 5-10 years of resentment.
- Schedule it explicitly. Don't do it over dinner. Do it in a 3-hour block with a whiteboard.

## Frame
You are not dividing credit for past work. You are dividing responsibility for future risk and upside.

## The inputs (discuss each one)

### Contribution so far
- Weeks/months of full-time work to date
- Key IP, relationships, or deals contributed
- Cash invested
- Idea origination (weight carefully — ideas are cheap, execution is expensive)

### Role going forward
- Who will be CEO (final decision authority)
- Who drives fundraising (huge lever, often underweighted)
- Who owns the critical scarce skill (e.g., you have a researcher who's 10x on the tech)
- Relative replaceability (harder to replace = more leverage)

### Risk asymmetry
- Who quit the highest-paying job
- Who has other responsibilities (kids, mortgage, aging parents)
- Who has runway to survive 2 years unpaid

### Commitment
- Part-time vs full-time
- Any hedges (still consulting, still in grad school)

## Walk through the Slicing Pie or Noam style exercise
Two approaches:

### Dynamic (Slicing Pie): split changes based on inputs over time until funded.
Pros: adjusts to reality. Cons: hard to operationalize pre-funding.

### Static with earn-out: fixed initial split + most/all shares on a reverse-vest schedule.
Pros: standard, investor-friendly. Cons: locks in decisions at moment of lowest info.

Recommendation: static split + 4-year reverse vest + 1-year cliff. Everyone's shares vest. If someone leaves early, unvested shares return to the pool.

## Suggested split ranges

### 2 founders, equal time and risk, overlapping skills
- 50/50: only if genuinely equal contribution and role AND you're both convinced you'd choose it again after 6 months of partnership. Otherwise it signals conflict avoidance.
- 55/45: CEO gets the point. Standard if one is clearly driving fundraising and external.
- 60/40: one person was there 6+ months earlier and/or brings the defining skill.

### 2 founders, unequal
- 65/35 or 70/30: one full-time, one part-time. One leading, one executing. One took much more risk.
- 75/25 or 80/20: uneven enough that the smaller holder is more "first employee" than co-founder.

### 3 founders
- Equal (33/33/33): rare but possible if all three are in-kind.
- Lead + equals (50/25/25 or 40/30/30): common.
- Lead + different weight (50/30/20 or 60/25/15): when roles are very different.

## Scripts

### If your co-founder proposes 50/50 and you disagree
> "I've thought a lot about this and I want to be honest because I think we should set this up to last. I think the right split is [X/Y], and here's my reasoning: [list factors]. I want us to be equal partners in how we work — I think the equity should reflect the difference in [factor]. Tell me how you see it."

### If they get emotional
> "I get it. This isn't a small conversation. Let's take a break for an hour and pick it back up. I want to solve this in a way we're both proud of in 5 years."

### If they say "take it or leave it" on 50/50 and you don't believe in it
> "If 50/50 is non-negotiable for you, we need to talk about whether we're the right team. I'd rather find out now than after 3 years."

That's a real moment. Some of you shouldn't be co-founders.

### If you're the one with less experience and they want more
> "I'm willing to accept a smaller initial split given [X]. I want a commitment in writing: if I'm performing at founder level at 18 months as judged by [agreed criteria], we revisit to adjust."

Adjustments happen. Most of them die in drawers. Paper it.

## Lock it

Once you agree:
- Document in writing immediately. Email + notarize if possible.
- Paper it properly: founder stock purchase agreement with 83(b) election filed within 30 days (US).
- Assume reverse-vest on all founder shares.
- Include decision-making rules: who decides what, what requires unanimity, what happens if you deadlock.
- Include "bad leaver" provisions: what happens if someone leaves in the first 12 months.

## After
- Revisit at 12 months. Not to re-split, but to acknowledge how it's going.
- If roles have diverged significantly (one is doing 2x the work), talk about a one-time refresh grant, not a re-split.
