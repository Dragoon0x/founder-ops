# Down-Round Negotiation

## Context
You need more money. Current valuation not supported by market. You're raising below last round's price.

## Before the call
- Accept this is happening emotionally before you negotiate. Denial kills deals.
- Calculate runway carefully. 18 months minimum. Don't take a round that leaves < 12 months.
- Model dilution carefully for founders and employees.
- Talk to existing investors FIRST. They will either bridge, participate, or release.

## Existing investor conversations

### Ask existing investors to lead bridge or down round
> "Here's where we are: runway is [X] months. Market for our space is tough. I want to be transparent about our options: we can raise a bridge or a small down round. I'd love for you to lead. If it's not right for you, I want to know so we can plan our next moves."

### Ask them to commit to future rounds
> "If we take [lead investor]'s terms, pro-rata is going to get heavy for you given the dilution. What's your capacity?"

## With new investors

### They'll want aggressive terms. Common asks:
- 2x participating preferred (fight hard)
- Full ratchet anti-dilution (never accept)
- Pay-to-play provisions that wipe non-participating prior investors (decide case-by-case)
- Board control (fight, offer independent)
- Founder re-vesting (accept if you've been at it 5+ years with vested shares)

### Scripts
> "I understand you're adjusting for risk at this price. Here's what I can accept: [lower price but clean terms]. Here's what I can't: [participating preferred, full ratchet, pay-to-play that wipes existing preferred]. The mechanics that seem cheap to you are expensive for long-term alignment with the team."

## With employees
- Communicate BEFORE closing.
- Refresh option grants post-close (at new lower FMV) for key people.
- Be honest about dilution but frame the upside of more runway.
- Some senior folks will leave. Plan for it.

## With other co-founders and board
- Align before external negotiation. A split board tanks deals.
- Board approval typically required for new financings.

## Term sheet walk away triggers
- Price < 60% of last round with 0 milestone progress
- Participating preferred > 1x
- Full ratchet
- Cram down of existing preferred holders without their consent (legal liability for you as director)

## After close
- Reset team expectations. Hold an all-hands.
- Reset plan. Old plan was based on higher valuation expectations.
- Acknowledge what happened. Don't spin.
