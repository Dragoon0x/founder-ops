# Acquirer Negotiation (early M&A conversations)

## Posture
You're not for sale. You might be for sale at the right number. These two frames look identical externally and feel completely different internally.

## First contact scripts

**"We're interested in talking about an acquisition."**
> "I appreciate the interest. We're building for the long term — I'm not running a sale process. That said, I'm always happy to learn. Tell me what you're thinking and why us. I'll be direct about whether there's a conversation worth having."

Don't: jump to numbers, share financials, mention board approval needed.
Do: learn their thesis, their timeline, their typical acquisition structure.

## Signals they're serious vs fishing
- Serious: they know your metrics, they've done diligence, they bring specific integration thesis.
- Fishing: vague questions about revenue, "open to all conversations," no stated thesis.

Don't dance with fishers. It leaks.

## Information control
- NDA before anything financial shared. Mutual NDA, narrow definition of confidential, 3-year term.
- Before detailed financial diligence: IOI (Indication of Interest) in writing with a price range.
- Before full data room: LOI (Letter of Intent) with exclusivity of no more than 45 days.

## Valuation conversation
Industry multiples:
- B2B SaaS: 5-10x ARR mature, 15-30x for fast-growing strategic assets
- Consumer: highly variable, 1-5x revenue typical
- Acqui-hire: $500K-$3M per engineer + debt assumption + minor premium

Multiple on what metric matters. Push for ARR multiples if you're high-growth SaaS. Push for DAU/user multiples if consumer and growing. Force the framing.

## Script: "What would it take?"
> "I can't give you a number off the cuff. What I can tell you is: at the right number, with the right terms, with the right home for the team, a conversation is possible. Give me your range first. If it's in the zone, we keep talking. If it's not, we save each other time."

## Red flags
- They want earn-outs > 30% of price. Earn-outs rarely pay out. Assume you'll get 50% of stated earn-out at most.
- They want key-person retention > 24 months. Locks you in, lets them reset later.
- They refuse reps and warranties insurance. Means they want to claw back from you personally. Fight this hard.
- Their financing for the deal isn't confirmed. Get proof.

## Board and investors
- Loop board early — they have fiduciary duty to all shareholders.
- Investors with large preferences may block sales below a threshold. Know your stack.
- Common trap: acquirer offers price that's great for founders but bad for investors. Investors block. Deal dies. Be transparent about what different price points mean for each party.

## Walk-away script
> "The price and structure aren't in the zone we need to take this seriously. I'm happy to stay in touch — if you come back with something meaningfully different, I'll pick up. Meanwhile, we're keeping building."
