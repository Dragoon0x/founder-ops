# Hiring Offer Negotiation

## Before
- Have a comp band, not a point. (E.g., L5 SWE: $180-220K base, 0.15-0.35% equity, $10-20K signing.)
- Know your comp philosophy and be able to articulate it (e.g., "60th percentile of Bay Area market per [benchmark], higher equity vs cash trade, 4-year vest with 1-year cliff").
- Confirm what the candidate told you about their current comp + alternatives. Assume 10-20% inflation on current.

## Opening
- Make the offer verbally first. Read their reaction. If they pause, they're negotiating. If they say "wow," still offer to send a written version.
- Send written offer within 2 hours of verbal. Include: base, equity (number of shares + % + strike + FMV if known), signing, benefits summary, start date, deadline to accept (5-7 business days typical).

## Candidate: "Can you go higher on base?"
Diagnose first:
> "What would make this work for you? Is it base specifically, or is it total comp? Sometimes we can structure it differently — equity, signing bonus, earlier review."

If they want base and you have room:
> "I hear you. Base at our stage is tight because [reason: runway, internal equity with existing team]. I can go to $X on base if we hold equity at [current grant]. Or I can offer $Y on base and increase equity to [higher grant]. Which works better for you?"

If you don't have room:
> "I can't go higher on base — it would put you 15% above our most senior engineer, and I can't justify that within the team. What I can do: [signing bonus / earlier review / higher equity]. Let's figure out what makes this a yes for you."

## Candidate: "Can you match Google's [offer]?"
Big company offers have structure that startups can't match dollar-for-dollar.
> "I can't match the base, and I'll be honest about why. What I can offer is equity in a company where you'll own a meaningful piece of a potentially large outcome, direct impact on what we build, and a level of responsibility you won't get in your first 3 years at Google. If those things matter less to you than guaranteed cash, this isn't the right fit. If they matter more, let's talk about how to structure equity to reflect that tradeoff."

## Candidate: "I need more equity"
If they're senior and you under-granted:
> "Good point. At your level, our standard range is [X-Y]%. Happy to move to [Y]% — that reflects the scope we're asking you to own."

If you're already at the top of the band:
> "We're at the top of what we can grant at your level without rebalancing internal equity. What I can do: commit to a refresh grant at 12 months tied to performance, which should move you above current grant if we deliver on the year."

## Candidate: "I need to think about it" (stalling)
> "Of course. Before you do — is there anything specific worrying you I can address now? Sometimes it's easier to talk through it than sit with it."

Then set deadline clearly:
> "We'd like you to start on [date], so the offer is open through [date]. If you need more time, tell me — we'll figure it out. But I want to be honest with other candidates in the pipeline."

## Candidate: "Company X matched my ask"
First, understand if it's real:
> "Congrats. Which role and company? I want to be straight with you — if Company X is a better fit for what you want, take it. I don't want to win a bidding war and end up with someone who'll be gone in 6 months."

If they're looking for a counter, decide in advance if you'll match. Don't decide live.
> "Let me think about whether we can do something. I'll get back to you tomorrow."

## Candidate: "Can I work 4 days a week?"
Depends on role seniority and company stage:
- Early stage founder/VP level: probably no, at least for the first year.
- IC role, senior: negotiable with proportional pay.
- Script: "I can offer 4 days at 80% pay. At our stage I can't carry a part-time senior leader — the role needs consistency. If that tradeoff works for you, let's do it."

## Closing
When they're in:
> "I'm thrilled. I'll send the formal offer letter within the hour with all terms. We'll need signature within 5 business days and start on [date]. Any blockers I can help with?"

## Walking away
If they keep asking for more after you've stretched:
> "I've offered everything I can. If this isn't the right package for you, I understand. We should both want you to want to be here — if you don't, this isn't going to work out, and better to know now."

No hard feelings. The ones who come back after this are the best fits.

## After
- Offer sent in writing within 2 hours of verbal agreement.
- Reference checks in parallel if not done (assume the offer, verify the person).
- Weekly nudge if accepted but not started: stay in the loop. Counter-offer season lasts 2 weeks.
