# Founder Secondary Sale Negotiation

## When to raise secondary
- Series B or later, when company is clearly derisked
- Founder has significant personal financial pressure (mortgage, family)
- Dilution history means founder ownership is low
- Sophisticated investors who understand it's healthy (reduces founder pressure to sell too early)

## How much is reasonable
- Typical Series B: 5-15% of founder shares sold for secondary
- Total founder take-home: $500K-$3M depending on round size and stage
- Any amount that normalizes your life. Not retirement money.

## Structure

### In a priced round
- Run primary first. Get the round done with the intended investors at the intended terms.
- Secondary tranche at same valuation or slightly discounted (5-15% common).
- Taxed as long-term capital gains for founder (if held > 1 year).

### Tender offer
- Typically led by company or lead investor in a later round (Series C+).
- Open to all equity holders (though often capped).
- Company facilitates; investors buy existing shares.

## Scripts

**Ask your lead to include secondary in the round:**
> "As part of Series B, I'd like to include a $[X] secondary allocation to rebalance my personal finances. I've been running lean for [Y] years and my [family situation / mortgage / financial anchor] would benefit from this. It's small relative to the round — [Z]% — and I think it keeps me focused for the next several years without pressure. What's your take?"

**Investor pushback: "Founders shouldn't take secondary."**
> "I hear the concern — taking money off the table before the work is done. Here's my frame: taking $[X] doesn't change my motivation. What it does change is the pressure to exit early or take a bad acquisition offer when personal cash gets tight. The research on founder secondary [cite Andrew Chen / others] supports this. I'd rather have this conversation with you now than surprise you with an early exit push later."

## Tax considerations
- Confirm QSBS status before sale (if US). Section 1202 exclusion only applies to original-issuance shares held 5+ years.
- Talk to a tax advisor BEFORE signing. Not optional.

## Hard limits
- Don't sell more than 15% of your position at any one time. Signals weak conviction.
- Don't take secondary if the round is already hard to close. Signals you're bailing.
- Don't take secondary if morale among employees is shaky. Optically terrible.

## Documentation
- Secondary Purchase Agreement (separate from primary docs).
- Right of First Refusal (ROFR) notifications if required.
- Board approval (often required).
