# Negotiation Scripts

Scripts for the conversations founders negotiate most often. Each file follows the same shape:

1. **Before the call** — prep list, what to decide in advance, what to research.
2. **Opening** — first 3-5 minutes. Tone, what to say, what not to say.
3. **Main body** — the moves, objections, counter-moves.
4. **Closing** — how to lock a commitment, how to walk away cleanly.
5. **After the call** — follow-up timing, what to document, next steps.

Inspired by: Chris Voss (tactical empathy), Stuart Diamond (Getting More), Herb Cohen (You Can Negotiate Anything), and Patrick McKenzie's "Salary Negotiation."

## Core principles
- **Talk price last.** Understand constraints, interests, and alternatives first. Numbers come after context.
- **The person who can walk away wins.** Build a real BATNA before the conversation. If you can't walk, you're not negotiating — you're begging with extra steps.
- **Silence is a weapon.** After a strong ask or an objection, don't fill the silence. Let them respond first.
- **Label emotions.** "It sounds like you're frustrated with X" lowers the temperature. Don't argue feelings; name them.
- **Calibrated questions.** "How am I supposed to do that?" moves the problem to their side without being adversarial.
- **Write the summary email.** Whoever writes the follow-up controls what the meeting "said."
