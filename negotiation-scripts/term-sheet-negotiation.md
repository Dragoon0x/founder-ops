# Term Sheet Negotiation

## Before the call
- Have your lawyer redline the term sheet. Not optional. $3K-$8K now saves 10-100x later.
- Benchmark every clause against NVCA model term sheet. Anything materially worse needs a specific reason to accept.
- Confirm your alternative: other term sheets? runway without this raise? strategic acquirer interest?
- Decide your walk-aways in writing before the call. Typically:
  - Valuation more than 25% below ask: walk or major restructure needed
  - Liquidation preference > 1x non-participating: hard stop
  - Participating preferred: hard stop unless unique circumstances
  - Super pro-rata beyond standard: negotiable only if check is right
  - Full ratchet anti-dilution: hard stop, demand weighted average broad-based

## Opening (first 3-5 min)
- Thank them, genuinely. They chose you.
- Confirm mutual understanding before diving in: "Before I walk through our thoughts on the term sheet — is there anything you want me to address first? I want to make sure we start from the same place."
- Set frame: "I want us to close quickly and well. There are a handful of things I want to discuss so we don't have surprises in docs."

## Main body — clauses to push

### Valuation
Don't lead with valuation unless they opened there. Lead with control and rights.

If they pushed valuation down:
> "I hear you. What's driving the $X vs $Y we discussed? I want to understand whether it's market comp, your fund's construction, or something about our numbers."

Their answer tells you which lever moves. Market comp → you bring comps. Fund math → look for creative structure (secondary, smaller round, tranched). Our numbers → get specific on what would change their mind.

### Liquidation preference
Target: 1x non-participating. Accept: 1x non-participating with no multiple.
Never accept: participating preferred, preferences > 1x, preference stacks.
Script if they propose 1.5x:
> "1x non-participating is market at our stage. What's driving the 1.5x? If it's risk-adjustment, let's talk about what would make you comfortable at 1x — maybe pre-money, maybe board composition."

### Board composition
Target pre-seed/seed: 2 founder, 1 investor, 0 independent → or 2-1-0 / 2-2-1 depending on round size.
Target Series A: 2 founder, 2 investor, 1 independent (jointly agreed).
Red flag: "We need board control." That's a Series B+ conversation, not Series A, unless they're leading a large round.
Script:
> "On board, we're proposing [X]. My thinking: at this stage, day-to-day operator focus needs to outvote any single investor. I know that'll change by Series B — let's map a glide path."

### Protective provisions
Accept: standard NVCA protective provisions (block major changes that harm them).
Push back on: approval rights over annual budget, hiring senior roles, product direction. Those are operator decisions.
Script:
> "I'm fine giving protective provisions over [sale, liquidation, change to pref stack, new pref class]. Day-to-day operating decisions — budget, key hires, product — I need to own and be accountable for. Board-level oversight, not approval, is the right tool there."

### Option pool
The pool comes out of founder equity pre-money. 10% is common but may be too much if you have 18 months of hires already.
Script:
> "A 10% pool would be a dilution hit for us given our hiring plan over the next 18 months only needs about 6%. Can we size to that plan and top up if we miss?"

### Vesting (on founder shares)
If you've been at it < 1 year: full 4-year vest from the round date is standard.
If you've been at it 3+ years: push for credit for time served. Common: 25% vested at close, remaining on a 2-3 year schedule.
If they demand acceleration on termination without cause (you want it), ask for double-trigger (CoC + termination).

### Pro-rata rights
Major investors (>$500K check): fine to grant pro-rata.
Smaller checks: limit to major investors only.
Super pro-rata (right to > their stake): only grant if the check is meaningful and they've earned it through follow-on consistency.

### Anti-dilution
Target: weighted average broad-based.
Hard stop: full ratchet.

### Information rights
Give: quarterly financials, annual audited (if required), material litigation.
Don't give: monthly board decks to all preferred holders. That's for board members only.

## If they escalate
> "I hear you've got constraints. Tell me the single thing that's non-negotiable for you — then let's figure out what structure solves for that without breaking what I need on my side."

## Closing
- Summarize agreements and disagreements. "Here's what I heard: we agreed on X and Y. We're still apart on Z. My next step is [talk to co-founder / call lawyer / think overnight]. I'll send an email by [time] confirming."
- Don't say yes to anything big on the call. Any "yes" is for things you pre-decided.

## After the call
- Summary email within 4 hours. Lists every agreement and open item with status. This is the record.
- Loop your lawyer with the email. They'll draft follow-up points.
- If term sheet changes, get a revised version before counter-signing anything.

## Standard walk scripts
> "This isn't going to work for us as structured. I'd rather part friends now than sign something we resent. Happy to stay in touch — if you can restructure the [specific clause], let me know. Otherwise, thank you for the process."

No drama. No burn. 60% of the time they come back with something.
