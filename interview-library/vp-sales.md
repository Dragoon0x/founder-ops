# Interview Questions: VP Sales

## When to hire
Do NOT hire a VP Sales before:
- $1M ARR and a repeatable motion
- 2+ AEs already hitting 80% of quota
- Clear ICP definition
- 6 months of MoM growth data

If any of the above is missing, hire a player-coach Head of Sales
(who sells AND hires) instead. A VP is an org-builder; without an
org to build, they're overhead.

## Overall loop (8–10 hours total)
1. Recruiter screen (20 min)
2. CEO 1st (45 min): culture, vision, fit
3. CRO or board member (45 min): scale pattern-match
4. Case: "Build a 12-month plan" (1.5 hours)
5. Panel with sales team (1 hour)
6. Panel with exec team (1 hour)
7. Deep dive with CEO (90 min)
8. 5–7 reference calls
9. Decision and offer

## Questions by category

### Sales philosophy (pick 3)
1. "Walk me through your framework for building a sales team from
   scratch."
2. "What's the right mix of inbound vs outbound for our ICP?"
3. "How do you think about SDR vs AE ratios?"
4. "Tell me about the most important sales hire you've made. Why
   that role, that person?"
5. "What's your view on MEDDIC / MEDDPICC / Challenger / SPIN? What
   do you actually use?"
6. "When do you switch from founder-led sales to rep-led?"

**What good answers sound like:**
- Specific frameworks, specific numbers (e.g., "5:1 pipeline coverage").
- Stage-appropriate thinking ("at seed I'd do X, at Series B I'd do Y").
- Methodology preferences with reasons, not fads.
- Understands founder-led sales isn't a bug to fix.

**Red flags:**
- Generic "I build strong teams."
- Wants to install Salesforce, Outreach, Gong, LinkedIn Nav immediately.
- Wants to hire 6 reps in month 1.
- Hasn't read your product.
- Doesn't know your ACV or your sales cycle.

### Numbers and rigor (pick 3)
1. "What were your team's numbers at your last company? Quota,
   attainment, ramp?"
2. "What's your approach to pipeline management? How often do you
   inspect?"
3. "What's a sales metric you changed your mind about in the last 2
   years?"
4. "How do you forecast? How often is your forecast accurate?"
5. "Tell me about a quarter you missed. What happened and what did
   you do?"

**What good answers sound like:**
- Specific numbers, unhesitant.
- Forecasts within 10–15% typically.
- Weekly pipeline reviews with specific cadence.
- Owns misses, doesn't blame the team or product.

**Red flags:**
- Doesn't know their past numbers precisely.
- "We blew away quota" with no numbers.
- Forecasts are "aspirational."
- Misses were always because of product or marketing.

### Hiring and management (pick 3)
1. "What's your process for hiring AEs?"
2. "Tell me about the last AE you fired. Why?"
3. "How do you ramp a new AE?"
4. "What's your quota-setting philosophy?"
5. "Describe your onboarding curriculum for new hires."

**What good answers sound like:**
- Specific process (scorecard, structured interviews).
- Has fired people and learned from it.
- 3–6 month ramp with milestones.
- Quotas set with math, not gut.
- Onboarding has week-by-week specifics.

**Red flags:**
- Hired people they knew, didn't interview rigorously.
- Never fired anyone (unrealistic at VP level).
- "We onboard as needed."
- Quotas are "market" without explanation.

### Alignment with CEO and exec team (pick 3)
1. "What do you need from the CEO?"
2. "How do you work with marketing? What goes wrong most often?"
3. "Describe your relationship with your last CEO. What worked, what
   didn't?"
4. "How do you handle a request from the CEO to close a specific
   deal?"
5. "What should a founder-CEO stop doing in sales once you're hired?"

**What good answers sound like:**
- Specific needs (e.g., "weekly 30-min 1:1, clarity on pricing").
- Marketing-sales alignment with joint metrics.
- Last CEO relationship has nuance (both good and hard).
- Comfortable with founder involvement, has a handoff plan.

**Red flags:**
- "I don't need much from the CEO."
- Blames marketing entirely.
- Last CEO was "bad at sales."
- Wants the founder completely out of sales on day 1.

### Pipeline / case study questions
Run a case study (1.5 hours, live):
"You're joining our company. Here's our current state: $1.5M ARR,
2 AEs, $30K ACV, 3-week sales cycle, 6 months of MoM data attached.
Build a 12-month plan. Walk me through it."

**What good looks like:**
- Asks clarifying questions first.
- Identifies what's working vs what to change.
- Specific hiring plan tied to revenue targets.
- Specific metrics they'd track.
- Doesn't over-invest in tooling.
- Stages the changes over 12 months.

**What bad looks like:**
- Starts with tooling (Salesforce rollout).
- Wants to hire 10 reps immediately.
- Doesn't acknowledge what's already working.
- No metrics framework.
- Ignores ramp time for new hires.

## Reference check script (critical for VP)

Call at least 5 references, including:
- 2 CEOs they reported to
- 2 AEs who worked for them
- 1 CMO or marketing leader
- 1 peer executive (COO, CTO)

Questions:
1. "Walk me through the team they built. Sizes, structure, key hires."
2. "What were their numbers during their tenure?"
3. "What was their biggest contribution?"
4. "What was hardest about working with them?"
5. "How did they handle missed quarters?"
6. "Would you hire them again if you were a founder?"
7. "Is there anything I should be aware of that I haven't asked?"

The last question surfaces what's true but unmentioned. If 3+
references hedge, don't hire.

## Red flags across the loop

### Do not hire signals
- Can't describe a specific process they built
- Past numbers are vague
- Wants to install a full tech stack in month 1
- Fired people don't come up naturally
- References are all positive with zero nuance
- Hasn't researched your company
- Wants to hire only from their previous network
- No opinion on sales methodology
- Wants 6+ AEs in month 1

### Hire signals
- Specific plan for month 1, 3, 6, 12
- Owns misses, credits team for wins
- Has opinions with reasons
- Understands your ACV, sales cycle, ICP from doing research
- Has a handoff plan with the founder
- References give nuanced, honest answers
- Already identifies gaps in your current motion
- Brings frameworks without forcing them

## The "am I ready to hire a VP Sales" test
Before opening the role:
- [ ] $1M+ ARR
- [ ] 2+ AEs consistently hitting 80% of quota
- [ ] Defined ICP (written, agreed)
- [ ] Sales cycle tracked and forecasted within 30%
- [ ] Win rate tracked
- [ ] 6+ months of MoM growth data
- [ ] 18+ months of runway
- [ ] Founder ready to hand off deal closing

If more than 2 are no, you need a Head of Sales, not a VP.
