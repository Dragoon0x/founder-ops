# Example: Customer Discovery Interview Synthesis

*This is an example of what /founder-ops discovery produces after an interview.*

## Interview — Sarah Chen (VP Eng, MidCo) — 2026-04-08

### Direct Quotes (Signals)
> "We spend probably 6 hours a week just synthesizing our sprint retros."
> "I've built three different scripts to try to automate this."
> "If someone told me there was a tool that just did this, I'd buy it tomorrow."
> "We've looked at [Competitor X] but it's built for enterprise and we're 12 people."

### Problems (Mentioned Unprompted)
1. Sprint retro synthesis takes 6 hours/week across the team
2. Action items from retros get lost (not tracked anywhere)
3. Can't see patterns across retros (is this a recurring issue?)
4. Tools built for larger orgs don't fit mid-stage team

### Workarounds (What They Do Today)
- Manual Notion template with action items
- Slackbot that reminds about action items
- One engineer runs custom script to extract recurring themes
- Quarterly manual review by VP (6 hours)

### Competitive Map
- Aware of: Culture Amp (too expensive), Retrium (too complex)
- Considered: Building internally, hired contractor
- NOT aware of: [Us]

### Changed My Thinking
The pain is MORE acute than I thought. They're actively spending engineering
time ($X/week) on workarounds. This is a buying signal.

### Willingness to Pay Signal
> "I'd buy it tomorrow" — but we should test this with a specific price

### Next Interview Hypothesis
Other mid-stage companies (50-200 people) have the same pattern. Need to
validate that sprint retros specifically are the pain (vs generic team
retrospectives).

---

### Confidence: 8/10

**Why 8:** Strong specific pain, explicit willingness to pay, workarounds
indicate real cost of problem.

**Why not 10:** One interview. Need 5-10 more from similar companies to
confirm pattern.

**What would change my mind:** If 3 more similar-size companies say this
problem isn't urgent, the pattern isn't real.
