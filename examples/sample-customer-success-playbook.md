# Sample Customer Success Playbook

**Audience:** Helio CS team (currently 1 CSM + CEO backup)
**Last updated:** 2026-04-05

---

## Mission

Our CS job is not "response to tickets." It is "make customers successful enough that they renew and expand." Ticket response is one tool; proactive outreach, training, and business review are the others.

---

## Segmentation

We treat customers in three buckets:

| Tier | Definition | Touch model | CSM ratio |
|------|------------|-------------|-----------|
| Key | ACV > $30k or strategic | Named CSM, monthly 1:1 | 1:20 |
| Core | ACV $10-30k | Pooled CSM, quarterly 1:1 | 1:60 |
| Self-serve | ACV < $10k | Digital + in-product | 1:300 |

As of 2026-Q1, we have 12 Key, 46 Core, 37 Self-serve.

## Lifecycle stages

### Stage 1: Onboarding (week 0-4)

Goal: customer reaches "first value moment" (first real call captured, summarized, synced to CRM) within 5 business days of kickoff.

Actions:
- Day 0: Kickoff call scheduled
- Day 1: Admin setup checklist sent
- Day 3: Technical integration verified
- Day 7: First user training session
- Day 14: "Are you live?" check-in
- Day 28: Onboarding closeout; move to ongoing

Kill signal: No active user in first 21 days. Escalate to CS lead.

### Stage 2: Adoption (month 2-6)

Goal: sustained weekly active usage from 60%+ of seats.

Actions:
- Week 6: Business review #1 (30 min; usage data + blockers)
- Month 3: Training refresher session
- Month 4: Expansion opportunity check (more seats, more teams?)
- Month 6: Business review #2 (usage trend + ROI calc)

Key metric watched: weekly active seats / total seats.

Red flag: WAU/seats under 40% at month 3. Trigger rescue.

### Stage 3: Value delivery (month 7-12)

Goal: customer able to articulate measured ROI.

Actions:
- Quarterly business reviews
- Annual renewal conversation starts at month 10
- Case study or reference pitch for happy customers
- Expansion conversation

Red flag: No identified ROI by month 9. Escalate.

### Stage 4: Renewal and expansion (month 11-13)

Goal: 95%+ logo retention, 110%+ NRR.

Actions:
- Month 11: Renewal conversation, multi-year options, expansion discussion
- Month 12: Contract signed
- Month 13: New-term kickoff

## Playbooks by situation

### Playbook A: New champion joins

Trigger: Primary contact leaves or is replaced.

Steps:
1. Within 2 business days, outreach to new contact
2. Share onboarding package (tailored to new contact's role)
3. Offer training session for them
4. Reconnect with other users in the account as backstops

### Playbook B: Usage drop >30% month-over-month

Trigger: WAU drops 30%+ vs prior month.

Steps:
1. Day 1: Slack CS lead, pull usage data
2. Day 2: Email champion asking neutral question ("how are things going?")
3. Day 7: If no response, call
4. Day 14: If still no response, executive sponsor email
5. Document in CRM throughout

### Playbook C: Escalation from support

Trigger: Support case marked "executive escalation" or "potential churn."

Steps:
1. CSM takes over from support within 4 hours
2. Executive call within 24 hours (CEO for Key tier)
3. Written summary of issue, cause, remediation within 48 hours
4. Follow-up within 7 days; confirm resolved

### Playbook D: Expansion opportunity detected

Trigger: Customer using product beyond seat limits, asking about new features, or new teams onboarded.

Steps:
1. Flag in CRM as expansion opp
2. CSM books 30-min call to scope
3. Sales (or CEO) joins for commercial discussion
4. Expansion close target: 30 days

### Playbook E: Renewal risk

Trigger: Month 10 and any of: usage declining, champion disengaged, product complaint unresolved, competitive signal.

Steps:
1. CSM + sales leader review account in weekly pipeline meeting
2. Customer executive call within 2 weeks
3. Written plan to de-risk (may include price concession, added service, product commitment)
4. Weekly updates in pipeline meeting until resolved

## Metrics we track

- Logo retention rate (quarterly cohort)
- Net revenue retention
- Time to first value (days)
- Weekly active seats / total seats
- Case resolution time (P50, P90)
- CSAT score on closed cases
- QBR attendance rate

---

## What "great CSM" looks like at Helio

- Knows the top 5 blockers for each Key account without looking it up
- Catches churn signals 3 months before renewal, not 3 weeks
- Writes crisp executive summaries, not novels
- Pushes back on bad product behavior (telling us no when customers are silent but mad)
- Brings expansion signals to sales proactively

---

## Not our job (explicitly)

- Doing the customer's work for them (we train; we don't operate)
- Unbounded free professional services
- Customizing the product per customer
- Product roadmap promises
