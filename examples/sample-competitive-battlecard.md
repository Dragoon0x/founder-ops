## Win themes (when Helio wins)

### 1. Voice-first workflow
Our reps capture sales calls by voice from the car. Acme requires desktop input. In deals where field motion is primary, we win 75% of the time.

**Talk track:**
> "Your reps drive 200 miles a day. How long are they spending typing notes at 9pm? In Helio, they talk for 30 seconds after a call and we structure the CRM entry for them. Acme has not built this workflow because they were built for inside sales."

### 2. Accuracy on specialized sales data
Our model is tuned on the field-sales vocabulary. In tests (N=1000 call snippets), our entity extraction accuracy is 91% vs. Acme's 74%.

**Proof point:** Case study (Mountain Valley Co-op) — eliminated 6hrs/rep/week of data entry.

### 3. Price-value ratio in the mid-market
At $399, we're above Acme's $295 starter but below their $495 enterprise tier where AI is only available.

---

## Lose themes (when Acme wins)

### 1. Brand and trust
Acme has been around 10 years. Buyers know the name. "Nobody gets fired for choosing Acme."

**Counter:** "If 3000 Acme customers had solved this problem, we wouldn't have 95 customers who tried Acme first and switched."

### 2. Integration ecosystem
Acme has 400+ integrations. We have 18.

**Counter:** "We prioritized the 18 that matter for field sales. Which integrations are you actually using day-to-day?"

### 3. Procurement standardization
Enterprise IT standards list Acme.

**Counter:** Unless the prospect has specific field-sales pain, do not chase deeply-Acme-standardized accounts. ICP check.

## Objection library

> "We're already using Acme."

"Great. How is the mobile/field workflow going for your outside reps? Specifically, how much time are they spending on CRM data entry after hours?"

> "Acme just added AI features."

"They did. Which version? What's your upgrade cost? How does the feature work when the rep is offline? These are the gaps where we win."

> "Acme is cheaper."

"Acme's base tier does not include AI. Their AI tier is $495. Let's compare like to like."

> "What if you go out of business?"

"Fair question. We're venture-funded through [month/year], our month-over-month growth is 14%, and our churn is under 2%. Our customer contract includes a data export guarantee. Happy to share our team and investor background."

## When to walk away

Do not chase:
- Inside-sales-only teams (Acme wins; our wedge doesn't apply)
- Highly-customized Salesforce shops (switching cost too high)
- Sub-5-rep teams (we're not price-competitive at that scale)

Reward the AE who qualifies out early; punish the AE who chases for 6 months and loses.
