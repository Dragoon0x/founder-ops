**Equivalent 100-point: 90**

### Decision: HIRE

**Confidence: 9/10**

### Strengths
1. **Take-home quality:** Built a working solution that handled 5 of 5 edge cases including the unstated ones (idempotency, retries, ordering). Doc explained tradeoffs better than most senior engineers we've interviewed.
2. **Ownership stories:** Each "tell me about a time" answer was specific, owned outcomes (good and bad), and showed reflection. Not the typical rehearsed STAR-format answers.
3. **Pulse on the problem:** Has personally been on-call at scale. Asked questions that revealed deep understanding, not surface-level interest.
4. **Reference signal:** All 3 references were 8-10 strong recommendations. Two volunteered "would 100% hire them back if we had a role." Back-channel reference (former teammate they didn't list) confirmed pattern.

### Concerns
1. **Live communication slightly less polished** than written. They acknowledged this — "I think better when I can write." Workable for our async-first culture, but worth noting.
2. **Series B → Seed transition:** Have they only worked at established companies? Yes — but the way they described their last role, it was effectively a startup-within-a-company (small autonomous team). Should be fine.
3. **Compensation expectations:** They're asking $215K base. Our band for this role tops at $200K. Need to negotiate or stretch the band. Worth it given the score.

### Comparison to Bar
We've interviewed 47 candidates for this role. Top 3 advanced to founder chat.
- Candidate A (this one): 4.50 weighted
- Candidate B: 4.10 weighted
- Candidate C: 3.85 weighted

This candidate is clearly above bar.

### Recommendation
**Extend offer.** Base $210K (above band but justified), 0.4% equity, $20K signing bonus.

**Push for fast close:** They're interviewing at 2 other places. Make offer within 24 hours of this scorecard.

**Concerns to raise in offer call:** None — present the offer clean.

### Adversarial Check

**What if I'm wrong?**
- They've never been in a true startup environment. Could struggle with ambiguity. → Mitigated by their take-home (handled an ambiguous brief well)
- Senior engineer at Series B might want senior at us with similar comp/title. → Aligned via offer structure (above-band comp)
- References might be cherry-picked. → Back-channel reference (random teammate from LinkedIn) confirmed positive pattern

**Worst case:** They join, struggle for 3 months, we let go. Cost: ~$70K + 3 months of opportunity cost. Acceptable risk given upside.

**Best case:** They scale to staff engineer in 18 months, become foundational technical leader. Likely outcome based on signal.
