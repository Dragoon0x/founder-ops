# Example: Runway Analysis

*This is an example of what /founder-ops finance (runway submode) produces.*

## Runway Analysis — April 2026

### Current Position
- Cash on hand: $1,840,000
- Current MRR: $49,000 ($588K ARR)
- Monthly gross burn: $82,000
- Monthly net burn: $33,000 (after revenue)
- Current runway: **55 months** at current net burn

### Scenario A — Status Quo (No Changes)
Assumes MRR grows at recent trend (12%/month), burn stays flat.

| Month | MRR | Burn | Net Burn | Cash |
|-------|-----|------|----------|------|
| Apr 2026 | 49K | 82K | 33K | 1,807K |
| Jul 2026 | 69K | 82K | 13K | 1,752K |
| Oct 2026 | 97K | 82K | -15K (profit) | 1,779K |
| Jan 2027 | 136K | 82K | -54K | 1,922K |

**Projection:** Hits profitability in ~Oct 2026. Runway: effectively unlimited if growth holds.

### Scenario B — Hire Aggressively
Add 4 engineers + 2 GTM hires over Q2 ($42K/mo extra burn).

| Month | MRR | Burn | Net Burn | Cash |
|-------|-----|------|----------|------|
| Apr 2026 | 49K | 82K | 33K | 1,807K |
| Jul 2026 | 69K | 124K | 55K | 1,640K |
| Oct 2026 | 97K | 124K | 27K | 1,550K |
| Jan 2027 | 136K | 124K | -12K | 1,530K |
| Apr 2027 | 191K | 124K | -67K | 1,683K |

**Projection:** 22 months runway, break-even Jan 2027. Riskier but higher ceiling.

### Scenario C — Hit Aggressive Growth Target
Assume 20%/month growth (vs 12% current) — same burn.

| Month | MRR | Burn | Net Burn | Cash |
|-------|-----|------|----------|------|
| Apr 2026 | 49K | 82K | 33K | 1,807K |
| Jul 2026 | 85K | 82K | -3K | 1,801K |
| Oct 2026 | 146K | 82K | -64K | 1,937K |

**Projection:** Hits profitability in ~Jul 2026. Strong fundraising position.

### The Real Question
You don't have a cash problem. You have a growth problem (which is a better
problem to have). Decide: do you want to:

**(a)** Stay lean, hit profitability in Q4, raise later from a position of strength
**(b)** Hire aggressively now, accelerate growth, raise Series A in Q4-Q1
**(c)** Push growth experiments without hiring — maximize Scenario C

### Recommendation
**Option (b) if you can raise Series A within 12 months.**
Confidence: 7/10.

Rationale:
- You have 22 months runway even under Scenario B — plenty of buffer
- At current 12% growth, you're not fast enough for a competitive Series A story
- Hiring now sets you up for $5-8M raise in Q4 2026 or Q1 2027 at better terms

### What Would Change My Mind
- If growth drops below 8%/month for 2 months → pause hiring
- If a major competitor raises a megaround → consider accelerating
- If customer acquisition cost payback goes above 18 months → fix that first
