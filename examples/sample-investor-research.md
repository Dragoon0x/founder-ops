# Example: Investor Research Brief

*This is an example of what /founder-ops fundraise (research submode) produces.*

## Investor Research — Sarah Park (Catalyst Ventures)

### Investment Thesis
Catalyst invests in infrastructure for modern software teams. Stage: Seed
to Series A. Check size: $500K – $3M for seed, $3-8M for Series A.
Specifically: developer tools, data infrastructure, AI-native workflows.

Sarah joined from partner track at Greylock in 2024. Her thesis (from her
Substack): "The next Notion is built for engineers, not PMs." She writes
about developer experience and internal tools.

### Recent Investments (last 12 months)
- **Lumen.dev** ($2.5M seed, Oct 2025) — AI pair programmer for testing. Sarah led.
- **Framewise** ($1.8M seed, Jan 2026) — Design tokens platform. Co-led with Founders Fund.
- **Grail Analytics** ($4M Series A, Mar 2026) — Observability for LLM apps.

Pattern: She backs technical founders building for technical users.
Prefers companies with working product + early revenue over deck-only.

### Portfolio Fit Score: 9/10

**Why 9:**
- Perfect stage (we're seed-ish, she does seed)
- Same category (infrastructure for dev teams)
- Portfolio comps: Lumen.dev is adjacent (testing vs our observability), won't be conflict
- She writes publicly about the exact pain we solve

**Why not 10:**
- She led Grail (observability) 3 months ago — might have recency bias that blocks us if she thinks category is "done"
- Need to clearly differentiate from Grail (we're infra-level, Grail is app-level)

### Partner to Target
**Sarah Park** — only partner at Catalyst investing in this category.
Do not email other partners; they'll forward to her anyway and it looks spammy.

### Warm Intro Paths
1. **[Name X]** — our customer, previously worked at Greylock with Sarah. Strong path.
2. **[Name Y]** — Lumen.dev founder (Sarah's portfolio). Medium path — we'd need to ask carefully.
3. **[Name Z]** — Grail CEO. Lowest priority — she'd wonder why we need Catalyst if Grail exists.

### Questions She'll Ask (based on her writing + portfolio pattern)
1. How is this different from Grail / Datadog / Honeycomb? (Be ready with 3 concrete differences, not slogans)
2. What's your GTM motion? (She favors bottoms-up product-led growth)
3. Why won't the big 3 (Datadog, New Relic, Splunk) just build this? (She asks this in every meeting — have answer)
4. Show me your retention cohorts, not just MRR. (She writes about this publicly)
5. Who else is in the round? (She often co-leads with specific funds — know the answer)

### Red Flags (deprioritize if any hit)
- **Current portfolio conflict check:** Grail Analytics is adjacent but app-level — not a direct conflict. Lumen is in testing, not observability. Others in Catalyst portfolio: no conflicts found.
- **Partnership tenure:** She's new at Catalyst (18 months). Still building reputation internally. May mean longer committee process — plan for 4-6 weeks from first meeting to term sheet.

### Recommendation
Target Sarah via warm intro from [Name X]. Confidence: 8/10.
Back-up: Cold outreach with the template above if no warm intro by [date].

### What Would Change My Mind
- If she passes on a close comp (similar-stage infra company) in next 30 days → deprioritize
- If Catalyst leads a direct competitor → deprioritize or negotiate competitive restrictions
