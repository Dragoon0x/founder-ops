---

## Before day 1 (manager prep)

Checklist owned by the hiring manager:

- [ ] Offer accepted, paperwork complete
- [ ] Laptop ordered and shipped (tracking to new hire 5+ days before start)
- [ ] Day 1 accounts created: email, Slack, GitHub, Linear, Notion, 1Password, AWS
- [ ] Day 1 calendar pre-populated with meetings (see below)
- [ ] Buddy assigned; buddy briefed
- [ ] Welcome email sent with first-day logistics
- [ ] Announcement in #team-announcements written, scheduled for day 1
- [ ] Starter project scoped and written up in Linear
- [ ] Onboarding doc for this role reviewed and updated if stale

---

## Day 1 (Monday)

### 9:00 - 9:30: Welcome (manager)
- Intro to company context, team, role
- Q&A
- Logistical setup confirmation
- Goals for first week

### 9:30 - 10:30: Setup block (async)
- Log into accounts
- Install tooling (we have a setup script in the eng repo: `./scripts/setup-engineer.sh`)
- Read README in our top 3 repos
- Slack self-intro in #team-announcements

### 10:30 - 11:00: Buddy coffee (virtual or in-person)
- Informal intro
- Tour of Slack, Notion, team norms
- Any-question session

### 11:00 - 12:00: Deep context — product and technical overview (CTO)
- What the product is, who uses it, why
- High-level architecture
- Current top 3 technical priorities

### 12:00 - 1:00: Lunch (solo or with team, preference-based)

### 1:00 - 3:00: Reading block
Assigned reading:
- Company CLAUDE.md / team handbook
- Engineering style guide
- Incident playbook
- Last 3 engineering all-hands decks
- Top 5 open engineering decisions in Notion

### 3:00 - 3:30: 1:1 with CEO
- Why we exist
- What matters in next 6 months
- Q&A

### 3:30 - 5:00: First commit
Goal: small PR merged by end of day 1.

Pre-scoped issue waiting for them. Buddy is reachable. Small enough that failure is essentially impossible.

### 5:00 - 5:30: Day 1 retro (manager)
- How did today go?
- What was confusing?
- What would have helped?

---

## Day 2-5

### Each morning
- 15 min standup with team
- 30 min 1:1 with buddy or manager

### Structured learning blocks
- **Day 2:** Codebase deep dive, lead by senior engineer
- **Day 3:** Customer exposure — listen to 3 recorded customer calls
- **Day 4:** Build and test pipeline; ship a small feature
- **Day 5:** Demo first real contribution at Friday demo

### Rolling meetings
- Monday: Sprint planning
- Wednesday: Engineering all-hands
- Friday: Demo + team retro

---

## Expected by end of week 1

- First PR merged
- Second feature scoped and started
- 1:1 with every team member completed
- Runbook read for on-call (even if on-call rotation not yet scheduled)
- Questions and confusions documented (we expect 20+ — silence is a red flag)

---

## Expected by end of week 2

- Leading at least one small feature end-to-end
- Participated in on-call shadow (if on-call role)
- Met 3 people outside direct team
- First async technical writing in Notion

---

## Expected by end of month 1

- Independent feature ownership
- Code review participation
- Contributed to engineering standards doc
- First customer-impacting production change

---

## Expected by end of month 3

- On-call rotation full member
- Proposed at least one process improvement
- Mentored another engineer on something
- Quarterly review written with manager

---

## Bad signals (manager watches for)

- Silent. 0 questions is worse than 20.
- Weekly 1:1s canceled repeatedly
- No PR by end of week 1
- Slack activity near zero
- Isolated from team social (optional but signal)

If any of these, manager intervenes within 48 hours.

---

## Manager feedback cadence

- Week 1 Friday: Informal check-in, 15 min
- Week 2 Friday: Formal feedback, 30 min
- Week 4 Friday: First month review, 45 min
- Week 12: Quarterly review

Bad fit should be visible by week 6 if at all. 90-day mark is the decision point.

We keep this doc canonical and copy per new hire. Each new hire's copy is an actual checklist their manager works through.

Updated after each new hire with new-hire feedback on what was missing.
