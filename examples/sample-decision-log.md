## Decision #14: Pricing Restructure to Value-Based Tiers
**Date**: 2026-02-15
**Decision Maker**: CEO
**Status**: Decided — Revisit by 2026-05-15

**Context**: Current flat-rate pricing ($99/mo) leaves money on the table with larger customers and doesn't differentiate our offering from cheaper alternatives.

**Options Considered**:
1. Per-seat pricing ($15/seat/mo) — Pros: scales with customer size, familiar model / Cons: discourages adding users, penalizes collaboration
2. Three-tier model (Starter $49, Growth $149, Enterprise custom) — Pros: captures different willingness to pay, clear upgrade path / Cons: requires feature gating work, some customers may downgrade
3. Usage-based ($0.10/event) — Pros: aligns with value, low barrier / Cons: revenue unpredictable, hard to forecast, customer anxiety

**Decision**: Option 2 (Three-tier model). Starter includes core features for teams < 5. Growth adds analytics, integrations, and priority support. Enterprise adds SSO, audit logs, and dedicated CSM.

**Expected Outcome**: Average ARPA increases from $99 to $130 within 6 months. < 5% of existing customers downgrade to Starter.

## Decision #13: Hire VP Engineering Before VP Sales
**Date**: 2026-01-20
**Decision Maker**: CEO + CTO
**Status**: Decided

**Context**: We have budget for one VP-level hire in Q1. Both engineering and sales need leadership.

**Options**:
1. VP Engineering first — Pros: unblock product velocity, reduce CTO bottleneck / Cons: sales remains founder-led
2. VP Sales first — Pros: professionalize revenue, free up founder time / Cons: product velocity stays limited

**Decision**: VP Engineering. Rationale: at $80K MRR, our growth is product-limited, not sales-limited. Founder can continue leading sales for another 2-3 months. Product velocity is the bottleneck.

**Expected Outcome**: Ship 2 major features in Q2 that were blocked by eng leadership gap.

**Review Date**: 2026-04-20

**Actual Outcome**: VP Eng started March 1. Two features shipped by April 10. Product velocity up 40%. Sales still founder-led, working but nearing capacity.
