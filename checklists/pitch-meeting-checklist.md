# Pitch Meeting Checklist

Run this before every investor meeting.

## 72 Hours Before

- [ ] Partner research complete (see `prompts/investor-research-brief.md`)
- [ ] Their portfolio mapped (who they've invested in adjacent to you)
- [ ] Recent writing / tweets read (2-3 they've done)
- [ ] Mutual connections identified (for backchannel awareness)

## 48 Hours Before

- [ ] Deck final
- [ ] Demo working on your laptop + backup
- [ ] 2-minute pitch rehearsed (say it out loud 3x)
- [ ] Top 10 expected questions → best answers prepped
- [ ] Metrics memorized (don't check phone during meeting)

## 24 Hours Before

- [ ] Confirm meeting logistics (time, location, zoom link)
- [ ] Calendar reminder for 15 min prior to leave early
- [ ] Laptop battery charged / adapter packed
- [ ] Water bottle, notebook, pen

## 2 Hours Before

- [ ] Review your own deck in full
- [ ] Review their partner page one more time
- [ ] 10-min walk (clears head, not prep)
- [ ] Eat something light

## Entering the Meeting

- [ ] Arrive 5 min early (not earlier — awkward)
- [ ] Phone on silent
- [ ] Strong posture, calm energy
- [ ] Warm open (NOT launching into pitch)

## In-Meeting Framework

### First 5 min (connection, not pitch)
- [ ] Reference something specific they did / said / wrote
- [ ] Ask how much time you have (they'll say 30/60 min)
- [ ] Ask "how would you like to spend the time?" (often they'll say "walk me through it")

### Middle 40-45 min (the real conversation)
- [ ] Walk through the deck at THEIR pace, not yours
- [ ] Pause after key slides — watch face for engagement
- [ ] When they ask questions, STOP presenting and answer fully
- [ ] Take notes on their questions (write, don't rely on memory)

### Last 5-10 min (close strong)
- [ ] Ask: "What's your process from here?"
- [ ] Ask: "What would help you make a decision?"
- [ ] Ask: "Is there anything I haven't answered?"
- [ ] Propose concrete next step ("Want me to send X?")

## Things to NOT Do

- [ ] Don't dodge a hard question
- [ ] Don't over-explain beyond answer
- [ ] Don't ask for commitment on the spot
- [ ] Don't disparage competitors
- [ ] Don't pretend to have metrics you don't
- [ ] Don't tell them who else is in the round (unless they ask)
- [ ] Don't leave without a clear next step

## Within 24h After

- [ ] Follow-up email:
  - Thank you
  - Answer(s) to anything you punted on
  - Specific asset promised (docs, customer list, etc.)
  - Next step proposed with timing
- [ ] Add them to your investor update list
- [ ] CRM updated with meeting notes
- [ ] Debrief with co-founder (5 min): what worked, what didn't

## The Meeting Quality Test

Answer honestly:
- Did they engage (lean in, ask hard questions) or coast?
- Did they ask about their process unprompted?
- Did they mention partners/process in future tense?
- Did they connect you to someone (even offhandedly)?

Strong engagement on 3+ = real interest.
Coasting on 3+ = polite pass coming.
