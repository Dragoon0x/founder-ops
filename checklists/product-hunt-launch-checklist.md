# Product Hunt Launch Checklist

Product Hunt is noisy but still valuable for distribution to makers and
early adopters. A #1 day gets you 5-20K visitors, 500-3000 signups,
maybe 20-100 paying customers.

## T-14 Days

- [ ] Decide: Today is the Today (pick a Tuesday-Thursday)
- [ ] Verify: do you have a Hunter? (Someone with PH authority)
  - If not: engage a known Hunter 2 weeks in advance, brief them
  - Top hunters: check ProductHunt "Hunters" leaderboard
- [ ] Verify: is your product PH-appropriate?
  - Finished enough to use (not "join waitlist")
  - Free tier or generous free trial
  - Appeals to makers / early adopters / tech-adjacent

## T-7 Days

### Assets (exact sizes matter)
- [ ] Logo: 240x240 PNG, transparent background, recognizable at small size
- [ ] Cover image / main: 635x380 (shows on launch card)
- [ ] Gallery: 4-6 screenshots at 1200x630 or 1270x760
- [ ] Video (recommended): 60-90 sec, MP4, thumbnail set

### Copy
- [ ] Tagline: 60 chars, specific outcome-focused (not feature list)
- [ ] Description: 260 chars, what + for whom + differentiator
- [ ] First comment from maker: 150-200 words, story + CTA

## T-3 Days

### Ship product
- [ ] Submit to PH "Ship" queue (if you have access)
- [ ] Scheduled launch date confirmed
- [ ] Set visibility to "Unlisted" until go-live

### Build amplification list
- [ ] 30-50 people who'll upvote in the first 4 hours (key window)
- [ ] Messaging to them prepped (don't mass-blast, send personal)
- [ ] Makers you want Kevin-style feedback from

## T-1 Day

- [ ] Final review of all assets
- [ ] Last test: fill out the whole PH page, preview
- [ ] Launch time CONFIRMED (00:01 PT = midnight Pacific = goes live 12:01 AM)
- [ ] Calendar blocked for the full day
- [ ] Sleep 8 hours

## Launch Day

### 12:01 AM PT (or your timezone equivalent)
- [ ] Product goes live (automated based on ship schedule)
- [ ] Maker comment posted immediately (within first 5 min)
- [ ] Tweet: "We're live on Product Hunt!" with PH link
- [ ] DM first 10 strongest supporters (don't mass-blast — personal)

### First 2 hours
- [ ] Reply to every comment within 15 min
- [ ] Monitor ranking (you want to be top-5 in first 2 hours to stay visible)
- [ ] If not top-5: activate broader network (newsletter, LinkedIn, Slack groups)

### Hours 3-8
- [ ] Consistent comment replies (every comment gets your time)
- [ ] Post daily micro-updates in the comments ("Thanks for the feedback! Shipped this small fix already.")
- [ ] Team amplifies (but doesn't upvote from same IP = bans)

### Hours 9-16
- [ ] Second amplification push (newsletter, different Slack groups)
- [ ] Founder AMA offer in comments (invites engagement)

### Final hours (to midnight PT)
- [ ] Rally the remaining amplifiers
- [ ] Late-day push (US East Coast day is 3-11 PM PT)
- [ ] Final "thanks for a great day" post

### End of day
- [ ] Note your final rank
- [ ] Download all comments for your records
- [ ] DMs saved / tracked

## Post-Launch

### Day 2-3
- [ ] Thank-you to every upvoter (through PH, no spam)
- [ ] DM strongest commenters
- [ ] Follow up with leads from comments
- [ ] Import signups to your CRM

### Week 1-2
- [ ] Newsletter recap (if you have one)
- [ ] Blog post: "What we learned from launching on PH"
- [ ] Case study from launch-day customers

## Rules You Must Follow
- **No asking for upvotes directly.** PH bans for this.
- **No buying upvotes.** PH bans for this.
- **No coordinated IP upvoting.** PH bans for this.
- **No launching the same product twice.** Major features = OK. Minor = not OK.
- **Respond to every comment.** The engagement keeps you visible.

## What "Winning" Looks Like

- Top 5 of the day → solid launch, ~2-5K visitors, 300-1000 signups
- #1 of the day → strong launch, ~5-15K visitors, 1000-3000 signups
- Top 20 of the week → ongoing reputation boost
- Featured in the newsletter → 2-day tail of traffic

Don't obsess over rank. Obsess over conversions.

## Anti-Patterns
- Treating PH as your only launch channel (it's one of several)
- Spending more time on PH than on your product after launch
- Over-polishing assets (time better spent on landing page conversion)
- Ignoring the comments (the signal is there)
