# PR Launch Checklist

Only use this if you have a genuinely newsworthy moment (Series A close,
product that solves a named problem, first-of-kind launch). PR for a
generic "we exist" story is a waste.

## T-21 Days (plan)

- [ ] Define the angle: what's the STORY (not the product)?
- [ ] Identify 2-3 angle alternatives (for different outlets)
- [ ] Exclusive decided? (Exclusive = 1 outlet gets first; others get embargo)
- [ ] Embargo date chosen (Tuesday-Thursday, 6 AM PT / 9 AM ET)

## T-14 Days (build list)

- [ ] List 15-25 journalists who cover your beat
  - Name, outlet, 3 articles they wrote showing beat fit
  - Last thing they published (recency)
  - Their preferred contact (Twitter DM, email, Signal)
- [ ] Rank: Tier 1 (5) = you want, Tier 2 (10) = you'd take, Tier 3 (10) = backup

## T-10 Days (assets)

- [ ] Written announcement (400-600 words, no fluff)
- [ ] Founder headshots (high-res, professional or natural — not corporate)
- [ ] Product screenshots (3-5, high-res)
- [ ] Logo (light + dark version, SVG + PNG)
- [ ] Fact sheet (one-pager: founded, team, funding, customers, metrics)
- [ ] Customer quote (written permission, attributable)

## T-7 Days (initial outreach)

- [ ] Tier 1 outreach: personal emails, 100 words max
  - Specific reference to their recent coverage
  - 2-sentence story pitch (the angle)
  - Offer: exclusive / first demo / founder interview
  - Explicit embargo date
- [ ] Attach: fact sheet, not full press release

## T-4 Days (confirm)

- [ ] Tier 1 replies: schedule calls
- [ ] No reply from Tier 1: nudge once, then move to Tier 2
- [ ] Tier 2 + 3: shorter email, same angle
- [ ] Confirm exclusive (if offered) or embargo understanding

## T-2 Days (prep interviews)

- [ ] For each scheduled interview:
  - Journalist's recent 3 articles read
  - Anticipated questions prepped
  - Talking points (3 max, not a pitch)
  - Quotable line (the thing you want them to quote)
- [ ] Press kit published at private URL
- [ ] Exclusive article reviewed (if they send draft)

## T-1 Day (embargo prep)

- [ ] Final press release + assets in press kit
- [ ] Distribute press kit link to confirmed journalists
- [ ] Reconfirm embargo time in writing

## T-0 (launch day)

### 6:00 AM PT
- [ ] Embargo lifts
- [ ] Exclusive piece goes live (if applicable)
- [ ] Your own channels go live (Twitter, blog, etc.)
- [ ] Tier 1 reminders if they haven't published

### 9:00 AM PT
- [ ] Check coverage: who published, who didn't
- [ ] Second-wave outreach to Tier 2 with "as covered by [Tier 1 outlet]"

### 12:00 PM PT
- [ ] Syndication: pick up by aggregators (Techmeme, etc.)
- [ ] Social amplification of coverage

## T+1 to T+3

- [ ] Individual thank-you to every journalist who covered
- [ ] Follow-up pitch to Tier 2/3 who missed launch day
- [ ] Capture all coverage in a tracker (`data/press-coverage-[launch].md`)

## Follow-Up Rhythm

- Good journalist = nurture for 6 months (respond to their tweets, share their articles, pitch again when relevant)
- Great journalist = build relationship over years
- Never ghost after getting coverage. They remember.

## Anti-Patterns
- Generic press release to "press@" addresses (ignored)
- Exclusive to multiple outlets (kills the relationship with all)
- Breaking embargo (kills future coverage with every journalist)
- Embargoing internally leaky news (they'll just scoop it)
- No follow-through on journalist follow-up questions

## Metric of Success
- Tier 1 coverage: target 1-2 outlets
- Tier 2 coverage: target 3-5
- Total pickups: target 8-15
- Inbound from coverage (users / customers): target 5x baseline that day
