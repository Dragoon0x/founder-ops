# New Hire First Week

The first week's goal: by Friday, they shipped something real and they
know they made the right decision joining.

## Monday — See Day 1 checklist

## Tuesday

### Morning
- [ ] First real task scoped (2-4 hours of work, shipable)
- [ ] Access to required tools / docs confirmed
- [ ] Technical mentor / buddy assigned (if applicable)

### Afternoon
- [ ] Shadow a relevant meeting (product, customer, team sync)
- [ ] 1:1s with 2 peers (30 min each)

## Wednesday

### Morning
- [ ] Ship first small thing (even if trivial — a PR merged, a doc updated, a bug fixed)
- [ ] Pair with mentor for 1-2 hours

### Afternoon
- [ ] Customer call (shadow) or customer-facing meeting
- [ ] Start scoping first "real" project

## Thursday

### Morning
- [ ] Work session on first real project
- [ ] 1:1 with manager (30 min — check in on pace, blockers)

### Afternoon
- [ ] Cross-functional intro meeting (whoever they'll work with most outside their team)
- [ ] Company all-hands if scheduled (participate, don't just observe)

## Friday

### Morning
- [ ] Finish / ship first week deliverable
- [ ] Post in a public channel: "Shipped X this week — here's what I learned"

### Afternoon
- [ ] End-of-week 1:1 with manager
  - [ ] What went well?
  - [ ] What was confusing or frustrating?
  - [ ] Did reality match what you thought from interview?
  - [ ] What do you want more of next week? Less of?
  - [ ] 30-60-90 plan — finalize together

### End of Week
- [ ] They send a written reflection (to themselves or manager — helps consolidate)
- [ ] Manager sends brief update to team: "Welcoming [name] — here's what they shipped in week 1"

## The Friday Reflection Questions (for manager)

- Did they ship something?
- Did they seem energized (vs. drained)?
- Are they comfortable asking questions?
- Have they connected with 3+ teammates meaningfully?
- If they quit Monday, would we be surprised?

## Red Flags — Address in Week 2 1:1

- They're still blocked by access or tools
- They haven't talked to anyone outside their team
- Their first task is still not shipped
- They seem more confused than day 1
- They didn't ask questions (engagement = asking)

## Green Flags

- They pushed back on something small (healthy)
- They've asked 1-2 "why do we do it this way" questions (learning)
- They've made 1 person laugh (connecting)
- They took initiative on something not assigned

## Week 2-4 Rhythm

- Daily quick-sync with manager (async standup or 5-min call)
- Shipping velocity increases each week
- By week 3: they're teaching someone else something
- By week 4: you've forgotten they're new
