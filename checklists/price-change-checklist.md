# Price Change Checklist

Whether raising or restructuring, price changes are high-risk high-reward.

## T-30 Days (Strategic Prep)

### Clarity
- [ ] Why are you changing? (Value delivered, cost, positioning)
- [ ] What problem are you solving? (Revenue, competition, mis-positioning)
- [ ] What's the hypothesis? (ARPU ↑, conversion ↓ X%, net neutral)

### Analysis
- [ ] Current pricing performance (conversion by tier, expansion, churn by tier)
- [ ] Competitive pricing benchmarked (their list and actual)
- [ ] Segment analysis (which ICPs are under-paying vs. over-paying?)
- [ ] New pricing modeled: impact on new sales, existing customers

### Decision
- [ ] What's changing (prices, tiers, packaging)
- [ ] Who it affects (new only, new + renewal, all immediately)
- [ ] Grandfather policy (lock existing customers at current price for X months?)

## T-14 Days (Internal Prep)

### Team comms
- [ ] Leadership aligned
- [ ] Sales team briefed
- [ ] Support team briefed (expected questions + answers)
- [ ] Customer success briefed (for existing customers)

### Materials
- [ ] New pricing page draft
- [ ] FAQ for customers
- [ ] Email templates (new prospects, existing customers, grandfathered)
- [ ] Sales talk tracks updated
- [ ] Contracts / quotes updated

### Systems
- [ ] Billing system updated (new SKUs, new prices)
- [ ] Website updated (draft, not live)
- [ ] Trial → paid conversion flow updated
- [ ] Analytics tracking new pricing tiers

## T-7 Days (Final Checks)

- [ ] QA on billing (test signup with new pricing)
- [ ] Stripe / payment processor tested
- [ ] Internal tools updated (CRM, admin panels)
- [ ] Legal review of customer comms
- [ ] Announcement timing chosen (Tue-Thu, 9 AM)

## T-30 Days (Notice to Existing)

- [ ] Email sent to all affected existing customers
- [ ] Email template features:
  - Clear new prices
  - Specific date change takes effect
  - Why (brief, honest — value delivered, cost of service, etc.)
  - Grandfathering details
  - Contact for questions
- [ ] Large accounts get a personal call, not just email

## Launch Day

### Morning
- [ ] New pricing page live
- [ ] Internal comms to team: "It's live"
- [ ] Social announcement if appropriate

### Throughout day
- [ ] Monitor: new signups (conversion impact)
- [ ] Monitor: support volume
- [ ] Monitor: customer replies / pushback
- [ ] Founder replies personally to top customers

## Day 2-7

### Data watch
- [ ] Conversion rate: did it drop?
- [ ] Support volume: spike?
- [ ] Churn: any increases?
- [ ] Sentiment: social / reviews
- [ ] Existing customer actions: downgrades, cancellations

### Response plan
- [ ] Churn spike >X%: emergency review (see `playbooks/customer-churn-spike.md`)
- [ ] Conversion drop >X%: diagnose (value prop disconnect?)
- [ ] Sentiment backlash: founder post / personal outreach

## Week 2-4

### Optimization
- [ ] Are prospects confused? (Messaging fix)
- [ ] Are specific segments churning? (Targeted comms)
- [ ] Are sales discount requests up? (Expected — hold the line mostly)

## Month 2

### Review
- [ ] Net revenue impact (up or down vs. baseline)
- [ ] ARPU change (new customers)
- [ ] Churn change (existing customers)
- [ ] Was the hypothesis right?

### Lessons
- [ ] What worked in the rollout
- [ ] What caused unexpected issues
- [ ] What we'd do differently next time

## Specific Scenarios

### Price increase (most common)
- [ ] 30-day notice to existing customers MINIMUM
- [ ] Grandfather option for loyalty (12-month lock at old price)
- [ ] Offer annual prepay at old price to avoid increase

### Freemium to paid
- [ ] Clear "why we're changing"
- [ ] Generous transition window (30-60 days)
- [ ] Tiered options (can't afford paid? Here's a sunset path)

### Restructuring tiers (splitting, combining)
- [ ] Clear mapping from old to new for existing customers
- [ ] Side-by-side comparison in comms
- [ ] No one accidentally pays more without notification

### Adding enterprise tier
- [ ] Existing customers unaffected
- [ ] Clear delineation of what enterprise gets
- [ ] Don't strip features from existing tiers to push up

## Anti-Patterns

- Surprise price increase without notice (illegal in many places, always bad practice)
- Raising prices without adding value (customers notice)
- Changing prices when retention is weak (compounds churn)
- Coward-pricing (unfair to customers and yourself)
- Hiding price changes in obscure parts of site

## When NOT to Change Prices

- You just launched a new product (wait 6 months)
- Churn is elevated (fix churn first)
- You're in active fundraising (investors dislike mid-raise pricing changes)
- Major product issues recent (bad timing)
- Competitive pressure is making you lower (think strategically — are you differentiated?)
