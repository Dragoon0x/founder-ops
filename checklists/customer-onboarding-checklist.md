# Customer Onboarding Checklist

For B2B SaaS new customers, especially mid-market and enterprise. First
90 days determine lifetime value.

## Day 0 (Contract Signed)

### Same-day actions
- [ ] Welcome email from founder (personal, not automated)
- [ ] Invoice sent (if not prepaid)
- [ ] Champion added to your CRM with detailed notes
- [ ] Internal Slack channel created: #customer-[name]
- [ ] Dedicated Slack Connect channel with customer (if applicable)
- [ ] Calendar invite: kickoff call (within 3 days)

### Within 24 hours
- [ ] Welcome kit sent:
  - What's next (day-by-day for first week)
  - Key contacts on our side (with photos if possible)
  - Customer-dedicated dashboard link
  - Quick-start guide for their role
- [ ] Usage dashboard set up for you (track their adoption)

## Week 1

### Day 1-2: Technical setup
- [ ] Kickoff call (all key stakeholders)
  - Goals confirmed (what does success look like in 90 days?)
  - Team introduced on both sides
  - Timeline walked through
  - Any integration/data requirements
- [ ] Account provisioned + test user created
- [ ] Any integration requirements documented
- [ ] Success criteria documented (written, shared)

### Day 3-5: First value
- [ ] Core user(s) activated (logged in, did the first key action)
- [ ] First "aha moment" achieved (measurable)
- [ ] Training session booked (or async video watched)
- [ ] Any blockers identified

## Week 2-4

### Expansion of use
- [ ] Week 2 check-in call (15 min, are we on track?)
- [ ] Additional users invited / onboarded
- [ ] Workflows customized for their use case
- [ ] Initial ROI hypothesis tested (what would "working" look like by day 30?)

### Documentation
- [ ] Customer-specific docs created (if complex use case)
- [ ] Access to broader team confirmed

## Month 2

### Mid-point check
- [ ] 30-day business review (30-45 min)
  - What's working
  - What's not
  - Adoption metrics vs. target
  - Expansion opportunities
- [ ] Additional training if needed (new users, deeper features)
- [ ] Connect with CS manager (if different from original contact)

### Relationship building
- [ ] Introduce to another user at similar company (community building)
- [ ] Invite to customer advisory board if applicable
- [ ] Feedback solicited on specific features

## Month 3

### Business review
- [ ] 90-day business review (formal)
  - Did we hit success criteria?
  - ROI achieved (specific numbers)
  - Roadmap alignment (what's next for them)
  - Renewal conversation preview
- [ ] Case study opportunity discussed
- [ ] Expansion conversation (more seats, new modules)

### Handoff to steady-state
- [ ] Onboarding specialist → Success manager handoff (if different)
- [ ] Quarterly review cadence established
- [ ] Support tier confirmed

## Tracking Health

### Signals of GREAT onboarding
- [ ] Usage metrics match target by day 30
- [ ] Champion is actively recruiting other users
- [ ] Expansion conversation from customer side (not yours)
- [ ] NPS of 9-10 at 90 days
- [ ] Unsolicited referral

### Signals of AT-RISK onboarding
- [ ] Usage 50% below target
- [ ] Key user hasn't logged in 2 weeks
- [ ] Champion requesting cancellation or scope reduction
- [ ] No response to 3 outreach attempts
- [ ] NPS below 6

## Escalation Path

### For AT-RISK customers
- [ ] Executive sponsor call (CEO / VP reaches out to their exec)
- [ ] Success plan redesign
- [ ] Root cause analysis (why is adoption failing?)
- [ ] Concrete recovery milestones
- [ ] Temporary discount or credit if bug/blocker is on your side

## Specific Scenarios

### Self-serve / PLG customer
- [ ] Automated welcome sequence (not cold)
- [ ] Milestone emails (behavior-triggered)
- [ ] In-app guidance
- [ ] Human check-in at risk moments (50% of trial, days 3/7/14)

### Enterprise contract >$100K
- [ ] Dedicated CS manager
- [ ] Weekly first month, biweekly month 2, monthly thereafter
- [ ] Quarterly executive business reviews
- [ ] Service SLA monitoring
- [ ] Named support escalation

## Anti-Patterns

- "Self-serve" for customers who need white-glove
- Over-automated onboarding that feels generic
- Not celebrating the first success moment
- Waiting for customer to ask for help (proactive > reactive)
- Skipping the 30-day review when you're busy
