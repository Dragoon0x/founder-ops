# Vendor Selection Checklist

For picking a new software vendor or service provider. Skipping steps
here creates 2-year regret.

## Need Clarity (Before You Start)

- [ ] Problem statement: what specific need are we solving?
- [ ] Is this a new problem or a replacement? (If replacement: what's wrong with current?)
- [ ] Who uses this? (Roles, frequency)
- [ ] What's the budget range? ($X-Y/month or $W-Z/year)
- [ ] What's the make-vs-buy analysis? (Could we build this?)

## Research Phase

- [ ] Shortlist: 3-5 vendors (no more)
- [ ] Source the list from:
  - Peer recommendations (other founders, same-stage)
  - Reddit / HN / G2 reviews
  - Analyst reports (Gartner if relevant)
  - "Best X" SEO articles (lowest signal, use cautiously)

## Vendor Screening (30 min each)

### Product
- [ ] Does it solve the specific problem we have?
- [ ] Does it integrate with our stack?
- [ ] Does it scale to our projected size?
- [ ] What's the UI like? (Actually click through)

### Company
- [ ] How long have they existed? (Under 2 years = risk; over 20 = maybe stagnant)
- [ ] Funding status (well-capitalized = won't disappear)
- [ ] Team size (are they under-resourced for support?)
- [ ] Customer count (brand proof)

### Pricing
- [ ] Published pricing on site (transparent = good signal)
- [ ] "Contact sales" only = add 30% premium to budget
- [ ] Seat-based, usage-based, or flat?
- [ ] Free tier or trial?

## Demo / POC (60-90 min each, top 2-3)

### Questions to ask
- [ ] "Show me doing [SPECIFIC TASK we need]"
- [ ] "What's the onboarding look like?"
- [ ] "Who on your team supports us post-sale?"
- [ ] "Can we talk to 2 reference customers like us?"
- [ ] "What's your roadmap for next 6-12 months?"
- [ ] "What's the weakest part of your product?" (Watch the answer)

### Things they should say unprompted
- [ ] Specific use cases, not just features
- [ ] Customers at your scale / industry
- [ ] How they handle churn / bad experiences

### Things to watch
- [ ] Pushiness (signal of overreach / churn problem)
- [ ] Can't demo the specific use case you asked about
- [ ] Vague roadmap answers
- [ ] Only showing beautiful demos (not real-world messy data)

## Reference Calls (CRITICAL — most skip)

### Who to call
- [ ] Two customers the VENDOR provides (biased positive)
- [ ] One backchannel reference (find via LinkedIn, same stage/industry)

### Questions
- [ ] How long have you used them?
- [ ] What did onboarding feel like?
- [ ] How's their support when things go wrong?
- [ ] What's their weakest area?
- [ ] Would you choose them again?
- [ ] What did you almost choose instead, and why didn't you?

## Security & Compliance (if applicable)

- [ ] SOC 2 report (request + review)
- [ ] Data residency (where is data stored?)
- [ ] Subprocessor list (who do they share with?)
- [ ] Encryption standards (at rest, in transit)
- [ ] Incident response history (ask them)
- [ ] GDPR / HIPAA / other compliance as relevant

## Commercial Terms

### Pricing
- [ ] Annual vs. monthly (annual usually 10-20% cheaper)
- [ ] Multi-year discount (rarely worth it for startups — flexibility > savings)
- [ ] Volume discounts
- [ ] Free tier or dev/sandbox

### Contract
- [ ] Can you terminate for convenience?
- [ ] What notice period?
- [ ] Auto-renewal terms
- [ ] Price lock or escalation

### Startup programs
- [ ] Many vendors have startup discounts (often 50-90% off)
- [ ] Check before signing full price

## Decision Framework

### Scoring (0-10 per dimension)
- Fit to need
- UX / usability
- Integration ease
- Support quality (reference-validated)
- Company health
- Pricing value
- Security posture
- Roadmap confidence

### Tiebreaker questions
- Which vendor would be harder to switch away from later? (That's commitment.)
- Which vendor's people would I actually want to work with?
- Which one has the most honest answer about their weakness?

## Negotiation

### Standard discount levers
- [ ] Annual prepay (10-20% off)
- [ ] Longer term (10-20% off)
- [ ] Volume commitment (10-30% off)
- [ ] Case study rights (5-10% off)
- [ ] End-of-quarter close (5-10% off, use their deadline pressure)

### What not to negotiate
- [ ] Security terms (hurt yourself)
- [ ] Data ownership (keep yours)
- [ ] Support SLA (keep reasonable)

## Post-Decision

- [ ] Contract redlined (see `checklists/contract-review-checklist.md`)
- [ ] Losing vendors told respectfully (you might want them later)
- [ ] Implementation kickoff scheduled
- [ ] Internal champion assigned
- [ ] Success criteria written down BEFORE usage (so you know in 6 months)

## 6-Month Review (on calendar)

- [ ] Are we getting the value we expected?
- [ ] Are we using the features we're paying for?
- [ ] Should we expand, contract, or replace?
- [ ] Peer comparison (has a better vendor emerged?)

## Anti-Patterns

- Picking based on loud marketing, not demo
- Skipping reference calls
- Signing without legal review
- Over-indexing on price (often wrong vendor = 10x cost in time)
- Under-negotiating (you almost always get something)
