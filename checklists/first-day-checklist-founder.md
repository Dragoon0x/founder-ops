# First Day Checklist (Founder's To-Dos on Day 1 of New Hire)

These are the things YOU the founder must personally do — not delegate to HR.

## Night Before

- [ ] Personal welcome message drafted for Slack intro
- [ ] Calendar invite sent for founder 1:1 (end of Day 1, 30-45 min)
- [ ] Equity grant status confirmed (legal/counsel has everything)
- [ ] Public team Slack intro message prepped

## Day 1 Morning

- [ ] First message in Slack (personal, not HR-templated)
- [ ] Physical or video greeting in first hour
- [ ] Ensure their first Slack intro gets replies (seed 3 welcomes from team)

## Day 1 — Founder 1:1 (30-45 min, end of day)

### Questions that matter
- [ ] "What made you pick us over your other options?" (Memorize the answer.)
- [ ] "What's something you've been told about us that I should know?" (Signals what they heard on backchannel)
- [ ] "What do you want to know about the company I might not have shared?"
- [ ] "Anything about today that made you think 'hmm'?" (Create the invitation to flag problems)
- [ ] "What would make this feel like a great fit in 90 days?"

### Share with them
- [ ] The REAL current state of the company (metrics, challenges, wins) — beyond the pitch
- [ ] What keeps you up at night
- [ ] Where you need help from them specifically
- [ ] Direct access: "DM me anytime. No question too small."

### End of 1:1
- [ ] "Anything unclear from me, right now?"
- [ ] "What questions did you not ask?"

## First Week (Founder Touches)

- [ ] Day 2: Casual check-in ("How was yesterday?")
- [ ] Day 3: Intro to one specific person you think they'll click with
- [ ] Day 5: End-of-week lunch or coffee (not about work)
- [ ] Public mention of their first ship ("Great first-week ship from @[name]")

## First Month (Founder Touches)

- [ ] Weekly 1:1 (reduce to biweekly after month 2 if role allows)
- [ ] One "skip-level" moment (if they're not direct report) — inviting them to a strategic meeting
- [ ] Thank them specifically for one thing they did differently than predecessor

## Things to NEVER Do

- [ ] Don't miss the Day 1 greeting (signals you don't care)
- [ ] Don't skip the founder 1:1 (however busy you are)
- [ ] Don't overshare drama (they need to feel they joined a steady ship)
- [ ] Don't use first day to dump work on them
- [ ] Don't ask them to fix inherited problems in week 1

## The Sophisticated Signal

The quality of Day 1 predicts 6-month retention. Investment here has outsized ROI.
