# Negotiation: Partnership Agreement

## Preparation
- Define what you want from this partnership (revenue, distribution, credibility, technology)
- Define what they want (same dimensions)
- Understand power dynamics: who needs whom more?
- Research their other partnerships: what terms do they typically offer?

## Key Terms

### Revenue sharing
| Model | Typical Split | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Referral fee | 10-20% of first year | Lightweight, intro-based partnerships |
| Revenue share | 20-40% ongoing | Deep integrations, co-selling |
| Reseller | 30-50% discount on your list price | Distribution partnerships |
| Co-development | Custom | Joint products |

### Integration responsibilities
- Who builds the integration? (Usually the smaller partner)
- Who maintains it? (Maintenance costs more than building)
- Who pays for the maintenance? (Usually each side covers their own)
- What happens if one side breaks the integration with an update?

### Marketing commitments
- Co-branded content: agree on quantity (2-4 pieces per quarter) and type (blog posts, webinars, case studies)
- Launch announcement: joint press release or blog post
- Event participation: booth sharing, speaking slots, joint demos

### Exclusivity
- Avoid exclusivity unless the partner offers guarantees (minimum revenue, minimum leads)
- If you grant exclusivity: time-bound (12 months max) with performance minimums
- Non-exclusive with "preferred partner" status is usually the sweet spot

### Term & Termination
- 12-month initial term, renewable annually
- 60-90 day termination notice
- Wind-down provisions: what happens to shared customers?
- Data: each side retains their own data. Shared data is deleted within 30 days of termination.

## Tactics
1. Start with a pilot: 90-day trial before committing to a full partnership
2. Define success metrics upfront: what would make this partnership worth continuing?
3. Identify your champion at the partner organization (the person who will fight for the partnership internally)
4. Document everything: partnership terms in writing, not handshakes
5. Review quarterly: is this partnership delivering value for both sides?
