# Negotiation: Salary (from Founder's Side)

## Preparation
- Know your compensation bands (see library/compensation-bands.md)
- Know the candidate's current comp and alternatives
- Know your budget ceiling for this role
- Decide in advance: what's your walk-away? What's your stretch?

## Framework

### Cash compensation
- Offer within your band. If the candidate is above your band, either adjust the band (if justified) or pass.
- Performance-based raises at 6 and 12 months are a good way to bridge a gap
- Don't negotiate against yourself. Make one offer, explain the reasoning, and ask for a response.

### Equity
- Early stage (< 10 employees): 0.5-2.0% for key hires, 0.1-0.5% for other roles
- Seed stage (10-30 employees): 0.1-0.5% for key hires, 0.05-0.25% for other roles
- Series A (30-100 employees): 0.05-0.25% for key hires, 0.01-0.1% for other roles
- Always: 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff

### Signing bonus
Use when:
- Candidate is forfeiting a bonus at their current company
- Candidate has competing offers with higher base (bridge the gap with one-time payment)
- Size: typically 5-15% of annual base salary

## Tactics
1. **Anchor with the full package**: Present total comp (salary + equity value + benefits), not just base salary
2. **Sell the upside**: Equity is worth $X today but if the company hits $Y ARR, it could be worth $Z
3. **Be transparent**: "Here's our band for this role. Here's where you fall in it and why."
4. **Give them time**: 48-72 hours to decide. Pressure tactics backfire.
5. **Ask what matters**: "What would make this a clear yes?" Sometimes it's flexibility, not money.

## Common Mistakes
- Lowballing to save money (you'll lose the candidate or get a resentful employee)
- Negotiating equity on grant size without explaining strike price and preference stack
- Not having comp bands (ad hoc negotiation creates pay inequity)
- Over-investing in one hire at the expense of team balance
