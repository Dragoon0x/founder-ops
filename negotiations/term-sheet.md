# Negotiation: Term Sheet

## Preparation
Before negotiating, know:
- Your BATNA (Best Alternative): Do you have other term sheets? Can you grow without raising?
- Your walkaway: What's the worst deal you'd accept?
- Market data: Current multiples for your stage, sector, and metrics
- Your leverage: How much do they want this deal?

## Key Terms to Negotiate

### Valuation
- Pre-money vs post-money (make sure you're talking about the same thing)
- Impact on dilution: At $10M pre on a $3M raise, founders dilute by 23%. At $15M pre, 17%.
- Don't over-optimize: A $12M vs $15M pre-money difference matters less than getting the right partner

### Board composition
- Seed: 2 founders + 1 investor (or no board at all)
- Series A: 2 founders + 1 investor + 1 independent (or 2+1)
- Never give up board control before Series B unless the investor is exceptional
- Independent directors should be mutually agreed, not investor-appointed

### Liquidation preference
- 1x non-participating preferred is standard and fair
- Participating preferred (double-dip) is investor-friendly. Push back.
- > 1x liquidation preference is a red flag (means investors get 2x+ before you see anything)

### Anti-dilution
- Broad-based weighted average is standard and fair
- Full ratchet is investor-friendly and punitive. Push back hard.

### Protective provisions
- Standard: investor approval for new equity issuance, debt, M&A, charter changes
- Non-standard: investor approval for hiring, spending, strategy changes (too much control)
- Push back on anything that requires investor consent for day-to-day operations

### Pro-rata rights
- Standard: investors can participate in future rounds to maintain their ownership %
- Usually fine to grant. Signals investor commitment.

## Tactics
1. **Create competition**: Having 2+ term sheets is the single best negotiating lever
2. **Focus on what matters**: Valuation gets attention, but governance terms matter more long-term
3. **Know your lawyer's style**: Some lawyers fight every point. This burns goodwill. Pick battles.
4. **Speed matters**: Negotiate quickly. Drawn-out negotiations cool investor enthusiasm.
5. **Ask for references**: Talk to 3+ founders the investor has backed. Ask about behavior during hard times.

## Common Traps
- Exploding offers (24-48 hr deadlines): Push back. Serious investors give you a week.
- "We always do it this way": Every term is negotiable. Don't accept "standard" without verifying.
- Side letters: Watch for terms added in side agreements that conflict with the main docs.
