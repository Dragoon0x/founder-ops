# Negotiation: Vendor Contract

## Preparation
- Know exactly what you need (must-haves vs nice-to-haves)
- Research the vendor's pricing model and competitors
- Understand your leverage: are you a small customer or a potential case study?
- Know the vendor's fiscal year end (best discounts in their Q4)

## Key Terms

### Pricing
- Always ask for 15-25% discount on list price (they expect it)
- Annual prepay typically gets 10-20% discount
- Multi-year gets 20-40% (but risky for startups; negotiate exit clause)
- Ask about startup programs (many vendors offer discounted rates for early-stage)

### Contract length
- Prefer annual with opt-out over multi-year
- If multi-year: negotiate a termination-for-convenience clause (60-90 day notice)
- Auto-renewal: require 60-day notice window and cap price increases at 5-7%

### SLA
- Define uptime expectations (99.9% is standard for business-critical tools)
- Include financial penalties for SLA breaches (credits, not just "we'll try harder")
- Specify support response times by severity level

### Data
- Data portability: you can export your data at any time, in a standard format
- Data deletion: vendor must delete your data within 30 days of contract termination
- Data processing: if vendor processes customer data, require DPA (Data Processing Agreement)

## Negotiation Tactics
1. Get quotes from 2-3 competitors before negotiating
2. Start negotiating at the end of the vendor's quarter (they have quotas to hit)
3. Ask: "What's the best you can do?" before making a counter-offer
4. Bundle: negotiate multiple tools from the same vendor for a package discount
5. Reference: offer to be a case study in exchange for better pricing

## Walkaway signals
- Vendor won't negotiate on any terms (inflexible = bad partner)
- No data portability clause (you're locked in)
- Auto-renewal with no notice period (hostage situation)
- Price increase uncapped (they can raise 50% at renewal)
