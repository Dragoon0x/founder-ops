# Anti-Pattern: Treating Fundraising as a Milestone

## What It Looks Like
The company celebrates raising a round as if it's an achievement. TechCrunch announcement, team dinner, internal messaging all focus on the raise. The actual business milestones (PMF, revenue targets, customer wins) get less attention.

## Why It Happens
- External validation feels good
- Media reinforces it (funding announcements get press coverage)
- Investors encourage it (good for their portfolio signaling)
- It's a concrete event in an otherwise ambiguous journey
- Comparison with peers who announce raises

## Detection Signals
- Company OKRs include "raise Series A" as an objective
- Post-fundraise energy drops (the "goal" was achieved)
- Spending increases immediately after close without clear ROI plan
- Team equates company value with valuation
- Founder spends > 25% of time on investor relations outside active fundraise

## The Reality
Fundraising is not a milestone. It's taking on a liability (dilution + expectations).
The money is fuel, not the destination. The only real milestones are:
- Customers pay for your product and keep paying
- More customers want your product than you can serve
- Your unit economics work
- Your team can execute without the founder in every meeting

## Recovery Playbook
1. **Reframe internally**: "We raised $X to accomplish [specific business goals]. The raise is step 0. Let's talk about steps 1-10."
2. **Tie spend to milestones**: Every dollar of the raise is allocated to specific outcomes, not vague "growth"
3. **Celebrate customers, not capital**: Make customer wins the thing that gets announced at all-hands
4. **Board meetings focus on business, not fundraise**: Reserve < 5 min of board meetings for fundraise-related updates (unless actively raising)
