# Anti-Pattern: Premature Scaling

## What It Looks Like
Hiring aggressively, spending heavily on marketing, and building enterprise features before you've nailed product-market fit. You're building a machine to scale something that doesn't work yet.

## Why It Happens
- Fundraise pressure: "We raised $5M, we need to grow fast"
- Ego: headcount feels like progress
- Fear of competition: "If we don't move fast, someone else will"
- Misreading early traction as PMF (10 customers saying "this is cool" is not PMF)

## Detection Signals
- Burn rate increasing faster than revenue
- Hiring plan based on competitor headcount, not your needs
- Sales team exceeding 30% of company before $500K ARR
- Customer acquisition cost rising month over month
- Lots of "we'll grow into this" reasoning

## The Numbers
Startup Genome Project data: premature scaling is the #1 cause of startup death. Companies that scale prematurely are 3x more likely to fail. They spend 2-3x more on customer acquisition and build 3.5x more features than their peers.

## Recovery Playbook
1. **Freeze hiring** immediately (exceptions only for critical gaps)
2. **Audit the team**: who is essential to the next 6 months of learning?
3. **Reduce burn to 18+ months runway**
4. **Redefine PMF criteria**: what specific metric would prove PMF? (retention curve flattening, NPS > 40, organic growth > 30% of new customers)
5. **Focus all energy on that metric** for 90 days
6. **Only resume scaling when the metric is hit** for 2 consecutive months
