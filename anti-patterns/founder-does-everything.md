# Anti-Pattern: Founder Does Everything

## What It Looks Like
The founder is the top sales rep, the product manager, the support team, and the office manager. Nobody else can make decisions. Everything bottlenecks through one person.

## Why It Happens
- "Nobody else can do it as well as I can" (true at first, becomes false fast)
- Trust deficit: hasn't hired people they trust
- Identity: founder's self-worth tied to being needed
- Cost anxiety: "We can't afford to hire for this yet"

## Detection Signals
- Founder working > 70 hours/week consistently
- Decisions delayed because founder hasn't reviewed them
- Team members say "waiting on [founder]" in standups
- Founder context-switching between > 5 functions daily
- No one else in the company can handle customer calls alone

## The Cost
A founder doing $50/hr tasks is not saving $50/hr. They're losing the value of the $500/hr strategic work they're not doing. Every hour spent on operational tasks is an hour not spent on fundraising, recruiting, product vision, or customer relationships.

## Recovery Playbook
1. **Time audit**: Track every hour for 1 week. Categorize: only-I-can-do-this vs someone-else-could-do-this
2. **Delegate the bottom 50%**: Hire, promote, or outsource the tasks that don't require founder judgment
3. **Decision framework**: Write down which decisions you must make, which you want to be consulted on, and which others can make autonomously (RACI or RAPID)
4. **First hire should be your weakness**: If you're a product founder, hire a business generalist. If you're a sales founder, hire a technical lead.
5. **90-day handoff plan**: For each function you're delegating, spend 30 days doing it together, 30 days supervising, 30 days fully letting go

## The Test
Can you take a 1-week vacation without the company stalling? If no, you haven't delegated enough.
