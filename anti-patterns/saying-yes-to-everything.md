# Anti-Pattern: Saying Yes to Everything

## What It Looks Like
Every customer request becomes a roadmap item. Every partnership inquiry becomes a project. Every investor intro becomes a meeting. The founder can't say no because everything seems like an opportunity.

## Why It Happens
- Scarcity mindset: "What if this is the one big opportunity we miss?"
- People-pleasing: founder avoids disappointing others
- FOMO: seeing competitors pursue multiple opportunities
- Inability to distinguish signal from noise

## Detection Signals
- Roadmap has > 20 items in "planned" status
- Founder's calendar is 80%+ booked with external meetings
- Team is working on > 5 initiatives simultaneously
- The word "maybe" appears more than "no" in founder communications
- Customer requests are added to roadmap without evaluation

## The Cost
Each "yes" is a "no" to something else. With limited resources, every commitment dilutes focus on the most important work.

A team of 10 working on 3 things will outperform a team of 10 working on 10 things. Every time. Focus is not about doing less. It's about doing the right things well.

## Recovery Playbook
1. **Default to no**: Every new request starts as a "no" unless it passes a specific bar: "Does this directly advance our top 3 priorities this quarter?"
2. **The hell-yes test**: If it's not a "hell yes," it's a no. Lukewarm enthusiasm for an opportunity means it's not worth the opportunity cost.
3. **Calendar audit**: Block 40% of the founder's calendar for deep work. Decline meetings that don't have a clear agenda and expected outcome.
4. **Saying no gracefully**: "Thank you for thinking of us. This isn't a fit right now, but let's reconnect in [timeframe] when [specific condition changes]."
5. **Strategic filter**: Create a one-page strategy doc. Every opportunity is evaluated against it. If it doesn't fit, it's a no regardless of how good it sounds.
