# Anti-Pattern: Consensus Culture

## What It Looks Like
Every decision requires buy-in from everyone. Meetings are for alignment, not decisions. "Let's get everyone's input" is the default. Decisions take weeks because someone always has concerns.

## Why It Happens
- Conflict avoidance: founder dislikes saying no to teammates
- Democratic ideal: "Everyone should have a voice"
- Fear of being wrong: if everyone agrees, the blame is shared
- Confusing alignment with agreement

## Detection Signals
- Average decision takes > 5 business days for non-critical items
- Meetings end with "let's think about it more" instead of a decision
- Team members veto decisions outside their domain
- The phrase "I'm not comfortable with this" blocks progress without a specific objection
- Reversible decisions get the same process as irreversible ones

## The Cost
Speed is the startup's primary advantage over incumbents. Consensus culture destroys that advantage. A mediocre decision made quickly and iterated on beats a perfect decision made slowly.

## Recovery Playbook
1. **Classify decisions**: Type 1 (irreversible, high-stakes) vs Type 2 (reversible, low-stakes). Type 2 decisions should be made by the person closest to the problem, within 24 hours.
2. **RAPID framework**: For each decision, assign: Recommend, Agree (has veto), Perform, Input (consulted), Decide (one person)
3. **Disagree and commit**: Once the decision-maker decides, everyone commits. Disagreement is expressed before the decision, not after.
4. **Time-box decisions**: "We'll decide by Friday. If no objections by then, we proceed with option A."
5. **Model it from the top**: Founders make visible, fast decisions and publicly own the outcomes, good or bad.
