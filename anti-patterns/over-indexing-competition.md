# Anti-Pattern: Over-Indexing on Competition

## What It Looks Like
The roadmap is a response to competitor feature launches. Every competitor announcement triggers a scramble. The team knows more about competitors' products than their own customers' problems.

## Why It Happens
- Fear: "They're going to win our customers"
- Comparison bias: easier to see what competitors have than what customers need
- Sales team pressure: "We keep losing deals because we don't have [competitor feature]"
- Public competitor launches are visible; customer insights require work

## Detection Signals
- Roadmap items justify themselves with "Competitor X has this"
- Win/loss analysis focuses on feature gaps, not buyer journey
- > 50% of all-hands Q&A is about competitors
- Team checks competitor websites/social more than customer data
- "Feature parity" is a stated goal

## The Reality
You will never achieve feature parity with all competitors. And customers don't buy checklists. They buy solutions to their specific problem, delivered in a way that fits their workflow.

The question is never "do we have this feature?" It's "do we solve this problem better for our target customer?"

## Recovery Playbook
1. **Customer-first roadmap**: Every roadmap item must map to a customer problem validated by research (interviews, data, support tickets), not competitor features
2. **Win/loss discipline**: Analyze wins and losses based on full buying journey. Usually you lose on trust, timing, or price, not features.
3. **Competitive awareness, not obsession**: Check competitors bi-weekly (see competitive-scan workflow), not daily
4. **Differentiation over parity**: Double down on what makes you different, not what makes you the same
5. **Build for your best customers**: Your happiest, most successful customers chose you for a reason. Build more of what they love, not what your weakest customers wish you had.
