# Anti-Pattern: Ignoring Churn

## What It Looks Like
All energy goes to acquiring new customers. Nobody tracks why customers leave. The response to churn is "we'll make it up with new logos." Revenue grows but the bucket has holes.

## Why It Happens
- New customers are exciting; churn is depressing
- Sales is celebrated; retention is invisible
- Churn data is hard to collect (exit surveys go unanswered)
- Early-stage companies don't feel churn yet (small base)
- "We'll fix retention after we have more customers"

## Detection Signals
- No one can answer "why do customers leave?" with data
- Churn rate not tracked or not reported to leadership
- No exit interview process
- Customer success team focused on onboarding only, not retention
- NPS or CSAT not measured regularly
- Net revenue retention not calculated

## The Math
At 5% monthly churn, you lose 46% of customers annually.
To grow 50% net, you need to acquire 96% new customers relative to your base every year.
That's a treadmill. And it speeds up every quarter.

At 2% monthly churn, you lose 21% annually.
To grow 50% net, you need 71% new. Still hard, but survivable.

The difference between 5% and 2% monthly churn is the difference between a flywheel and a treadmill.

## Recovery Playbook
1. **Measure it**: Track logo churn and revenue churn separately. Segment by customer size, cohort, and acquisition channel.
2. **Exit interviews**: Contact every churned customer within 48 hours. 5-minute call or 3-question survey. Categorize reasons.
3. **Churn autopsy**: Monthly review of all churn. Identify the top 3 reasons. Make one of them the product team's priority.
4. **Health scoring**: Build a customer health score based on: login frequency, feature adoption, support ticket trends, NPS responses, contract renewal date
5. **Proactive outreach**: When health score drops below threshold, CS team reaches out before the customer cancels
6. **Fix onboarding first**: 80% of churn is decided in the first 30 days. If your onboarding is broken, nothing else matters.
