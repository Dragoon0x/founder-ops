# Bottom-Up TAM Model

## Why Bottom-Up
Top-down TAM ("the market is $50B") is meaningless for startups. You won't capture 1% of a $50B market.
Bottom-up TAM starts with your actual customer and works up. Investors trust this more and it's actually useful for planning.

## Structure

### Step 1: Define your ICP precisely
Not "businesses" or "companies." Specifically:
- Industry: [e.g., B2B SaaS companies]
- Size: [e.g., 50-500 employees]
- Geography: [e.g., US and UK]
- Trigger: [e.g., using Salesforce, has > 5 sales reps]

### Step 2: Count addressable companies
Sources for company counts:
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator (filter by industry, size, geography)
- Industry association databases
- Census data / Bureau of Labor Statistics
- Analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester)

Example:
- B2B SaaS companies in US with 50-500 employees: ~12,000
- Of those, using Salesforce: ~7,200 (60%)
- Of those, with > 5 sales reps: ~4,300 (60%)
- **Addressable companies: 4,300**

### Step 3: Calculate ACV potential
- Average contract value for this segment: $15,000/year
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): 4,300 × $15,000 = $64.5M**

### Step 4: Apply penetration rate
Realistic market penetration over 5 years:
- Year 1: 0.5-1% → $322K-$645K
- Year 3: 3-5% → $1.9M-$3.2M
- Year 5: 8-15% → $5.2M-$9.7M

### Step 4: Expansion opportunity
- Adjacent segments (different company sizes, geographies)
- Additional products (upsell to existing TAM)
- Price increases (ARPA growth over time)

```
TAM = SAM × Expansion multiplier (typically 3-10x)
TAM = $64.5M × 5x = $322.5M
```

## Presentation Format
Lead with SAM (what you can actually capture), then show TAM for growth narrative.
Investors want to see: "Here are 4,300 companies we can sell to today at $15K each. Here's how we expand from there."

## Common Mistakes
1. Using top-down market size reports as your TAM
2. Not validating company counts with actual data
3. Assuming 100% addressable (not everyone needs your product)
4. Double-counting across segments
5. Not discounting for competitors already serving the market
