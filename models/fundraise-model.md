# Fundraising Amount Model

## How Much to Raise

### Formula
```
Raise Amount = Monthly Burn × Months to Next Milestone × Safety Buffer

Where:
- Monthly Burn = Current (or planned post-raise) monthly expenses
- Months to Next Milestone = time to hit the metric that unlocks the next round
- Safety Buffer = 1.5x (always plan for things taking 50% longer than expected)
```

### Milestones by Round
| Round | Target Milestone | Typical Months | Typical Raise |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Pre-seed | MVP + first 10 customers | 12-18 | $500K-$2M |
| Seed | PMF signals + $50-100K MRR | 18-24 | $2-5M |
| Series A | Clear PMF + $100-300K MRR + repeatable GTM | 18-24 | $8-20M |
| Series B | $1-3M MRR + scalable GTM + strong unit economics | 18-24 | $20-50M |

### Example: Seed Round
- Current burn: $40K/month
- Post-raise burn (with new hires): $80K/month
- Milestone: $100K MRR (currently $15K MRR)
- Estimated time: 18 months
- Required: $80K × 18 × 1.5 = $2.16M
- Round: raise $2.5M (round up for negotiation room)

### Dilution Planning
| Round | Typical Dilution | Post-Round Founder Ownership |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| Pre-seed | 10-15% | 85-90% |
| Seed | 15-25% | 65-75% |
| Series A | 15-25% | 50-60% |
| Series B | 15-20% | 40-50% |
| Series C+ | 10-15% | 35-45% |

### Common Mistakes
1. Raising too little (runway < 18 months = you're always fundraising)
2. Raising too much (excessive dilution, pressure to spend, high valuation expectations for next round)
3. Not accounting for slower-than-expected progress (the buffer matters)
4. Planning burn based on ideal hiring timeline (every hire takes longer than expected)
5. Not modeling the cap table impact (know your dilution before you agree to valuation)
