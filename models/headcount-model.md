# Headcount Planning Model

## Planning Framework

### Revenue per Employee
| Stage | Target Revenue/Employee | Notes |
|-------|----------------------|-------|
| Seed (< $1M ARR) | $50-100K | Low revenue, building team |
| Series A ($1-5M ARR) | $100-200K | Scaling, but still investing |
| Series B ($5-20M ARR) | $150-250K | Efficiency expectations increase |
| Series C+ ($20M+ ARR) | $200-350K | Approaching public-company efficiency |

### Department Mix by Stage
| Department | Seed (10 people) | Series A (30) | Series B (80) | Series C (150+) |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Engineering | 50-60% | 40-50% | 35-45% | 30-40% |
| Sales & Marketing | 10-20% | 20-30% | 25-35% | 30-40% |
| Customer Success | 5-10% | 10-15% | 10-15% | 10-15% |
| G&A | 10-15% | 10-15% | 10-15% | 10-15% |
| Product & Design | 10-15% | 10-15% | 8-12% | 8-12% |

### Hiring Cadence Rules
1. Hire in pairs: every new function needs at least 2 people (one person can't cover vacations, sick days, or quit without disruption)
2. Hire managers when span of control exceeds 7 (one manager per 5-7 ICs is optimal)
3. Hire ahead of revenue for engineering (12 months lead time) but in line with revenue for sales (3-6 months)
4. Every hire should be justified with: what problem this solves, what happens if we don't hire, and when we'll know if this hire is working

### Cost Model Per Hire
| Component | IC | Senior IC | Manager | Director | VP |
|-----------|-----|-----------|---------|----------|-----|
| Base salary | $80-150K | $120-200K | $150-250K | $200-300K | $250-400K |
| Benefits (% of base) | +25-35% | +25-35% | +25-35% | +25-35% | +25-35% |
| Equity (annual value) | $10-30K | $20-50K | $30-80K | $50-150K | $100-300K |
| Recruiting cost | $5-15K | $10-20K | $15-30K | $20-50K | $50-100K |
| Equipment | $2-5K | $2-5K | $2-5K | $2-5K | $2-5K |
| Ramp time | 2-3 months | 2-3 months | 3-4 months | 3-6 months | 3-6 months |

### Common Mistakes
1. Hiring for headcount targets instead of outcomes
2. Not accounting for ramp time (a hire made in Q1 isn't productive until Q2-Q3)
3. Hiring executives too early (VP of Sales at $200K ARR is a waste)
4. Not budgeting for recruiting costs (agencies charge 20-25% of first-year salary)
5. Assuming all hires work out (plan for 15-20% of hires not working within 12 months)
