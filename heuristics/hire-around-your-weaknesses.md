# Hire around your weaknesses, not your strengths

> The most common hiring mistake: founders hire mini-versions of themselves.

## The principle

- Product-founder hires a product-oriented VP Eng. Eng-founder hires an eng-oriented PM. Both leave gaps.
- The best early team covers what the founder cannot and will not do.
- If you love selling, you don't need a VP Sales yet. You need operations, finance, or PM.
- If you hate selling and it's a sales-led business, your first serious exec hire is probably a VP Sales.

## Signals it's time to act

- Be ruthlessly honest about your weaknesses. Ask 3 people who've worked with you to list them.
- Then hire for those.

## Notes

- A team where everyone has the same strengths is brittle. One where people's weaknesses are covered is durable.
