# No surprises

> The #1 rule with boards, investors, and co-founders: they should never be surprised by bad news.

## The principle

- If the metric is going to miss, tell them 4 weeks before the report.
- If a key person is leaving, tell them the day you know.
- If a deal you've been talking up is falling apart, tell them before they ask.
- Surprise costs trust 10x faster than bad news does.

## Signals it's time to act

- The corollary: front-load bad news in every communication. Don't bury it in paragraph 4.
- 'Bad news first' is the fastest way to earn the trust to deliver bad news again.

## Notes

- A board that learns you hide bad news will never trust your good news either.
