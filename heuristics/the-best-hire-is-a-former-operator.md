# The best first exec hire is a former operator at your stage

> Your first VP hire should have scaled a team at the stage you're in now, not the stage you hope to be.

## The principle

- VPs who scaled from $100M to $500M are often bad fits for a $2M company. Different muscle.
- VPs who've taken a team from 5 to 20 at a venture-backed startup are usually the right fit for your 5-person function.
- Exception: if you're raising aggressively and targeting hyper-growth, you may need someone one stage ahead.

## Signals it's time to act

- Signal: has this person done a thing roughly the size of my current thing before? Not twice as big. Roughly the size.
- Ask in interviews: 'What did your first 90 days at a previous company at my stage look like?'

## Notes

- A $500M-experienced VP at a $5M company usually adds process nobody needs and gets frustrated within 6 months.
