# You will be wrong, publicly, often

> Making decisions with bad information is the job. Getting them wrong is normal. Hiding from being wrong is fatal.

## The principle

- Announce you were wrong when you realize it, not 2 months later.
- Admit what specifically was wrong and what you changed.
- Then decide: is the original direction salvageable, or do we change?
- The fastest-growing companies iterate their own wrongness fastest.

## Signals it's time to act

- The failure mode is the founder who doubles down on a bad bet because they can't admit the initial call.
- The other failure mode is the founder who flip-flops with every new piece of data.

## Notes

- The right cadence: commit to a direction for a quarter. Review every month. Change course when the evidence is strong, not when it's suggestive.
