# The meeting before the meeting

> Any meeting where a decision gets made should be pre-wired. If you're discovering opinions in the room, you're wasting everyone's time.

## The principle

- Before the board meeting: 1:1 with each board member. Preview the plan. Surface objections. Adjust.
- Before the all-hands: preview the big message with 2-3 trusted ICs. See what lands and what confuses.
- Before a customer negotiation: pre-align with your champion on what they need internally.
- Before a pricing change: pre-test with 3 sympathetic customers.

## Signals it's time to act

- Pre-wiring doesn't mean manipulation. It means making sure the room can have a real conversation about the real decision, not spend 30 minutes on clarifying questions.

## Notes

- If you find yourself saying 'wait, I didn't know' in any decision meeting, someone (often you) failed to pre-wire.
