# Opinions before evidence are expensive

> Founders often have strong opinions before they have data. It's energizing. It's also dangerous.

## The principle

- Treat your opinion as a hypothesis to be tested, not a truth to be executed.
- The 3-question test before committing to an opinion-driven bet:
- 1. What would have to be true for this to be wrong?
- 2. What's the cheapest experiment to test that?
- 3. What's the deadline to have an answer?

## Signals it's time to act

- Opinions become evidence when: we interviewed 20 customers, we ran a 4-week pilot, we saw the cohort behavior, we talked to 3 experts.
- Opinions stay opinions when: 'I just feel it.'

## Notes

- Strong opinions loosely held, not strong opinions permanently installed.
