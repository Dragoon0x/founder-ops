# One throat to choke

> Every important thing needs a single owner. Not a committee. Not a 'steering group.' One person.

## The principle

- If two people own a thing, nobody owns it.
- The DRI (directly responsible individual) owns the outcome, not the activity.
- A DRI can (and should) delegate tasks. They cannot delegate ownership.
- If you find yourself saying 'we decided' about a mistake, there was no DRI.

## Signals it's time to act

- Write the DRI next to every metric, every quarterly goal, every open role.
- At the monthly review, the DRI presents — not the team — so everyone sees who's accountable.

## Notes

- If a DRI leaves, decide in the first week who the new DRI is. Not 'acting DRI.' The new one.
