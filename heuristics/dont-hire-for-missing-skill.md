# Don't hire for a skill you don't have

> The worst executive hires happen when a founder hires someone to do a job the founder has never done even roughly.

## The principle

- If you've never done sales, your first VP Sales hire will almost certainly fail. You can't evaluate them and you can't coach them.
- Do the function yourself, badly, for 3-6 months first. You'll learn what to look for.
- If you truly cannot do it yourself (you're non-technical and need a CTO), hire a fractional first, build a relationship, and let them help you hire.

## Signals it's time to act

- This applies especially to: VP Sales, VP Marketing, VP Eng, Head of Product, Head of Finance.
- It applies less to: controllers, recruiters, designers, individual ICs in a craft you don't practice.

## Notes

- The counter-pattern is: hire a great advisor before you hire the exec. Pay them $5K/mo for 4 months. Learn enough to hire well.
