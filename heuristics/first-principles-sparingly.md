# First principles sparingly

> First-principles thinking is expensive. Use it for things that actually matter. Everything else: copy what works.

## The principle

- Things worth first-principles thinking: your ICP, your pricing model, your hiring bar, your go-to-market motion.
- Things NOT worth first-principles thinking: your 401(k) plan, your expense policy, your engineering stack defaults, your meeting structure.
- If there's a well-known best practice for X, use it. Save your rebel energy for where it wins.

## Signals it's time to act

- Most famous 'first principles' stories are survivor bias. For every Elon Musk you hear about, 50 others burned time reinventing something they should have borrowed.

## Notes

- The test: if you can't name two concrete ways your version is better than the default, you're probably just procrastinating.
