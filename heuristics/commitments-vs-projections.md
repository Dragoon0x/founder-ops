# Commitments vs. projections

> A commitment is what you bet your job on. A projection is a best guess. Founders often conflate them. Investors hate it. Teams hate it more.

## The principle

- **Commitment:** 'Q4 net new ARR will be $1.2M.' If we miss, I come to the board with a specific explanation and a revised plan.
- **Projection:** 'Q4 net new ARR is forecasted at $1.2M.' If we miss, we update the model.
- Commit to fewer things. 3-5 commitments per quarter, max. The rest is projection.
- When you commit, write down what you'll do if you miss. Pre-committing the response is part of the commitment.

## Signals it's time to act

- Example commitment: 'Ship SOC 2 by March 1. If we slip, VP Eng and I will present a recovery plan at the March board meeting.'
- Example projection: 'We expect $2M in Q1 ARR.'

## Notes

- Commitments are how trust compounds. Break three and you'll spend 2 years earning it back.
