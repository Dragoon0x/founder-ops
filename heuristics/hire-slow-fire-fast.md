# Heuristic: Hire Slow, Fire Fast

## The rule
Take more time to hire than feels comfortable. Act on the
decision to let someone go faster than feels comfortable.

## Why it works

### Hiring slow
- Every hire is a multi-hundred-thousand-dollar, multi-year
  commitment.
- The cost of a wrong hire compounds: their work has to be redone,
  their team culture shifts, their replacement takes 3–6 months.
- "I'll know in 30 days" is usually false. You'll rationalize for 6
  months instead.

### Firing fast
- You almost always know sooner than you act.
- Everyone else also knows. Inaction damages your credibility.
- The person often feels the misfit too. A clear conversation is
  kinder than slow drift.
- The cost of keeping a bad hire is ~3x their salary (opportunity
  cost, team drag, customer impact).

## When the rule applies
- Any full-time hire
- Especially: first hire in a function, any executive, any senior IC
- Any "culture decides" moment

## When the rule breaks
- **Hire slow breaks** when you're in genuine urgency (critical role,
  funding dependent on hire) AND you have 2+ qualified candidates and
  high confidence in at least one. Then speed matters.
- **Fire fast breaks** when you're in a short-term sprint where
  replacement isn't possible (e.g., CTO in the middle of a major
  migration). Even then: plan the transition, don't extend their role.

## Operational version

### Hire slow
- 4–6 weeks from first conversation to offer is normal.
- Don't skip reference checks, even for "obvious" hires.
- Never hire after one interview.
- Have at least one dissenting opinion in the debrief.

### Fire fast
- Once you've made the decision, execute within 2 weeks.
- Don't build a paper trail for weeks unless legally required.
- Pay severance if possible, even if not required. It makes the
  conversation cleaner.
- Don't cram feedback into a PIP that's actually "we've decided."
  That's dishonest.

## The version that works at scale

At 50+ employees:
- **Hire slow** requires structured interview processes, scorecards,
  debrief templates. Without these, speed creeps in.
- **Fire fast** requires manager training. Most managers don't fire
  fast because they don't know how. Train them.

## Common misapplications

1. **Hiring slow ≠ delay.** Slow means thorough, not undecided. Decide
   quickly between candidates once you have them.
2. **Firing fast ≠ recklessness.** Do it cleanly, with severance, with
   dignity. Fast means decisive, not brutal.
3. **"We hire slow" is sometimes an excuse for not hiring.** If you
   have an open role for 6 months and no hires, that's not
   thoughtfulness, that's broken process.

## Exceptions

- Re-hiring someone you've worked with for years: faster is fine.
- Firing for cause (ethics, harassment, theft): immediate, no process.
- Startup at < 5 people: firing takes a different approach (direct
  conversation, equity return, possible consulting transition).
