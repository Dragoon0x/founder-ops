# Say no to your best customer

> The biggest customer's idea will look like a crown jewel and act like a scope bomb. Learn to say no gracefully.

## The principle

- A 'yes' to your biggest customer's pet feature often becomes: 8 weeks of engineering, 2 weeks of integration, and a one-off feature that no other customer uses.
- Instead of yes/no, offer: 'We'll add this to the roadmap. Here's the 3-quarter picture of what we're prioritizing. Where does this fit?'
- Or: 'We'll build it as a paid service engagement. $75K, 6 weeks, we own it.'
- Or: 'We'll give you the API. Build it yourself and we'll promote it.'

## Signals it's time to act

- Never bluff the roadmap to keep a customer. They'll remember exactly what you promised.
- Never build a feature just for one customer without pricing the work separately.

## Notes

- 'No, because...' is much more trustworthy than a slow yes.
