# Your moat is customer love, not features

> Features get copied. Prices get cut. Love is hard to replicate.

## The principle

- The customers who'd be upset if you disappeared are your real moat.
- Count them every quarter. If the number isn't growing, you have a problem even if revenue is.
- Love is built by: responsiveness, honesty, sweating details, remembering context, fixing things fast.
- Love is destroyed by: delays, missed promises, ignoring complaints, 'we'll add it to the roadmap,' automated responses.

## Signals it's time to act

- Every quarter, ask your top 20 customers: 'If we disappeared tomorrow, what would you use instead?' If the answer is 'nothing good' or 'I'd be really upset,' you're building love. If the answer is 'a competitor' or 'we'd manage,' you're not.

## Notes

- Early on, a founder who personally answers every support ticket for 2 years builds a company that customers fight to keep using.
