# Hire slow, fire fast — the nuance

> The slogan is famous. The nuance is missed. Slow means thorough, not dithering. Fast means clear, not cruel.

## The principle

- **Slow** means run a full process: sourcing, screening, 2+ technical signals, 3+ behavioral signals, backchannel, reference. 4–6 weeks.
- **Slow** does NOT mean 12 weeks of indecision after a strong finalist. That's cowardice.
- **Fast** means: once you know, act within 2 weeks. Document the gaps, give the person a chance to close them, and if they don't, make the change.
- **Fast** does NOT mean firing someone the same week you realize things aren't going well. That's panic.

## Signals it's time to act

- If you're writing a second '90-day plan' for someone already 9 months in, it's time.
- If the team has been quietly working around someone for 3+ months, the team has already decided.
- If you dread every 1:1, that's real data.

## Notes

- Firing someone doesn't feel heroic. It feels bad. That doesn't mean it's wrong.
- The hardest firings are the nice, competent, not-quite-right people. Do them anyway.
