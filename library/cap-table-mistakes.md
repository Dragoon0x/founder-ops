# Common Cap Table Mistakes

Cap table mistakes compound. Fix early. Some are unfixable later.

## Foundational Mistakes (fix immediately)

### 1. No Founder Vesting
**Problem:** Co-founder leaves after 6 months with 30% of company permanently
**Fix:** All founders on 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff, even if it feels weird at start

### 2. Verbal "Co-Founder" Agreement
**Problem:** Friend "helped at the start," now claims they should have founder equity
**Fix:** Founders' agreement signed BEFORE you incorporate. Specifies who's a founder, equity split, vesting, IP assignment.

### 3. Unclear IP Assignment from Contractors
**Problem:** Hired a contractor to build the MVP. Code is theirs.
**Fix:** Every contractor signs IP assignment + work-for-hire BEFORE work starts. No exceptions.

### 4. Missing 83(b) Election
**Problem:** Founder didn't file 83(b) within 30 days. Now has phantom income tax on each vesting period.
**Fix:** File within 30 days of equity grant. Always.

### 5. No Employee Equity Pool
**Problem:** Need to hire engineer 4 quickly, no equity to give them, founders have to dilute themselves
**Fix:** Set aside 10-20% pre-funding option pool. Refresh at each round.

## Investor Round Mistakes

### 6. Pre-Money Option Pool (Hidden Dilution)
**Problem:** Investors require option pool top-up BEFORE the investment. Pool comes from founder equity, not new shares.
**Fix:** Negotiate post-money option pool (less common, requires negotiation)

### 7. Stacking SAFEs Without Tracking
**Problem:** 12 SAFEs at different valuations. Conversion at next round is a math nightmare.
**Fix:** Use Carta or Pulley from day 1. Model conversion BEFORE accepting new SAFEs.

### 8. SAFEs at Different Valuation Caps
**Problem:** Early SAFE at $5M cap, later SAFE at $15M cap. Early investor effectively owns 3x as much per dollar.
**Fix:** Try to keep SAFE caps consistent or use post-money SAFEs for clarity

### 9. Selling Too Much in First Round
**Problem:** Sold 35% of company in pre-seed. Now investors will own 70%+ by Series A.
**Fix:** First institutional round: 15-25% sold. Anything more limits future rounds.

### 10. Multiple Lead Investors
**Problem:** Two investors both think they're "leading" the round. Term sheets conflict.
**Fix:** One lead, period. Others co-invest at lead's terms.

## Equity Grant Mistakes

### 11. No Equity Refresh for Long-Tenured Employees
**Problem:** Year 4 hire is fully vested. They start interviewing because their grant is "done."
**Fix:** Refresh grants annually or at promotion. Top performers get larger refreshes.

### 12. Below-Market Equity Bands
**Problem:** Trying to "save" equity. Top candidates accept other offers.
**Fix:** Match market bands by stage and role. Reference `library/compensation-bands.md`.

### 13. No Acceleration Provisions for Senior Hires
**Problem:** VP joins, gets fired in acquisition. Loses unvested equity.
**Fix:** Double-trigger acceleration (acquisition + termination) standard for execs.

### 14. Inconsistent Equity Across Same-Level Hires
**Problem:** Engineer A negotiated harder, got 0.5% more than Engineer B at same level
**Fix:** Set ranges by level. Allow narrow negotiation within range. Document.

### 15. Granting Stock Instead of Options (or Vice Versa)
**Problem:** Granted Restricted Stock when company was worth $1M. Employee owes income tax on $1M.
**Fix:** Use ISO/NSO options at fair market value. Use restricted stock only for founders pre-funding.

## Advisor Equity Mistakes

### 16. Over-Granting to Advisors
**Problem:** Gave 5 advisors 0.5% each. Now have 2.5% in unproductive equity.
**Fix:** Standard advisor grants: 0.1-0.5% with vesting, FAST agreement template (CooleyGo)

### 17. No Vesting on Advisor Equity
**Problem:** Advisor disappears after 3 months, keeps full equity grant
**Fix:** 1-2 year vesting, monthly. No cliff (advisors don't have one usually).

### 18. Too Many Advisors
**Problem:** 12 advisors, none deeply engaged. Investors view as red flag.
**Fix:** 3-5 advisors maximum. Each with specific contribution.

## Tax Mistakes

### 19. Not Doing 409A Valuation
**Problem:** Granting options at "guess" strike price. IRS audit risk; employee tax issues.
**Fix:** 409A valuation annually (or at major events). $3-5K per Carta/Pulley/specialist.

### 20. Wrong Strike Price on Options
**Problem:** Strike price too low ("we're not worth much"). Triggers AMT and tax issues.
**Fix:** Use 409A FMV as strike price. Document the reasoning.

### 21. Not Filing 83(b) for Restricted Stock
**Problem:** See #4. Massive recurring tax issue.
**Fix:** File within 30 days. Send certified mail to IRS.

### 22. ISO Limit Violations
**Problem:** Granting more than $100K worth of ISOs vesting in one calendar year. Excess becomes NSO.
**Fix:** Track $100K calendar year vesting limit per employee.

## M&A / Exit Mistakes

### 23. No Liquidation Preference Hierarchy Clarity
**Problem:** Different rounds have different preference levels. Distribution at exit is chaotic.
**Fix:** Document waterfall in detail. Run simulations at different exit values.

### 24. Liquidation Preference Eats Common
**Problem:** Sold for $50M. After preferences, founders + employees get $5M.
**Fix:** Negotiate 1x non-participating preferred from day 1. Avoid 2x+ preferences.

### 25. Acceleration Triggers Confusion
**Problem:** "Single trigger" vs "double trigger" interpreted differently.
**Fix:** Spell out exact conditions in equity docs and offer letters.

## Operational Mistakes

### 26. Cap Table Stored in a Spreadsheet
**Problem:** Errors compound. Investors lose trust.
**Fix:** Use Carta, Pulley, or Capbase from incorporation.

### 27. No Stock Plan Approved by Board
**Problem:** Issuing options without proper authorization
**Fix:** Board-approved equity incentive plan. Sized for projected hires.

### 28. Granting Equity Without Documentation
**Problem:** Verbal "you'll get 0.5%" promises. Disputes later.
**Fix:** Every equity grant: board approval, signed grant agreement, vesting schedule.

### 29. Ignoring Common-vs-Preferred at Acquisition
**Problem:** Common shareholders (founders, employees) get less than preferred at low exit
**Fix:** Understand the waterfall before negotiating any exit

### 30. No Communication About Equity Value
**Problem:** Employees think their equity is worth current 409A x shares (it's not)
**Fix:** Educate at hire. Reinforce annually. Use Pulley/Carta employee dashboards.

## When to Get Professional Help

- Founders' agreement: lawyer
- First investor round (any size): lawyer
- Stock plan setup: lawyer
- Annual 409A: specialist
- Tax planning around exit: tax advisor
- M&A: lawyer + investment banker

Spending $5-20K on cap table and legal early can save $1M+ later.
