# Term Sheet Clauses — Decoded

Common clauses in venture term sheets. Plain-English explanation +
what's standard vs founder-unfriendly.

## Pre-Money vs Post-Money Valuation

**Pre-money:** company value BEFORE the new investment
**Post-money:** company value AFTER the new investment
**Formula:** post-money = pre-money + investment amount

**Why this matters:** Same dollar invested at $10M post is more dilutive
than at $10M pre. ALWAYS clarify which the term sheet refers to.

## Liquidation Preference

**Standard:** 1x non-participating
**Means:** Investor gets their money back first, then chooses to convert to common
**Founder-unfriendly:** 2x or 3x preferences, participating preferred
**When 2x+ is justified:** Down rounds, troubled companies

## Participating Preferred ("Double Dip")

**Bad:** Investor gets liquidation preference AND share of remaining proceeds
**Standard now:** Non-participating preferred (must choose pref or convert to common)
**Negotiation:** If they insist on participating, cap it at 2-3x

## Anti-Dilution

**Standard:** Broad-based weighted average
**Acceptable:** Narrow-based weighted average
**Founder-unfriendly:** Full ratchet (worst — adjusts to match new lower price)
**When triggered:** Down round (next round at lower valuation)

## Vesting (founders)

**Standard:** 4 years, 1-year cliff
**Means:** No equity vests until year 1, then monthly for 3 years
**Founder protection:** Acceleration on termination without cause + change of control
**Common:** Single-trigger acceleration (just acquisition) for advisors;
double-trigger (acquisition + termination) for executives

## Acceleration on Change of Control

**Single trigger:** All equity vests on acquisition
**Double trigger:** All equity vests if acquired AND terminated within 12 months
**Standard for execs:** Double trigger
**Pre-acquisition founder vesting:** Often 100% if board fires you within 12 months

## Pro Rata Rights

**Standard:** Investors can maintain ownership % in future rounds
**Means:** They get to invest in next round to avoid dilution
**Limits:** Often only "major investors" (above some threshold)
**Founder note:** Not a problem for founders unless overly broad

## Right of First Refusal (ROFR)

**On secondary sales:** If you want to sell shares, company / investors get first chance to buy
**Standard:** Yes, with reasonable timeline (30-60 days)
**Watch for:** Overly long timelines that prevent any sale

## Drag-Along Rights

**Means:** If majority approves a sale, minority must go along
**Standard:** Yes, but should require significant majority approval (e.g., 65%+)
**Founder concern:** Make sure board AND stockholder approval needed

## Tag-Along Rights

**Means:** If founders sell shares, investors can sell their pro rata too
**Standard:** Yes
**Founder note:** Limits ability to do secondary sales

## Information Rights

**Standard:** Quarterly financials, annual budget, monthly metrics for major investors
**Reasonable:** Yes
**Watch for:** Overly broad inspection rights, board observer seats for non-major investors

## Board Composition

**Pre-Series A:** 2 founders, 1 investor
**Series A:** 2 founders, 1-2 investors, 1 independent
**Series B:** 2 founders, 2 investors, 1-2 independent
**Series C+:** 1-2 founders, 2-3 investors, 2-3 independent
**Founder-friendly:** Founders + independents have majority over investors

## Board Voting Thresholds (Protective Provisions)

What requires board / preferred shareholder approval beyond normal majority:
- Sale of company
- Issuance of senior preferred
- Increase in option pool
- Material business changes
- Annual budget approval
- CEO change (sometimes)

**Negotiation:** Limit to a tight list. Preferred shareholders should not have to
approve operational decisions.

## Option Pool

**Pre-money option pool:** Pool created from EXISTING (founder) shares — bad for founders
**Post-money option pool:** Pool created from new (post-investment) shares — better
**Standard size:** 10-20% of post-money cap table
**Negotiation:** Match pool size to actual hiring plan, not "for safety"

## No Shop / Lock-up

**Standard:** 30-45 day no-shop after term sheet signed
**Means:** Can't talk to other investors during this period
**Founder-friendly:** Shorter is better

## Conditions Precedent

**Standard:** Due diligence completion, legal docs signed, key reps & warranties
**Watch for:** "Subject to investor approval at any time" (= they can walk for any reason)

## Founder-Specific Items

### Founder Equity Re-Vesting
- Standard: Founder shares are re-vested under acquisition
- Founder protection: Already-vested shares stay vested

### Founder Salaries
- Standard: Set by board (post-investment)
- Pre-Series A: Often $80-150K
- Post-Series A: $150-250K typical
- Watch for: Below-market salary requirements

### Founder Sales (Secondary)
- Common at Series B+: 5-10% of founder shares can be sold to incoming investors
- Allows founders to take some money off the table without selling control
- Negotiate this when possible (especially at later stages)

## Common Term Sheet Red Flags

| Term | Why It's Bad |
|------|-------------|
| Multiple liquidation preference | Misaligned incentives |
| Full ratchet anti-dilution | Massive dilution if down round |
| Participating preferred | "Double dip" |
| Investor-friendly board | Loss of control |
| Veto on operational decisions | Slow decision-making |
| Short founder vesting (e.g., 2 years) | Less long-term commitment |
| No founder acceleration | Stuck if fired |
| Pre-money option pool | Hidden dilution to founders |
| No standardized voting threshold | Single investor can block |
| Excessive information rights | Burden on small team |

## Standard "Clean" Series A Term Sheet (2026)

For a healthy Series A, a clean term sheet looks like:

- 1x non-participating liquidation preference
- Broad-based weighted average anti-dilution
- 4-year founder vesting with 12-month cliff (already in place)
- Double-trigger acceleration for founders/execs
- Pro rata rights for major investors
- Standard ROFR / co-sale on secondary
- Standard information rights (quarterly financials, annual budget)
- 2-2-1 board (2 founders, 2 investors, 1 independent)
- 10-15% post-money option pool refresh
- 30-day no-shop
- Standard reps and warranties

If your term sheet differs significantly, ask why — and get a lawyer involved.
