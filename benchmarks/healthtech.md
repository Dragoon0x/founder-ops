# Healthtech Benchmarks

Healthtech is slower, more regulated, higher-stakes. Benchmarks differ
significantly between B2B (sell to providers/payers) and B2C (sell to patients).

## B2B Healthtech (sell to hospitals, payers, providers)

| Metric | Range |
|--------|-------|
| Average sales cycle | 9-18 months (enterprise health) |
| ACV | $50K-$500K (mid), $500K-$5M (enterprise) |
| Pilot duration | 3-12 months before full purchase |
| Win rate | 10-25% |
| Customer count for $10M ARR | 30-100 (enterprise) |
| Implementation time | 3-12 months |
| Annual churn | 5-12% |
| NRR | 110-130% |

**Reality:** Selling to hospitals takes 18+ months. Plan for it.

## Provider Productivity Tools

For tools sold to physicians/clinics:
- ACV per provider: $1K-$10K/year
- Sales cycle: 30-90 days (small practice), 6-12 months (large group)
- Churn: 8-15%

## Compliance Costs

- HIPAA-compliant infrastructure: +30-50% on standard infra
- BAA-required vendors only (no Slack free tier, etc.)
- SOC 2 Type II + HITRUST: $50K-$200K/year ongoing
- FDA submission (510k, De Novo, PMA): $50K-$5M depending on class
- IRB approval for clinical evidence: $5K-$50K per study

## Reimbursement Reality

If your revenue depends on reimbursement:
- CPT codes take 1-3 years to secure
- Coverage decisions (CMS, commercial payers) take 12-36 months after CPT
- "Coverage" doesn't mean reimbursement at your price point
- Out-of-pocket model is faster but capped market

## Regulatory Pathway Timelines

- 510(k) clearance: 3-12 months (with predicate)
- De Novo: 12-24 months
- PMA: 18-36 months
- Software as Medical Device (SaMD): added 6-12 months for cybersecurity
- HIPAA security review by enterprise customer: 30-90 days

## Healthtech Sales Process Specifics

- Always sign BAA before any data exchange
- Security review questionnaire is 50-200 questions, takes 30-60 days
- Procurement vendor onboarding: 30-90 days after legal close
- Total time from term sheet to first dollar: 6-9 months for large health systems
