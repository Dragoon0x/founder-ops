# SMB vs Mid-Market vs Enterprise SaaS

The same business model produces wildly different operations at different
deal sizes. Know which segment you're in and stop benchmarking against the wrong one.

## Segment Definitions

| Segment | ACV (Annual Contract Value) | Examples |
|---------|----------------------------|----------|
| Self-serve (PLG) | <$1K | Early Notion, Calendly |
| SMB | $1K-$10K | Hubspot SMB, QuickBooks |
| Mid-market | $10K-$100K | Hubspot Pro, Atlassian |
| Enterprise | $100K-$1M | Salesforce Service Cloud |
| Strategic enterprise | $1M+ | Workday, ServiceNow |

## Sales Cycle by Segment

| Segment | Sales Cycle | Decision-Makers | Touches Required |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Self-serve | Hours-days | 1 (the user) | 0-1 |
| SMB | 7-30 days | 1-2 | 1-3 |
| Mid-market | 30-90 days | 3-5 | 5-15 |
| Enterprise | 90-180 days | 5-15 | 15-40 |
| Strategic | 6-18 months | 10-30+ | 40+ |

| Segment | Discovery → Demo | Demo → Pricing | Pricing → Close | End-to-end |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|

## Churn by Segment (annual gross logo churn)

| Segment | Annual Churn |
|---------|-------------|
| Self-serve | 30-50% |
| SMB | 15-25% |
| Mid-market | 8-15% |
| Enterprise | 3-8% |
| Strategic | 1-3% |

SMB churn is dominated by business mortality (companies dying), not product issues.
You can't churn-engineer your way past 15% SMB churn — buy more customers.

## Headcount Cost per Closed Deal

| Segment | Sales Cost per Deal |
|---------|---------------------|
| Self-serve | $0-50 (paid ads, automation) |
| SMB | $200-2,000 |
| Mid-market | $5,000-25,000 |
| Enterprise | $50,000-200,000 |

Use this to back into "is this deal worth chasing?"

## Sales Team Composition

**Self-serve / PLG**
- No traditional AEs
- PLG ops, growth eng, lifecycle marketers

**SMB**
- BDR + AE pods (1 BDR : 1 AE)
- Inside sales, no field
- AE owns full cycle
- $700K-$900K quota typical

**Mid-market**
- BDR + AE + SE (sales engineer)
- Some field activity
- 2:1 BDR ratio, 0.5 SE per AE
- $1M-$1.5M quota typical

**Enterprise**
- AE + SE + Solutions Architect + Customer Success
- Account-based selling
- Pod model: 1 AE owns 5-15 named accounts
- $1.5M-$2.5M quota typical

## Product Implications

| Decision | SMB Choice | Enterprise Choice |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Onboarding | Self-serve, in-product | White-glove, dedicated CSM |
| Pricing display | Public, on website | "Contact us" |
| Free trial | Standard 14-day | Pilot or POC, 30-90 days |
| SOC 2 | Type I optional | Type II required |
| SSO | Pro tier feature | Required (often bundled in enterprise tier) |
| Custom contracts | Standard ToS | MSA negotiable |
| Procurement | None | Vendor onboarding, security review, MSA, SOW |
| Customer council | Optional | Required for retention |
| Roadmap commitments | Generic public | Customer-specific in writing |

## Picking Your Segment (or accidentally being in two)

**Symptoms of segment confusion:**
- ACV varies 50x within the same product (mixing self-serve and enterprise)
- Sales process inconsistent (sometimes touch, sometimes none)
- Team doesn't know which deal type to prioritize
- Pricing page tries to serve everyone, serves no one

**Fix:**
- Pick a primary segment for the next 18 months
- Pricing, GTM, support model all align to that segment
- Don't actively pursue the other segments unless they come inbound and accept your terms
