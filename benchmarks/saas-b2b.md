# B2B SaaS Benchmarks by Stage

## ARR Growth Rate (year-over-year)

| Stage | Bottom 25% | Median | Top 25% | Top 10% |
|-------|-----------|--------|---------|---------|
| <$1M ARR | 100% | 200% | 400% | 600%+ |
| $1-5M ARR | 80% | 150% | 250% | 400%+ |
| $5-10M ARR | 60% | 100% | 175% | 250%+ |
| $10-25M ARR | 50% | 80% | 130% | 200%+ |
| $25-50M ARR | 40% | 65% | 100% | 150%+ |
| $50-100M ARR | 35% | 50% | 80% | 120%+ |
| $100M+ ARR | 25% | 40% | 60% | 90%+ |

**Triple-Triple-Double-Double-Double** = legendary trajectory:
$1M → $3M → $9M → $18M → $36M → $72M (5 years from $1M to $72M).
Less than 5% of SaaS hits this.

## Gross Margin (subscription revenue, excludes services)

| Tier | Range |
|------|-------|
| Excellent | 80%+ |
| Healthy | 70-80% |
| Concerning | 60-70% |
| Broken | <60% |

AI/inference-heavy products: 50-65% is more realistic until model costs fall.

## Magic Number (sales efficiency)

`Magic Number = (Q4 ARR - Q3 ARR) × 4 / Q3 sales & marketing spend`

| Magic Number | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| <0.5 | Slow GTM investment, fix pipeline |
| 0.5-0.75 | Cautious, optimize before scaling |
| 0.75-1.0 | Healthy, can invest more |
| 1.0-1.5 | Strong, accelerate |
| 1.5+ | Exceptional, push hard |

## CAC Payback Period (months)

| Segment | Healthy Payback |
|---------|-----------------|
| Enterprise (>$50K ACV) | 18-24 months |
| Mid-market ($10-50K ACV) | 12-18 months |
| SMB ($1-10K ACV) | 6-12 months |
| Self-serve (<$1K ACV) | <6 months |

## LTV:CAC

Target: 3:1 minimum, 5:1+ excellent.
Below 3:1 = unit economics broken.
Above 8:1 = under-investing in growth.

## Burn Multiple (Bessemer)

`Burn Multiple = Net Burn / Net New ARR`

| Burn Multiple | Tier |
|---------------|------|
| <1x | Best (highly efficient) |
| 1-1.5x | Great |
| 1.5-2x | OK |
| 2-3x | Suspect |
| >3x | Bad |

## Sales Efficiency Benchmarks

- AE quota: $700K-$1.2M ARR (mid-market), $1.2M-$2M (enterprise)
- AE ramp time: 6-9 months to full quota
- AE attainment: 60-75% hit quota in healthy orgs
- SDR-to-AE ratio: 1:1 (mid-market), 0.5:1 (enterprise SE-led)
- Pipeline coverage: 3x quota minimum, 4-5x ideal
- Win rate: 20-25% (mid-market), 15-20% (enterprise)
- Sales cycle: 30-60 days (SMB), 60-90 (mid), 90-180 (enterprise)

## Customer Success / Support

- CSM ratio: 1 CSM per $1-2M ARR managed
- Support ticket per 100 MAU: 5-15 (healthy), 25+ (problem)
- First response time target: <2 hours business hours
- CSAT target: 90%+
- Time-to-value (first onboarding milestone): <14 days SMB, <30 days enterprise

## Product Engagement

- DAU/MAU for daily-use B2B SaaS: 40-60% (excellent), 25-40% (good)
- Feature adoption (any new feature, 90 days): 25-40% of MAU is healthy
- Power user share (top 10% by usage): 30-50% of total usage

## Pricing Architecture

- Annual contract discount vs monthly: 15-20% standard, up to 25% for enterprise
- Multi-year discount: additional 5-10% per year
- 3 tiers (Starter / Pro / Enterprise) is the dominant structure
- Pro should be 2.5-3.5x Starter (anchor effect)
- Enterprise should be 4-10x Pro

## Headcount Ratios (mature SaaS, $50M+ ARR)

| Function | % of total headcount |
|----------|---------------------|
| Engineering | 30-40% |
| Sales | 15-25% |
| Customer success / support | 10-15% |
| Marketing | 8-12% |
| Product / Design | 7-10% |
| G&A (finance/legal/HR/IT) | 8-12% |
| Operations | 3-5% |

Earlier stage: engineering can be 50-60% of headcount.
