# Prompt: Pitch Practice

## Output
`reports/pitch-practice-[date].md`

---

## Prompt Template

```
I have a pitch meeting coming up with [INVESTOR NAME] in [DAYS] days.

Context:
- The round: $[X] at [Y] cap/pre
- My 2-minute pitch: [PASTE CURRENT PITCH]
- Top concerns I have: [WHAT I'M WORRIED THEY'LL ASK]

Run /founder-ops fundraise pitch-practice. Don't be nice.

## ROLE-PLAY AS THE PARTNER
Act as a skeptical partner at their fund. Given their portfolio pattern and 
stated thesis, role-play asking me the 10 toughest questions I'd face.

For each question:
- Ask it the way they would (their known style/framing)
- Wait for my answer (I'll respond)
- Push back or follow up
- Flag weak answers with specifics

## THE CATEGORIES THEY'LL HIT
Prepare me for likely probing in:
- Market size (is this big enough to matter?)
- Wedge (is your beachhead real or contrived?)
- Moat (what prevents copying?)
- GTM (can you actually reach customers?)
- Unit economics (does the math work?)
- Team (why is this the right team?)
- Competition (why you vs. incumbent / startup competitors)
- Risk (what's the #1 reason this fails?)
- Capital efficiency (can you do this with the $ you're asking for?)
- Ego check (are you coachable? how do you handle being wrong?)

## MY ANSWER QUALITY
After I respond to each, rate the answer:
- 10/10 (crystal, specific, memorable)
- 7/10 (solid but generic)
- 5/10 (evasive or weak)
- <5/10 (don't say this in the meeting)

For weak answers, give me a concrete rewrite.

## THE "TRICK" QUESTIONS
Prepare me specifically for:
- "What's the one thing keeping you up at night?" 
- "Tell me about a time you were wrong"
- "What do you think of [competitor]?"
- "Why haven't [X] big companies built this?"
- "What's your honest biggest risk?"

For each, coach me on:
- The TRAP (what most founders say wrong)
- The TRUTH-FORWARD answer that wins

## OPENING 2 MINUTES
- Scripted version of my 2-minute pitch (not memorized tone, just structured)
- Where to pause for engagement
- Slide timing (if using deck)

## CLOSING
- What to say in the last 5 minutes
- What NOT to agree to on the spot
- How to ask for commitment without begging

Save as reports/pitch-practice-[DATE].md.
```
