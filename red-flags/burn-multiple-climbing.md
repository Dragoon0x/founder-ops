# Red Flag: Burn Multiple Climbing

## The pattern
Your burn multiple is trending up over 2+ consecutive quarters. What
was 1.5x is now 2.1x. What was 2x is now 3x.

## Why it's a red flag
Burn multiple going up means one of:
- You're spending more to get less (inefficiency).
- Your market is cooling (less demand per dollar of effort).
- You over-hired and outputs haven't caught up.
- Your Net Revenue Retention is eroding.

At Series A and later, this is one of the first things investors look
at. A climbing burn multiple in a stable market is a signal that
you've lost operational discipline.

## Diagnosing severity

### Level 1 (monitor)
- Burn multiple up 0.2–0.5x over 2 quarters.
- Explainable by one-time costs (e.g., office move, big hire batch).
- New logo growth still strong.

### Level 2 (act within 1 quarter)
- Burn multiple up 0.5–1.0x over 2 quarters.
- No clear one-time explanation.
- Growth rate stable or slipping.

### Level 3 (immediate action)
- Burn multiple up 1.0x+ over 2 quarters.
- OR burn multiple > 3x at Series A+.
- Growth decelerating.
- Runway < 18 months.

## What to do

### At Level 1
- Audit the top 3 cost line items. Is growth in those justified?
- Review net revenue retention. Is NRR stable?
- Continue current plan with heightened monitoring.

### At Level 2
- Freeze hiring for 60–90 days.
- Audit tools: cancel anything with <70% utilization.
- Audit marketing spend: cut anything without ROI > 3x after 90 days.
- Review the last 3 hires: are they producing at plan? If not, why?

### At Level 3
- Immediate hiring freeze.
- Layoff consideration: cut 10–20% if needed to reach 18 months runway.
- Focus the company on 1 product / 1 segment for 2 quarters.
- Kill all experimental channels and products.
- Renegotiate all contracts: hosting, SaaS, real estate.
- Consider a bridge from insiders to extend runway.

## How to debug the underlying cause

Step 1: Decompose burn.
```
Burn = People + S&M non-people + Hosting + Tools + Other

What % of increase came from each category?
```

Step 2: Decompose Net New ARR.
```
Net New ARR = New + Expansion - Churn - Contraction

Is the problem low New, or is retention slipping?
```

Step 3: Compare period-over-period.
```
Q1: Burn $1.5M, Net New $900K, BM = 1.67x
Q2: Burn $1.8M, Net New $850K, BM = 2.12x
Q3: Burn $2.2M, Net New $800K, BM = 2.75x

Burn grew 47%, Net New shrunk 11%. Both are problems.
```

Step 4: Identify the root cause.
- If burn is the driver: over-hiring, unused tools, marketing waste.
- If Net New is the driver: motion breaking down, PMF eroding.

Usually it's both. Fix both.

## Common founder mistakes when BM climbs

1. **Hire more sales to "grow out of it."** This raises burn further.
   You need efficiency, not more spend.
2. **Discount to close more deals.** You'll hurt ACV and margin.
3. **Pretend it's a one-time issue.** Burn climb patterns rarely
   reverse without action.
4. **Wait another quarter to act.** Each quarter of inaction makes
   the cut deeper when it comes.
5. **Blame the market.** Even in bad markets, best-in-class operators
   hold burn multiple.

## What to report to the board

If burn multiple is climbing:
- Show the trend over 4 quarters.
- Decompose by category.
- Explain what changed (mix of market and execution).
- Propose 3 specific actions.
- Commit to specific improvement target by next quarter.

Don't wait for the board to ask. Proactively lead the conversation.

## The turnaround pattern

Companies that recover from rising burn multiple usually:
1. Acted early (within 1 quarter of pattern, not 3).
2. Cut aggressively (more than they thought necessary).
3. Focused on 1–2 things (not everything).
4. Communicated clearly to the team (no surprises).
5. Held burn multiple stable for 2 quarters before spending again.

Companies that failed usually:
- Waited 2+ quarters hoping it would self-correct.
- Cut too shallow (pretended the market was the only problem).
- Kept expanding in parallel with cuts.
- Tried to grow their way out of inefficiency.
