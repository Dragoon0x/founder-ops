# Board deck getting longer every quarter

> More slides ≠ more signal. Often the opposite.

## What to look for

- Watch for: deck going from 20 to 45 slides over 4 quarters, usually because the team is adding context to defend misses.
- Good board decks tighten, not expand. The job is not to justify; it's to decide.
- Aim for 15-20 slides. If a slide doesn't drive a decision or surface a risk, cut it.

## What to do

Once you've seen the signal, open a direct conversation. Don't let it linger. Red flags compound faster than founders realize.
