# Cash Flow Review Workflow

## Trigger
Weekly, every Friday. Non-negotiable until you have > 18 months runway.

## Weekly Review (30 min)

### Step 1: Pull the numbers (5 min)
From your banking dashboard (Mercury, Brex, SVB):
- Current cash balance (all accounts)
- Cash in this week (revenue collected, not invoiced)
- Cash out this week (payroll, vendors, one-time expenses)
- Net cash flow this week

### Step 2: Runway calculation (5 min)
```
Monthly burn = (Total expenses last 3 months) / 3
Runway = Cash balance / Monthly burn
```

Track runway weekly in a simple spreadsheet. Plot the trend.

| Runway | Status | Action |
|--------|--------|--------|
| > 18 months | Comfortable | Focus on growth |
| 12-18 months | Planning zone | Start fundraising prep |
| 6-12 months | Active zone | Fundraising or cut burn |
| < 6 months | Danger zone | Emergency cost reduction |
| < 3 months | Crisis | Immediate action required |

### Step 3: Cash flow forecast (10 min)
Forward-looking 13-week cash flow:
- Expected revenue collections (confirmed + probable)
- Known expenses (payroll, rent, subscriptions, contractors)
- Planned expenses (hires, marketing spend, equipment)
- One-time items (legal fees, tax payments, conference costs)

Flag any week where cash balance drops below 2 months of burn.

### Step 4: Action items (10 min)
- Any invoices outstanding > 30 days? Follow up today.
- Any expenses that can be deferred without impact?
- Any revenue acceleration opportunities? (Annual prepay discounts, faster invoicing)
- Does the forecast match the plan? If not, why?

## Monthly Deep Dive (first Friday of each month, 2 hrs)

### Actuals vs budget
- Revenue: actual vs projected, variance analysis
- Expenses by category: payroll, hosting, marketing, office, other
- CAC and LTV calculations updated
- Department-level budget adherence

### Cash management
- Optimize cash placement (operating account vs savings/money market)
- Review credit facilities (if applicable)
- Tax obligation estimate (quarterly estimated taxes due?)
- Insurance renewals coming up?

## Burn Rate Levers
When you need to reduce burn, these are your options in order of speed:

| Lever | Impact | Speed | Reversibility |
|-------|--------|-------|---------------|
| Pause hiring | Medium | Immediate | Easy |
| Reduce marketing spend | Medium | 1 week | Easy |
| Renegotiate vendor contracts | Small-Medium | 2-4 weeks | Medium |
| Reduce contractor hours | Medium | 2 weeks | Easy |
| Salary reductions | Large | 1 month | Hard |
| Layoffs | Large | 2-4 weeks | Very hard |
