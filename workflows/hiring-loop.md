# Hiring Loop Workflow

## Trigger
Approved headcount request with signed-off job description.

## Pre-conditions
- Role justified with business case (revenue impact or cost of not hiring)
- Compensation band approved by finance
- Interview panel identified (4-5 people)
- Scorecard defined with 4-6 must-have competencies

## Phase 1: Pipeline Building (Week 1-2)

### Sourcing targets
For each role, you need:
- Inbound applications: 50-100 (from job boards, careers page)
- Outbound sourced: 20-40 (LinkedIn, referrals, communities)
- Referral candidates: 5-10 (best conversion rate, typically 40-60%)

### Channel priority by role type
| Role Type | Best Channel | Expected Response Rate |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Engineering IC | Referrals > GitHub/OSS communities > LinkedIn | 15-25% outbound |
| Design | Dribbble/portfolio > Referrals > LinkedIn | 10-20% outbound |
| Sales | LinkedIn > Referrals > Recruiters | 20-30% outbound |
| Marketing | Referrals > LinkedIn > Twitter/communities | 15-25% outbound |
| Executive | Recruiter > Board network > Referrals | 5-15% outbound |

### Outreach template rules
- First message: < 100 words
- Personalization: reference ONE specific thing they did (not "I was impressed by your background")
- Clear value prop: what's interesting about this role, specifically
- Low-friction CTA: "Would a 20-min call make sense?" not "Please apply through our portal"

## Phase 2: Screening (Ongoing)

### Recruiter screen (30 min)
Pass/fail on:
- Relevant experience (does their background map to the role?)
- Motivation (why this company, why now?)
- Logistics (timeline, comp expectations, location/remote)
- Communication quality (can they articulate clearly?)

Kill the call at 15 min if it's clearly a no. Everyone's time matters.

### Hiring manager screen (45 min)
Deeper dive on:
- Technical depth in their domain
- Specific examples of impact (STAR format, but don't be rigid about it)
- How they think about problems (give a lightweight scenario)
- Culture signals (how do they handle disagreement, ambiguity, failure?)

Score 1-4 on each competency from the scorecard. No 3s allowed (it's a cop-out). Strong yes, yes, no, strong no.

## Phase 3: Interviews (1-2 days)

### Panel structure
| Interview | Duration | Focus | Interviewer |
|-----------|----------|-------|-------------|
| Technical deep-dive | 60 min | Core skill assessment | Senior IC in function |
| Work sample / case | 60 min | Applied problem-solving | Hiring manager |
| Cross-functional | 45 min | Collaboration, communication | Adjacent team lead |
| Values / culture | 30 min | Alignment, working style | Team member |
| Executive / closer | 30 min | Vision alignment, sell the role | CEO or VP |

### Debrief rules
- Happens within 24 hrs of final interview
- Each interviewer submits written scorecard BEFORE debrief (prevents anchoring)
- Discussion order: junior interviewers speak first
- Decision: hire / no-hire. "Let's see more candidates" is only valid if you have a specific concern and a specific competency gap identified

## Phase 4: Offer & Close (1-3 days)

### Offer construction
- Base salary: within approved band, anchored to candidate's level
- Equity: standard for stage (seed: 0.25-1.0% for early employees, Series A: 0.1-0.5%)
- Signing bonus: only if competing with a specific counter-offer
- Start date: 2-4 weeks typical, accommodate notice periods

### Verbal offer call
- Hiring manager delivers, not recruiter
- Walk through: role, team, comp, equity, start date
- Ask: "What would make this a clear yes for you?"
- Give 48-72 hrs to decide. Don't pressure.

### Reference checks (parallel to offer consideration)
- 3 references minimum: 1 manager, 1 peer, 1 direct report (if applicable)
- Ask: "On a scale of 1-10, how likely would you be to hire this person again?" Anything below 8 is a flag.
- Ask: "What should I know about managing this person effectively?"

## Metrics
| Metric | Target | Alert |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Time to fill | < 45 days | > 60 days |
| Offer acceptance rate | > 80% | < 60% |
| Pipeline-to-offer ratio | 20:1 | > 40:1 |
| 90-day retention | > 90% | < 80% |
| Referral % of hires | > 30% | < 15% |
| Candidate NPS | > 50 | < 30 |
