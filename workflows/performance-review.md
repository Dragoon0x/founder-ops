# Performance Review Workflow

## Trigger
Bi-annual (every 6 months). Mid-year (July) and end-of-year (January).

## Timeline

### T-4 weeks: Self-reviews
Every team member writes a self-review covering:
1. **Key accomplishments** (3-5, each with quantified impact)
2. **Areas of growth** (what skills/capabilities developed)
3. **Challenges faced** (what was hard and how you handled it)
4. **Goals for next period** (2-3 specific, measurable objectives)
5. **Feedback for manager** (what could they do differently)

Template tip: each accomplishment should follow the format: "Did [action] which resulted in [measurable outcome]"

### T-3 weeks: Peer feedback
Each person selects 2-3 peers to provide feedback.
Peer feedback questions:
1. "What is this person's superpower? Give a specific example."
2. "What's one thing this person could do differently to be more effective?"
3. "On a scale of 1-10, how much do you want to work with this person on your next project?"

### T-2 weeks: Manager reviews
Managers write reviews for each direct report:
- Performance rating (Exceeds / Meets / Below expectations)
- Accomplishments (validated, with manager's perspective on impact)
- Development areas (specific, actionable, with resources/support offered)
- Compensation recommendation (if applicable)

### T-1 week: Calibration
All managers meet with their skip-level to calibrate ratings.
Purpose: ensure consistency across teams.
Rules:
- Not everyone can be "Exceeds." Target distribution: 15-20% Exceeds, 65-75% Meets, 10-15% Below.
- If a "Below" rating is a surprise to the employee, the manager failed (should have given feedback earlier)
- Compensation changes must fit within budget. Finance provides the envelope.

### T-0: Review conversations
Manager meets 1:1 with each direct report (45-60 min):
- Start with the employee's self-review (their perspective first)
- Share the rating with context (not just the label, but the reasoning)
- Discuss development areas with specific improvement plan
- Discuss goals for next period
- If compensation change: share it clearly, explain the reasoning
- End with: "What support do you need from me?"

## Rating Framework

### Exceeds Expectations
- Consistently delivers beyond what's asked
- Impact is visible beyond their immediate team
- Others seek them out for help and guidance
- Specific example: shipped a feature that moved a company metric by > 10%

### Meets Expectations
- Reliably delivers what's expected at their level
- Growing in their role
- Good team citizen
- Specific example: consistently hits sprint commitments, quality is solid

### Below Expectations
- Not meeting the bar for their level/role
- May need role change, additional support, or performance plan
- Must be accompanied by a clear improvement plan with 30/60/90 day milestones
- If no improvement after 90 days, proceed to separation

## Common Review Mistakes
1. **Recency bias**: Only remembering the last 2 months. Fix: keep a running doc of accomplishments/issues throughout the period.
2. **Avoidance of hard feedback**: Giving everyone "Meets" to avoid tough conversations. Fix: calibration process forces honest assessment.
3. **No follow-through**: Great conversation, nothing changes. Fix: documented development plan with check-ins.
4. **Surprise ratings**: Employee didn't know they were underperforming. Fix: continuous feedback, not just bi-annual.
