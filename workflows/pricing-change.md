# Pricing Change Workflow

## Trigger
Decision to adjust pricing structure, tiers, or rates.
This is one of the highest-leverage and highest-risk changes you can make. Handle with extreme care.

## Phase 1: Analysis (Week 1)

### Current state assessment
Pull these numbers before you change anything:
- Revenue by plan/tier (MRR contribution of each)
- Customer distribution across plans (what % on each tier)
- Feature utilization by plan (are customers using what they're paying for?)
- Expansion revenue vs new revenue split
- Churn by plan tier (which plans have highest churn?)
- Competitive pricing landscape (what do alternatives charge?)

### Change modeling
For each proposed change, model:
- **Best case**: all customers accept, expansion revenue opportunity
- **Expected case**: X% accept, Y% downgrade, Z% churn
- **Worst case**: significant churn spike, negative PR

The expected case should assume:
- 5-15% of affected customers will complain loudly
- 2-5% of affected customers will churn
- 10-20% of prospects in pipeline will re-evaluate

If the worst case threatens survival, don't do it. Find a gentler path.

## Phase 2: Design (Week 2)

### Pricing change principles
1. **Grandfather generously**: Existing customers keep their current pricing for 6-12 months minimum
2. **Communicate early**: 60-90 days notice for price increases
3. **Add value first**: Pair the price increase with a genuine improvement
4. **Segment the message**: Enterprise, SMB, and individual customers hear different things
5. **Give an out**: Offer an annual commitment at the old price as an alternative

### Internal alignment
Before external communication:
- Finance signs off on revenue impact model
- Sales team briefed on how to handle objections
- Support team briefed on expected inbound volume
- CS team has list of at-risk accounts for proactive outreach
- Legal reviews terms of service for contractual constraints

## Phase 3: Communication (Week 3-4)

### Communication sequence
1. **T-90 days**: Email to all affected customers. Subject: "Changes to our pricing, effective [date]"
2. **T-60 days**: Follow-up email with FAQ. In-app notification.
3. **T-30 days**: Final reminder. Personal outreach to top 20% of accounts by revenue.
4. **T-0**: New pricing live. Blog post explaining the "why."

### What to say (and not say)
**Say:**
- What's changing, specifically (old price → new price)
- When it takes effect
- What's being grandfathered and for how long
- What new value they're getting
- How to contact you with questions

**Don't say:**
- "We're aligning our pricing with the value we deliver" (corporate nonsense)
- "Due to market conditions" (vague and evasive)
- "We hope you understand" (patronizing)

**Do say:**
- "We're raising prices because [specific reason: costs increased, we've added X/Y/Z features, we need to invest in Z]"
- Be direct. Customers respect honesty more than spin.

## Phase 4: Monitoring (Weeks 4-12)

### Metrics to track weekly
| Metric | Expected Impact | Alert Threshold |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Churn rate | +1-2% temporary spike | > 5% increase |
| Support ticket volume | 2-3x for 2 weeks | > 5x sustained |
| NPS score | -5 to -10 temporary dip | > -20 drop |
| Downgrade rate | 5-10% of affected customers | > 20% |
| Expansion revenue | Should increase within 60 days | Flat or declining |

### Recovery plays
If churn exceeds 5%:
- Pause the rollout for remaining customers
- Offer a 6-month bridge at old pricing for at-risk accounts
- Revisit the pricing structure (maybe the change was too aggressive)
