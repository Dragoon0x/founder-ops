# Competitive Scan Workflow

## Trigger
Bi-weekly, or ad-hoc when a competitor makes a major move.

## Bi-Weekly Scan (1 hr)

### Sources to check
For each competitor in your top 5:
1. **Website**: New features, pricing changes, messaging shifts
2. **Blog/changelog**: Product updates, company news, thought leadership themes
3. **Social media**: Twitter/LinkedIn posts from their founders and team
4. **Review sites**: G2, Capterra new reviews (filter by last 2 weeks)
5. **Job postings**: What roles are they hiring for? (Signals strategic direction)
6. **Press**: Google News alerts for company name
7. **Community**: Reddit, HN, Slack communities, Discord servers

### What to look for
- **Pricing changes**: Did they raise or lower prices? Add a free tier? Change packaging?
- **Feature launches**: What did they ship? Does it overlap with your roadmap?
- **Messaging shifts**: Are they repositioning? Targeting a new segment?
- **Hiring patterns**: Lots of sales hires = going enterprise. Lots of ML hires = AI push.
- **Funding/M&A**: Did they raise? Acquire someone? Get acquired?
- **Customer sentiment**: Are their customers happy or frustrated? About what?

### Output format
Post in #competitive-intel channel:

```
## Competitive Scan — [Date]

### [Competitor Name]
- **Signal**: [What you observed]
- **Implication**: [What it means for us]
- **Action**: [What we should do, if anything] or [Monitor]
```

## Ad-Hoc Response (when competitor makes a major move)

### Step 1: Assess impact (30 min)
- Does this affect our current customers? (Will they ask about it?)
- Does this affect our pipeline? (Will prospects compare us?)
- Does this affect our positioning? (Do we need to change our narrative?)

### Step 2: Decide response level
| Competitor Move | Response Level | Action |
|----------------|---------------|--------|
| Minor feature launch | No response | Log and monitor |
| Major feature that overlaps our roadmap | Internal briefing | Update battlecard, brief sales |
| Pricing undercut | Evaluate | Model impact, consider response |
| Direct attack (comparison page, ads) | Respond | Update positioning, arm sales |
| Funding announcement | Monitor | No external response needed |
| Acquisition of adjacent player | Strategic review | Assess threat to our market position |

### Step 3: Arm the team
For any response level above "monitor":
- Update the competitive battlecard within 48 hrs
- Brief sales team in next standup
- If customer-facing: prepare FAQ for support team
- If positioning-level: schedule exec discussion within 1 week

## Quarterly Competitive Review (2 hrs)
Every quarter, do a deeper analysis:
- Market map update: who's gaining, who's losing, new entrants
- Win/loss analysis: why are we winning and losing deals? Is competitor X a factor?
- Feature gap analysis: where are competitors ahead? Where are we ahead?
- Strategic implications: do we need to adjust our roadmap, pricing, or positioning?
