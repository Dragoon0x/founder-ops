# Monthly Board Prep Workflow

## Trigger
T-7 days before board meeting.

## Timeline

### T-7: Data collection (2 hrs)
Pull all metrics for the period:
- Revenue: MRR, ARR, growth rate, net new ARR
- Unit economics: CAC, LTV, LTV/CAC ratio, payback period
- Engagement: DAU/MAU, activation rate, retention curves
- Burn: monthly burn, runway in months, cash balance
- Team: headcount, open roles, regrettable attrition

Source of truth: your metrics dashboard, not manually assembled spreadsheets.
If you're manually assembling, you have a tooling problem. Fix it before next month.

### T-5: Narrative draft (3 hrs)
Write the board memo. Structure:
1. **Executive summary** (3 sentences: what happened, what it means, what we're doing about it)
2. **Key metrics table** with RAG status and trend arrows
3. **What went well** (2-3 items, each with quantified impact)
4. **What didn't go well** (2-3 items, each with root cause and remediation plan)
5. **Key decisions needed** from the board (max 2, with your recommendation)
6. **Forward outlook** (next 90 days, 3 priorities with measurable targets)
7. **Ask** (if any: intro requests, hiring help, strategic guidance)

### T-3: Internal review (1 hr)
Share draft with co-founder(s) and CFO/finance lead.
Questions to stress-test:
- "Is anything here going to surprise a board member?" (If yes, call them before the meeting)
- "Are we hiding bad news in good framing?" (Don't)
- "Can a board member act on our ask?" (If not, sharpen it)

### T-1: Pre-read distribution
Send board memo + appendix by 5 PM the day before.
Never present information for the first time in the meeting.

### T-0: Meeting day
- First 5 min: "What questions do you have from the pre-read?"
- Do NOT re-present the memo. They read it. If they didn't, that's a board composition problem.
- Spend 80% of meeting time on the 1-2 decisions that need board input
- Last 10 min: closed session (board without founders), then founder feedback

### T+1: Follow-up (1 hr)
Within 24 hrs, send:
- Meeting minutes with decisions made
- Action items with owners and deadlines
- Any materials board members requested

## Board Memo Quality Bar
- Total length: 3-5 pages, not 30 slides
- Every claim backed by a number
- Bad news stated plainly, not buried
- Forward-looking sections have specific milestones, not vague aspirations
- Visual: one dashboard screenshot, max 2 charts
