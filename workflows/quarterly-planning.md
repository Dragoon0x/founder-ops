# Quarterly Planning Workflow

## Trigger
Starts 2 weeks before quarter end.

## Phase 1: Reflection (Days 1-3)

### Step 1: Grade last quarter's OKRs
Score each Key Result 0.0-1.0:
- 0.0-0.3: Failed. Why? External factor or execution gap?
- 0.4-0.6: Partial. What got in the way?
- 0.7-1.0: Hit. Was the target ambitious enough?

Target average score: 0.6-0.7. If you're consistently hitting 0.9+, your OKRs are sandbagged.

### Step 2: Post-mortem the misses
For each KR scored below 0.4:
- Was the target wrong? (Bad forecasting)
- Was the approach wrong? (Bad strategy)
- Was the execution wrong? (Bad ops)
- Was there a surprise? (Uncontrollable external factor)

Each bucket requires a different fix. Don't apply execution fixes to strategy problems.

### Step 3: Collect wins
Document every meaningful win, quantified:
- Revenue impact ($ or %)
- Efficiency gain (hours saved, cost reduced)
- Strategic progress (partnerships signed, markets entered)
- Team development (hires made, promotions, skill gaps closed)

## Phase 2: Planning (Days 4-8)

### Step 4: CEO writes the strategic context memo
One page. Covers:
- What changed in the market this quarter
- What we learned about our customers
- What our competitive position looks like
- The 1-2 big bets for next quarter and why

This memo is the input to all team planning. Without it, teams plan in a vacuum.

### Step 5: Team leads draft OKRs
Each team proposes 2-3 Objectives with 3-4 Key Results each.
Rules:
- KRs must be measurable (number, date, or binary)
- At least one KR per objective should scare you slightly
- No KR should be business-as-usual work renamed as an OKR
- Each KR needs a designated owner (one person, not a team)

### Step 6: Cross-functional alignment session (half day)
All team leads in one room.
- Each team presents their OKRs (5 min per team)
- Identify dependencies: "I need X from team Y by date Z"
- Identify conflicts: two teams want the same resource
- Resolve or escalate to CEO

### Step 7: Resource allocation
Map headcount and budget to objectives:
- If an objective doesn't have resources allocated, it's not real
- If resources are spread across > 5 objectives, you're not focused enough
- The uncomfortable question: "What are we NOT doing this quarter?"

## Phase 3: Finalization (Days 9-10)

### Step 8: Publish OKRs
- Company-wide doc, accessible to everyone
- Each OKR has: owner, target, current baseline, check-in cadence
- CEO presents at all-hands: here's what we're doing and why

### Step 9: Set up tracking
- Weekly check-in rhythm for each KR
- Monthly progress review in exec sync
- Automated dashboard where possible
- Calendar the mid-quarter gut-check (week 6)

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Total company OKRs: 3-5 objectives, not 15
- [ ] Each KR has a baseline number and target number
- [ ] Dependencies identified and owners agreed
- [ ] At least one objective directly tied to revenue
- [ ] At least one objective focused on product/technical foundation
- [ ] Resource conflicts resolved, not deferred
- [ ] "What we're NOT doing" list published alongside OKRs
