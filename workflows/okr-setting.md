# OKR Setting Workflow

## Trigger
Quarterly, aligns with the quarterly-planning workflow.

## OKR Architecture

### Company level: 3-5 Objectives
Each Objective:
- Qualitative, inspiring, and time-bound
- Answers: "What do we need to achieve this quarter?"
- Not a metric (that's a Key Result)

Each Objective has 3-4 Key Results:
- Quantitative, measurable, time-bound
- Answers: "How will we know we achieved the Objective?"
- Has a clear starting baseline and target number
- Owned by one person (even if a team contributes)

### Team level: 2-3 Objectives per team
- At least one must map to a company Objective
- One can be team-specific (technical debt, process improvement, team health)

### Individual level: Optional
For ICs, OKRs are often unnecessary overhead. Better to have clear expectations and regular 1:1s. Use individual OKRs only for senior ICs or people on development plans.

## OKR Quality Checklist

### Good OKR example:
**Objective**: Win the mid-market segment
- KR1: Increase mid-market ARR from $400K to $700K
- KR2: Close 15 new mid-market accounts (100-500 employees)
- KR3: Reduce mid-market sales cycle from 45 days to 30 days
- KR4: Achieve 90% activation rate for new mid-market accounts within 14 days

### Bad OKR example:
**Objective**: Improve the product
- KR1: Ship more features
- KR2: Reduce bugs
- KR3: Make customers happier

Problems: Objective is vague. KRs are not measurable. No baselines. No targets. "More" and "happier" are not numbers.

### OKR scoring
Score each KR at end of quarter on 0.0-1.0 scale:
- 0.0: No progress
- 0.3: Some progress but fell short
- 0.5: Made significant progress, about halfway
- 0.7: Hit a good target (this is the sweet spot)
- 1.0: Nailed it completely

Target average across all KRs: 0.6-0.7
- Average > 0.8: Your goals aren't ambitious enough
- Average < 0.4: Goals were unrealistic or execution broke down

## Common OKR Failure Modes
1. **Too many OKRs**: > 5 objectives = no focus. If everything is a priority, nothing is.
2. **OKRs as task lists**: Key Results should be outcomes, not outputs. "Ship feature X" is a task. "Increase activation by 15%" is an outcome.
3. **Set and forget**: OKRs reviewed only at quarter-end. Fix: weekly check-ins on KR progress.
4. **No hard conversations**: When a KR is clearly going to miss by week 4, do something about it. Don't wait until week 13 to acknowledge reality.
5. **Gaming**: Teams pick easy KRs to look good. Fix: calibration + stretch targets.
