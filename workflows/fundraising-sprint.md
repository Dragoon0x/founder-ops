# Fundraising Sprint Workflow

## Trigger
Board + CEO decision to raise. Typically triggered when runway < 12 months or strategic opportunity requires capital.

## Pre-Sprint (Weeks -4 to -1)

### Materials preparation
Build these before you talk to a single investor:
1. **Deck** (12-15 slides): Problem, solution, market, traction, team, model, ask
2. **Data room**: Financials (P&L, cash flow, projections), cap table, key contracts, org chart
3. **Memo** (2-3 pages): For investors who prefer reading over slides
4. **One-pager**: For warm intro emails

### Target list construction
Build a tiered list:
- **Tier 1** (10-15 firms): Dream investors. Thesis match. Portfolio synergy. Check size fits.
- **Tier 2** (15-20 firms): Good fit. Less brand, but smart money.
- **Tier 3** (10-15 firms): Viable alternatives. Angels, smaller funds.

For each investor, document:
- Partner name (the specific human, not the firm)
- Thesis relevance (which of their blog posts maps to your space?)
- Portfolio conflicts (do they have a competitor?)
- Warm intro path (who in your network knows them?)

### Warm intro requests
Draft the forwardable email:
- 3 sentences max
- What you do (one sentence)
- Why it's working (one metric)
- Why this investor specifically (one sentence)

Send intro requests 1 week before sprint starts. Batch them so meetings cluster.

## Sprint Execution (Weeks 1-6)

### Week 1-2: First meetings
- Schedule 8-12 first meetings
- Front-load Tier 2 firms (practice rounds, not throwaways)
- Tier 1 firms in week 2-3 once you've refined your pitch
- Take notes after every meeting: objections raised, questions asked, vibe

### Week 3-4: Partner meetings & deep dives
- Interested firms request data room access, customer calls, technical deep dives
- Respond to every data request within 24 hrs
- Schedule customer reference calls (pre-brief your customers)
- Track momentum: firms that don't schedule a follow-up within 5 business days are likely passing

### Week 5-6: Term sheets & negotiation
- Target: 2-3 term sheets for leverage
- Key terms to negotiate: valuation, board seats, pro-rata rights, liquidation preferences, anti-dilution provisions
- Have your lawyer review every term sheet (budget $5-15K for legal)
- Decision deadline: give yourself 72 hrs, not 2 weeks

### If it's not working by week 4
Be honest with yourself:
- < 25% of first meetings converting to second meetings: Your pitch or market positioning is off
- Lots of "interesting but too early": You don't have enough traction for this round size
- Lots of "come back when you have X": Listen to what X is. Is it achievable in 3-6 months with current runway?

Options: pause the raise, reduce the round size, bridge with existing investors, or pivot the narrative.

## Post-Close (Week 7-8)

### Closing mechanics
- Sign term sheet
- Due diligence (1-2 weeks): legal, financial, IP review
- Definitive docs: stock purchase agreement, investor rights agreement, board consent
- Wire: confirm receipt, update cap table

### After the money hits
- Internal announcement (same day)
- Public announcement (if desired, coordinate with lead investor's comms team)
- Board seat setup: first board meeting within 30 days
- Deploy plan: present 12-month plan to new board within 60 days
- Thank every person who made an intro, even the ones that didn't convert

## Metrics to Track During Raise
| Metric | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Meetings per week | 6-10 | < 3 |
| 1st → 2nd meeting conversion | > 40% | < 20% |
| Time from 1st meeting to term sheet | 3-4 weeks | > 6 weeks |
| Data room requests | > 50% of 2nd meetings | < 25% |
| Term sheets received | 2-3 | 0 after 5 weeks |
