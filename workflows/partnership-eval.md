# Partnership Evaluation Workflow

## Trigger
Inbound partnership inquiry or strategic decision to pursue a specific partnership.

## Framework: PARTNER Score

Rate each dimension 1-5. Minimum viable score: 20/35.

| Dimension | 1 (Bad) | 3 (Okay) | 5 (Great) |
|-----------|---------|----------|-----------|
| **P**roduct fit | No integration path | Some overlap | Natural extension |
| **A**udience overlap | < 10% | 30-50% | > 70% |
| **R**evenue potential | < $10K/yr | $50-200K/yr | > $500K/yr |
| **T**echnical effort | > 3 months eng | 1-2 months | < 2 weeks |
| **N**etwork effects | One-time value | Linear growth | Compounding |
| **E**xecutive alignment | No champion | Mid-level sponsor | C-suite champion |
| **R**isk | High dependency | Moderate | Low, reversible |

## Evaluation Process (1-2 weeks)

### Step 1: Initial qualification (Day 1-2)
30-min call with the partner. Answer:
- What does each side get? (If you can't articulate mutual value in 2 sentences, pass)
- What's the integration surface? (API, co-marketing, bundling, referral?)
- Who's the internal champion on their side? (No champion = no deal)
- What's their timeline? (If they want something in 2 weeks and you need 2 months, misalignment)

### Step 2: Due diligence (Day 3-7)
- Check their product: sign up, use it, read reviews
- Check their customers: overlap with yours? Complementary or competitive?
- Check their financials: are they funded? Growing? Stable?
- Check references: talk to 1-2 of their existing partners
- Check their team: who would you work with day-to-day? Are they competent and responsive?

### Step 3: Structure negotiation (Day 8-10)
Define:
- Revenue share or referral fee structure
- Integration responsibilities (who builds what)
- Marketing commitments (co-branded content, events, launches)
- Data sharing boundaries (what's shared, what's not, who owns what)
- Exclusivity terms (avoid exclusivity unless the upside is massive)
- Term length and exit clauses (start with 6-12 months, renewable)

### Step 4: Legal review (Day 11-14)
Partner agreement covering:
- IP ownership (each side owns their own, joint work is specified)
- Confidentiality
- Data processing terms (if customer data flows between systems)
- Liability caps
- Termination notice period (60-90 days)

## Post-Partnership Tracking
Monthly review for first 6 months:
- Leads generated
- Revenue attributed
- Integration usage (API calls, co-feature adoption)
- Customer satisfaction with integration
- Partner responsiveness (support quality, SLA adherence)

Kill criteria: if after 6 months the partnership hasn't generated measurable value (revenue, users, or strategic positioning), sunset it gracefully.
