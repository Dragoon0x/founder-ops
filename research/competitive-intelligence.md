# Competitive intelligence

## Purpose
Track competitors enough to know what's true and what's noise. Don't track them
so much that you start chasing their roadmap.

## What to actually track

| Signal | Source | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Pricing changes | Their pricing page | Monthly |
| Major launches | RSS / blog / Twitter | Weekly |
| Hiring (signals strategy) | LinkedIn jobs | Monthly |
| Job postings by team (where are they investing) | LinkedIn jobs | Quarterly |
| Funding announcements | Crunchbase, Pitchbook | Monthly |
| G2 / Capterra reviews trend | Direct | Quarterly |
| Customer wins / losses | Press, LinkedIn announcements | Monthly |
| Headcount estimates | LinkedIn employee count | Quarterly |
| Documentation changes | Their docs site (use Wayback) | Quarterly |

## What to ignore

- Their tweets unless they announce something concrete
- Their conference talks (high signal-to-noise but rare)
- Their employees' personal opinions
- Their TAM claims (everyone inflates)
- Their growth claims (most are massaged)

## Build a battlecard per top-3 competitor

See `templates/battlecard-template.md`. The 1-page battlecard contains:
- One-sentence positioning of theirs
- Three things they do well
- Three places they're weak (verifiable)
- The trap they set for prospects (and your counter)
- The trap you set for prospects (and their counter)
- Pricing comparison
- Common objections from their customers
- Common objections from your customers about them

Update quarterly. Battlecards rot fast.

## Competitive interview synthesis

When you lose a deal to a competitor, do a 30-min loss interview within 7 days.
Ask:
1. What made you choose them?
2. What did we do that almost won it?
3. What did they do that won it?
4. If their pricing doubled, would you still have chosen them?
5. What would have made you choose us?

Aggregate quarterly. Patterns are more useful than individual losses.

## When you win, also ask

Why did you choose us over [competitor]? The answers tell you your real
positioning, which is rarely what's on your homepage.

## Win/loss ratios by competitor

Track this. If you win 70% against competitor A and 20% against competitor B,
you have very different relationships with each. The strategy for each is
different.

## Failure modes

1. **Roadmap-by-competitor** — they shipped X, so we should ship X. Almost
   always wrong. Their X is for their customers, not yours.
2. **Battlecard theater** — fancy battlecards no one uses. Make them 1 page.
3. **Tracking too many** — pick top 3. Watch the rest casually.
4. **Defensive positioning** — "We do X, but better." Real positioning is
   orthogonal to competitors, not relative to them.
5. **Lying to yourself about strengths/weaknesses** — your battlecard is wrong
   about you, too. Get external review.
