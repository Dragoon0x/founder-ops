# United Kingdom and Ireland

## Business culture
- More polite, less direct than US. "I have some minor concerns" often means major objection. "We'll take it under advisement" means no.
- Understatement is a feature. If a UK investor says "quite interested," that's strong enthusiasm.
- Long-winded email etiquette. Get to the point in paragraph 2, not paragraph 1.

## Hiring (UK)
- No at-will employment. Statutory notice period from day 1 (1 week) increasing with tenure. Typical contracts: 1-3 months notice.
- Employees accrue rights after 2 years (unfair dismissal protections). Before 2 years, still need a fair reason + process.
- Redundancy (layoffs) requires consultation period: 30 days for 20+ employees, 45 days for 100+.
- Statutory holiday: 28 days minimum including public holidays. Most tech companies offer 25+8.
- Pension auto-enrolment: employer must contribute min 3% of qualifying earnings.
- IR35 rules for contractors: HMRC will look through the contract. Get it wrong and you owe back PAYE + NI.

## Hiring (Ireland)
- Similar to UK but Ireland has mandatory sick leave (5 days paid in 2026, up from 3).
- Corporation tax 12.5% — major attraction for EMEA HQ.
- Working Time Act caps at 48 hrs/week averaged over 4 months.

## Fundraising
- SEIS: £250K/company, 50% income tax relief to investor, CGT exemption. Magic tool for UK pre-seed. Must be pre-trading or <2 years.
- EIS: £5M/year, 30% tax relief. Standard UK seed/Series A investor sweetener.
- Advanced Assurance from HMRC before raising saves headaches. Takes 4-6 weeks.
- Typical UK seed: £500K-£2M. Series A: £3M-£10M. Valuations typically 20-30% below US equivalents.

## Equity
- EMI options: most tax-advantaged scheme globally for startups. Must qualify (gross assets < £30M, < 250 employees).
- Set strike price at Unapproved valuation reasonable. HMRC tends to accept 409A-style logic.
- Standard 4-year vest, 1-year cliff, same as US.

## Taxes
- Corporation tax 25% (UK) / 12.5% (Ireland). Small profits rate 19% if < £50K profit (UK).
- VAT: 20% UK / 23% Ireland. Must register if turnover > £90K (UK) / €37.5K services or €75K goods (IE).
- R&D tax credit (UK): SME scheme 186% deduction. Worth a lot in early years.

## Data protection
- UK GDPR + Ireland GDPR (EU). Fines up to 4% global turnover or €20M.
- Must have a DPO if core activities involve large-scale processing. Most B2B SaaS doesn't.
- SCCs / adequacy decisions for data transfers. UK-US Data Bridge live but review annually.

## Foot-guns
- Firing someone in the probation period without process. Still need reasons documented.
- Offering "contractor" role to someone who works only for you 40hrs/week. HMRC will reclassify.
- Not registering for VAT quickly enough. Penalties are painful.
- Using US legal templates for UK employment contracts. They don't hold.
