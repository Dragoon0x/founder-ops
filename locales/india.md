# India

## Market character
- Two separate markets: domestic Indian enterprise/SMB (different buyer, price points, sales cycle) and Indian teams selling to the US/EU. Don't confuse them.
- Relationship-driven sales. First meeting is rapport, not pitch. 2-3 meetings minimum before serious discussion.
- Price sensitivity is extreme in domestic SMB. Enterprise less so but procurement is grueling.

## Entity setup
- Private Limited Company is the standard. Two directors minimum, one must be Indian resident.
- OPC (One Person Company) possible but restricted.
- LLP common for services firms but less VC-friendly.
- Registration via MCA. Takes 2-4 weeks. DIN/DSC required.
- GIFT City (IFSC) is a special zone with US-like regulatory regime. Relevant for deep-tech and fintech.

## Flip structures (common pattern)
- Indian startups selling globally often set up Delaware C-corp parent + Indian subsidiary. Called a "flip."
- Must be done carefully — reverse flip from Delaware to India triggers taxes. Government has been easing this.
- RBI approval required for outbound remittance > certain limits.

## Hiring
- Notice periods are culturally 30-90 days. Often negotiable but not always.
- Provident Fund (PF): 12% employer + 12% employee on basic up to ₹15K. Mandatory if 20+ employees.
- ESI mandatory if salary < ₹21K/month.
- Gratuity: 15 days salary per year worked, payable after 5 years.
- Notice buyout common (company pays to shortcut notice).
- Firing: no at-will, but "non-compliance with probation" widely used. Be careful — termination disputes go to labor courts.

## Compensation (2026 typical, for global-tier engineer)
- Junior SWE (0-2 yr): ₹8-15 LPA ($10K-$18K)
- Mid SWE (3-5 yr): ₹18-35 LPA ($22K-$42K)
- Senior SWE (6-10 yr): ₹40-80 LPA ($48K-$96K)
- Staff/Principal: ₹80 LPA-₹2 Cr ($96K-$240K) — FAANG-adjacent comp in metros
- Equity uptake weak — cash still dominant. Need to explain options patiently.

## Taxes
- Corporate tax: 25% for domestic companies (with turnover < ₹400 Cr), 22% under concessional regime, 15% for new manufacturing companies. DDT abolished.
- GST: 18% standard on most software/SaaS. Export of services zero-rated (with LUT).
- Startup India eligible companies: 3 years tax holiday within first 10 years + other concessions.
- TDS everywhere. 10% on software payments typically.

## Customer nuance (domestic enterprise)
- Net 90-180 payment terms common at Indian enterprises. Plan working capital accordingly.
- Invoice formatting strict under GST. Get it wrong and customer can't claim input credit.
- Purchase orders mandatory. No PO, no payment.

## Foot-guns
- Hiring via contractor to avoid PF/ESI. Labor inspectors will reclassify. Plus employees win in disputes.
- Not registering for GST before ₹20L turnover. Penalties and interest.
- Sending employment offer emails without formal appointment letters. Appointment letter is the legal doc.
- Ignoring POSH Act (Prevention of Sexual Harassment). Internal Complaints Committee mandatory at 10+ employees. Penalties and reputational risk significant.
