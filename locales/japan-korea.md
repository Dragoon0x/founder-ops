# Japan and South Korea

## Japan
- KK (Kabushiki Kaisha) standard entity. GK (Godo Kaisha) simpler, lower prestige.
- Rep office / branch possible but limiting for growth.
- Corporate tax: ~30% effective.
- Consumption tax: 10% (8% for food). Required to register > JPY 10M.
- Hiring: no formal at-will. Dismissal requires "reasonable grounds" (sommokuteki goriteki riyu). Practically very hard.
- Typical notice: 30 days, but cultural norm is longer + mutual agreement.
- Bonus system: 2x/year (summer + winter), each ~1-3 months salary. Factor into total comp planning.
- Labor unions less confrontational than EU but present.
- Business culture: decisions consensus-driven (nemawashi). Formal first meeting, exchange business cards (meishi) with both hands. Long sales cycles — 6-18 months typical for enterprise.
- Payment terms: Net 30-60. Punctual to the day.

## Korea
- Chusik Hoesa (Inc.) standard, Yuhan Hoesa (LLC) possible.
- Corporate tax: 9-24% progressive.
- VAT: 10%.
- Labor: 4-week severance per year worked. Mandatory severance into retirement pension if 4+ employees.
- 40-hour work week cap (52 including overtime) enforced since 2018.
- Chaebol dominance still strong in enterprise procurement. Network access matters.
- Fast decision-making once approved at top. Top-down culture.
- Hiring: loyal talent, lower churn than SF. Equity adoption rising but still cash-heavy.

## Cross-cutting
- Business cards (meishi / myungham) are identity documents. Treat with respect.
- Age and seniority matter in communication register. Get honorifics wrong = disrespect.
- Contracts in local language always. English version is reference only in disputes.
- Entertainment (dinners, karaoke) part of trust-building. Alcohol-adjacent norms easing post-COVID but still relevant.
