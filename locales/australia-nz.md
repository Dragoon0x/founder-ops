# Australia and New Zealand

## Australia
- Pty Ltd is standard. ACN from ASIC required. One Australian resident director required.
- Corporate tax: 25% (base rate) or 30% (larger companies > AUD 50M turnover).
- GST: 10%. Register > AUD 75K turnover.
- Superannuation: 11.5% employer contribution (rising to 12% by July 2025). Non-negotiable, separate from salary.
- Fair Work Act — strong employee protections. Unfair dismissal protection after 6 months (12 months for small biz < 15 employees).
- Long service leave: 6+ weeks paid after 7-10 years depending on state.
- ESOP rules improved in 2022-2024 for startups. Taxing-point moved from grant to exit for qualifying companies.
- R&D tax incentive: 43.5% refundable credit for companies < AUD 20M turnover. Major lever.

## New Zealand
- Limited company via Companies Office. Very fast (~1 day) registration.
- Corporate tax: 28%.
- GST: 15%.
- KiwiSaver: voluntary but common employer 3% matching.
- Employment: 90-day trial period legal for new hires (documented in contract). Easier to part ways in first 90 days.
- 4 weeks statutory holiday + 11 public holidays.

## Cross-cutting
- Directness close to US but warmer delivery. Honest feedback expected.
- Flat hierarchy culturally. Calling founders by first name in formal settings normal.
- Time zones brutal for US collaboration. AEDT/NZDT ~16-18 hrs ahead of PST.
- Small market domestically. Strategy often "launch AU/NZ, then US or Asia."
