# Canada

## Entity
- Federal incorporation (CBCA) or provincial (OBCA in Ontario, most common for startups).
- Director residency requirement: federal 25% Canadian, Ontario no longer requires Canadian residency (since 2021). Québec requires 1 resident director.

## Taxes
- Corporate tax: ~26.5% combined federal + provincial (varies).
- Small Business Deduction: 9% federal on first CAD 500K active business income. Requires CCPC status. Foreign ownership breaks CCPC.
- GST/HST: 5-15% depending on province. Register > CAD 30K revenue.
- SR&ED tax credit: 35% refundable for CCPCs on first CAD 3M R&D. Huge. Requires technical documentation.

## Hiring
- No at-will. Common-law notice (employers owe "reasonable notice" at termination). Without clear contract, courts often award 1 month per year worked + more for senior roles.
- Proper termination clauses in contracts are critical. Wallace damages / Bhasin-Honda damages if bad faith.
- Statutory holidays: 5-9 depending on province.
- Vacation: 2 weeks minimum statutory, 3 weeks typical tech.

## Fundraising
- BDC, OVIN, NRC-IRAP grants — significant non-dilutive for deeptech / hardware.
- Québec has strong subsidy ecosystem (Investissement Québec, tax credits for multimedia and AI).
- Valuations typically 15-25% below US equivalents at seed; gap narrows by Series B.

## Foot-guns
- CCPC status lost if US VCs take controlling stake. Plan for it; don't accidentally trip it.
- Ignoring provincial registrations when hiring remote-from-home employees in new provinces.
- Québec has specific French language requirements for consumer-facing businesses (Bill 96). Contracts, product copy.
