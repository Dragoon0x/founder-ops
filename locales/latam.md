# Latin America (Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, Argentina)

## Mexico
- S.A. de C.V. standard entity. 2 shareholders minimum.
- Corporate tax: 30%. IVA (VAT): 16%.
- PTU: 10% of pre-tax profit distributed to employees annually. Real cost.
- INFONAVIT, IMSS contributions ~27-35% of payroll on top of salary.
- Aguinaldo: 15 days salary minimum as Christmas bonus. Standard is 30.
- Termination: 3 months salary severance + 20 days per year worked + 12 days per year seniority premium. No at-will.

## Brazil
- Ltda or S.A. standard. Brazilian resident director required.
- Corporate tax: ~34% effective (IRPJ + CSLL).
- Heavy labor law (CLT). 13th salary (mandatory), 1/3 extra on vacation (mandatory).
- FGTS: 8% of salary to a government fund per month. Paid out on termination.
- Terminating without cause: 40% fine on accumulated FGTS + advance notice.
- Simples Nacional regime: simplified taxes if revenue < R$4.8M (~$900K). Huge for early stage.

## Colombia
- SAS (Sociedad por Acciones Simplificada) — flexible, single shareholder ok. Fast setup (1-2 weeks).
- Corporate tax: 35%.
- Medellín and Bogotá main tech hubs. Rising quality for remote dev teams.
- Labor: cesantías (severance fund, 8.33% annually), prima de servicios (half-month bonus twice a year), vacaciones (15 days).

## Argentina
- Economic volatility. Peso devaluation ongoing. Cost of USD-hedged comp skyrockets.
- Strong engineering talent, often priced below Mexico/Colombia due to FX.
- SA or SRL entity. Setup slow.
- Complex payroll taxes. PEOs (Deel, Remote, Ontop) widely used.
- Blue chip swap / CCL for USD repatriation — common but needs counsel.

## Cross-cutting
- Spanish is default except Brazil (Portuguese). Translation matters — don't machine-translate contracts.
- Relationship-heavy. Quick rapport, long trust-building, lots of in-person where possible.
- Work hours: later than US. Lunch 1-2 hours normal.
- Senior talent priced at 40-60% of US comp for comparable experience; early-career closer to 20-30%.
