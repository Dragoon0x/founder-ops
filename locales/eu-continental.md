# EU Continental (Germany, France, Netherlands, Nordics)

## Overall character
- Employee protection heavy. Firing is slow and expensive everywhere.
- Works councils (Betriebsrat in DE, CSE in FR) have real power above certain thresholds.
- Strong preference for written agreements, stamped documents, formal processes.

## Germany
- GmbH is the standard entity. €25K minimum share capital (half paid up at registration).
- UG (haftungsbeschränkt) allows €1 capital but you must retain 25% of profits until you reach €25K. Looks second-tier.
- Dismissal protection kicks in after 6 months for companies with 10+ employees.
- Works council required at 5+ permanent employees if employees request it.
- Vacation: 20 days statutory, 25-30 typical.
- ESOP is painful: phantom stock common (virtual shares, payout on exit) because real options are tax-disadvantaged.
- Corporate tax: ~30% effective (15% federal + trade tax 14-17%).

## France
- SAS is the startup-friendly entity. SASU for sole founder.
- BSPCE stock options: only company-shares scheme with reasonable tax treatment. Requires French tax resident at grant, company < 15 years old, < 25% publicly traded.
- CDI (permanent contract) is the default. Very hard to terminate. CDD (fixed-term) strictly limited.
- Severance grows with tenure: 1/4 month per year for first 10, then 1/3 month. Plus damages for wrongful dismissal.
- 35-hour work week. Overtime paid at 125-150%.
- JEI (Jeune Entreprise Innovante) status: massive social charge exemptions for R&D staff. Huge lever if you qualify.
- Corporate tax: 25%.

## Netherlands
- BV is standard. €0.01 minimum capital.
- 30% ruling for foreign hires: 30% of salary tax-free for 5 years if they meet income threshold. Changing in 2026-2027 — check current rules.
- Termination requires either UWV approval (economic grounds) or cantonal court (personal grounds). Mutual termination agreement (beëindigingsovereenkomst) is the fast path. Usually costs 1 month/year worked.
- Corporate tax: 19% on first €200K, 25.8% above.
- Innovation Box: IP income taxed at 9% effective. Big lever for deeptech.

## Nordics (Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Finland)
- Flat culture, consensus-driven, high trust. Expect strong pushback before alignment, then rock-solid execution.
- Fika (Sweden) and similar rituals matter. Skipping them reads as antisocial.
- High taxes on comp — aggressive equity programs harder. Qualified stock options exist (SE: Kvalificerade Personaloptioner) with limits.
- Works council requirements at 50+ in Sweden.
- Very strong parental leave rights (480 days in Sweden, split between parents).

## Common foot-guns across continental EU
- Hiring via PEO without local entity eventually breaks at scale. Works for 2-3 hires; painful at 10+.
- Offering "contractor" status to full-time workers. Reclassification risk massive. Germany (Scheinselbstständigkeit) and France (salariat déguisé) enforce hard.
- Using English-only contracts where local language required (FR explicitly requires French translation for employees).
- Not understanding that "notice period" is non-negotiable cost of firing, not a guideline.
