# MENA (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt)

## UAE (Dubai, Abu Dhabi)
- Free zones (DIFC, ADGM, DMCC) offer 100% foreign ownership, 0% corporate tax up to AED 375K profit, then 9% (from 2023).
- Mainland company now allows 100% foreign ownership in most sectors.
- No personal income tax. VAT: 5%.
- Emiratization quotas in mainland entities with 50+ skilled workers. Fines if not met.
- Employment: end-of-service gratuity (21 days per year first 5 years, 30 days after). Labor Card + Emirates ID required for employees.
- Golden Visa 10-year for founders, investors, specialized talent.

## Saudi Arabia
- Massive transformation under Vision 2030. Regional Headquarters program requires foreign companies bidding on gov contracts > SAR 1M to have RHQ in KSA.
- MISA license required for foreign ownership. 100% possible in most sectors.
- Corporate tax: 20% on foreign-owned share + 2.5% Zakat on Saudi share.
- No income tax. VAT: 15%.
- Saudization quotas by sector (Nitaqat). Fines and license restrictions for non-compliance.
- Friday-Saturday weekend. Ramadan reduced hours (6h/day).

## Egypt
- LLC most common. 100% foreign ownership possible.
- Corporate tax: 22.5%. VAT: 14%.
- FX controls — repatriation can be slow. Dollar availability sensitive to macro.
- Very price sensitive consumer market. Strong engineering talent at ~30-40% of USD-adjusted costs.
- Cairo and Alexandria main hubs. Strong Arabic-English bilingual skills.

## Cross-cutting
- Relationships first, always. Two or three meetings before substantive business talk.
- Prayer times during the day. Don't schedule through Friday prayers in particular.
- Written agreements are mandatory; handshake deals reframe as opening positions.
- Legal system mixes civil code + sharia (for certain matters). Use specialist counsel.
- Public sector dominated in Saudi, more private-led in UAE.
