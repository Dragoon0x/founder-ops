# Ceremony: Internal Demo Day

## Purpose
Teams showcase what they've built. Celebrates shipping. Creates cross-functional visibility. Inspires the team.

## Frequency
Bi-weekly or monthly, 45-60 min.

## Format

### Structure
| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Opening | 5 min | Quick context: what the team has been focused on |
| Demos | 30-40 min | 3-5 demos, 6-8 min each |
| Audience reactions | 10 min | Questions, kudos, ideas |
| Closing | 5 min | What's coming next |

### Demo format
Each demo follows:
1. **Problem** (30 sec): What user problem are we solving?
2. **Solution** (30 sec): What did we build?
3. **Live demo** (3-5 min): Show it working. Use real data if possible.
4. **Impact** (30 sec): What metrics do we expect to move?
5. **What's next** (30 sec): Next iteration or follow-up

### Rules
- Live demos only. No slides about features. Show the thing.
- Celebrate shipped work. Don't demo mockups or plans.
- Anyone can demo. Not just engineers. Design, support, ops, sales.
- Keep it informal. Applause and reactions are encouraged.

## Why It Matters
- Engineers see the impact of their work on customers
- Non-technical teams understand what's being built and why
- Creates a culture of shipping (if we demo bi-weekly, we must ship bi-weekly)
- Surfaces ideas and feedback from unexpected places

## Anti-Patterns
- Only engineering demos (everyone ships; everyone demos)
- Slide presentations instead of live demos (show, don't tell)
- Demos of planned work, not shipped work (kills the "shipping culture" signal)
- Canceling when "not enough to show" (demo a bug fix, a process improvement, a customer win)
