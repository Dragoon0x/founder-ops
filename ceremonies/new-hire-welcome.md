# Ceremony: New Hire Welcome

## Purpose
Make every new team member feel valued and set up for success from day one.

## Timing
First day and first week.

## Day 1 Ceremony

### Welcome message (before start date)
- Personal email from CEO: "Here's why we're excited you're joining"
- Slack channel introduction (with fun facts the new hire shared during interview)
- Buddy assigned: a peer who answers all the "dumb" questions

### First morning (60 min)
| Time | Activity | Who |
|------|----------|-----|
| 9:00 | Welcome coffee/chat | Hiring manager |
| 9:15 | Equipment setup + access | IT/Ops |
| 9:30 | Tour (physical or virtual) | Buddy |
| 9:45 | Meet the team (casual, 2 min per person) | Team |
| 10:00 | Onboarding doc walkthrough | Hiring manager |

### First day essentials
By end of day 1, the new hire should:
- [ ] Have all equipment and software access working
- [ ] Know where to find documentation
- [ ] Have their first 1:1 scheduled with their manager
- [ ] Have lunch/coffee with at least 2 team members
- [ ] Have a 30-day plan with clear goals
- [ ] Feel welcomed, not overwhelmed

## First Week Structure
| Day | Focus | Key Activity |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Welcome + setup | Orientation, meet the team |
| 2 | Product deep dive | Use the product as a customer, guided by PM |
| 3 | Domain context | Customer recordings, market overview, competitive landscape |
| 4 | First contribution | Small, completable task that ships |
| 5 | Reflection | 30-min check-in with manager: "How was week 1?" |

## 30-60-90 Day Milestones

### 30 days
- Understands the product, market, and key metrics
- Has shipped at least 1 meaningful contribution
- Has met all cross-functional counterparts
- Can articulate the company's strategy in 2 sentences

### 60 days
- Operating independently on most tasks
- Contributing to team discussions and decisions
- Identified one improvement opportunity and proposed a solution

### 90 days
- Fully productive (performing at the level expected for the role)
- Has built relationships across the organization
- Manager and new hire agree on ongoing goals

## Anti-Patterns
- **Sink or swim**: No onboarding plan, just "figure it out" → Fix: 30-60-90 plan ready before they start
- **Death by meetings**: 8 hours of presentations on day 1 → Fix: spread context over the first 2 weeks
- **No buddy**: New hire has no one to ask small questions → Fix: assign a buddy who checks in daily for the first 2 weeks
- **Delayed access**: Laptop or software not ready → Fix: IT checklist completed 3 days before start date
