# Ceremony: 1:1 Meeting

## Purpose
The most important meeting a manager has. It's the employee's meeting, not the manager's. The goal is to surface concerns, provide coaching, and build trust.

## Frequency
Weekly, 30 min. Same time, same day. Cancel only as a last resort.

## Attendees
Manager + direct report.

## Agenda Template

### Check-in (5 min)
"How are you doing?" (Not about work. About them.)
Listen. Don't problem-solve unless asked.

### Their topics (15 min)
The employee brings 1-3 topics. Examples:
- A decision they need your input on
- A concern about the team or project
- A career development question
- Feedback on a process or tool
- Something they're proud of

Manager listens, asks clarifying questions, provides input when asked.

### Your topics (5 min)
Manager brings 0-1 topics. Examples:
- Feedback on a specific deliverable
- Context about a company decision
- A heads-up about an upcoming change
- Recognition for good work

### Career development (5 min, monthly)
Once a month, dedicate part of the 1:1 to career growth:
- "Where do you want to be in 12 months?"
- "What skills do you want to develop?"
- "What's one thing you'd like to take on that you're not doing today?"

## Facilitation Tips
- Take notes. Follow up on action items from last week.
- Don't cancel. Consistent 1:1s build trust. Inconsistent ones erode it.
- If the employee says "everything's fine" every week, something is wrong. Dig deeper.
- Don't turn it into a status update. You have standups for that.
- If you have difficult feedback, deliver it here. Not in public. Not in Slack.

## Anti-Patterns
| Pattern | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Manager talks 80% of time | It's not their meeting | Ask questions, then stop talking |
| Only discuss tasks | Misses the human element | Start with "how are you?" every time |
| Cancel frequently | Signals the employee doesn't matter | Reschedule, don't cancel |
| Avoid hard topics | Issues fester | "I have something difficult to share. Can we talk about it?" |
