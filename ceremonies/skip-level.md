# Ceremony: Skip-Level Meeting

## Purpose
A meeting between a leader and their direct reports' reports. Provides unfiltered signal about team health, manager effectiveness, and cultural issues that managers might not surface.

## Frequency
Monthly or quarterly, 30 min per person (or small group of 3-4).

## Format Options

### Individual skip-level (30 min)
Best for: teams > 20 people, when you want candid feedback.
Questions:
1. "What's going well on your team?"
2. "What's frustrating or slowing you down?"
3. "How well does your manager support you?"
4. "What would you change about how we work?"
5. "Is there anything you want me to know?"

### Group skip-level (45-60 min)
Best for: teams < 20 people, when you want group discussion.
Same questions, but people hear each other's perspectives. Can surface systemic issues.

## Ground Rules
- Confidentiality: feedback is kept anonymous unless the person explicitly asks you to act on it directly
- Not a reporting channel: this is not for going "over your manager's head" with decisions
- Not an escalation path: operational issues go through the manager
- Follow-up: share themes (not names) with the manager to help them improve

## Facilitation Tips
- Be warm, curious, and non-judgmental
- Don't react defensively to criticism of the company or leadership
- Don't promise action on every piece of feedback, but do acknowledge it
- If you hear consistent negative feedback about a specific manager, address it with the manager directly (without identifying sources)

## Anti-Patterns
- Using skip-levels to bypass managers (undermines their authority)
- Never acting on feedback (people stop sharing)
- Only doing skip-levels when there's a problem (should be proactive)
