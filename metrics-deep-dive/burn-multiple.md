# Burn Multiple

The cleanest capital efficiency metric. Popularized by David Sacks (Craft Ventures).

## Formula

Burn Multiple = Net Burn / Net New ARR

Over a trailing period (monthly or quarterly).

## Interpretation

| Burn Multiple | Verdict |
|---------------|---------|
| < 1 | Outstanding — you're generating more ARR than you're burning |
| 1-1.5 | Great |
| 1.5-2 | Good |
| 2-3 | Suspect — review spend |
| > 3 | Bad — spending doesn't convert to growth |

## Example

Q1 net burn: $1.5M
Q1 net new ARR: $1.2M
Burn Multiple = 1.5 / 1.2 = 1.25 → Great

## Why this metric matters

It directly answers: for every dollar we burn, how much ARR do we add?

Low burn multiple means your spending is productive. High burn multiple means your spending isn't creating growth — you're paying rent on top of what growth you'd have had anyway.

## Burn Multiple vs Magic Number

- **Magic Number:** net new ARR × 4 / prior quarter S&M. Sales-specific.
- **Burn Multiple:** net new ARR / net burn. Whole-company.

Burn Multiple is broader: includes R&D, G&A, infra. More accurate for overall capital efficiency.

## What drives a bad Burn Multiple

1. Hiring ahead of revenue (common)
2. Over-investment in experimental products
3. Inefficient go-to-market (long sales cycles with low close rates)
4. High G&A spend for stage
5. Ongoing churn eating gross ARR

## How to improve

- Pause hiring until ARR catches up
- Focus on highest-converting channels
- Raise prices on new deals
- Cut costs ruthlessly (see runway-extension-moves.md)
- Improve retention (higher net new ARR from same gross)

## Reporting

Monthly to board and internally. Show trailing 3-month and 12-month windows.

If trailing 3-month burn multiple > 2 and 12-month is < 1.5, you have a recent issue. If trailing 12-month > 2, structural.

## Burn Multiple at scale

Expect slight deterioration as you scale (easy wins → harder ones). Acceptable range by stage:

| ARR | Healthy Burn Multiple |
|-----|----------------------|
| <$1M | 2-3 (experimenting) |
| $1-5M | 1.5-2.5 |
| $5-20M | 1-2 |
| $20-50M | 0.8-1.5 |
| $50M+ | <1 |
