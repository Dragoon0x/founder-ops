# Magic Number

A single ratio that captures sales efficiency. Developed by Scale Venture Partners. Still one of the cleanest SaaS health metrics.

## Formula

Magic Number = (Net new ARR in quarter × 4) / (S&M spend in PRIOR quarter)

Why prior quarter for S&M: acknowledges that investment creates pipeline that closes 1 quarter later.

## Interpretation

| Range | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| > 1.5 | Invest harder — you're leaving growth on the table |
| 1.0-1.5 | Invest at current pace — healthy |
| 0.5-1.0 | Slow down new hires, optimize current investment |
| < 0.5 | Pull back hard — investment is unprofitable |

## The intuition

Magic number of 1 means: for every $1 you spend on S&M this quarter, you get $1 of annualized ARR back the next quarter. You'll pay back the investment in 12 months at 100% gross margin. With real gross margin (75%), payback is 16 months.

Magic number > 1 = faster payback. Magic number < 1 = slower.

## Why it's useful

- Single number, easy to track
- Forward-looking (helps decide whether to hire more reps)
- Captures efficiency, not just growth
- Works at any stage

## Limitations

- Quarterly data noisy at early stage
- Doesn't capture retention quality
- Doesn't distinguish new from expansion ARR
- Assumes S&M spend is the driver (organic can distort)

## How it moves with scale

Typical trajectory:
- Pre-product-market-fit: < 0.5 (you're investing before revenue reliably converts)
- Early PMF: 0.5-1.0
- Scale mode: 1.0-1.5
- Maturity: 0.7-1.0 (as you saturate easy ICP)
- Post-dominance: 0.5-0.8 (diminishing returns)

Few companies hold >1.5 for long. It usually signals you're under-investing in sales capacity.

## Decision rules

- If Magic Number > 1.5 for 2 consecutive quarters: accelerate hiring (sales reps, marketing)
- If Magic Number drops below 0.5: pause sales hiring, review channels
- If quarter-over-quarter decline > 0.3 for 2+ quarters: investigate structural issue

## Benchmarks

- Public SaaS median: 0.6-0.8
- Top quartile SaaS: 1.0-1.3
- Best-in-class: 1.5+

Snowflake was ~2.0 during peak growth. Exceptional.
