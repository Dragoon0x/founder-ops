# FCF Margin

Free cash flow margin. The cleanest measure of capital efficiency for a mature business.

## Formula

FCF Margin = Free Cash Flow / Revenue

Free Cash Flow = Operating Cash Flow - CapEx

Or more simply: cash generated by operations after investing in PP&E.

## Why not EBITDA margin?

EBITDA excludes:
- Stock-based comp (real dilution)
- CapEx
- Changes in working capital

In SaaS, EBITDA often looks better than FCF because of stock comp. In hardware, EBITDA looks better because of CapEx.

FCF margin is more honest.

## Benchmarks

| Stage | FCF Margin |
|-------|-----------|
| Burning (pre-scale) | -30% to -100% |
| Approaching break-even | -10% to -30% |
| Break-even | 0% to -10% |
| Profitable | 5-15% |
| Mature best-in-class | 20-35% |
| Elite (Adobe, Microsoft) | 35%+ |

## FCF margin and Rule of 40

R40 = growth + FCF margin. The two combine. A 60% grower with -20% FCF margin = 40 (passes). A 20% grower with 20% FCF margin = 40 (passes).

Different paths. Same bar.

## Common gaming

- Non-GAAP FCF that adds back restructuring, one-time items
- Excluding stock comp (classic SaaS trick)
- Deferring CapEx to juice the quarter
- Capitalizing software development costs (legal but distorts)

Use consistent definitions. Report both GAAP and non-GAAP when relevant.

## FCF vs earnings

GAAP earnings can diverge from FCF significantly. In SaaS with heavy annual prepay:
- FCF > earnings (you collect cash ahead of revenue recognition)
- Deferred revenue is a tailwind

In hardware:
- FCF < earnings (you pay suppliers ahead of customer payment)
- Working capital is a headwind

Know which direction your business bends.

## When to care

- Late Series B onwards (pre-IPO)
- Any company that claims to be profitable
- Comparing to public comps
- Planning toward break-even

Pre-Series A: FCF margin is mostly theater. Focus on burn multiple.
