# Rule of 40

The standard SaaS efficiency rule: growth rate + profit margin should equal or exceed 40%.

## Formula

Rule of 40 = YoY growth rate (%) + FCF margin (%) ≥ 40

Or, acceptable variants:
- Growth rate + EBITDA margin
- Growth rate + operating margin

FCF is preferred because it reflects actual cash.

## Examples

| Company | Growth | Margin | R40 |
|---------|--------|--------|-----|
| A | 80% | -40% | 40 ✓ |
| B | 40% | 0% | 40 ✓ |
| C | 20% | 25% | 45 ✓ |
| D | 60% | -30% | 30 ✗ |
| E | 10% | 20% | 30 ✗ |

Company A is growing hard, burning hard. Still passes.
Company D is growing hard but burning too hard.
Company E is profitable but growing too slow.

## Why this rule exists

Public SaaS investors discovered: growth AND efficiency both matter, and companies that balance both tend to outperform companies that optimize one dimension.

Pre-2021: growth at any cost was acceptable.
Post-2022: Rule of 40 (or stricter) is the new bar.

## When it applies

- Late Series B+ companies
- Post-$10M ARR
- Any company approaching profitability

Doesn't apply to:
- Pre-revenue
- Seed/Series A (growth is the only goal)
- Companies in a pivot

## Common gaming

- Using GAAP revenue growth (includes services) to inflate growth %
- Using non-GAAP margins that exclude stock comp, restructuring, etc.
- Picking a baseline quarter that's artificially low

Use consistent definitions. Compare apples to apples.

## The new bar

In 2024-2025, best-in-class public SaaS target Rule of 60-80, not 40. 40 has become a floor, not a target.

Private companies approaching IPO or late-stage raises should target 50+ at minimum.

## Related metrics

- Rule of X (various), where X is a bespoke threshold
- EV/revenue multiples correlate with Rule of 40 score
- Durable growth > efficient growth > unprofitable growth

## Limitations

- Two companies with same R40 can have very different trajectories (high growth + losses vs. mid growth + profit)
- Doesn't capture quality of growth (NRR, retention)
- Single-period metric — trend matters

Report R40 quarterly with growth and margin separately. The composite can hide both.
