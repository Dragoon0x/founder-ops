# Sales Efficiency

Sales is the most expensive function in SaaS. Measure it ruthlessly.

## Key metrics

### Rep attainment

% of reps hitting quota.

- Target: 60-70% of reps at or above quota
- <50% = quotas too high, or hiring wrong profile, or ICP off
- >80% = quotas too low (leaving money on the table)

### Ramp time

Time from rep hire to full productivity.

- SMB: 3-4 months
- Mid-market: 4-6 months
- Enterprise: 6-9 months

Measure per rep. If >30% over benchmark, onboarding is broken.

### Quota attainment by tenure

| Tenure | Expected attainment |
|--------|---------------------|
| 0-3 months | 0-30% (ramp) |
| 3-6 months | 40-70% |
| 6-12 months | 80-100% |
| 12+ months | 100%+ |

## Sales productivity per rep

Annual ARR generated per rep.

Benchmarks (by ACV segment):
- SMB ($5k-20k ACV): $800k-1.5M per AE
- Mid-market ($20k-100k ACV): $1M-2M per AE
- Enterprise ($100k+ ACV): $1.5M-4M per AE

Below these = efficiency issue.
Above these = quota too low OR elite sales team (rare).

## Cost per rep

Fully-loaded cost per AE:
- Base $150-250k
- OTE $250-400k (with commission at target)
- Benefits, tools, management allocation: +30% on base
- Ramp cost: 6-12 months of cost before full productivity

Total: $300-500k/year.

## Sales-to-CS ratio

Reps per CSM varies:
- SMB: 1 CSM per 5-10 AEs (CSM serves many accounts)
- Mid-market: 1 CSM per 2-4 AEs
- Enterprise: 1 CSM per 1-2 AEs (maybe dedicated CSM per whale)

## Pipeline per rep

Each rep should own 3-4x pipeline coverage of their quarterly quota.

If rep has $300k quarterly quota, needs $900k-1.2M pipeline.

## Close rate (win rate)

% of qualified opps that close as won.

Benchmarks:
- SMB: 20-30%
- Mid-market: 15-25%
- Enterprise: 10-20%

Below benchmark = qualification is weak OR sales motion is weak OR competitor too strong.

## Sales cycle length

Median time from SQL to close:
- SMB: 14-30 days
- Mid-market: 30-90 days
- Enterprise: 90-270 days

Longer cycle = more working capital needed, more pipeline needed.

## Bookings vs billings

- **Bookings:** contract value signed
- **Billings:** revenue billed (hits AR)
- **Revenue:** recognized over time

These can diverge. 3-year contract worth $300k = $300k bookings, $100k billings (if billed annually), $100k revenue/year for 3 years.

Report all three.

## Red flags

- Rep productivity declining quarter over quarter
- Ramp times extending (onboarding problem)
- Win rate dropping (ICP drift, competitor pressure, or pricing issue)
- 2+ reps missing by >30% (likely manager/systemic problem, not reps)
- One rep at 300% of quota (territory imbalance OR they're eating others' deals)

## Sales comp design

Key choices:
- Base/variable split: 50/50 for AEs is standard
- Accelerators: 1.5-2x commission above 100% of quota
- Quota: 3-5x OTE (so rep who hits quota is profitable to company)
- Clawbacks for churn within 12 months: strongly recommended
- SPIFs (short-term incentives): for specific launches, sparingly
