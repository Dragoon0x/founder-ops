# Lead Velocity Rate (LVR)

Forward indicator for pipeline. Popularized by Jason Lemkin.

## Formula

LVR = (qualified leads this month - qualified leads last month) / qualified leads last month

Percentage growth, month over month, in qualified leads.

## Why it matters

Revenue is a lagging indicator. LVR is a leading indicator. If LVR is growing 10-20% MoM, revenue will follow 3-6 months later (for SaaS with 3-6 month sales cycle).

If LVR stalls, revenue will stall 3-6 months from now.

## Target ranges

| LVR | Verdict |
|-----|---------|
| > 20% MoM | Strong growth ahead |
| 10-20% MoM | Healthy |
| 5-10% MoM | Slowing; investigate |
| 0-5% MoM | Warning sign |
| Negative | Revenue will drop in 3-6 months |

## Prerequisites for the metric to be meaningful

1. Consistent definition of "qualified lead" (MQL with criteria)
2. Attribution model that's stable
3. Pipeline stage criteria that don't shift
4. Channel mix not dramatically changing

## How to use LVR

- **Early signal:** if LVR drops, act on marketing/outbound NOW, don't wait 3-6 months for revenue impact
- **Hiring trigger:** LVR consistently >15% MoM → hire another BDR/AE to capture flow
- **Channel ROI:** compute LVR by channel to see which channels are growing capacity

## Failure modes

- Counting unqualified leads (bloats numerator)
- Changing lead definitions (invalidates comparisons)
- Seasonal swings (November vs January in B2B — use YoY comparison)
- One-time campaign spikes (one webinar = not real LVR)

## Combined with pipeline coverage

LVR tells you pipeline is growing. Pipeline coverage tells you if you have enough for this quarter. Watch both.

If LVR is 15% and coverage is 3.5x, you're in good shape for 2 quarters.
If LVR is -5% and coverage is 3x, you're okay this quarter but in trouble next.
