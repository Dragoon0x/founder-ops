# Churn (all flavors)

Four types. Most founders conflate them.

## Gross churn (logo / revenue)

**Gross logo churn:** % of customers who cancelled, no expansion credit.

**Gross revenue churn:** % of MRR that cancelled, no expansion credit.

Both are bounded at 0-100%. Lower is better.

## Net churn (revenue)

**Net revenue churn:** gross revenue churn minus expansion revenue from existing customers.

Net revenue churn can be NEGATIVE (expansion exceeds churn). Negative net churn = NRR > 100%. Holy grail.

## Which to report

- **Internal health:** gross logo churn AND gross revenue churn AND net revenue churn (all three, always)
- **Board:** gross revenue churn + NRR (these tell the complete story)
- **Investors (fundraise):** NRR usually headlines. Back it with gross retention for credibility.

## Benchmarks (annual)

| Segment | Gross logo churn | Gross rev churn | NRR |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|-----|
| Consumer subscription | 30-60% | 30-60% | 70-100% |
| SMB SaaS | 10-25% | 8-20% | 90-110% |
| Mid-market SaaS | 5-15% | 3-12% | 105-125% |
| Enterprise SaaS | 3-8% | 2-6% | 110-140% |
| Best-in-class any | 2-5% | 1-3% | 125%+ |

## Why logo churn ≠ revenue churn

You can lose 20% of logos but only 5% of revenue if the small accounts churn. Or lose 5% of logos and 30% of revenue if a whale churns.

Report both to avoid this trap.

## Churn reasons (categorize weekly)

Standard categories:
1. Budget cut / economic
2. Product didn't deliver value
3. Competitor replaced us
4. Missing features
5. Poor support
6. Champion left / org change
7. Underutilization (they never really used it)
8. M&A (acquired / merged)
9. Pricing / ROI
10. Going out of business

Categorize every churned account. Aggregate monthly. The top 2 categories tell you what to fix.

## Voluntary vs involuntary churn

- **Voluntary:** customer chose to cancel
- **Involuntary:** credit card failed, customer didn't respond to dunning

Involuntary is usually 15-30% of consumer churn and 3-10% of B2B. Retrievable with better payment handling.

## Churn cohort vs churn rate

A 5% monthly churn rate hides that your newest cohort churns 15% in month 1 and your oldest cohort churns 2%.

Use cohorts for diagnosis. Use churn rate for reporting.

## Predicting churn

Signals (in order of predictive power):
1. Declining product usage (30-90 day window)
2. Champion role change (LinkedIn monitoring)
3. Support ticket volume spike with negative sentiment
4. Non-responsive to QBRs
5. Payment failures
6. Downgrade events
7. Competitor signal (showed up on a competitor's webinar list)

Build a churn risk model. Top 20% riskiest accounts get proactive outreach.

## Saves

A good CS team saves 30-50% of at-risk churn. Tactics:
- Discount extensions (not permanent discounts)
- Pause instead of cancel (consumer)
- Product usage training
- Executive sponsor outreach
- Pricing negotiation

Track save rate. If < 20%, motion isn't working.
