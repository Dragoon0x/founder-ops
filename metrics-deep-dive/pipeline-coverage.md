# Pipeline Coverage

How much pipeline you need to hit your quota. Mistaken pipeline math is how most sales teams miss.

## Formula

Pipeline Coverage = (open pipeline) / (quarter's sales target)

## The standard rule

You need 3-4x pipeline coverage to hit plan.

Why: typical close rate on qualified pipeline is 25-33%. So to close $1M, you need $3-4M in pipeline.

## By segment

| Segment | Typical close rate | Coverage needed |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| SMB transactional | 20-30% | 3-5x |
| Mid-market | 15-25% | 4-6x |
| Enterprise | 10-20% | 5-7x |

Longer sales cycles = lower close rates = more coverage needed.

## Pipeline stages

Not all pipeline is equal. Standard stages:

1. Lead (MQL) — inbound or outbound identified
2. SQL — qualified, has budget/authority/need/timing (BANT) or equivalent
3. Opportunity — active sales motion
4. Proposal — formal proposal sent
5. Commit — verbal commit from buyer
6. Closed (won or lost)

Convert each stage to "effective pipeline" using historical close rates:

- Lead × 10% = effective pipeline
- SQL × 20% = effective pipeline
- Opportunity × 30%
- Proposal × 50%
- Commit × 80%

Sum effective pipeline. Divide by quarter target. If >1, you're on track. If <1, you won't hit plan.

## What kills pipeline

1. **Stale deals.** Anything not moving in 30 days is suspect. Not moving in 60 = probably dead.
2. **Optimistic stage assignments.** Reps promote deals to "proposal" when they're really still "lead." Audit weekly.
3. **Missing exit criteria.** Every stage should have explicit criteria to advance. Without them, pipeline is vibes.
4. **Relying on few big deals.** One $500k deal slipping is a quarter missed. Diversify.

## Pipeline velocity

Pipeline velocity = (deals in pipeline × win rate × avg deal size) / sales cycle length

This is the revenue you generate per unit time. Improve any of the four inputs and velocity goes up.

## Weekly forecast discipline

Every week, rep calls:
- Commit: deals rep is >80% confident will close this quarter
- Best case: deals rep hopes will close
- Pipeline: everything qualified
- Excluded: unqualified or dead

Sales leader rolls up commit from all reps. Historical calibration: commit should close 80-90%. If reps consistently commit deals that don't close, recalibrate their definition.

## Red flags

- Pipeline coverage < 3x with 4+ weeks left in quarter = will miss
- Pipeline coverage > 5x but close rates dropping = quality issue
- Avg deal size growing but velocity falling = picking fights you can't win
- 50% of pipeline in one stage (say "proposal") for 30+ days = motion is stuck
