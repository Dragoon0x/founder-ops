# North Star Metric

The single metric that best captures your product's core value. Get this right, everything else aligns.

## What makes a good north star

1. **Captures customer value.** Not internal efficiency — customer outcome.
2. **Leading indicator of revenue.** Growth in the metric predicts revenue growth 3-6 months later.
3. **Single metric or clean composite.** If you say "revenue and engagement and NPS," you have no north star.
4. **Teams can influence it.** Every team should see how their work moves it.
5. **Hard to game.** Incentives align with real customer value.

## Examples

| Company | North Star |
|---------|-----------|
| Airbnb | Nights booked |
| Facebook | Daily active users |
| Spotify | Time spent listening |
| LinkedIn | Weekly active users |
| Slack | Messages sent per team per day |
| Uber | Rides taken |
| Stripe | Total payment volume processed |
| Zillow | Home tours |
| HubSpot | Weekly active customers |

Notice: these are outcomes customers care about, not revenue. Revenue follows.

## The selection framework

1. List 10 candidate metrics.
2. Rate each on: captures customer value, leading vs lagging, single vs composite, influenceable, hard-to-game.
3. Top scorer = candidate.
4. Test: does it move correctly in last 24 months of data vs revenue?
5. Test: if this metric improves 20%, does revenue improve 20% (or more)?

## Common bad north stars

- **Revenue / ARR:** lagging, captures outcome but not value
- **Total users / total signups:** vanity, easily gamed
- **Total features shipped:** internal, not customer-facing
- **NPS:** directional, not movable at scale
- **Website traffic:** top of funnel only

## Input metrics

North star is the output. Input metrics are what teams influence:

- Airbnb: nights booked ← listings × search-to-book rate × repeat rate × avg nights per booking
- Slack: messages per team per day ← new teams × activation rate × messages per active user

Each input metric owned by a team. Teams optimize inputs. Inputs multiply to north star.

## Changing the north star

You'll need to change it 1-3 times in company lifetime. Signals:

- Business model shifts (consumer → B2B)
- Product expansion beyond original scope
- The metric becomes misleading at scale
- You hit a local max and need a different lens

When you change, show both old and new for 2 quarters.

## Reporting cadence

- **Weekly:** north star + key input metrics (to all of company)
- **Monthly:** north star + breakdown by segment
- **Quarterly:** trends, input contribution analysis
- **Annually:** did the north star correctly predict business health?

## One page to rule them all

The single-page summary every Monday should have:

- North star: current value, vs last week, vs 4-week trend
- 3-5 input metrics: same
- Key leading indicators (LVR, activation)
- One cautionary metric (churn, support volume)

If this single page doesn't tell you health, you have the wrong metrics.
