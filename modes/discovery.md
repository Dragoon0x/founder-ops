# Mode: Customer Discovery

## Purpose

Help the founder conduct customer discovery interviews that actually produce
insights, not just confirmations. Avoid the 3 biggest discovery mistakes:
leading questions, talking about your solution, and selection bias.

## The Discovery Stack

### 1. Pre-Interview Prep

Before any interview, produce:
- **Interview objective** (1 sentence: what decision will this help make?)
- **Hypotheses to test** (3-5 specific beliefs about the customer)
- **Pre-mortem** (what would make this interview fail?)
- **Question guide** (10-15 questions, not a script)

### 2. The Question Framework (Mom Test + Customer Discovery)

**Great questions:**
- About their life and past behavior: "Walk me through the last time you..."
- About specific pain: "What's the hardest part of [X]?"
- About workarounds: "What are you doing instead today?"
- About motivation: "Why did you start looking for a solution?"

**Bad questions (never ask):**
- "Would you use this?" (they always say yes)
- "Do you like this feature?" (confirmation bias)
- "How much would you pay?" (useless without behavior)
- "Would you pay $X?" (leading)

### 3. Post-Interview Synthesis

After each interview, extract:
- **Signals** (direct quotes, not paraphrases)
- **Problems mentioned unprompted** (high signal)
- **Workarounds described** (highest signal)
- **Who they'd buy from today** (competitive map)
- **One sentence they said that changed your thinking**

### 4. After 5-10 Interviews: Pattern Detection

Look for:
- **Problems mentioned by 3+ customers** (real pattern)
- **Magnitude of pain** (nice-to-have vs hair-on-fire)
- **Willingness to change** (paying for workarounds = buying signal)
- **Segment fit** (which customer archetype has the most pain?)

```
## Interview Prep — [Customer Name] — [Date]

Hypotheses:
1. [Belief about them]

Questions:

Red flags to watch for:
- [What would indicate you're leading them]
- [What would indicate politeness, not truth]

```

```
| Problem | Mentioned By | Unprompted? |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| [problem] | 7/10 | 5 unprompted |

### Segment patterns
- [Segment A] pain points differ from [Segment B]...

- N customers explicitly asked "when can I buy this?"

### Recommendation
[What to build next based on this data, confidence 1-10]

### What would change my mind
[Evidence that would flip the recommendation]
```

## Adversarial Check

For every recommendation based on discovery:
- Could this be selection bias? (you interviewed only people already interested)
- Are you hearing what you want to hear?
- Are problems severe enough to pay for solutions?
- Would these people actually buy, or are they just polite?
