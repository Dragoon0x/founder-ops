# Mode: Calendar & Meeting Ops

## Purpose

Prep for meetings that matter. Write follow-ups that move things forward.
Run agendas that don't waste time.

## Submodes

### 1. Meeting Prep

Before any meeting > 15 minutes:

```
## Meeting Prep — [Subject] — [Date]

### Context
- Who: [names + roles]
- What: [meeting purpose in one sentence]
- Goal: [what decision or outcome you need]

### Pre-reading (done)
- [Previous emails/notes relevant]
- [Their company/product/recent news]
- [Last meeting's outcomes]

### Your 3 priorities for this meeting
1. [Most important thing to land]
2. [Second thing]
3. [Third thing]

### Their likely priorities
[Based on research — what do they want from this?]

### Key questions to ask
- [Question that moves the conversation forward]
- [Question that reveals their thinking]
- [Question that tests assumptions]

### Potential objections / pushback
- [Likely objection 1] → [your response]
- [Likely objection 2] → [your response]

### Materials to have open
- [Deck, doc, dashboard, demo]

### Pre-agreed agenda (if sent)
[3-4 items with time allocations]

### Success definition
[This meeting succeeds if...]
```

### 2. Agenda Templates

**30-min decision meeting:**
```
5 min — Context / pre-read alignment
15 min — Options + tradeoffs discussion
5 min — Decision
5 min — Next steps / owners / dates
```

**60-min review meeting:**
```
10 min — Recap + context
30 min — Status update + discussion
10 min — Risks / blockers
10 min — Decisions + next steps
```

**1:1 (weekly):**
```
5 min — What's top of mind for them (their agenda)
10 min — Blockers / support needed
10 min — Their growth / development
5 min — Feedback both directions
```

### 3. Follow-Up Drafts

After every external meeting, send within 24 hours:

```
Subject: Re: [meeting subject] — follow-up

[Name],

Great to connect today. Quick summary of what we discussed:

### What we agreed
- [Specific commitment 1]
- [Specific commitment 2]

### My action items (by [date])
- [Your commitments]

### Your action items (by [date])
- [Their commitments — makes it explicit]

### Open questions
- [Questions still to resolve]

### Next step
[Specific: another meeting? Async follow-up? Decision by date?]

Thanks,
[Name]
```

### 4. Calendar Hygiene

Weekly calendar audit:

- [ ] Am I spending >50% on customer-facing work (sales, support, interviews)?
- [ ] Am I spending >20% on deep work (no meetings)?
- [ ] Do I have 30+ min buffer between back-to-back meetings?
- [ ] Are my recurring meetings still needed?
- [ ] Can any 60-min meeting be a 30-min meeting?
- [ ] Can any meeting be an async Loom or doc?

**The meeting test:**
Can this meeting be:
1. An email? (One-way info sharing)
2. A Loom? (Visual explanation)
3. A doc? (Requires thought before response)
4. A decision log entry? (Just needs sign-off)

If yes to any, cancel the meeting.

### 5. Time Blocks

Weekly time allocation for early-stage founders:

| Activity | % of time | Hours/week |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Customer conversations (sales, support, interviews) | 30-40% | 12-16 |
| Product/engineering work | 20-30% | 8-12 |
| Fundraising (if active) | 10-30% | 4-12 |
| Hiring | 10-15% | 4-6 |
| Team 1:1s and leadership | 10-15% | 4-6 |
| Deep work (strategy, writing) | 10-15% | 4-6 |

Red flags:
- >50% in meetings
- 0% deep work blocks
- No time for customer conversations
- Same meeting cadence as last quarter (when company was smaller)
