# Mode: Culture Document

## Purpose

Write the document that explains how your company actually works.
Not aspirational fluff. Concrete, behavioral, applied.

The best examples: Netflix Culture Deck, Stripe Culture Doc, GitLab Handbook.

## What a Culture Document IS

- A description of how decisions get made
- A description of how people get promoted, fired, raised
- A description of how disagreements are resolved
- A description of what's celebrated and what's punished
- A document new hires read on day 1 and reference forever

## What a Culture Document IS NOT

- A list of generic values everyone has ("integrity", "innovation")
- An aspirational vision of who you want to be
- A marketing document for recruiting
- A static document written once and forgotten

## The Sections

### 1. How We Make Decisions

**Document your real decision-making model:**

- Who has authority over what?
- When do decisions need consensus vs single-owner?
- What's the escalation path?
- How fast do we expect decisions to be made?

**Example (made up):**
> Engineering tech choices: Tech lead decides, after consulting team.
> Product priorities: PM decides, after consulting eng + design + sales.
> Hiring: Hiring manager decides, with VP approval for senior roles.
> Pricing changes: CEO decides, with input from sales + product.
> Disagree-and-commit timeline: 1 week of debate, then decision is final.

### 2. How We Communicate

- Sync vs async expectations
- Response time expectations by channel (Slack: 4hr, email: 24hr, etc)
- Meeting culture (defaults, agenda requirements, recording)
- Document culture (what gets written down vs what stays in chat)
- Notification etiquette (when to @-mention, when not)

### 3. How We Hire

- The hiring bar (specifically — what does "great" look like?)
- The interview process (stages, who's involved, time commitment)
- The promotion criteria (what does each level mean?)
- The references we check and don't check
- Compensation philosophy (50th / 75th / 90th percentile?)

### 4. How We Give Feedback

- 1:1 cadence (weekly mandatory? monthly?)
- Performance review cadence (continuous? quarterly? annual?)
- Public vs private feedback expectations
- How disagreements with managers get resolved
- The "calibration" process for ratings/promotions

### 5. How We Pay

- Compensation philosophy: percentile we target
- Equity philosophy: who gets it, how much, vesting terms
- Bonus structure (or absence)
- Raise process (annual? tied to perf review? merit-based?)

### 6. How We Treat Time Off

- PTO policy (specific days vs unlimited — and what unlimited actually means)
- Holiday calendar
- Sabbatical policy (if any)
- Sick leave / mental health days
- Parental leave (be specific — weeks, % pay)

### 7. How We Work Remotely (or not)

- Office days expected (specific days, not "flexible")
- Core hours (overlap windows)
- Travel expectations
- Co-working stipends (if remote)
- Async-first vs sync-first practices

### 8. How We Handle Conflict

- Disagreement protocols
- Escalation paths
- HR/People function (or absence)
- Mediation processes
- What happens if a manager and report can't resolve

### 9. How People Leave

- Resignation expectations (notice period — be specific)
- Severance policy (if any — explicit, not "case by case")
- Equity treatment on departure (cliff, vested portion)
- Reference policy (what we will / won't say)
- Re-hiring policy

### 10. What We Won't Compromise On

The 3-5 things that, if violated, lead to firing regardless of performance:

Examples:
- Lying to colleagues, customers, or investors
- Treating support staff disrespectfully
- Sharing customer data inappropriately
- Discrimination or harassment
- Insider trading-equivalent behavior with company information

## The Honesty Test

For every statement in your culture doc, ask:
- **Is this what we actually do today?** (Not what we wish we did.)
- **Would current employees agree?** (Survey them.)
- **Will I fire someone for violating this?** (If not, it's not a value.)

If you can't answer yes to all three, rewrite or remove.

## Update Cadence

- Quarterly: Audit for accuracy
- Annually: Major revision based on what's changed
- After significant events: Update relevant section

## Distribution

- Public: Recruiting page, company website
- Onboarding: Day 1 reading
- Hiring: Send to candidates BEFORE final interview
- Annually: All-hands review

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Fix |
|--------------|--------------|-----|
| Generic values nobody references | Wastes the doc | Behavioral, specific, applied |
| Aspirational lies | Erodes trust | Document reality, change reality if needed |
| Written by HR, never read | Performative | Written by founders, used in decisions |
| Static document | Becomes irrelevant | Living doc, version-controlled, dated |
| 50 pages long | Nobody reads | 10-15 pages max, cuttable to 3-page summary |
