# Mode: Investor & Stakeholder Updates

## Purpose

Write monthly investor updates that maintain trust, signal progress honestly,
and make asks specific. Board decks for board meetings. Stakeholder comms
for team/employees.

## Investor Update (monthly)

**Structure:**
```
Subject: [Company] — [Month] [Year] Update

## Highlights (3 bullets max)
- [Most important thing that happened]
- [Second most important]
- [Third]

## Key Metrics
| Metric | Last month | This month | Trend |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| MRR | $X | $Y | ↑ Z% |
| Customers | X | Y | ↑ Z |
| Net retention | X% | Y% | → |
| Burn | $X | $Y | → |
| Runway | X months | Y months | → |

## What's Working
[2-3 things working well, specific]

## What's Not Working / Concerns
[Be honest. This is what builds trust.]

## Asks
[Specific asks for intros, feedback, help. Not "let me know if you have thoughts."]

## Looking Ahead (next month priorities)
1. [Specific, measurable]
2. [Specific, measurable]
3. [Specific, measurable]

## Thank You
[One sentence + sign-off]
```

**Rules:**
- **Monthly, on time, every time.** Consistency matters more than perfection.
- **Lead with honesty.** Hide nothing. Investors find out anyway.
- **Specific asks.** "Intros to X, Y, Z" not "any help appreciated."
- **Numbers, not adjectives.** "We grew 34%" not "great month."
- **Under 500 words.** Investors skim. Make every word count.

## Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| "Record month!" without numbers | Empty | 
| Skipping concerns | Loses trust |
| "Excited to announce" (for everything) | Loses meaning |
| Vague asks ("feedback welcome") | Gets ignored |
| Burying bad news in paragraph 7 | Betrays trust |
| Inconsistent metrics (different numbers each month) | Looks shady |

## Board Deck

For quarterly board meetings, 10-15 slides max:

1. **Agenda** (what we'll cover)
2. **Key metrics dashboard** (one page, all the numbers)
3. **Quarter highlights** (3-5 bullets)
4. **Quarter lowlights** (what didn't work)
5. **Deep dive 1** (biggest opportunity or risk)
6. **Deep dive 2** (biggest opportunity or risk)
7. **Financials** (MRR, burn, runway)
8. **Hiring** (open roles, recent hires, upcoming)
9. **Next quarter goals** (3-5 specific targets)
10. **Discussion topics** (where you want input)
11. **Open discussion**

**Rules:**
- Pre-read the deck 48 hours before meeting
- Meeting is for DISCUSSION, not presentation
- Have a "decisions needed" section explicit
- Track decisions and commitments
- Send follow-up within 48 hours

## Stakeholder Comms (Team)

**Weekly all-hands update:**
```
## Week of [date]

### What shipped
[Specific things]

### What's up next
[Focus for next week]

### Company context
[Anything the whole team should know]

### Shoutouts
[Recognition for specific work]
```

**Rules:**
- Same format every week (predictability builds trust)
- Celebrate specifics, not generalities
- Share hard news early, not later
- Be honest about uncertainty
- Invite questions explicitly

## Fundraise Announcements

If announcing a raise, optimize for:
- Recruiting (top candidates will see this)
- Customer confidence (shows you'll be around)
- Partnership opportunities
- NOT: Ego

**Structure:**
```
- One line: what we raised, from whom, at what stage
- Why this matters for customers (specific)
- What we're doing with the money (3-5 concrete things)
- Who we're hiring
- One sentence about the journey
- Thanks to specific people (team, investors, customers)
```

Under 300 words. Let the news speak. Don't over-celebrate.
