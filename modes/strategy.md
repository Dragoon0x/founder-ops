# Mode: Strategy

## Purpose

Define and refine company strategy. Where to play, how to win, what to
say no to. Most early-stage companies don't have strategy — they have
opportunities and confusion.

## The Strategy Stack (Hambrick & Fredrickson)

Five questions every company must answer:

### 1. Arenas (Where will we be active?)
- Which customers?
- Which geographies?
- Which use cases?
- Which channels?
- Which technologies?

### 2. Vehicles (How will we get there?)
- Internal development?
- Partnerships?
- Acquisitions?
- Open source?
- Product-led vs sales-led?

### 3. Differentiators (How will we win in chosen arenas?)
- Product (features, design, performance)
- Price (premium, value, free)
- Service (white glove, self-serve, community)
- Positioning (the brand, the message)
- Speed (time to value, support response)

### 4. Staging (What's our sequence?)
- What do we do first?
- What unlocks what?
- What can wait?
- What's the order of operations?

### 5. Economic Logic (How will we generate returns?)
- Where does the value come from?
- Who pays for it?
- What are the unit economics?
- Where's the moat?

## Strategy Documents

### The 1-Page Strategy

```
## [Company] Strategy — [Year]

### Mission
[One sentence: why we exist]

### Vision (3-year)
[Where we're trying to get]

### This year's North Star
[One specific measurable target]

### Where we play
- Customers: [specific segment]
- Geography: [specific markets]
- Problem: [specific pain]

### How we win
- Differentiator: [the one thing we're best at]
- Why this is defensible: [moat]

### What we say NO to
- [Tempting but distracting opportunity]
- [Tempting but distracting opportunity]
- [Tempting but distracting opportunity]

### This quarter's 3 priorities
1. [Specific, measurable]
2. [Specific, measurable]
3. [Specific, measurable]

### What would force us to change strategy
[Specific market signals or internal conditions]
```

### The Strategic Bet

For each major strategic bet:
```
## Bet: [Name]

### Hypothesis
[What we believe about the world]

### Why we think this is right
[Evidence: data, customer signal, market trend]

### Why we might be wrong
[Counter-evidence we've seen]

### What we're committing
- People: [N]
- Money: $[X] over [Y]
- Time: [duration]

### Success criteria
[Specific metrics + timeline]

### Kill criteria
[When we'd abandon this bet]

### Reversibility
[Type 1 or Type 2 — see decisions mode]
```

## The "What We Don't Do" List

Maintain an explicit list of things you've decided NOT to do.
This is more powerful than a roadmap.

```
## What We Don't Do (Q[X] [Year])

- We don't pursue [opportunity] because [reason]
- We don't sell to [segment] because [reason]
- We don't compete in [category] because [reason]
- We don't build [feature] because [reason]
- We don't use [channel] because [reason]
```

When a new opportunity comes in: check this list.
If it's on the list: easy no.
If it's tempting AND not on the list: discuss + add to list.

## Strategy Reviews

**Quarterly strategy review (90 min):**
1. Review last quarter's bets — outcomes vs hypothesis
2. Are our arenas still right? (or are we drifting?)
3. Is our differentiator still working? (or is competition catching up?)
4. What did we learn that should change next quarter?
5. What 3 bets are we making next quarter?
6. What are we explicitly NOT doing?

**Annual strategy refresh:**
- Full stack review (all 5 questions)
- Compare initial 3-year vision to current trajectory
- Adjust as needed (don't ignore reality)

## Strategy Anti-Patterns

| Anti-pattern | Cost | Fix |
|--------------|------|-----|
| Strategy = list of goals | No actual strategy | Answer all 5 Hambrick questions |
| Strategy without saying "no" | Diluted focus | Explicit "what we don't do" list |
| Strategy never revisited | Drift into irrelevance | Quarterly reviews |
| Strategy that sounds good but unmeasurable | Can't tell if working | Specific metrics |
| Strategy that copies competitors | Following, not winning | Find your unique fit |
| Confusing strategy with tactics | Tactical thrash | Strategy = where + how to win; Tactics = what we do this week |

## Output
- `data/strategy.md` (the 1-pager, updated quarterly)
- `data/strategic-bets.md` (running log of all bets)
- `data/what-we-dont-do.md` (the no list)
- `reports/strategy-review-[quarter].md`
