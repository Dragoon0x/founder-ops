# Mode: Partnerships

## Purpose

Evaluate, structure, and manage partnerships. Co-marketing, integrations,
referral, channel, distribution. Most partnerships fail. Avoid the 5 traps.

## The 5 Partnership Traps

1. **Logo trade** (vanity, no revenue impact)
2. **Asymmetric exchange** (you do all the work, they get most of the credit)
3. **No champion** (deal closes, then dies because no one owns it)
4. **Scope creep** (started as "co-marketing", became custom integration)
5. **Exclusive too early** (lock yourself out of better partners later)

## Submodes

### 1. Partnership Evaluation Scorecard

For any proposed partner, score 1-10 on each:

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Audience overlap | 20% | Same ICP? Same buyer? |
| Audience size | 15% | How many of OUR ICP they reach |
| Brand reputation | 10% | Halo effect (positive or negative) |
| Trust capital | 10% | Their audience trusts them on relevant topics |
| Resource cost (us) | 15% | Negative weight: more = worse |
| Resource cost (them) | 5% | Negative weight: their willingness signal |
| Strategic fit | 15% | Aligns with our positioning |
| Reversibility | 10% | Can we exit if it doesn't work |

Total: /100

- 80+: Pursue aggressively
- 60-79: Pursue selectively (specific terms)
- < 60: Decline politely

### 2. Partnership Types & Templates

**Co-marketing partnership** (low commitment):
- Joint webinar
- Co-branded content
- Cross-promotion in newsletters
- Mutual case study

Template terms:
- 90-day pilot, no exclusivity
- Each side commits 1 named resource
- Shared metrics defined upfront
- Auto-expires unless both renew

**Integration partnership** (medium commitment):
- API integration between products
- Co-engineered features
- Shared roadmap visibility

Template terms:
- 6-month commitment, mutual exit clause
- Each side commits engineering hours
- Joint launch + co-promotion
- Revenue share NOT included unless > $500K influenced ARR

**Channel partnership** (high commitment):
- They sell our product
- Reseller / referral / OEM relationship

Template terms:
- 12-24 month, exclusivity by region/segment carefully scoped
- Margin: 20-30% (referral) to 40-50% (full reseller)
- Quota expectations
- Mutual marketing requirements
- MFN clause carefully negotiated

### 3. Partnership Outreach

**Cold outreach to potential partner (under 150 words):**
```
Subject: [Specific shared customer or thesis overlap]

Hi [Name],

[One sentence: specific shared context — same customer, same conference,
their recent talk]

We're both seeing [specific pattern in market]. [Your company] does X,
yours does Y. We've had [N] customers ask about Y workflows.

I think there's a co-marketing experiment worth running — specifically
[one specific idea, not vague].

Worth a 20-min call to explore?

[Name]
```

### 4. Partnership Agreement Checklist

Before signing ANY partnership:

- [ ] Specific deliverables (each side, with dates)
- [ ] Specific success metrics (with targets)
- [ ] Term length (start with shortest reasonable)
- [ ] Mutual exit clause (30-90 days notice)
- [ ] No automatic renewal
- [ ] Exclusivity scoped narrowly (if any)
- [ ] IP ownership for joint work
- [ ] Marketing approval rights (we approve their messaging about us)
- [ ] Customer data: who can use what data, how
- [ ] Termination data handling
- [ ] Public reference rights (logos, case studies)
- [ ] Dispute resolution
