# Mode: Product Marketing

## Purpose

Bridge product and market. PMM is NOT marketing — it's the discipline of
turning product capabilities into customer-relevant messages and equipping
sales/marketing to use them.

## The 4 Core PMM Deliverables

### 1. Positioning Document
*Already covered in `positioning.md` — PMM owns this.*

### 2. Messaging House

```
HEADLINE
[The one promise — 8 words max]

SUBHEAD
[Expand the headline — one sentence with credibility signal]

3 PILLARS              3 PILLARS              3 PILLARS
[Pillar 1: claim]      [Pillar 2: claim]      [Pillar 3: claim]
[Proof points]         [Proof points]         [Proof points]
[Customer quote]       [Customer quote]       [Customer quote]
[Use case]             [Use case]             [Use case]

FOUNDATION
[Mission / why we exist]
```

**Every external asset must trace back to this.** Landing page = pillars 1-3.
Sales deck = pillars 1-3. Product page = pillars 1-3. If a piece of marketing
doesn't map to a pillar, kill it.

### 3. Launch Plan (per significant release)

| Tier | Definition | Investment | Timeline |
|------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Tier 1** | Net-new product or company milestone | 4-8 weeks prep, all-channel push | Major announcement |
| **Tier 2** | Major new feature, persona expansion | 2-4 weeks prep, 3-channel push | Blog + email + social |
| **Tier 3** | Standard feature release | 1 week prep, blog + changelog | Single channel |
| **Tier 4** | Bug fixes, minor improvements | Changelog only | No marketing |

**Tier 1 launch checklist (run T-8 weeks):**
- [ ] T-8: Positioning aligned, messaging house drafted
- [ ] T-6: Landing page wireframe, demo recorded
- [ ] T-4: Sales enablement deck, internal training
- [ ] T-3: Press list built, embargo strategy
- [ ] T-2: Asset production complete (videos, decks, blog, emails)
- [ ] T-1: Internal dry run, customer preview list
- [ ] T-0: Launch
- [ ] T+1: Sales follow-up, metrics tracking
- [ ] T+4: Retro on launch performance

## Win/Loss Analysis (run quarterly)

After every closed deal (won or lost), within 7 days, ask the buyer:

**Won deals:**
1. What problem were you originally trying to solve?
2. Why did you start looking now (vs 6 months ago)?
3. Who else did you evaluate?
4. What made us the choice?
5. What almost made you not buy?
6. If you had to do it again, what would you change?

**Lost deals:**
1. What problem were you originally trying to solve?
2. What was the deciding factor against us?
3. What did the winner offer that we didn't?
4. What could we have done differently?
5. Would you reconsider us in the future? Under what conditions?

**Pattern detection (every 5 wins, 5 losses):**
- Common deciding factors
- Misalignment between our messaging and buyer needs
- Competitive blind spots
- Pricing friction patterns
