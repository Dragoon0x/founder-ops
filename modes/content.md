# Mode: Content

## Purpose

Write content that sounds like a founder, not a content marketer. Drive
specific outcomes: signups, trust, inbound leads, audience building.

## Submodes

### 1. Blog Post

**Structure:**
```
## [Title: specific claim or question, not generic]

### The hook (1 paragraph)
[Something specific and concrete that makes them want to read more.
Not "In today's fast-paced world..."]

### The point (2-3 sentences)
[State the thesis upfront. What will the reader believe by the end?]

### The argument (5-10 paragraphs)
[Build the case with specific examples, data, stories — NOT generic advice]

### The action (1 paragraph)
[What should the reader DO with this?]

### About the author (optional)
[One line about why you're the one writing this]
```

**Rules:**
- One idea per post. If you have two, write two posts.
- Specific > generic. "I interviewed 23 founders" > "many founders"
- Personal > third-person. "Here's what I learned" > "Here's what founders should know"
- Show work > state conclusions. Bring the reader on the journey.
- 800-1500 words is the sweet spot (long enough to be substantive, short enough to finish)

### 2. Tweet Thread

**Structure:**
```
Tweet 1: Hook (make them tap)
Tweet 2: Context (why this matters)
Tweet 3-7: The content (one idea per tweet)
Tweet 8: Summary / CTA
```

**Rules:**
- First tweet decides if anyone reads the rest
- No clickbait (promises you can't deliver = lost trust)
- Each tweet must stand alone AND build on the previous
- End with a CTA (follow, RT, reply — pick ONE)
- 7-10 tweets sweet spot

**Hook patterns that work:**
- "I did X for N time. Here's what I learned:"
- "Stop doing X. Here's what to do instead:"
- "[Specific insight] that [specific outcome]"
- "[Contrarian take backed by evidence]"
- "I analyzed N [things]. Here are the N patterns:"

**Anti-patterns:**
- "🧵 1/"
- "A thread on..."
- Generic advice with no specificity
- Humble-bragging
- Engagement bait ("RT if you agree")

### 3. Landing Page Copy

**Above the fold:**
```
[Headline: specific value proposition in 8-12 words]
[Subhead: one sentence expanding the headline + credibility signal]
[Primary CTA: 2-3 words, action-oriented]
[Social proof: logos / testimonial / number]
```

**Headline formulas that work:**
- "[Specific outcome] for [specific audience]" ("HR software for startups under 50 people")
- "[Time/effort saved] for [specific task]" ("Close books in 2 days, not 2 weeks")
- "[Contrarian positioning]" ("The CRM that doesn't feel like work")
- "[Category + qualifier]" ("Payroll for international teams")

**Body sections (in order):**
1. The problem (they'll nod in recognition)
2. Why existing solutions fail (not competitor bashing, category critique)
3. Your solution (one paragraph, then show it)
4. Social proof (case study with specific numbers)
5. How it works (3-4 steps max)
6. Pricing (don't hide it)
7. FAQ (objections you've heard)
8. Final CTA

### 4. Newsletter

**Structure:**
```
Subject line: [Specific + curiosity + under 50 chars]
Preview text: [Second chance to get them to open]

[Lead: personal, hook them in first sentence]

[Body: ONE thing. Not a digest. ONE insight/story/lesson.]

[CTA: one specific action — reply, click, share]

[Signoff: human, brief]

P.S. [Optional: tease something for next week]
```

**Subject line formulas:**
- "Why I [surprising action]"
- "The [metric] that matters most for [audience]"
- "What I learned from [specific experience]"
- "[Number] [thing] to [outcome]"

Never:
- "Newsletter #[N]" (nobody cares)
- "[Month] update" (boring)
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver

### 5. Cold Email / Sales Email

Under 100 words:
```
Subject: [Specific question or relevant observation]

[Name],

[One sentence: specific context — their tweet, their portfolio, their recent post]

[Two sentences: why I'm emailing you. What I built + one specific metric]

[One sentence: specific ask — 15-min call, or question they can answer async]

[Name]
[One line: credibility — title, company, relevant fact]
```

**Anti-patterns:**
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "Quick question" subject
- Attaching a deck unprompted
- Generic compliments ("I love what you're doing")
- Meeting requests without context

## Voice Rules

Every piece of content goes through these checks:

1. **Would I send this to my smartest friend?** (If it sounds too marketing-y, rewrite)
2. **Does it sound like me, or like ChatGPT?** (Cut filler, AI tone, generic phrases)
3. **Is there a specific moment or example?** (Abstract advice = forgotten)
4. **Did I kill the passive voice?** ("Mistakes were made" → "I made mistakes")
5. **Did I earn every paragraph?** (Cut anything that's filler)
