# Mode: Brand

## Purpose

Brand is what people say about you when you're not in the room.
Decide what you want them to say. Then build everything to reinforce it.

## The 5 Brand Layers

```
1. Purpose      → Why we exist (10-year time horizon)
2. Values       → How we behave (what we won't compromise)
3. Personality  → How we sound (voice, tone, character)
4. Identity     → How we look (visual: logo, type, color, motion)
5. Experience   → How we feel (every touchpoint)
```

**Decide top-down.** Identity changes don't fix purpose problems.

## Voice & Tone System

**Voice = consistent (who we are)**
**Tone = contextual (how we adjust to the situation)**

### Voice Principles (3-5, with examples)

```
PRINCIPLE: We use plain English
- Yes: "Save 4 hours a week"
- No: "Synergize productivity workflows"

PRINCIPLE: We're specific over generic  
- Yes: "Cut invoice processing from 3 days to 15 minutes"
- No: "Streamline your operations"

PRINCIPLE: We acknowledge tradeoffs
- Yes: "We're slower than competitor X for [scenario], because we [tradeoff]"
- No: "We're the best at everything"
```

### Tone Map

| Context | Tone Shift | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Marketing site | Confident, magnetic | "The fastest way to ship code." |
| Product UI | Helpful, direct | "Saved. You're good to go." |
| Error states | Apologetic, helpful | "Something went wrong. Here's what we know..." |
| Legal/security | Precise, clear | "We collect: name, email, usage data. We don't: sell or share." |
| Customer support | Warm, accountable | "That's frustrating. Let me fix this for you." |
| Bad news (outage) | Direct, accountable | "We were down for 47 minutes. Here's what happened..." |

## Visual Identity (decide before designing)

### Logo
- **Wordmark only** (Stripe, Notion): Simplest, type-driven brand
- **Wordmark + symbol** (Slack, Figma): Most flexible, scalable
- **Symbol only** (Apple, Nike): Requires extreme brand recognition — risky for early stage

**Rule:** If you're under $10M ARR, wordmark + simple symbol is the safest bet.

### Color Palette
- **Primary** (1 color): Brand recognition, used 80% of the time
- **Secondary** (2-3 colors): Accents, secondary CTAs
- **Neutrals** (4-5 grays + black/white): UI, content
- **System** (success/warning/error): Functional UI states

**Anti-pattern:** Trying to be every color. Pick a primary and own it.

### Typography
- **Display** (1 typeface): Headlines, hero sections
- **Body** (1 typeface, often same family): Content, UI

**Rule:** 2 typefaces max. More = chaotic. Less = boring (acceptable).

### Component System
- **Spacing:** 4px or 8px base unit. Consistent throughout.
- **Border radius:** 0px (sharp), 4px (subtle), 8px (modern), 12px+ (playful). Pick one. Use everywhere.
- **Shadow:** 0-3 levels. Pick depth philosophy (flat / subtle / dimensional).

## Brand Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Fix |
|--------------|--------------|-----|
| Brand = logo | Logo is 5% of brand | Start with purpose, voice, personality |
| Copying [Stripe / Linear / Apple] aesthetics | You're not them | Find your own visual voice |
| Too many fonts/colors | Looks unprofessional | 2 fonts, 1 primary color, max |
| Inconsistent voice (legal vs marketing) | Confusing | Voice principles applied everywhere |
| Brand for brand's sake | Burns money | Brand serves business outcomes |
| Re-brand every year | Confuses customers | Evolve, don't reinvent |
