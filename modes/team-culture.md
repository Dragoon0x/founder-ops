# Mode: Team & Culture

## Purpose

Build a team that doesn't break under pressure. Catch culture decay early.
Run 1:1s, performance reviews, and skip-levels that produce real signal.

## Submodes

### 1. 1:1 Cadence and Structure

| Direct Report | Frequency | Length |
|--------------|-----------|--------|
| Direct report (skip-2) | Weekly | 30 min |
| Direct report (skip-1) | Weekly | 45 min |
| Skip-level (alternate week) | Bi-weekly | 30 min |
| Critical role transitions | Daily for first week | 15 min |

**1:1 structure (45 min):**
```
0-5 min   Their agenda first (let them lead)
5-20 min  Blockers + support needed
20-35 min Growth + development
35-40 min Feedback both directions
40-45 min Logistics + commitments
```

**Their agenda first** is the most important part. If they have nothing,
the relationship is in trouble.

### 2. Skip-Level 1:1s

Bi-weekly with each manager's direct reports:

```
## Skip-Level — [Person] — [Date]

### What's working
[Their answer to "what's the best part of working here right now?"]

### What's not working
[Their answer to "what would you change if you could change one thing?"]

### About their manager
[Indirect: "How can [manager] support you better?"]

### Career growth
[Where do they want to be in 12 months?]

### Things I should know
[Anything bubbling up that hasn't reached me]

### My commitments back
[What I'll do based on this conversation]
```

**Don't skip skip-levels.** They surface culture issues, manager problems,
and engagement decay before they explode.

### 3. Performance Review Framework

Twice a year. Calibrated across teams. Not surprise-based.

```
## Performance Review — [Person] — [Period]

### What you delivered
[Specific shipped work, projects, outcomes]

### How you delivered it
[Behaviors: ownership, communication, collaboration, growth]

### Where you exceeded
[2-3 specific things that stood out]

### Where you fell short
[Specific, with context — not blame]

### Growth focus next period
[2-3 specific areas with concrete actions]

### Compensation / leveling
[Decision with reasoning]
```

**Continuous feedback throughout > big surprise reviews.**
If review contains anything they're hearing for the first time, you failed.

### 4. eNPS (Employee Net Promoter Score)

Quarterly anonymous survey. Single question:

> On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend [Company] as a place to work?

Plus 1 follow-up:
> What's the primary reason for your score?

| Score | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| 9-10 | Promoter |
| 7-8 | Passive |
| 0-6 | Detractor |

**eNPS = % Promoters - % Detractors**

| eNPS | Health |
|------|--------|
| 50+ | Excellent |
| 30-49 | Good |
| 10-29 | OK, watch |
| < 10 | Problem |
| Negative | Crisis |

### 5. Attrition Signals (catch before they leave)

Watch for:
- Stops volunteering for new projects
- Disengaged in meetings (camera off, minimal contribution)
- Stops giving feedback (was active, now silent)
- Updates LinkedIn (notification = signal)
- Asks fewer questions
- Takes more vacation days
- Mentions side projects more
- Skips optional team activities

When 2+ fire on a high performer → 1:1 specifically about engagement and
career trajectory. Not "are you leaving" but "what would make you stay
another 2 years?"

### 6. Culture Decay Indicators

Quarterly culture audit:

- [ ] Is shipping velocity declining?
- [ ] Are decisions taking longer?
- [ ] Are meetings increasing while output stays flat?
- [ ] Are "us vs them" comments emerging (Eng vs Sales, etc)?
- [ ] Is feedback going silent?
- [ ] Are top performers becoming average?
- [ ] Has trust declined (people not giving each other benefit of doubt)?
- [ ] Are people apologizing for things that aren't their fault?
- [ ] Has gallows humor emerged?

If 3+ → run a culture intervention (off-site, restructure, or candid all-hands).

### 7. Firing Well

The hardest part of leadership. Do it with respect.

**Before firing:**
- Document the gap clearly
- Have at least one performance conversation with explicit consequences
- Give a real chance to improve (30-60 days, not weeks)
- Get a second opinion (skip-level, peer, board member)

**The conversation:**
- Direct, in person (or video if remote), short (10-15 min)
- "Today is your last day at [Company]. Here's why: [specific, factual]"
- Don't argue or negotiate. Decision is made.
- Cover logistics: severance, benefits, last day, reference policy

**After:**
- Tell their team within 24 hours
- Be honest without being cruel ("X is no longer with us")
- Don't bad-mouth — protects everyone
- Reflect: did your hiring process fail? Your management?

### 8. Comp Bands

By role + level + location. Refreshed annually.

| Level | Eng / Product | Sales / GTM | Marketing | Ops |
|-------|---------------|-------------|-----------|-----|
| L3 (Mid) | $X | $X | $X | $X |
| L4 (Senior) | $X | $X | $X | $X |
| L5 (Staff/Lead) | $X | $X | $X | $X |
| L6 (Principal/Director) | $X | $X | $X | $X |
| L7 (Senior Director/VP) | $X | $X | $X | $X |

Adjust by location: SF/NYC = 1.0, secondary US cities = 0.85-0.90,
international = market-rate.

Equity bands separate from cash. Higher cash = lower equity band, vice versa.
