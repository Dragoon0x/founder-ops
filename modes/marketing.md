# Mode: Marketing Strategy

## Purpose

Marketing strategy beyond content. Brand positioning, demand generation,
PR, analyst relations, lifecycle marketing. The big picture, not just
this week's blog post.

## Submodes

### 1. Marketing Mix by Stage

| Stage | Primary | Secondary | Avoid |
|-------|---------|-----------|-------|
| Pre-seed | Founder content | Direct sales | Paid ads, PR firm |
| Seed | SEO + community | Founder content | Brand campaigns, agencies |
| Series A | SEO + paid + content | Events, partnerships | Mass-market PR |
| Series B+ | Multi-channel + brand | Analyst, PR | Single-channel dependence |

### 3. Demand Generation

| Channel | Best For | Budget % |
|---------|----------|----------|
| SEO + content | Long-term, low-CAC | 25-35% |
| Paid search | High intent, scalable | 15-25% |
| Paid social | Brand + remarketing | 10-20% |
| Email lifecycle | Existing audience | 5-10% |
| Events / webinars | Mid-funnel education | 10-15% |
| Partnerships | Trust through association | 5-10% |
| Referral | Lowest CAC, hardest to scale | 5-10% |

### 4. Content Strategy (beyond /founder-ops content)

**Pillar content** (4-8 per year):
- Definitive guides (5K+ words)
- Original research (data nobody else has)
- Industry reports
- Tools/calculators

**Cluster content** (40-80 per year):
- Supporting articles around pillars
- How-to tutorials
- Case studies
- Comparison pages

**Engagement content** (daily):
- Tweets, LinkedIn posts
- Newsletter
- Community posts

### 5. PR Strategy

**Pre-Series A:** Don't hire PR firm. Founder is the spokesperson.

**Tier-1 publications by stage:**
| Stage | Realistic targets |
|-------|------------------|
| Pre-seed | Local press, niche industry blogs |
| Seed | TechCrunch, VentureBeat, niche trades |
| Series A | TechCrunch features, Forbes, Fast Company |
| Series B+ | NYT, WSJ, Bloomberg, podcast circuit |

**News hooks that get covered:**
1. Funding announcements (most reliable)
2. Major customer wins (logos)
3. Hiring announcements (notable executives)
4. Product launches (with novel angle)
5. Original research (data hooks)
6. Trend pieces (your CEO as expert source)
7. Founder personal narrative (rare, powerful)

**News hooks that DON'T work:**
- Generic feature releases
- "We're growing" updates
- Anniversaries
- Vanity metrics

### 6. Analyst Relations

For B2B companies > Series A:

**Key analysts:**
- Gartner (most important for enterprise)
- Forrester (decision-maker influence)
- IDC (data-driven)
- 451 Research (deep tech)

**Engagement playbook:**
1. Join their analyst inquiry program (paid)
2. Brief them quarterly (free, but respect their time)
3. Pitch for "Cool Vendor" inclusion (Gartner)
4. Build toward Magic Quadrant inclusion (multi-year)

### 7. Lifecycle Marketing

For each user lifecycle stage, design messaging:

| Stage | Goal | Tactics |
|-------|------|---------|
| Awareness | Get on their radar | Content, ads, PR |
| Consideration | Educate, build trust | Webinars, comparison content, demos |
| Trial | Activate to value | Onboarding emails, in-app |
| Conversion | First purchase | Conversion series, urgency, ROI |
| Retention | Habit formation | Feature emails, success stories |
| Expansion | Grow account | Power user emails, new use case content |
| Advocacy | Refer / case study | Reference program, swag, recognition |

### 8. Marketing Metrics Hierarchy

```
North Star: ARR (or NRR for mature)
  ├─ New ARR
  │   ├─ Pipeline Generated ($)
  │   │   ├─ MQLs (marketing-qualified leads)
  │   │   ├─ SQLs (sales-qualified leads)
  │   │   └─ SAOs (sales-accepted opportunities)
  │   └─ Conversion rates between stages
  └─ Marketing influence on retention
      ├─ Engagement scores
      ├─ Content consumption
      └─ Event attendance
```

**Vanity metrics to ignore:**
- Twitter followers
- Page views (without engagement)
- Email subscribers (without opens)
- "Impressions"
- Ad reach
