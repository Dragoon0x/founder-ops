# Mode: Competitive Intelligence

## Purpose

Know your competitive landscape deeply enough to position clearly, price
confidently, and explain your unique value without sounding defensive.

## Submodes

### 1. Competitor Deep Dive

For each competitor, research:

```
## [Competitor Name]

### Basics
- Founded: [year]
- Funding: $[raised] from [investors]
- Team size: [estimate]
- HQ: [location]
- Key execs: [names + backgrounds]

### Product
- Positioning: [how they describe themselves]
- Target customer: [their ICP]
- Pricing: [tiers + prices]
- Key features (in their order of emphasis)
- Recent product updates (last 90 days)
- Missing features (what they don't do)

### Go-to-Market
- Primary channel: [SEO/sales/partnerships/content]
- Content strategy: [what they publish]
- SEO keywords they rank for: [top 5-10]
- Sales model: [self-serve / inside sales / field sales]

### Customer Signal
- Public customers (logos on site)
- Case studies (specific numbers)
- Reviews (G2/Capterra — score + common complaints)
- Social proof

### Strengths
[Things they do well]

### Weaknesses
[Gaps in their offering or execution]

### Threat Level
[1-5 with reasoning]

### Strategy vs Them
[How we position against them]
```

### 2. Market Map

Create a 2x2 matrix with two axes that matter:

Common axes:
- Self-serve vs Sales-led
- Technical users vs Business users
- Narrow/deep vs Broad/shallow
- Premium vs Value
- Enterprise vs SMB
- Horizontal vs Vertical

For each quadrant:
- Who plays here
- Size of quadrant
- Where you fit
- Where the white space is

### 3. Positioning Framework

After research, answer:
- **Who is our customer?** (Specific ICP, not "businesses")
- **What alternative do they use?** (Could be a competitor OR a spreadsheet)
- **Why do they switch?** (Specific moment of pain)
- **What's our unique value?** (One thing we do better than anyone)
- **What do we give up?** (You can't be best at everything)

Output:
```
## Positioning Statement

For [ICP],
who are frustrated by [alternative],
[product] is a [category]
that [unique value].
Unlike [main competitor],
we [specific differentiation].
```

### 4. Competitive Talk Track (for sales/demos)

When a prospect mentions a competitor:
```
- Acknowledge their strength (don't trash them)
- Pivot to the customer's actual need
- Give one specific example of your differentiation
- Offer to connect them with a customer who switched
```

Example:
> Prospect: "How are you different from [Competitor]?"
>
> You: "[Competitor] is solid — especially if you need [their strength].
> We built [product] specifically for [specific customer segment with specific pain].
> The biggest thing is [one concrete differentiation with example].
> Would it help if I connected you with [customer who switched]?"

## Competitive Monitoring

Monthly review:
- [ ] Check competitor homepages (copy changes?)
- [ ] Check pricing pages (pricing changes?)
- [ ] Check job postings (what are they hiring for? = roadmap signal)
- [ ] Check funding news (fresh capital = new initiatives)
- [ ] Check their content (new themes? new audiences?)
- [ ] Check G2/Capterra reviews (what are customers complaining about?)

## Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Trashing competitors | Makes you look small | Acknowledge strengths, pivot to customer need |
| "We're the only ones" | Never true, reduces credibility | Show the landscape, show your fit |
| Feature-by-feature comparison | Commoditizes the category | Compare on outcomes customers care about |
| Ignoring competitors | They're not ignoring you | Monthly monitoring minimum |
| Copying competitor features reactively | Following, not leading | Only copy if customers explicitly want it |
