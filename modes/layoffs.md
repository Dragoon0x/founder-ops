# Mode: Layoffs

## Purpose

If you're reading this, you're already in pain. The job is to do this
in a way that protects:
1. The people being let go (severance, dignity, references)
2. The people staying (clarity, stability, trust)
3. The company (legal, operational, reputational)

In that order.

## Pre-Decision (do not skip)

### Confirm layoffs are necessary

A layoff is appropriate when:
- Burn requires cutting > 20% to extend runway 12+ months
- A division/product is being shut down
- A merger / reorg eliminates redundant roles
- Performance issues spanning multiple people aren't solvable individually

A layoff is NOT appropriate for:
- Cyclical revenue dips (use bonuses, salary cuts, hiring freeze first)
- Single-person performance issues (manage that person individually)
- Founder anxiety about runway when alternatives exist

### Confirm the size

**Rule:** Cut deep enough that you don't need to do this twice.
Second-round layoffs do 5x the cultural damage of first-round.

**Math:**
```
Target runway after = 18+ months (for funded startups)
Required cost reduction = current burn - (cash / 18)
Required headcount reduction = required cost reduction / avg fully-loaded cost per person
Round UP — better to over-cut than under-cut
```

### Get expert help

- **Employment lawyer** (mandatory) — review process, severance, releases, multi-state issues
- **HR consultant** (if no internal HR) — process design, communications
- **Outplacement firm** (recommended) — career services for departing employees
- **Communications advisor** (if > 50 people) — internal and external messaging

## Pre-Layoff Week (T-7 to T-1)

### Day -7: Final list approved
- CEO + leadership team finalize list
- Lawyer reviews for protected class concentration (age, gender, race, disability)
- Adjust if any group is disproportionately impacted

### Day -5: Manager prep
- Each manager who will deliver news gets:
  - Script for the conversation
  - Severance and benefits details
  - FAQ they can answer
  - Practice session (yes, really)
  - Manager support number for hard questions

### Day -3: Technical preparation
- IT prepares access termination
- Systems flagged for revocation at exact time
- Severance offer letters drafted and reviewed

### Day -1: Communications drafted
- Email to departing employees
- Email to remaining employees (immediately after)
- All-hands script
- External communications (if relevant)
- Customer-facing communications (if account-facing roles affected)

## Layoff Day (T-0)

### 8:00 AM: All-hands announcement
- CEO announces layoffs are happening today
- Numbers (% of company, departments affected)
- Timeline (when conversations happen)
- Express acknowledgment of pain, take responsibility
- DO NOT say "we'll come back stronger" or other corporate platitudes
- DO commit to specific support for those leaving

### 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM: Individual conversations
- 15-30 minutes each
- Manager + HR/founder if no HR
- Script (verbal, not read):
  - "I have hard news. Your role is being eliminated."
  - "This is not about your performance."
  - "Here's what's next: severance, benefits, references."
  - "Take time to process. We're here to support."
- Hand them: severance offer, benefits info, FAQ
- Tell them when access is being revoked (be honest)
- Allow goodbyes if they want, give space if they don't

### 12:00 PM: Access revocation
- Technical systems revoked at the agreed time
- This protects the person from feeling abandoned mid-conversation
- This protects the company from data risk

### 1:00 PM: All-hands for remaining team
- Acknowledge the day's pain
- Be transparent about why
- Address the inevitable questions:
  - "Is there a second round coming?" (Be honest. If no: state firmly. If maybe: don't pretend.)
  - "What happens to the work my colleague was doing?"
  - "How is leadership taking pay cuts?"
- Give people the rest of the day off

### 5:00 PM: External communications (if needed)
- LinkedIn post from CEO
- Customer email if account-facing roles affected
- Press response if asked (don't proactively pitch)

## Severance Standards

What "fair" looks like (this varies by stage and geography):

| Stage | Severance | Benefits | Other |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Pre-seed/Seed | 1-2 months | 1-2 months health | LinkedIn rec, intro list |
| Series A | 2-3 months | 2-3 months health | + outplacement |
| Series B+ | 3-4 months | 3-6 months health | + outplacement + extended option exercise |

**Always include:**
- Accelerated vesting consideration (industry standard for layoffs: 0% extra, but consider 1-3 months for seniors)
- COBRA / health insurance coverage paid
- Outplacement services (LinkedIn, resume, coaching)
- Reference letters
- Inclusion in talent network for company

## Post-Layoff (T+1 to T+90)

### T+1: Check-ins
- Each remaining employee gets a 1:1 with their manager
- Address concerns, recommit to mission, listen

### T+7: Pulse survey
- Anonymous, 5 questions about how the layoff was handled
- Specifically test: trust in leadership, confidence in mission, intent to stay

### T+30: Productivity check
- Some productivity loss is normal (3-6 weeks)
- If still down at week 6, address culture issues directly

### T+90: Retention check
- Voluntary attrition typically spikes 1-3 months post-layoff
- Be ready with retention conversations for high performers

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Consequence | Fix |
|--------------|-------------|-----|
| Layoffs over Slack/email | Catastrophic for departing + remaining | Always 1:1, even if remote |
| Cutting too shallow | Second round, double damage | Cut deep, don't repeat |
| Performance-blaming | Legal risk + cultural damage | Be honest: "role eliminated, not performance" |
| No severance | Reputational + legal risk | Even early stage: 1+ month |
| Hiding from remaining team | Trust collapses | All-hands within hours |
| Founder/exec doesn't take pay cut | Hypocrisy | If asking sacrifice, share it |
| Re-hiring within 90 days | Insulting to laid-off employees | Hire ONLY for genuinely new roles |

## Founder Self-Care

This will be one of the hardest things you do. Plan for it:
- Tell your support system in advance
- Don't drink heavily through it
- Don't make other major decisions in the same week
- Talk to other founders who've done this
- Consider a therapist appointment for the week after

You will feel guilt. Some of it is appropriate. Some of it isn't.
Distinguish between "I made decisions that led here" (true, learn)
and "I'm a bad person" (not true, address with help).
