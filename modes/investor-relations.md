# Mode: Investor Relations

## Purpose

Manage existing investor relationships AFTER the wire hits. Most founders
treat investors as transactional. The best founders treat them as a
multi-decade asset that compounds across companies.

## Investor Tiering

Not all investors deserve equal time. Tier them:

| Tier | Definition | Touchpoints |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **A — Strategic** | Lead investor, board member, repeat from prior co | Monthly call + monthly update |
| **B — Active** | Significant check, helpful network | Monthly update + quarterly call |
| **C — Standard** | Smaller check, less involved | Monthly update only |
| **D — Passive** | Tiny check, signed and disappeared | Quarterly update only |

**Time allocation:** 60% on Tier A, 25% Tier B, 10% Tier C, 5% Tier D.

## Cadences

### Monthly investor update (mandatory, on-time, every-time)
*See `modes/update.md` for template. The discipline matters more than the prose.*

**Rule:** Send by the 5th of each month, for the prior month. If you skip
or delay, investors assume something is wrong.

### Quarterly investor call (Tier A & B)
- **Format:** 30-min Zoom, recorded, agenda sent 48hr ahead
- **Structure:**
  - 5 min: Quarter recap
  - 10 min: Deep dive on one priority topic
  - 10 min: Asks (intros, feedback, hires)
  - 5 min: Q&A

### Annual deep-dive (Tier A only)
- **Format:** 90-min in-person or video, year in review + next year strategy
- **Deliverable:** Annual letter (3-5 pages) sent ahead of meeting

## Asks (the most underused investor lever)

Most founders never ask for help. Investors WANT to help — it's how they
generate carry. Make asks easy, specific, and tracked.

**Bad asks:**
- "Let me know if you have any feedback"
- "Any intros would be great"
- "Hope to keep you in the loop"

**Good asks:**
- "I need 5 intros to VPs of Engineering at Series B SaaS companies in NY"
- "Can you review our pricing page and tell me what's confusing?"
- "Do you know anyone who's done a transition from PLG to enterprise sales?"
- "Can you connect me to your portfolio CEO who built a sales team from scratch?"

**Track every ask:**
```
| Date | Investor | Ask | Response | Outcome |
|------|----------|-----|----------|---------|
| 2026-04-12 | [Name] | 5 intros to fintech CFOs | Sent 3 | 1 closed deal |
```

## Sensitive Conversations

Investors will ask hard questions. Have prepared, honest answers:

**"How long is your runway?"**
- Don't sugarcoat. Give exact number with assumptions.
- Have 3 scenarios ready (status quo / cuts / hits target).

**"Are you going to hit plan?"**
- Honest assessment with confidence interval.
- "We'll hit X in commit, Y in best case, Z in stretch."

**"Are you raising soon?"**
- If not actively raising: "Not actively, but always open to conversations."
- If raising: Be direct about timeline, terms, who's leading.
- Never play coy — investors talk.

**"Are you happy with [co-founder / executive]?"**
- Don't lie. If issues, share at the right level of detail.
- Co-founder issues: at minimum tell your lead investor.

**"Are you happy with us?"**
- If yes, say so specifically.
- If not, this is the time to address it. Investor relationships compound — fix now, not later.

## Bad News Protocol

When something bad happens (lost a key customer, missed quarter, exec leaves, security incident):

### < 24 hours: Notify Tier A
- Phone call to lead investor
- Email to other Tier A investors

### < 48 hours: Notify Tier B
- Email with: what happened, impact, plan, ask

### < 7 days: Address in monthly update
- Don't bury it
- Show what you're doing about it
- Show what you've learned

**Rule:** Bad news doesn't get better with age. Tell investors before they hear it elsewhere.

## Board Member Management (if you have a board)

### Pre-board meeting (T-7 days)
- Send pre-read (deck + financial summary)
- Schedule 1:1s with each director (30 min each)
- Identify decisions you need at the meeting
- Pre-align on contentious topics

### Board meeting (90 min)
- 5 min: Welcome, agenda, decisions needed
- 20 min: CEO update (numbers, highlights, lowlights)
- 40 min: Deep dives (1-2 topics requiring board input)
- 15 min: Decisions
- 10 min: Executive session (board only, no founder)

### Post-board (T+7 days)
- Send minutes within 7 days
- Track decisions and commitments
- Follow up on board member action items

## Investor Relations Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Consequence | Fix |
|--------------|-------------|-----|
| Skipping months when busy | Loses trust | Always send, even if 100 words |
| Hiding bad news | Catastrophic when discovered | Lead with it |
| Treating all investors equally | Wastes time | Tier and prioritize |
| Vague asks | No help received | Specific, written, trackable |
| Surprise board meetings | Frustrated directors | Pre-read 5 days ahead, always |
| Defensive in board meetings | Loses credibility | Acknowledge, then explain plan |
