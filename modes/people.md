# Mode: People Management

## Purpose

For founders managing their first reports. 1:1s, performance feedback,
career conversations, compensation, conflict resolution.

## Submodes

### 1. The Weekly 1:1

**Structure (30 min):**
```
0-2 min   — Brief life check-in (genuine, not interrogation)
2-12 min  — Their agenda (what's top of mind for THEM)
12-22 min — Blockers, support needed
22-27 min — Their growth / development
27-30 min — Bidirectional feedback
```

**Rules:**
- THEY drive the agenda. You ask "what's top of mind?"
- Take notes (in shared doc) so you remember
- End with 1-2 explicit action items each
- Never cancel without rescheduling within 48 hours

**Questions that surface real things:**
- "What's making you frustrated this week?"
- "What's energizing you this week?"
- "Where do you wish you had more support from me?"
- "What would make next week 10% better?"
- "What are you avoiding that you should do?"

### 2. Continuous Feedback

**The 24-hour rule:** Give feedback within 24 hours of the event.
Anything older feels political and lands poorly.

**SBI framework (Situation-Behavior-Impact):**
```
Situation: "In yesterday's customer call..."
Behavior: "When you interrupted the customer mid-question..."
Impact: "It made it harder for them to share their concerns,
         which is the goal of those calls."
```

**Praise in public, criticize in private.** Always.

**Feedback ratio:** Aim for 5:1 positive to corrective.
Not because criticism doesn't matter, but because trust comes from
consistent recognition.

### 4. Compensation Conversations

**Principles:**
- Comp should be discussed openly, not avoided
- Annual reviews + ad-hoc when warranted
- Document the math (band, percentile, why this number)

**Compensation framework:**
```
Base = [market rate for role + level + location] × [performance multiplier]
Equity = [vested grant] + [refresh based on performance]
```

**The raise conversation:**
```
"Based on your performance this year and current market data,
your new base will be $[X] effective [date]. This puts you at
the [Y] percentile for your role + level + location.

Equity refresh: [N] additional shares vesting over [time].

Bonus: [if applicable]"
```

**If they ask for more than budgeted:**
- Listen fully (don't immediately defend)
- Ask: "What number would address your concerns?"
- Be honest about constraints
- If you can't meet it: explain WHY, propose alternatives (equity, title, scope)
- If they leave over comp: you may have under-paid them for too long

### 5. Difficult Conversations

**The performance improvement plan (PIP) — a last resort, not a tool:**

If you find yourself drafting a PIP, ask first:
- Did I give clear feedback over the last 90 days?
- Did they understand what was failing?
- Did I support them with resources / coaching?
- Is the role still right for them?

If you've done all of the above and it's not working:
```
## PIP — [Name] — [Date]

### Performance gaps
1. [Specific behavior or outcome that's not meeting bar]
2. [Specific behavior or outcome]
3. [Specific behavior or outcome]

### Expectations (next 60 days)
1. [Observable change with specific metric]
2. [Observable change with specific metric]
3. [Observable change with specific metric]

### Support
- [Coaching, training, resources we'll provide]

### Check-in cadence
Weekly 30-min check-ins to review progress

### Outcome
- If gaps closed: continued employment, normal review cycle
- If gaps not closed: termination at end of 60 days
```

### 6. Termination Process

**When termination is the right call:**
- Performance gaps that didn't close after clear feedback + support
- Values misalignment (after one warning conversation)
- Misconduct (immediate)

**Termination conversation (10-15 min):**
```
1. Lead: "We're ending your employment at [Company] effective today."
2. Brief reason (don't relitigate): "The performance issues we discussed
   over the last [time] haven't improved to the level we need."
3. Logistics: severance, benefits, equipment return, last paycheck timing
4. Q&A — they may need to vent; let them
5. Practical: "[HR/Ops] will follow up with paperwork today."
```

**Rules:**
- Have HR / lawyer present if any risk
- Documented in writing afterward
- Severance offer + separation agreement (with non-disparagement)
- Communicate to team within 24 hours (brief, dignified, no detail)

### 7. Manager Anti-Patterns

| Anti-pattern | Cost | Fix |
|--------------|------|-----|
| Avoiding hard conversations | Issues fester, others see weakness | 24-hour rule on feedback |
| Over-promising career growth | Trust loss when reality hits | Be honest about scope |
| Inconsistent feedback (good in 1:1, bad in review) | Confusion, anger | Same message everywhere |
| Reactive vs proactive comp | Star employees leave | Annual review + ad-hoc raises |
| Cancelling 1:1s | "I don't matter" signal | Reschedule within 48 hrs |
| Cc'ing entire team in feedback | Public shaming | Praise public, critique private |
| Solving their problems for them | Stunts growth | Ask "what would you do?" |
