# Mode: Founder Wellbeing

## Purpose

Founder burnout kills more startups than any market force. Track your own
health like you track ARR. Catch the spiral before it catches you.

## Why This Matters

- 50%+ of founders report mental health issues
- Burnt-out founders make worse decisions
- Replacing yourself is impossible at early stage
- Your team mirrors your energy
- The company can't be healthier than you are

## Submodes

### 1. Weekly Health Check (5 min)

Score 1-10 on each dimension:

```
## Founder Health — Week of [date]

| Dimension | Score (1-10) | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Sleep quality | | Hours? Quality? |
| Physical activity | | Exercise this week? |
| Energy | | Morning? Afternoon? |
| Focus | | Could you concentrate? |
| Mood | | Generally up or down? |
| Connection | | Time with family/friends? |
| Purpose | | Felt aligned with what you're doing? |
| Stress | | (10 = no stress, 1 = overwhelmed) |
```

**If any score < 5 for 2+ weeks → red flag, take action.**

### 2. Burnout Warning Signs

Track these. They precede crisis by weeks/months:

- [ ] Working 60+ hour weeks for 4+ weeks consecutively
- [ ] Skipping meals (1+ per day, 3+ days/week)
- [ ] Sleep < 6 hours regularly
- [ ] Stopped exercising (no physical activity 2+ weeks)
- [ ] Stopped seeing friends socially (4+ weeks)
- [ ] Substance increase (caffeine, alcohol, other)
- [ ] Cynicism about the company / customers
- [ ] Cynicism about the team
- [ ] Resentment toward investors / co-founder
- [ ] Avoidance of difficult conversations
- [ ] Procrastinating on important decisions
- [ ] Loss of joy in things you used to enjoy
- [ ] Physical symptoms (chest tightness, jaw clenching, headaches, gut issues)
- [ ] Trouble sleeping despite exhaustion
- [ ] Numbness / emotional flatness
- [ ] Catastrophic thinking ("if X doesn't work, life is over")

3+ for 2+ weeks → see a therapist. 5+ → consider medical help. 8+ → emergency.

### 3. Founder Therapist / Coach

**Differences:**
- **Therapist:** Mental health, processing emotions, life issues
- **Executive coach:** Performance, leadership, business decisions
- **Founder coach:** Specific to founder issues — both
- **Peer mentor:** Pattern matching from someone who's been there

**Most founders need 2-3:**
1. Therapist (weekly): mental health
2. Coach (bi-weekly): performance & decisions
3. Peer mentor (monthly): pattern matching

**Cost:** $200-400/session typical. ~$1.5-3K/month combined.
**ROI:** One avoided bad decision pays for years of coaching.

### 4. Daily Routines That Compound

**Morning (30-60 min, non-negotiable):**
- Movement (walk, exercise, yoga) — 20-30 min
- Quiet time (meditation, journaling, reading) — 10-15 min
- Breakfast (real food)
- Plan top 3 priorities for the day

**Workday boundaries:**
- Hard start time
- Hard stop time (within 1 hour)
- Lunch away from desk
- No meetings before 9 AM or after 5 PM (founder discretion)
- Phone in different room during deep work

**Evening:**
- Dinner with someone (partner, friend, family) — 3+ times/week
- No screens 30 min before bed
- Same bedtime within 1 hour each night

**Weekends:**
- One full day off (no email, no Slack)
- Time outside (sunlight, fresh air)
- Time with humans you care about
- One activity that isn't work

### 5. Co-Founder Relationship

The #1 reason early-stage companies fail.

**Weekly co-founder 1:1 (45 min):**
- 15 min: company stuff
- 15 min: relationship — how are we working together?
- 15 min: each other's lives outside work

**Quarterly co-founder retro:**
- What's working in our partnership?
- What's not working?
- What do we each need from each other next quarter?
- Are roles still clear?

**Red flags in co-founder dynamic:**
- Avoiding each other
- Sniping in front of team
- Differential effort visible
- Different visions of success
- Resentment building
- Trust erosion

→ Co-founder mediator (specialized therapist) before it's too late.

### 6. Family / Significant Other

Startups consume relationships. Be intentional:

- Weekly date night (no phones)
- Monthly check-in: "How is this affecting you?"
- Quarterly: shared visioning of the next year
- Annual: celebrate the wins together

**Common patterns:**
- Spouse feels invisible after launch
- Spouse feels deceived if struggles hidden
- Spouse becomes resentful of the company

**Best practice:** Your spouse is your business partner whether they want to be or not. Treat them like one.

### 7. The "Hard Decision Test"

Before any major decision, check:

- [ ] Have I slept at least 6 hours the last 3 nights?
- [ ] Have I eaten in the last 4 hours?
- [ ] Am I making this from fear or from clarity?
- [ ] Have I run this by [trusted advisor]?
- [ ] If I waited 48 hours, would I make the same call?
- [ ] What would I tell another founder facing this?

**If any answer is concerning → don't decide yet.**
The decision can wait 24-72 hours unless someone's safety is at risk.

### 8. When to Step Back

Sometimes the answer is: take a real break.

**Sabbatical signals:**
- 3+ months of declining health scores
- Multiple burnout warning signs
- Repeated bad decisions
- Resentment dominating

**Structured break (1-2 weeks):**
- Hand off explicitly to co-founder or COO
- No Slack, no email
- Inform team and key stakeholders
- Real plans (travel, family, rest)

**Sabbatical (1-3 months):** Possible at Series B+ with strong leadership team.

**The hardest one: stepping down.** When you're the bottleneck and it's
not changing, sometimes the company needs a different leader. This is
brave, not weak.

## Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| "I'll rest after this milestone" | Always another milestone | Build rest into weekly routine |
| "I have to be on Slack 24/7" | Performative, not productive | Set boundaries, model them |
| Hiding struggles from team | Looks weak | Honest leadership builds trust |
| Hiding struggles from co-founder | Erodes partnership | Weekly real conversation |
| Hiding struggles from spouse | Damages relationship | Weekly check-in |
| No therapist because "I don't need it" | Pride before crisis | Preventive, not reactive |
| Using stimulants / alcohol to cope | Compounds, doesn't solve | Address the underlying |
| Making big decisions while dysregulated | Often wrong | Hard Decision Test |
