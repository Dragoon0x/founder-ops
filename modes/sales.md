# Mode: Sales

## Purpose

For founders running their own sales (pre-Head of Sales). Discovery calls,
demo flows, objection handling, proposal generation, follow-up cadences.

## Submodes

### 1. Discovery Call (30 min)

**Pre-call (5 min prep):**
```
- Their company: [size, stage, recent news]
- Their role: [title, tenure, what they own]
- Why they booked: [from form / email / referral source]
- Hypothesis about their pain: [what made them book]
- 3 questions to answer before this call ends
```

**Call structure (30 min):**
```
0-5 min   — Rapport + agenda confirmation
5-20 min  — DISCOVERY (their problem, current state, decision process)
20-25 min — Brief solution overview (NOT a demo)
25-30 min — Next step + close
```

**Discovery question bank:**

*Problem:*
- "Walk me through the last time [problem occurred]"
- "How are you solving this today?"
- "What's the cost of doing nothing for another 6 months?"
- "Who else is affected by this?"

*Decision process:*
- "If you decided to move forward, what would the next 30 days look like?"
- "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- "What would have to be true for this to be a no-brainer?"
- "What's your evaluation timeline?"

*Budget signal:*
- "What's the budget envelope you're working with?" (direct ask)
- "How are you funding this — existing budget or new line?"
- "What did you spend on [adjacent solution] last year?" (proxy)

**NEVER ask:**
- "Are you the decision maker?" (insulting)
- "Do you have budget?" (binary, useless)
- "Tell me about your business" (they hate explaining context)

### 2. Demo Flow

**Rule: 80% problem, 20% product.** Most founders flip this and lose deals.

**Demo structure (20-30 min):**
```
0-3 min   — Problem recap ("Here's what I heard you say...")
3-8 min   — Their specific use case in our product (LIVE, not slides)
8-15 min  — Walk through the workflow they described
15-20 min — Q&A
20-25 min — Next step
```

**Anti-patterns:**
- Showing every feature (= you didn't listen in discovery)
- Generic demo script (= they feel like #47 today)
- "And here's another cool thing" (= scope creep, dilutes value)
- Demo without their data/scenario (= abstract, not personal)

### 3. Objection Handling

**The 5 universal objections:**

| Objection | What it really means | Response framework |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| "Too expensive" | "I don't see the value" | Anchor to their current cost (time/money lost) |
| "We need to think about it" | "Something's unclear" | "What specifically would you want to think through?" |
| "We're doing this in-house" | "We don't trust you yet" | Show case study of similar company that tried both |
| "Bad timing" | "Not a priority" | "What would have to change for it to be a priority?" |
| "Need to talk to [other person]" | "I'm not the only buyer" | "Should we get them on the next call?" |

**Universal response pattern:**
```
1. Acknowledge ("That makes sense...")
2. Reframe ("What I'd add is...")
3. Specific evidence (case study, data, customer quote)
4. Question back ("Does that change how you see it?")
```

### 4. Proposal Generation

**For deals < $25K:** Email with terms. No proposal doc.
**For deals $25-100K:** 1-page proposal + MSA.
**For deals > $100K:** Full proposal + MSA + SOW + implementation plan.

**1-page proposal structure:**
```
## [Customer Name] × [Your Company]

### What you're getting
- [Specific deliverable 1]
- [Specific deliverable 2]
- [Specific deliverable 3]

### Investment
$[X] [annually | monthly | one-time]
- Includes: [list]
- Does not include: [list]

### Timeline
- Sign: [date]
- Kickoff: [date]
- Go-live: [date]
- First value: [date]

### Terms
- Payment: [Net 30 / annual upfront / monthly]
- Term: [12 months / month-to-month]
- Notice: [30 days]

### Next step
- Sign by [date] to lock pricing and start [date]
- Reply to this email or call [number]
```

### 5. Follow-Up Cadence

**After discovery call:**
- Day 0: Recap email within 4 hours (specific commitments + next step + date)
- Day 3: Light check-in if no response ("Sending what I promised")
- Day 7: Last follow-up ("Closing the loop — should I follow up later?")
- Day 14: Move to long-term nurture

**After demo:**
- Day 0: Recap + proposal within 24 hours
- Day 3: Follow up on proposal questions
- Day 7: Direct ask ("What's blocking a yes?")
- Day 14: "Closing my files unless..." (Breakup email — works surprisingly often)

### 6. Pipeline Stages (founder-led sales)

```
Stage 1: New — Just identified, not contacted
Stage 2: Contacted — Outreach sent, awaiting response
Stage 3: Discovery — Call scheduled or completed
Stage 4: Demo — Demo scheduled or completed
Stage 5: Proposal — Proposal sent
Stage 6: Negotiation — Active back-and-forth on terms
Stage 7: Close — Verbal yes, contract being signed
Stage 8: Won / Lost
```

**Stage advancement rules:**
- Move forward ONLY when there's a documented next step + date
- "I'll get back to you" = NOT advanced; back to previous stage
- Lost reasons MUST be specific (not "didn't fit" — WHY)

## Adversarial Check

For every proposal:
- Are we discounting because they pushed, or because there's a reason?
- Did we get a verbal "yes" before sending the contract?
- Is there a single decision-maker, or are we missing a stakeholder?
- What's the LOSS scenario, and have we addressed it explicitly?
