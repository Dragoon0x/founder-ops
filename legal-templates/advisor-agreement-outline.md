# Advisor Agreement Outline (FAST)

## What it is
The FAST (Founder/Advisor Standard Template) was created by Founder
Institute. It's the standard way to structure a formal advisor
relationship.

## The FAST equity framework

| Level | Time commitment | Standard equity (pre-seed) |
|-------|----------------|---------------------------|
| Standard | 4–8 hrs/month | 0.25% |
| Strategic | 8–16 hrs/month | 0.50% |
| Expert | 16+ hrs/month | 1.00% |

Adjust by stage:
- Pre-seed: full FAST percentages above
- Seed: 0.5x FAST (advisor dilution matters less when round sizes grow)
- Series A+: 0.25x FAST or just cash consulting

## Standard vesting
- **2-year vest**, monthly.
- Some templates include a **3-month cliff** (less common).
- **Termination for convenience** by either party with 30 days notice.

## The clauses that matter

### Scope of services
Specific or the agreement is worthless. Bad: "provide advice."
Good: "Attend monthly calls (60 min), review draft pitch materials,
provide 2 introductions per quarter to potential customers or
investors in the [industry] space."

### Time commitment
Specific monthly hours or cadence. Without this, you can't hold the
advisor accountable or terminate for non-performance.

### Compensation
Equity as above. Sometimes small cash for significant advisors
($500–2,000/month on top of equity).

### IP assignment
Any work product the advisor creates in the course of advising is
assigned to the company. Critical if they're writing code, docs,
or content for you.

### Confidentiality
Standard mutual NDA incorporated.

### Non-compete (sometimes)
Limit their advising to non-competing companies. If your advisor
advises three of your competitors, you're subsidizing their research.

### Termination
Either party, 30 days notice. Unvested equity returns to pool.

## Common mistakes

1. **No written agreement.** You "verbally agreed" to 1%. A year later
   the advisor forgot. You now owe more than you remember.
2. **Equity but no time commitment.** Advisor takes equity, does
   nothing, you can't terminate.
3. **No termination clause.** Stuck with an unproductive advisor.
4. **Vesting longer than 2 years.** Tied to advisor's interest, which
   fades.
5. **Equity as a favor, not for value.** If you don't expect real
   work, don't grant equity. Just stay in touch.
6. **Not getting IP assignment.** Especially if they review code or
   write content.

## The litmus test before signing
1. Can you name 3 specific things this advisor will do in the next 6 months?
2. Is their expertise directly relevant to your next 12 months?
3. Would you be willing to pay cash for the same value at market rates?
4. Are they willing to commit time in writing?
5. Do they have an exit plan if things don't work?

If any answer is "I'm not sure," don't sign.

## Termination guide

- If advisor delivers promised value: keep.
- If advisor delivers partial value: have a conversation, don't just
  terminate.
- If advisor delivers nothing 6 months in: terminate. No hard
  feelings. It happens.

Use the 30-day notice. Be professional. Unvested equity reverts.

## Tax notes
- Advisor equity is typically non-qualified stock options (NSOs).
- If granted at below-FMV strike, advisor has a tax hit.
- Get 409a before granting. Grant at or above FMV.

## Budget
- Total advisor equity at pre-seed: 2–5% of company.
- 4–8 advisors typical.
- Don't over-commit. Each advisor is 0.5–1% typically.
