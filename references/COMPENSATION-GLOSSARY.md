# Compensation Glossary

Reference for startup compensation: base, equity, bonus, benefits, structures.

## Base compensation concepts

**Base salary**: fixed annual cash. Paid on regular cadence.

**Total cash**: base + target bonus + commission. What's on a W2.

**Total comp**: total cash + equity value (at some assumed valuation).

**On-target earnings (OTE)**: base + commission at plan. Sales-specific.

**Sign-on bonus**: one-time payment at hire. Typically 5–25% of base. Usually clawed back if leaves within 12 months.

**Retention bonus**: payment if still employed at specific date. Used for key people post-acquisition.

## Equity types

**Stock options (ISOs)**: right to buy shares at strike price. Tax-advantaged if held properly.

**Non-qualified stock options (NSOs)**: options without ISO tax treatment. Taxed on exercise, then on sale.

**Restricted stock units (RSUs)**: grant of actual shares, usually over vesting. Typically at late-stage startups and public cos.

**Restricted stock (RSA)**: actual shares granted upfront, subject to vesting. Common for founders and earliest employees.

**Phantom equity / profit interest**: cash equivalent based on equity-like metric. Used when actual equity is impractical.

**Performance shares**: equity tied to company or individual performance metrics.

## Vesting mechanics

**Standard vest**: 4 years, 1 year cliff, monthly after. Means: 0% before year 1, 25% at year 1, 1/48th monthly thereafter.

**Cliff**: no vesting until a certain date. Standard 1 year.

**Acceleration**: faster vesting in certain events.
- Single trigger: acceleration on sale (rare for employees)
- Double trigger: sale + termination without cause (more common)

**Buyback on termination**: company can repurchase unvested (always) and sometimes vested shares if specific triggers. Founder-specific.

**Extended exercise window**: time after termination to exercise vested options. Standard 90 days; better practice 7–10 years.

**Early exercise**: ability to buy unvested shares for 83(b) election. Tax benefit if price low.

## Equity valuation

**Strike price**: price to exercise an option. Set at 409A fair market value on grant date.

**409A valuation**: independent appraisal of common stock, updated every 12 months or on material event. Sets strike price.

**Spread**: difference between strike price and current FMV. Taxed on exercise for NSOs; AMT consideration for ISOs.

**Preferred stock price**: price investors pay. Typically 3–5x common stock price.

**Common stock discount**: adjustment from preferred to common (typical 25–40%).

**Dilution**: reduction in ownership percentage as new shares issued. Affects existing employees.

**Option pool**: shares reserved for employees. Typically 10–15% at seed, 5–10% at A+.

## Compensation benchmarking

**Levels**: standardized career bands. L1–L9 in Google system; E1–E8 common variant.

**Bands**: pay ranges per level. Typical width: 30–50% (min to max).

**Percentile**: where a specific package lands. Market 25p / 50p / 75p.

**Radford / Option Impact / Carta**: benchmarking databases. Require data submission.

**Levels.fyi**: crowdsourced comp data. Better for public companies and big tech than startups.

**Pay equity**: analysis of compensation by gender, race. Required in some jurisdictions.

## Role-specific comp

### Engineering

- Junior: $110–160k base, 0.05–0.15% equity
- Mid: $140–200k base, 0.05–0.25% equity
- Senior: $160–240k base, 0.1–0.5% equity
- Staff: $200–300k base, 0.2–1.0% equity
- Principal: $250–400k base, 0.3–1.5% equity

Late-stage / public co multipliers: 1.5–3x these ranges.

### Sales

- SDR: $60–85k base, $80–120k OTE, 0.01–0.05% equity
- AE SMB: $80–110k base, $160–220k OTE, 0.05–0.15% equity
- AE MidMarket: $110–150k base, $220–300k OTE, 0.1–0.3% equity
- AE Enterprise: $140–200k base, $280–400k OTE, 0.15–0.5% equity
- Sales leader: $200–300k base, $400–600k OTE, 0.5–2.0% equity

### Executive

- VP Eng: $250–400k base, $300–500k OTE, 0.5–2.5% equity
- VP Sales: $250–400k base, $450–700k OTE, 0.8–3.0% equity
- CFO: $300–500k base, 0.5–1.5% equity
- COO: $300–500k base, 0.5–2.0% equity

## Bonus structures

**Annual bonus**: percentage of base tied to company and individual performance. Typical: 10–25% target.

**Quarterly commission**: sales-specific, paid monthly or quarterly. Tied to quota attainment.

**SPIF (Sales Performance Incentive Fund)**: short-term bonus for specific behavior.

**MBO (Management by Objectives)**: bonus tied to specific quarterly objectives. Used for non-sales leaders.

**Discretionary bonus**: awarded at year-end without formal structure. Common at early-stage.

## Benefits benchmarks

**Healthcare**: 100% employee premium paid is generous; 80–90% is standard; 50–70% is light. Dependent coverage: 50–75% employer-paid standard.

**Retirement**: 401(k) with 3–4% match becoming standard. Roth option expected.

**PTO**: unlimited (usually 15–20 days actual), fixed (20–25 days), minimum (15 days).

**Parental leave**: 16 weeks primary, 8 weeks secondary is increasingly standard; 12/6 was baseline.

**Equipment**: $1500–3000 stipend common, or company-provided.

**Learning budget**: $1000–3000/year typical.

**Wellness**: $50–100/month increasingly common.

## Promotion mechanics

**Merit increase**: annual raise for performing at level. 3–5% typical; 5–10% in growth years.

**Promotion increase**: on level change. 10–20% typical.

**Market adjustment**: off-cycle correction. Used to retain high-risk employees or fix benchmark gaps.

**Equity refresh**: additional grant on anniversary. Typically 30–50% of original, vesting from refresh date.

**Top-up**: additional grant mid-cycle for critical retention.

## International variations

**Remote adjustments**: typically 10–25% reductions from SF/NYC rates for lower-cost US; larger reductions for ex-US.

**Geo bands**: pay tied to location. Common; contested by employees.

**Location-independent pay**: single global band. Rare but exists.

**Exchange rate risk**: compensating in USD vs local currency. Material for long-tenured ex-US employees.

## Comp design principles

1. Pay transparency increases trust; rewards fairness
2. Bands prevent bias better than individual negotiation
3. Annual calibration prevents drift
4. Outside benchmarks every 6 months
5. Stock refresh matters as much as base increase for retention
6. Performance differentiation should show in comp (top decile: 20%+ delta)
