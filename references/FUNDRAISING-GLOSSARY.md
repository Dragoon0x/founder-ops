# Fundraising Glossary

Terms founders encounter during raises. Definitions are operational, not textbook.

## Valuation and terms

**Pre-money valuation**: value of the company before this round's investment. If you raise $5M at $20M pre, post-money is $25M.

**Post-money valuation**: value after investment. What everyone refers to publicly.

**Dilution**: ownership reduction. 25% of post-money goes to new investors = founders lose 25% of their ownership share.

**Fully diluted**: calculation that includes all outstanding shares, options, warrants, and convertible instruments. The number that actually matters.

**Option pool**: shares reserved for future employee grants. Typically 10–15% at seed, 5–10% at A.

**Option pool shuffle**: lead insists pool be expanded pre-round, diluting founders more than investors. Negotiable.

## Round mechanics

**Priced round**: investment at a specific valuation, issuing preferred shares. Contrasts with convertible instruments.

**SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)**: YC instrument. No repayment, converts at next priced round. Post-money SAFE is standard (favors investor).

**Convertible note**: debt that converts to equity. Has interest rate, maturity, cap. Less common now.

**Cap (valuation cap)**: maximum valuation at which a convertible converts. Favors investor if company does well.

**Discount**: percentage off the next round's price when convertible converts. Typically 10–20%.

**MFN (Most Favored Nation)**: if you give later investor better terms, earlier gets them too. Standard.

## Economic terms

**Liquidation preference**: money that comes back first in exit. 1x non-participating is standard at A+; 1x participating is harsher.

**Participating preferred**: investor gets preference back AND participates pro-rata in remainder. Avoid if possible.

**Anti-dilution**: protection if next round is at lower valuation. Weighted average is standard; full ratchet is harsh.

**Dividends**: rare but sometimes included. 6–8% cumulative is common when present.

**Redemption rights**: investor can force buyback after 5+ years. Rarely enforced but exists.

## Control terms

**Board composition**: who sits. Founder-favorable: founders + independent + investor (2-1-1). Investor-favorable: 2 investors + founder + independent + CEO (3-1-1-1).

**Protective provisions**: actions requiring investor approval. Standard: sale of company, new senior stock, option pool changes, dividends.

**Drag-along**: majority can force minority to sell in exit. Standard.

**Information rights**: quarterly financials, annual budget. Standard for $100k+ check.

**Pro rata rights**: right to participate in future rounds at same ownership %. Heavily negotiated.

**ROFR (Right of First Refusal)**: investor can match any third-party offer for your shares. Standard for secondary.

## Due diligence

**Term sheet**: non-binding summary of deal. 2–4 pages. Signed before full docs.

**Definitive agreements**: binding contracts. Stock purchase, voting, investor rights, management rights letter. 80–150 pages total.

**Data room**: organized document repository. Typically 12 categories (corporate, financial, technical, commercial, IP, employment, legal, tax, insurance, customer, product, market).

**Reps and warranties**: factual statements founders make about the company. Tested via indemnification.

**Indemnification**: if a rep is wrong, founders/company pay investor back. Baskets and caps matter.

**Closing conditions**: things that must be true at closing (reps still accurate, material adverse change didn't happen).

## Tactical terms

**Bridge**: small round between formal rounds. Same terms as last round usually.

**Extension**: adding to existing round, same terms. Different from bridge.

**Down round**: valuation lower than previous round. Triggers anti-dilution, signals distress.

**Flat round**: valuation matches previous round. Unusual, often negotiated with additional features.

**Up round**: valuation higher than previous round. Normal healthy progression.

**Inside round**: existing investors lead. Common when outside leads are hard to get. Signals: maturity, or difficulty.

**Party round**: many small checks, no single lead. Can work at seed, hard at A.

## Process terms

**Lead investor**: writes largest check, negotiates terms, often takes board seat.

**Following investor**: writes smaller check after lead is established. Less negotiating leverage.

**Bake-off**: multiple leads running parallel processes.

**Exploding offer**: term sheet with short fuse (48–72 hours). Pressure tactic.

**Term sheet signing**: founders and lead sign. Starts exclusivity clock (usually 30–60 days).

**Exclusivity (no-shop)**: can't shop terms during this window. Breaks: severe term changes by investor.

**Definitive docs**: 4–6 weeks typical from term sheet to close.

**Wire**: the actual money arriving. Signing doesn't equal funded; wire does.

## People terms

**Vesting**: ownership earned over time. Standard: 4 years, 1 year cliff.

**Acceleration on change of control**: ownership vests faster in acquisition. Single trigger (automatic on sale) or double trigger (sale + termination).

**Founder vesting**: founders typically vest over 4 years with 1 year cliff. Investors may require reset in priced rounds.

**Buyback rights**: company can repurchase unvested shares if founder leaves. Standard.

**Key person provisions**: investment tied to specific founder(s). If they leave, triggers review.

## Exit terms

**Fully diluted payout**: when you exit, everyone with any claim gets a share. Common is diluted more than founders expect.

**Preferred stack**: order of liquidation preferences. Later preferred often ranks senior to earlier.

**Over-the-top returns**: above the preference stack, everyone participates pro-rata.

**Management carve-out**: portion of exit proceeds set aside for team. 5–15% typical.

**Earnout**: portion of deal value tied to future performance. Often underperforms.

**Escrow**: portion held back for post-close claims. 10–15% for 12–18 months.

## Valuation benchmarks (2025–2026 for reference only)

| Stage | Pre-money range | Round size | Ownership |
|-------|----------------|------------|-----------|
| Pre-seed | $4–10M | $500k–2M | 10–20% |
| Seed | $8–25M | $2–6M | 15–25% |
| Series A | $25–80M | $8–25M | 15–25% |
| Series B | $100–300M | $25–60M | 15–25% |
| Series C | $300M–1B | $40–150M | 10–20% |
| Series D+ | $800M+ | $75M+ | 5–15% |

These shift quarter to quarter. Use recent comps for actual benchmarking.
