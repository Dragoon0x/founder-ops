# Reference: Frameworks Used in Founder-Ops

## Customer Discovery
- **The Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick):** Don't ask hypotheticals. Ask about past behavior. Don't pitch your idea — listen.
- **Jobs to Be Done (Clayton Christensen):** Customers "hire" your product to do a job. Understand the job, not the demographic.
- **Sequential hypothesis testing:** Each interview tests a specific belief. Update beliefs based on evidence.

## Prioritization
- **RICE (Intercom):** Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort
- **ICE (Sean Ellis):** Impact × Confidence × Ease (faster, less precise)
- **MoSCoW:** Must / Should / Could / Won't
- **Kano model (Noriaki Kano):** Basic / Performance / Delight / Indifferent

## Positioning
- **Obviously Awesome (April Dunford):** Competitive alternatives → Unique attributes → Value → Who cares → Market category
- **Positioning (Trout & Ries):** Position relative to competitors in customer's mind
- **Crossing the Chasm (Geoffrey Moore):** Different segments need different positioning

## Product Strategy
- **Hooked (Nir Eyal):** Trigger → Action → Reward → Investment
- **The Lean Startup (Eric Ries):** Build → Measure → Learn
- **Crossing the Chasm (Geoffrey Moore):** Innovators → Early adopters → Early majority → Late majority → Laggards
- **Switch (Heath brothers):** Direct the rider, motivate the elephant, shape the path

## Pricing
- **Value-based pricing (TJ Parker):** Price to value, not cost or competition
- **Price packaging (Patrick Campbell):** Tiers, features, addons map to willingness to pay
- **Anchoring:** First number sets the perception of the rest

## Growth
- **AARRR / Pirate Metrics (Dave McClure):** Acquisition / Activation / Retention / Referral / Revenue
- **Growth loops vs. funnels:** Loops compound, funnels leak
- **The North Star Framework (Sean Ellis):** One metric that captures the core value delivered to customers

## Hiring
- **Topgrading (Brad Smart):** Detailed work history interviews predict performance
- **Who: The A Method (Geoff Smart):** Scorecard → Source → Select → Sell
- **The Effective Executive (Drucker):** Effectiveness can be learned. Hire for it.

## Fundraising
- **The Power Law (Sebastian Mallaby):** VC returns are power-law distributed; pick partners who understand outliers
- **The Pitch Anything (Oren Klaff):** Frame control matters more than content
- **Venture Deals (Brad Feld):** Standard term sheet language and what's negotiable

## Decision Making
- **Type 1 vs Type 2 decisions (Bezos):** Reversible vs irreversible. Treat differently.
- **Pre-mortem (Gary Klein):** Imagine the failure first; identify what could cause it
- **OODA loop (John Boyd):** Observe → Orient → Decide → Act (faster cycles win)

## Strategy
- **Good Strategy / Bad Strategy (Richard Rumelt):** Diagnosis → Guiding policy → Coherent action
- **Playing to Win (A.G. Lafley):** Where will you play? How will you win?
- **Blue Ocean Strategy (Kim & Mauborgne):** Create new markets vs. competing in existing ones

## Operations
- **The E-Myth Revisited (Michael Gerber):** Work ON the business, not IN the business
- **Traction / EOS (Gino Wickman):** Vision / People / Data / Issues / Process / Traction
- **The Goal (Eli Goldratt):** Identify the bottleneck, optimize around it

## Execution
- **The 4 Disciplines of Execution (Covey):** WIG / Lead measures / Compelling scoreboard / Cadence of accountability
- **OKRs (John Doerr):** Objectives (qualitative) + Key Results (measurable, time-bound)
- **5 Whys (Toyota):** Ask "why" 5 times to find root cause

## Communication
- **Pyramid Principle (Barbara Minto):** Lead with the answer; support with structured arguments
- **The Effective Executive (Drucker):** Listening and writing are the most underrated executive skills
- **Crucial Conversations (Patterson et al.):** High-stakes, high-emotion conversations require specific structure

## Mental Models
- **First principles (Aristotle, Musk):** Decompose to fundamentals; reason up from there
- **Inversion (Charlie Munger):** What would guarantee failure? Avoid those.
- **Circle of competence (Buffett):** Operate within what you genuinely understand
- **Margin of safety (Graham, Buffett):** Build in buffer for unknown unknowns
- **Opportunity cost:** Saying yes to one thing means saying no to others
