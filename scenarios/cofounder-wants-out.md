# Scenario: Co-Founder Wants to Leave

## Trigger
Co-founder expresses desire to leave or you realize alignment is broken.

## Severity Assessment
- **Vesting status**: How much equity has vested? How much is unvested?
- **Function coverage**: What does this co-founder own? Who can step in?
- **Investor relationships**: Are investors invested because of this person?
- **Team impact**: How will the team react?
- **IP risk**: Does the leaving co-founder have critical IP knowledge?

## Immediate Response (1 week)
1. Have a direct, private conversation. No intermediaries.
2. Understand their motivation: Is it fixable? (burnout → fixable. Disagreement on vision → maybe not.)
3. If fixable: propose specific changes (role adjustment, leave of absence, reduced scope)
4. If not fixable: agree on timeline and principles for a clean separation
5. Engage a lawyer. This is too important for DIY.

## Separation Framework
- **Equity**: Unvested shares return to pool. Vested shares: negotiate. Standard: they keep vested minus any buyback provisions.
- **Transition**: 30-60 day transition period. Knowledge transfer is the priority.
- **Communication**: Joint announcement to team. Positive framing if genuine. Honest if needed.
- **Non-compete/non-solicit**: Standard 12-month provisions. Don't be vindictive.
- **Board seat**: If they had one, they resign it. Replace with independent director or leave empty.

## 30-Day Actions
- Redistribute responsibilities. Don't just spread them thin; actually restructure.
- Brief investors privately before public announcement
- Hire or promote a replacement for the critical function
- Update all legal documents (operating agreement, cap table, bank signers)

## Common Mistakes
- Avoiding the conversation until it explodes
- Fighting over unvested equity instead of negotiating cleanly
- Letting the departure become public before it's handled internally
- Assuming the team doesn't know (they always know something is off)
