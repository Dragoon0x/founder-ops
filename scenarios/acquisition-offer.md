# Scenario: You Receive an Acquisition Offer

## Trigger
Another company expresses interest in acquiring yours.

## Initial Assessment (1-2 weeks)
Before engaging, answer:
1. **Is this real?** (Is there a specific offer or just "let's explore"?)
2. **Who's asking?** (Strategic acquirer vs PE vs competitor)
3. **Why now?** (What changed in their business that makes this interesting?)
4. **What's your alternative?** (What does the company look like in 2 years if you stay independent?)

## Decision Framework
Consider selling when:
- Offer > 3x what you'd raise at in a funding round
- You've been offered a strategic home that accelerates your mission
- Founder is burned out and team would benefit from a larger platform
- Market conditions are deteriorating and this is the best exit window

Consider declining when:
- Offer < your fair standalone value (revenue multiple × growth premium)
- You're in the early innings of a large market opportunity
- The acquirer's culture would kill your product/team
- Accepting would betray commitments to investors, employees, or customers

## Process
1. **NDA**: Before sharing any data, execute mutual NDA
2. **Information request**: Share metrics but not IP or customer data initially
3. **Valuation discussion**: Get a specific range before opening the data room
4. **Advisory**: Engage M&A counsel and investment banker (if deal > $20M)
5. **Board involvement**: Inform board immediately. Fiduciary duty requires it.
6. **Due diligence**: 30-60 day process. Be prepared to share everything.
7. **Negotiation**: Price, retention packages, product roadmap commitments, employment terms
8. **Closing**: 60-90 days from LOI to close

## Employee Considerations
- Key employee retention bonuses (typically 15-25% of their annual comp, vesting over 1-2 years)
- Equity acceleration triggers (double-trigger is standard)
- Role clarity post-acquisition (who keeps their job, who doesn't)
- Communication timing: employees learn before the public, after the deal is signed
