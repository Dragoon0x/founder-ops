# Scenario: Economic Downturn / Market Crash

## Trigger
Broad economic contraction affecting your market. Funding dries up. Customers cut budgets.

## Severity Assessment
- How exposed is your revenue to discretionary spending? (Nice-to-have vs must-have)
- How dependent are you on VC funding for survival?
- What's your customer base's economic exposure? (SMBs affected more than enterprise)
- How long is your runway without new revenue growth?

## Immediate Response (Week 1-2)
1. **Cash conservation**: Freeze all non-essential spending immediately
2. **Revenue risk audit**: Contact top 20 customers. Are they renewing? Any budget cuts coming?
3. **Runway recalculation**: Model 3 scenarios: base case, -20% revenue, -40% revenue
4. **Hiring freeze**: Pause all open roles except revenue-generating positions

## 30-Day Plan
- Identify customers most likely to churn (SMBs, those in hard-hit industries)
- Offer flexible payment terms to retain at-risk accounts (quarterly → monthly, discount for early renewal)
- Shift positioning to cost-savings and ROI (not growth and innovation)
- Reduce burn by 20-30% through: contractor cuts, tool consolidation, marketing spend reduction

## 90-Day Plan
- If downturn is deepening: prepare for layoffs with 6+ months of runway post-cuts
- If stabilizing: cautiously resume hiring for critical roles
- Look for acquisition opportunities (competitors in trouble = your gain)
- Build cash reserves (raise if you can, even on worse terms than ideal)

## Opportunity in Downturns
- Talent becomes available (hire great people laid off from other companies)
- Competitors weaken or die (gain market share)
- Customers consolidate vendors (if you're essential, you survive; nice-to-haves get cut)
- Real-estate / infrastructure costs decrease
- Forces operational discipline that serves you well when conditions improve
