# Scenario: Your Largest Customer Churns

## Trigger
Customer representing > 15% of your ARR gives notice of cancellation.

## Severity Assessment
- Revenue impact: Calculate exact MRR/ARR loss
- Runway impact: Does this change your runway materially? (Recalculate with the revenue gone)
- Reference impact: Were they a key reference customer? Logo on your website?
- Product impact: Were features built specifically for them?
- Team impact: Did you hire people specifically to serve them?

## Immediate Response (48 hrs)
1. CEO calls their executive sponsor. Not email. Call.
2. Understand the real reason (not the official reason): Price? Product gap? Competitor? Internal change?
3. If saveable: What would it take to keep them? What are you willing to do?
4. If not saveable: Negotiate transition timeline (60-90 day wind-down, not immediate cut)
5. Internal comms: inform leadership team immediately, broader team within 48 hrs

## 30-Day Plan
- Financial reforecast: Updated projections without this revenue
- Pipeline acceleration: Can you close pending deals faster to offset?
- Cost adjustment: If revenue gap > 10% of ARR, identify expense cuts
- Root cause fix: If the churn reason is a product gap, prioritize fixing it before other customers follow
- Customer risk audit: Are other large customers showing similar signals?

## 60-Day Plan
- Revenue recovery: New logos + expansion of existing customers to close the gap
- Concentration policy: Implement a rule that no single customer should be > 10% of ARR
- Pricing review: If you lost on price, evaluate whether your pricing is competitive
- Win-back plan: In 6 months, reach out to see if circumstances have changed

## Kill Criteria
If losing this customer drops runway below 9 months and no fundraise is imminent: trigger the cash-crisis signal response immediately.
