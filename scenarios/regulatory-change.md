# Scenario: Major Regulatory Change Affects Your Business

## Trigger
New law, regulation, or enforcement action that directly impacts how your product works, who you can sell to, or what data you can collect.

## Examples
- GDPR-style privacy regulation in your primary market
- Industry-specific regulation (fintech licensing, health data requirements)
- AI regulation affecting your product's functionality
- Export controls limiting where you can sell
- Data localization requirements

## Immediate Response (1 week)
1. **Legal review**: Engage outside counsel with specific expertise in the regulation
2. **Impact assessment**: Which products, features, customers, and markets are affected?
3. **Timeline**: When does compliance become mandatory? (Grace periods vary)
4. **Competitive scan**: How are competitors responding?

## Compliance Plan
1. **Gap analysis**: Current state vs required state
2. **Engineering estimate**: What product changes are needed and how long?
3. **Legal cost**: What certifications, audits, or legal filings are required?
4. **Go/no-go**: Is compliance feasible and worth the cost, or should you exit the affected market?

## Communication
- Customers: proactive notification of what's changing and your compliance timeline
- Investors: impact assessment and remediation plan in next board update
- Team: clear communication about what changes and what doesn't

## Common Mistakes
- Ignoring regulations hoping they won't be enforced (they will)
- Over-investing in compliance before requirements are clear (regulations change during comment periods)
- Treating compliance as only a legal problem (it's a product and engineering problem too)
- Not seeing regulatory compliance as a competitive moat (if you comply and competitors don't, you win when enforcement starts)
