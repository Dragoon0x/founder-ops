# Case Study: Founder Burnout Year 4

## Setup
- Stage: Series B, company profitable
- Team: 85 people
- Revenue: $18M ARR, 40% YoY growth
- Runway: Infinite (profitable)
- Situation: Founder-CEO had been operating for 4.5 years. Persistent insomnia, 60-hour weeks, first panic attack in month 52. Marriage strained. Doctor strongly recommended a sabbatical.

## What happened
The founder told the board at a regular monthly meeting. Framing: "I need 6 weeks off. Here's the plan for how the company runs without me."

The plan:
- COO acts as interim CEO.
- Weekly async update from COO to founder (one paragraph, no decisions required).
- Three named decision carve-outs that require the founder's approval (hires >$250K, any press, any acquisition discussion).
- Everything else: COO decides.
- No Slack access on the founder's phone for 6 weeks.
- Board chair becomes the board's point person.

Board response: unanimously supportive. One board member said, "We'd rather you take 6 weeks now than 6 months later from a hospital."

## The 6 weeks
Week 1: Slept 10 hours a day. Didn't open a laptop. Went to therapy twice.
Week 2: Started exercising daily. Read two books unrelated to work.
Week 3: Had first moment of "I miss it." Did not respond to any emails.
Week 4: Therapist and founder drafted a "sustainable operating rhythm" for the return.
Week 5: Reviewed one Slack digest per day (COO sent). Did not reply.
Week 6: Met with COO twice. Reviewed what had happened. Returned week 7.

## What the COO did in those 6 weeks
- Closed 3 enterprise deals.
- Did not ship any new product initiatives.
- Did not make any pricing changes.
- Hired 2 planned roles, paused 2 unplanned ones.
- Ran a retention review of the team — 1 senior engineer was flight-risk, COO addressed it.

The company did not break. Revenue grew. Attrition stayed flat. One customer asked about the founder's absence and was told "on sabbatical, back in 6 weeks." They were fine.

## What changed after
The founder put these changes in place:
1. **Hard stop at 6pm, four nights a week.** No Slack past 6pm on those days.
2. **Two weeks of vacation booked in advance, every quarter.** Not "when I have time."
3. **One therapy session per week. Non-negotiable.** Paid by the company as a health benefit.
4. **COO given real authority, not theoretical.** The founder actively stopped making decisions that could be the COO's.
5. **No founder Slack DMs for "emergencies."** Emergencies go through the exec team first. If they can't handle it, they escalate.
6. **A "what breaks if I disappear" doc.** Updated quarterly. Pre-committed decision rules for the top 15 scenarios.

## Outcome (2 years later)
- Revenue: $42M ARR (2.3x growth in 24 months).
- Team: 160 people.
- Founder: still operating, still healthy, no further burnout episodes.
- COO: promoted to President year 5.

## What I'd do differently
1. **Take the first sabbatical at year 2, not year 4.** Don't wait for the breaking point.
2. **Build the "COO can run it" muscle earlier.** If you can't leave for 2 weeks without the company wobbling, you have an execution problem, not a time-off problem.
3. **Don't treat the sabbatical as a luxury.** It's infrastructure for a long operating career.

## Generalizable lesson
Founder burnout is a risk that compounds silently until it's a crisis. The companies that survive long-term build in recovery as a system, not as a reward. A CEO who can leave for 6 weeks without the company wobbling is a CEO who has built a real company. A CEO who can't is a bottleneck.

## The "can you take 2 weeks off" test
Ask yourself, then ask your COO:
- Do we have pre-committed decision rules for the top 10 recurring decisions?
- Does the exec team know what's a "founder-only" decision vs theirs?
- Are there any single-point-of-failure dependencies on the founder (a key customer relationship, a board dynamic, a hiring decision)?
- Can the team ship a normal week without founder input?

If the answer to any of these is no, you're a bottleneck. Fix it before you need to.

## Resources used
- Therapist (CBT-focused, familiar with high-stress roles). 2x/week during sabbatical, 1x/week after.
- Reboot.io (executive coaching). Joined a founder peer group.
- "When Things Fall Apart" (Pema Chödrön). Not a business book. Helpful.
- 6 weeks was the right length. 4 would have been too short. 8 would have started to atrophy the operational muscle.
