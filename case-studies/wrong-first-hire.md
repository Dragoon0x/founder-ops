# Case Study: Wrong First Hire (VP Sales Before PMF)

## Setup
- Stage: Seed, 6 months post-raise
- Team: 2 founders + 2 engineers
- Revenue: $8K MRR, 6 customers, no repeatable sales process
- Runway: 16 months
- Situation: Founders were getting inbound, closing 1 out of every 4 demos. They thought adding a VP Sales would let them scale.

## What happened
They hired a VP Sales from a Series B company. Package: $180K base + $180K OTE + 1.5% equity. Start date: month 7. The VP's first actions:
- Built out a 6-person sales pipeline (2 AEs, 1 SDR, Salesforce, Outreach, Gong, LinkedIn Sales Navigator). Total tooling cost: $38K/year.
- Set quarterly quotas before seeing a month of data.
- Rebuilt the demo script, removing the founder's opening ("here's why we built this") in favor of a standard discovery frame.
- Hired 1 AE at month 8.

By month 10:
- MRR had grown from $8K to $12K. Close rate dropped from 25% to 14%.
- The VP started blaming "unqualified leads" and pushed marketing to spend more on top-of-funnel.
- Founder sales involvement dropped to zero (as the VP had requested).
- Sales cycle doubled from 3 weeks to 6 weeks.

The founders let the VP go at month 11. Severance: 3 months base = $45K. Sunk cost including tooling, AE salary, and recruiting: ~$220K.

## What I'd do differently
1. **Don't hire a VP before $1M ARR and a repeatable motion.** The heuristic is: can you describe the 5-step sales process that works every time? If no, you don't have a process to scale, you have a founder hustle. A VP can't fix that by hiring a team.
2. **Hire 1 AE before you hire a VP.** The AE teaches you what's repeatable. The VP scales what you've proven. In that order.
3. **Interview for "I'll sell for 6 months before hiring anyone," not "I'll build a team."** The VPs who answer the second question are interviewing for Series B companies, not seed.
4. **The founder should have stayed on 50% of the demos.** The VP asked for autonomy. It was granted too fast.
5. **Don't let the VP buy Salesforce in week 2.** HubSpot starter plan is fine until $2M ARR.

## What happened after
- Founders sold the next 6 months themselves. Revenue grew from $12K to $38K MRR.
- Hired an AE at $6K MRR average deal size, $80K OTE, no equity premium.
- By month 18, hired a Head of Sales (not VP) at $150K OTE + 0.75% equity.
- Closed Series A at month 22.

## Generalizable lesson
The right first sales hire at seed is an AE or a player-coach head, not a VP. A VP's job is to build an org, not to sell. If you don't have enough sales to need an org, you're buying the VP a problem to solve, and they'll invent one.

## The test for "are you ready for a VP Sales"
- ARR > $1M annualized
- 3+ months of consistent MoM growth
- Close rate within 20% band for 6 months
- Average contract value you can forecast within 25%
- 2+ AEs already hitting 80% of quota

If any of these are no, hire an AE or a Head of Sales, not a VP.
