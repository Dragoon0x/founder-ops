# Case Study: Pivot After 6 Months (B2C → B2B)

## Setup
- Stage: Pre-seed, 9 months post-incorporation
- Team: 2 founders, 1 contractor
- Revenue: $400 MRR from 38 consumer subscribers
- Runway: 14 months
- Situation: Growth stalled at 40 new signups/month, CAC was $22, LTV was $18. Product was a habit-tracking app for adults. Founders noticed that the 3 customers with the most engagement were HR teams at small companies, not individuals.

## What happened
Founders ran 12 customer interviews in 2 weeks. Four were with existing individual users, eight were with HR leaders they found via LinkedIn. The signal from HR leaders was clear: they had budget, they had pain (employee wellbeing programs weren't being used), and they were buying point solutions at $8–15 per employee per month.

The founders made the pivot decision in a single weekend meeting:
1. Stop all B2C marketing spend (~$1,800/month).
2. Keep the product live for existing users, no new features for 60 days.
3. Build a 3-person admin dashboard on top of the existing app.
4. Target 50-person companies. Charge $10/employee/month.
5. Goal: 3 paid pilots within 90 days.

They killed their D2C email list strategy, cut the Instagram ads, and refocused the entire roadmap around admin visibility, SSO, and a simple reporting view.

## Outcome
Day 90: 2 paid pilots live, 1 verbal commit. Revenue: $4,200 MRR.
Day 180: 7 paid customers, $14K MRR, raised a $1.2M seed.
Day 365: $38K MRR, 19 customers, hired a head of sales.

The old consumer app was sunset at day 240. They refunded the remaining annual subscribers ($1,140 total).

## What I'd do differently
1. **Run the interviews earlier.** The signal was visible in the data at month 4. They waited until month 8 because pivoting felt like admitting failure.
2. **Refund the B2C users the day of the pivot, not 8 months later.** It was a distraction every time someone emailed support.
3. **Kill the Instagram account.** They kept it as a "brand asset" for 4 months. It was confusing to B2B buyers who googled the company.
4. **Don't try to reuse the B2C pricing page.** They did, and it took 6 weeks for conversion to improve after they rebuilt it for procurement buyers.

## Generalizable lesson
If your power users are a different ICP than your target ICP, your target ICP is wrong. Follow the engagement, not the plan. Pivoting is cheap at pre-seed, expensive at Series A, ruinous at Series B.

## Signals that suggested the pivot was working
- Sales cycle shrunk from "we don't have a sales cycle" to 3 weeks.
- Time-to-first-value went from 11 days (individual habit formation) to 2 days (admin sees first dashboard).
- Retention at day 60 went from 18% to 89%.

## Signals that would have said "this isn't working"
- Pilots converting at < 40% to paid.
- Admin dashboard usage below 1x/week.
- CAC payback longer than 14 months at the new price point.
