## Executive summary

Q3 delivered on revenue and gross margin targets but missed on net revenue retention and cost of sales. We're revising the plan for Q4 to focus on expansion motion (post-sale) and pausing new-market expansion until NRR recovers to 115%.

---

## What we're proud of

1. **Gross margin improved to 81%** — we re-architected our inference stack and cut compute cost per customer by 22%.
2. **Average deal size up 34%** — we moved upmarket faster than planned. Median ACV now $42K (was $31K).
3. **Time to first value down to 6 days** (from 14) — onboarding rebuild paid off.

## What we're worried about

1. **NRR dropped from 118% to 108%.** Three expansion deals we were counting on pushed to Q4. Two accounts downgraded seats. One enterprise logo (5% of ARR) is renewing flat, not expanding.
2. **S&M Magic Number fell to 0.7.** We hired aggressively in Q2 expecting Q3 ramp. AEs 2, 3, 4 are all below quota. Ramp is taking 4.5 months, not 3.
3. **Our #1 competitor (Orion) raised $40M and is now free for teams under 10.** We're seeing them in more deals and losing 60% head-to-head in SMB.

## Decisions we're making

1. **Pause new AE hiring for Q4.** We have 3 AEs under 60% quota. Get them to ramp before adding more.
2. **Launch an expansion team (2 CSMs + 1 expansion AE).** Reassign one existing AE. Target: 115% NRR by end of Q1.
3. **Accept that SMB is a losing position for us.** We'll sunset the $99/mo plan by Q1. Focus on $30K+ ACV. This will hurt logo count but improve unit economics.
4. **Delay UK expansion by 2 quarters.** Redirect that budget to expansion team and product-led onboarding.

## Q4 plan (revised)

- Net new ARR: $1.4M
- NRR: 115%
- Burn: $650K/mo (down from $735K — we're cutting 3 open reqs and reducing ad spend)
- Runway: 17 mo by EOY
