# Q3 → Q4 Strategy Review (Example)

**Purpose:** Once per quarter, we stop everything and ask: what did we learn this quarter that should change our strategy? This is NOT the OKR-planning meeting. This is one level above.

---

## What we believed at the start of Q3

1. SMB self-serve + mid-market sales-led would be our two motions
2. Our ICP was RevOps leaders at 200-800 person B2B SaaS companies
3. Orion was a competitor but we'd differentiate on enterprise features
4. We'd be ready to expand into UK by end of Q4

## What we learned in Q3

1. **SMB is a losing position for us.** Gross margin on SMB is 62% (vs. 83% on mid-market). Churn is 4x higher. We lose to Orion 60% of the time because they're 40% cheaper and the features don't matter at that tier.
2. **Our ICP is too broad.** The 200-800 range lumps companies that need 3 very different product experiences. The 500+ segment has 3.2x higher NDR and 2x higher ACV. That's our real ICP.
3. **Enterprise differentiation is real and working.** We're winning 70% head-to-head at >$30K ACV. Customers cite SOC 2, Salesforce integration, and our CS quality.
4. **UK expansion is a 2026 problem.** Our focus needs to be on doubling down on core ICP before we add complexity.

## What we're changing

### Change 1: Sunset SMB tier by Jan 1
- New minimum ACV: $12K (was $1,188)
- Expected impact: lose ~18 logos, gain focus, free up ~15% of support capacity
- Risk: appears as logo count drop in metrics for 1-2 quarters
- Mitigation: communicate to board proactively before they see it

### Change 2: Narrow ICP to 500+ person B2B SaaS with existing RevOps function
- Update outbound targeting
- Update pricing to reflect new ICP expectations ($24K ACV median target)
- Retire 3 marketing pages aimed at SMB

### Change 3: Delay UK expansion to Q2 2026
- Redirect UK budget ($180K) to expansion team

### Change 4: Commit to expansion motion as #1 priority
- 2 CSMs + 1 expansion AE
- Target: 130% NDR by end of Q1 2026

## What we're NOT changing

- Core product direction
- Pricing model (per-seat + usage, just raising floor)
- Engineering team size or structure
- Our positioning against Orion (we're the serious-mid-market alternative)

## What could make us wrong

1. If mid-market TAM is smaller than we think and we can't hit growth targets on mid-market alone
2. If UK is a must-have for one of our big deals (has happened twice)
3. If losing SMB volume hurts our PLG flywheel (right now SMB signups don't convert upmarket)

## Questions we're putting on hold

- Should we build API products? (Revisit Q2 2026)
- Should we raise earlier at higher ARR? (Revisit after Q4 numbers)
- Should we add a partnerships motion? (Revisit Q2 2026)
