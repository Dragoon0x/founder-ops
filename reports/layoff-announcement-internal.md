# Layoff Announcement — Internal Memo (Example)

**Note:** This is the hardest memo you'll ever write. Get it read by at least 3 people who've done this before. Do the layoffs in the morning, not at 5pm. Tell everyone face-to-face (or video) before the written memo goes out.

---

**From:** Founder
**To:** Team
**Sent:** 10:15am, Wednesday (15 minutes after the last 1:1 conversation ended)

---

Team,

By now, most of you know that we made the hardest decision of this company this morning. We let 8 people go — about 20% of the company. The people affected have been told individually.

This memo is for everyone who remains. I'm not going to soften what happened.

## What we decided

We cut 8 roles across engineering (3), sales (2), marketing (2), and operations (1). We also paused 4 open reqs. Combined, this extends our runway from 9 months to 17 months.

## Why

Our plan for this year was to hit $4M ARR by December and raise a Series B. We're going to hit $2.7M. The market shifted. Our competitive position shifted. We built a cost structure for a company growing 3x. We're growing 1.8x.

I should have made this decision 4 months ago. Instead, I waited to see if we'd course-correct. We didn't. Waiting cost us another $900K of runway and put more people at risk. That is on me.

## Who we let go

Every person who left today is an excellent professional. None of them were let go because of performance. All of them were let go because the roles they held are not the roles we need in the company we're becoming. I've written individual references for each of them. I'm personally helping each of them find their next role.

## What happens next for them

1. Severance: 10 weeks plus 1 additional week for each year of tenure
2. Benefits: full healthcare continues through February 28
3. Vesting: 3 additional months of vesting credit
4. Reference: I will personally speak to any potential employer
5. Network: we'll send a Slack thread (opt-in) to 200+ founders and hiring managers we know

## What happens next for us

I'm going to be unusually available for the next 2 weeks. Drop by. Ask anything. The 3 questions I know are coming:

**"Am I next?"**
No. The restructuring we just did is the restructuring we intend to do. If the business gets materially worse, we'd discuss it openly before anyone is surprised.

**"Why these people and not others?"**
Decisions were made by role, not person. We cut entire functions (paid marketing, mid-market outbound) and reduced headcount in others. Every surviving role is a role we're committed to for the next 18 months.

**"What's the plan now?"**
Three focus areas for the next 6 months:
1. Existing customer expansion (NDR from 108% to 125%)
2. Enterprise-only new logos (ACV from $31K to $60K+)
3. Product-led retention (cut churn from 4.2%/quarter to <2%)

Full plan in the all-hands on Friday.

## What I owe you

1. The truth, always, before you have to ask
2. A rational plan that doesn't require another layoff to work
3. My personal focus — I'm clearing my calendar of everything non-essential for the next 60 days
4. A commitment that we'll talk openly about how we're doing, in our 1:1s, whether we're on track or off

## What I'm asking of you

1. Reach out to the people leaving today. Not to ask about work. Just to be a friend.
2. Don't post internal information externally until Friday's public announcement.
3. Tell me what you need. I'd rather know now than guess.

I'm sorry this is the update I'm sending. I'm proud of this team. I'm going to spend every remaining dollar and hour on making this company worth what you've all put into it.

All-hands Friday at 11am. Open agenda.

— [Founder]
