# End-of-Year All-Hands (Example Talking Points)

**Format:** 90-minute all-hands, last business day of the year
**Structure:** 30 min talking points, 30 min team awards, 30 min Q&A

---

## Opening (5 min)

"Three years ago, there were 4 of us in a WeWork that smelled like stale coffee. Today there are 38 of us across 6 countries. Before I talk about numbers, I want to say something honest: we wouldn't be here without any one of you. Everyone in this room has done something this year that the rest of us literally couldn't have done. I'm going to call those out specifically."

## The numbers (10 min)

"Here's what you built this year."

- Revenue: from $1.3M to $4.8M (3.7x)
- Customers: from 112 to 310
- Team: from 18 to 38
- Gross margin: 71% → 82%
- NDR: 104% → 122%
- Runway: 11 months → 22 months (after Series A)
- Countries we operate in: 1 → 6
- Customers who've referred us to another customer: 22% (biggest growth channel we have)

"I want to spend one slide on what didn't go well, because pretending otherwise would be insulting to the work we did."

## What didn't work (5 min)

1. "We shipped ShipWatch v2 with a broken OAuth flow that hurt 40% of our new signups for 3 days. My fault for not requiring a fuller QA gate."
2. "We hired a VP Marketing we had to let go after 5 months. Process failure. I'm fixing it."
3. "We missed NDR target in Q3. We fixed it in Q4, but it cost us 2 quarters of growth trajectory."
4. "We delayed our Series A by a quarter. Market was soft. We closed at a fair price but probably left $10M of valuation on the table."

## What worked (10 min, with specifics)

"I'm going to name specific things I watched people do this year."

[10 specific shout-outs, each 30 seconds, naming a person and a concrete accomplishment]

## What matters next year (15 min)

"Three things."

### 1. We're going to be harder to buy from and better to work with

"We're moving upmarket. Average ACV target: $80K. That means fewer logos, more discipline, longer cycles, and much higher stakes on every deal. We're going to lose deals we would have won before. We need to be fine with that."

### 2. We're going to stop saying yes to things

"This year we built 14 features. Four of them got real adoption. Next year we're building half as many and we expect 100% adoption. Every feature gets a written kill criterion. If it doesn't hit, we remove it."

### 3. We're going to become a company that gets better faster

"Here's a metric I care about: how fast do we learn from a mistake? Right now it takes us about 6 weeks from mistake to institutional change. I want that at 2 weeks. We're going to do written retrospectives on every miss, every launch, every lost deal. Not as punishment — as an investment in next year's version of us."

## Private notes to self

- Do not allow the Q&A to go under 30 minutes. If people stop asking, wait.
- Name specific problems before celebrating. Reverse order loses people.
- Mention compensation review explicitly. Don't let people wonder.
- One mention of the mission, not five.
- Tell them how much more runway we have than people probably assume.
