# Blog Post — Why We're Pivoting (Example)

**Title: We're changing direction. Here's why.**

---

For the past 18 months, we've been building a marketing analytics platform. Today we're announcing that we're redirecting the company to serve RevOps teams instead.

This is a real pivot. Different users, different buyer, different problem. Same team, same engine underneath.

## The short version

We're better at the RevOps use case than we are at the marketing one. Our best customers already figured this out. We're following them.

## How we got here

We started ShipWatch because we'd run data teams at growing companies and were frustrated by the gap between marketing performance and real business outcomes. So we built a product to close that gap.

We got customers. Some used us as intended. Others used us for something we hadn't designed for: helping RevOps leaders understand pipeline, rep performance, and forecast accuracy.

The second group stayed longer, expanded more, and referred more. The first group churned when budgets tightened.

After a year of watching this pattern, we've concluded that the second group is our real customer. We're going to serve them well.

## What this means for current customers

Nothing immediate. You keep your current plan, your current features, and your current support. Over the next 90 days we'll talk with each of you about whether the new direction serves you better or whether we can help you find a better fit elsewhere.

For the ~30% of you already using us for RevOps workflows, you're going to love what's coming.

## What this means for our investors

You've seen the memo. The metrics supported this decision. We have runway. We have a team. We have a direction. Thanks for the trust.

## What this means for the market

We think RevOps is underserved by tools that actually understand the revenue process end-to-end. Most tools are point solutions stitched into Salesforce. We're building something different.

If you run RevOps or sell into RevOps leaders, we'd love to talk.

## What's next

- Q4: finalize the new product, transition customers who want to move
- Q1: public launch of the RevOps product
- Q2: we'll tell you if it's working

See you in a quarter.

— [Founder]
