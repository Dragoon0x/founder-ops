## Three things we're proud of

1. **We crossed $1M ARR in July.** Faster than our plan predicted. We did it with 11 people, not 20.
2. **We hired a VP Eng who we'd run through a wall for.** It took us 7 months and we rejected 4 finalists before finding the right one.
3. **Our NPS went from 38 to 62** after we rebuilt onboarding. This is the number we're proudest of.

## Three things we got wrong

1. **We built an analytics feature that nobody used.** 4 months of engineering. Shipped. Got 6% adoption. Killed it in September. Lesson: we had more opinions than evidence before we built.
2. **Our first marketing hire didn't work out.** 6-month ramp with no pipeline contribution. We terminated in August. We had interview process signal we ignored.
3. **We delayed raising the Series A by one quarter.** Market moved. We got it done at a reasonable price but we gave up ~$10M of valuation by being slow.

## 2026 plan (summary)

- Grow ARR to $6M (3.1x)
- Grow team to 42
- Hit 130% NDR
- Maintain gross margin above 78%
- End year with >18 months runway

Detailed plan in the attached deck.

## What's different in 2026

1. **Focus.** We're saying no to two adjacent markets (edtech and gov) to double down on our core (mid-market RevOps).
2. **Expansion motion.** Our top priority this year is expansion revenue from existing customers. Goal: 45% of net new ARR from existing accounts by Q4 2026.
3. **Slower hiring.** We're hiring 20 people in 2026 vs. 13 in 2025, but we're being more deliberate. Every hire needs to be 90%+ signal from the full loop, no "lean in" hires.

## What I learned about myself this year

I'm better at product than I thought and worse at sales than I thought. I pushed too many deals personally for too long. In 2026 I'm committing to <20% of my time in deals after we hire a VP Sales in Q1.
