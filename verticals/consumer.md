# Consumer — Operating Manual

## If you're new to consumer

Consumer products live and die by retention. Acquisition is rarely the
problem at scale — D30 retention is.

## Stage Operating Priorities

### Pre-PMF
- Find one cohort that comes back daily/weekly without prompting
- Track retention obsessively (D1, D7, D30)
- Talk to power users — they tell you what's missing
- Don't optimize CAC yet — fix the leaky bucket first

### Early PMF
- Healthy retention curve = "smile" (decay then stabilize)
- Activate users to "core action" within first session
- Build one acquisition channel deeply
- Goal: organic growth coefficient > 0

### Scaling
- Add second and third acquisition channels
- Tighten activation (first 7 days = make-or-break)
- Build for habit formation
- Lifecycle marketing kicks in

### Growth
- International expansion
- Brand investments
- Adjacent products / platforms
- Monetization optimization

## The Retention-First Order of Operations

Many consumer founders skip ahead. The right order:
1. **Retention** — does anyone come back?
2. **Engagement** — do they take the core action repeatedly?
3. **Activation** — can we get more new users to that point?
4. **Acquisition** — can we drive more new users in?
5. **Monetization** — can we extract more value per retained user?
6. **Virality** — can users bring more users?

Working out of order: spending on acquisition before retention is fixed
is the most expensive mistake in consumer.

## Mode Overrides for Consumer

### `metrics`
North star options:
- DAU (habit products)
- WAU (utility products)
- Sessions per user (content products)
- Transactions per user (commerce)
- Connections made (social)

Track DAU/MAU as stickiness gauge.

### `growth`
Acquisition channels for consumer (in rough order of efficiency):
1. Organic / SEO / word of mouth
2. App Store optimization (mobile)
3. Influencer (right ones)
4. TikTok / YouTube short content
5. Meta ads (efficient at small scale)
6. Google search ads
7. Apple Search Ads (mobile)
8. Out-of-home (only at scale, brand reasons)
9. TV (only at scale)

Don't run paid until organic activation is fixed.

### `pricing-deeper`
Consumer pricing sweet spots:
- App subscription: $5-15/month
- Annual: $50-150 (with 30-50% discount vs monthly)
- One-time IAP: $1-30 most common, $50-200 high-end
- Consumable: $1-10 per pack

Free + premium upsell ("freemium") works ONLY if free is genuinely useful.

### `discovery`
Consumer discovery is different from B2B:
- Users don't know what they want until they see it
- Power users >>> general users for product insight
- Behavior > stated preference
- Watch users use the product (not just interview)

## Consumer Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Optimizing acquisition before retention | Pour water into a leaky bucket |
| Building broad before deep | No one cares about a generic app |
| Copying what's working for someone else | Their audience ≠ yours |
| Adding features instead of fixing core loop | Bloat without engagement |
| Premium pricing without premium experience | Churn |
| Ignoring D1 retention | Most users churn day 1 |
| Marketing before activation works | Burn money proving acquisition isn't the problem |
