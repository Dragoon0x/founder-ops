# Deep Tech — Operating Manual

## If you're new to deep tech

Deep tech (hardware, robotics, biotech, climate, space, materials, energy)
operates on different timescales and capital structures than software.
Plan for 7-15 year journey to commercial success.

## Stage Operating Priorities

### Pre-seed (research → IP)
- Patents and IP strategy from day 1
- University tech transfer (if spinning out)
- Initial scientific or technical proof
- Academic / scientific advisor team
- Government grants (SBIR/STTR, DARPA, ARPA-E, NIH, NSF)

### Seed (lab → prototype)
- First working prototype outside controlled lab
- Cost engineering: what's BOM at scale?
- Manufacturing partner identification
- Pilot customers willing to test alpha hardware
- Convertible notes / SAFEs to bridge to Series A

### Series A (prototype → product)
- DFM (design for manufacturing) iterations
- First production runs (small batch, expensive per unit)
- Early commercial customers (often paying for innovation, not value)
- Operations team starts

### Growth (product → market)
- Volume manufacturing
- Multiple customers proving the use case
- Cost reduction toward profitable margins
- Sales/marketing team build

### Scale
- International manufacturing footprint
- Multi-product roadmap
- Strategic partnerships with large corporates
- IPO or strategic acquisition

## Capital Strategy for Deep Tech

Software startups raise $1-5M to get to PMF.
Deep tech raises $20-100M+ to get to commercial scale.

Funding sources:
- **Equity**: VCs (specialized in deep tech), corporate VCs
- **Grants**: SBIR/STTR ($300K-$2M), DARPA, ARPA-E, NSF, EU Horizon
- **Strategic partnerships**: Large corporates pre-paying for development
- **Debt** (later stage): venture debt, equipment financing
- **Government**: DoE loans, defense contracts, advanced market commitments

Most successful deep tech companies have all of the above by Series B.

## Mode Overrides for Deep Tech

### `metrics`
Deep tech metrics differ:
- TRL (Technology Readiness Level): NASA's 1-9 scale
- Cost trajectory (BOM at 10 → 100 → 1K → 10K units)
- Performance benchmarks vs incumbents
- Time to manufacture per unit
- Yield rate (% good units per batch)

### `hire`
First 10 hires very technical:
- 60%+ engineers, scientists, technicians
- Domain experts (PhDs, industry veterans)
- Operations / supply chain expertise early
- Less GTM until product proven

### `partnerships`
Critical for deep tech:
- Manufacturing partners (CMs, foundries, fab partners)
- Supply chain partners
- Regulatory consultants
- Customer pilots that fund development
- Co-development with strategic corporates

### `legal-deeper`
Deep-tech specific IP:
- Patent portfolio (10-50+ patents typical)
- Trade secrets (often more valuable than patents)
- Freedom-to-operate analyses
- IP licenses from universities (royalty obligations)
- Defense applications: ITAR/EAR compliance

## Deep Tech Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Software-style fundraising (small rounds) | Not enough capital to reach milestones |
| No grant strategy | Leave $1-5M on the table |
| Underestimating manufacturing | Lab to factory = 18-36 months |
| Ignoring cost trajectory early | "We'll fix margin later" doesn't work |
| Hiring sales too early | No product to sell yet |
| Pivoting too soon | Deep tech rewards stubbornness on the right thing |
| Not enough cash buffer | One bad quarter = company over |
