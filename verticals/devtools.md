# Developer Tools — Operating Manual

## If you're new to devtools

Developers are the buyers and the users. They hate marketing, love OSS,
test before they buy, and form strong opinions in public. Build something
remarkable, document it well, and let the community do the rest.

## Stage Operating Priorities

### Pre-PMF
- Build the tool you wish existed
- Ship in public on Twitter/X, write technical blog posts
- Get to "Hello World in <5 min"
- 100 GitHub stars is a healthy first signal
- Founders write all the code AND the docs

### Early PMF
- First paying customers (often individuals or small teams)
- Clear free vs paid line
- Documentation is half the product
- Hacker News launch (if you have 100+ users already)
- First conferences (sponsor or speak)

### Scaling
- First non-founder engineer
- DX engineer / technical writer
- Pricing solidifies (per-seat, per-resource, per-usage)
- First sales hire (only when warm enterprise inbound exists)

### Growth
- Enterprise tier with SOC 2, SSO, audit logs
- Team and company adoption playbook
- Developer advocacy program
- International expansion

## Free Tier Strategy

Three valid approaches:
1. **Generous free tier, low conversion** (Cloudflare model — needs platform play)
2. **Limited free tier, high conversion** (Linear, Vercel — most startups)
3. **No free tier, paid trial only** (sales-led from day 1, rare for devtools)

Most successful devtools: option 2.

Free tier should:
- Solve a complete problem for an individual
- Hit a wall at team/scale (forces upgrade decision)
- Cost <$5/free user/month

## Mode Overrides for Devtools

### `growth`
Channels that work:
- Organic SEO ("how to X with Y")
- Open source contribution
- Hacker News (when ready)
- Twitter/X tech community
- Sponsorship: technical newsletters, podcasts
- Conferences (speak > sponsor)

Channels that don't work:
- Cold sales calls to ICs
- LinkedIn outreach to engineers
- Display ads
- "Schedule a demo" without try-first

### `content`
Devtool content rules:
- Show code, not promises
- Compare honestly (don't trash competitors)
- Open source what you can
- Write deep technical posts (not "5 tips" listicles)
- Post on your blog AND syndicate (HackerNews, Dev.to, Reddit when relevant)

### `pricing-deeper`
Devtool pricing models:
- **Per-seat**: collaborative tools (Linear, Notion, Figma)
- **Per-resource**: infrastructure (Vercel projects, Render services)
- **Per-usage**: APIs (Stripe, Twilio, OpenAI)
- **Capacity**: managed services (MongoDB Atlas)
- **Hybrid**: most products end up here

### `hire`
Devtool first 10 typical:
- 70%+ engineers
- DX engineer / technical writer (early)
- Designer who can code (rare but valuable)
- First sales hire after 50+ paying customers
- No marketing for first 1-2 years (founders + devrel)

## Devtool Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Hiding pricing | Devs leave instantly |
| Required signup to read docs | Massive friction |
| Demo-required to try | Devs want to try first |
| Sales-led without self-serve floor | Wrong motion for devs |
| Generic enterprise messaging | Doesn't resonate |
| Closed-source for things devs expect open | Eroded trust |
| Multi-step signup (>3 fields) | Abandonment |
| Tracking pixels everywhere | Devs notice and care |
