# B2B SaaS — Operating Manual

## If you're new to B2B SaaS

The model: build software, charge recurring fees per user/account, retain customers
for years, expand revenue within accounts. Predictable revenue + high margin = high
multiple at exit.

## Stage Operating Priorities

### Pre-PMF (<$1M ARR)
- 80% of founder time on customer conversations
- Ship weekly, kill features ruthlessly
- Don't hire sales — founders sell
- Don't worry about churn metrics yet (sample too small)
- Goal: 10 paying customers who'd be miserable if you shut down

### Early PMF ($1M-$5M ARR)
- Repeatability is the question, not growth
- One channel working consistently > three "kinda working"
- First non-founder sales hire (after founders close 30+ deals personally)
- Onboarding becomes critical — bad onboarding = bad retention
- Goal: predict next quarter's revenue within 20%

### Scaling ($5M-$25M ARR)
- Sales motion solidifies (pod model, AE quotas)
- First marketing hire (likely demand gen)
- CS becomes a real function (1 CSM per $1-2M ARR)
- Build pricing/packaging muscle
- Series A territory

### Growth ($25M-$100M ARR)
- International expansion conversations
- Multi-product or platform decisions
- VP-level hires (VP Sales, VP Marketing, VP Eng)
- Series B / C
- Goal: NDR 120%+, gross margin 75%+

### Late stage ($100M+ ARR)
- Operational excellence > scrappy hustle
- M&A becomes a tool
- IPO or strategic optionality
- Quarterly board mode

## Mode Overrides for SaaS

### `pricing-deeper`
- 3-tier structure dominates: Starter / Pro / Enterprise
- Pro should be 2.5-3.5x Starter (price anchoring)
- Annual discount: 15-20% standard
- Always include enterprise "contact us" tier from day 1
- Per-seat pricing if collaboration product, usage if API/infra, flat if specific tool

### `hire`
First 10 hires roughly:
1-2: Co-founders (engineering + commercial)
3: Founding engineer (full-stack)
4: Designer or product hybrid
5-6: 2 more engineers
7: First sales hire (account executive who's done early-stage)
8: Customer success lead
9: Marketing lead (likely growth/demand)
10: Engineering manager OR head of product

### `discovery`
- Talk to 5+ prospects per week minimum at <$1M ARR
- Focus on people who CAN'T be your customer too — learn the segmentation edge
- Track: pain severity (1-10), workaround used, willingness to pay
- Cohort interviews by ICP segment

### `compete`
- Track Gartner / Forrester quadrant placement (mid-market+)
- Monitor competitor hiring (job posts = roadmap signal)
- G2 / Capterra reviews are key intel
- Don't trash — acknowledge strength, pivot to differentiation

## SaaS Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Selling to everyone | No ICP focus = unfocused product |
| Underpricing for "PMF" | Reinforces wrong signal, hard to raise later |
| Hiring sales before founders close 30 deals | No playbook to hand off |
| Ignoring NDR until later | Compounds for or against you |
| Building features per customer request | Complexity tax compounds |
| Free trial without activation goal | Trials decay without forcing function |
| One-size-fits-all onboarding | SMB vs enterprise need very different |

## Reference Benchmarks

See `benchmarks/saas-b2b.md` for full numerical benchmarks by stage.

Key targets to internalize:
- ARR growth: 200%+ (early), 100% (early growth), 60% (scale)
- Net revenue retention: 110%+ healthy, 130%+ best-in-class
- Gross margin: 75-85%
- CAC payback: <12mo SMB, <18mo mid, <24mo enterprise
- Magic number: >0.75 = invest more
- Burn multiple: <2x healthy
